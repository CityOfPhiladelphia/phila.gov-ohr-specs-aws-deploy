HOUSING AND COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT CONTRACTS MANAGER

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

          This is administrative contract work directing, through subordinate audit and program supervisors, the management, monitoring, and evaluation of contracts for the Department of Planning and Development’s Division of Housing and Community Development. The employee in the Contract Administration assignment manages subrecipient contracts, ensures compliance with regulations and reporting requirements, facilitates the administrative coordination of the release and management of Requests for Proposals (RFPs), and provides guidance to funded agencies. The employee in the Monitoring and Auditing Assignment directs monitoring and audits of contracts, evaluates performance and risk, ensures regulatory compliance, and recommends improvements to stakeholders to enhance program outcomes. Work involves developing and applying program and financial compliance standards for contracted providers. Contact with representatives of private, federal, state and local agencies is significant to the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior. 

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS:  (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.     Positions in this class must direct, through subordinate audit and program supervisors, the audit and evaluation of contracted agencies.

2.     No more than two positions shall be allocated to the class – one for each assignment.

3.     Positions in this class must perform the work in the Department of Planning and Development’s Division of Housing and Community Development.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

BOTH ASSIGNMENTS

          Develops, implements, and monitors contracts, and related policies, procedures, and performance standards for housing and community development grants; coordinates workflow, manages, assigns, and prioritizes work for staff through subordinate supervisors and monitors progress on program goals and performance metrics; provides technical guidance, training, and support to subordinate staff; reviews and approves work products, reports, and recommendations prepared by staff.

          Coordinates with other City departments, federal, state, and local agencies to ensure

compliance with applicable regulations; prepares, reviews, and submits required reports to funding or oversight agencies; participates in internal and external meetings to represent departmental programs and initiatives.

          Evaluates operational or programmatic effectiveness and recommends  improvements; ensures systems track and monitor compliance, risk, and key performance metrics; ensures adherence to internal controls, regulatory requirements, and program guidelines; assists in the development and review of budgets and financial reports; responds to inquiries and complaints related to programs and contracts.

          Performs related work as required.

 

 

CONTRACT ADMINISTRATION ASSIGNMENT

 

          Directs, through subordinate supervisors and staff, the administration of housing and community development contracts across federal, state, and local funding sources; manages the City’s use of the Integrated Disbursement and Information System (IDIS) for the disbursement of federal grants; coordinates with the Fiscal Unit to ensure timely and compliant drawdowns for reimbursable activities and adherence to drawdown target deadlines; ensures data accuracy, manages user access, and offers functional support for ACIS/OPAL; monitors administrative and program service caps; collaborates with the Monitoring Unit, Legal Counsel, City Finance, the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development, and technical assistance providers; directs Housing Counseling agencies, Housing Opportunities for Persons with Aids (HOPWA), Eviction Prevention and Mortgage Foreclosure programs, as well as community development initiatives including the cleaning and greening of vacant land.

          Prepares and submits required reporting to U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (“HUD”) standards and serves as liaison with HUD regional staff; participates in contract review committees, determines eligibility, and evaluates activities within contracts for compliance and conformance; provides recommendations to leadership on contracts and programs; directs economic contract administration and program monitoring activities;  of contracted agencies; develops schedules for site visits and monitors programmatic service standards; provides guidance and support to funded agencies on contract and program compliance.

          Directs, through subordinate supervisors, the review of program proposals, budgets, and subrecipient audits; resolves findings related to questioned costs, internal control weaknesses, and non-compliance with laws, regulations, accounting standards, and contract requirements; directs analysis of contract costs, trends, and rates, including adjustment to actual service costs and management of rate surveys; establishes and maintains systems to track compliance, risk, and performance metrics; responds to inquiries and complaints related to programs and contracts; evaluates operational and programmatic effectiveness and recommends improvements.

          Serves as the ACIS/Opal Administrator.

         

 

MONITORING AND AUDITING ASSIGNMENT

          Directs, through a subordinate supervisor, comprehensive monitoring and management reviews, proposed monitoring plan reviews, and summary reports of delegate and subrecipient agency activities for all funded housing, community and economic development programs; directs the process of ensuring compliance with government funding regulations and reporting on oversight of funded housing and community development activities; responds to compliance reports; develops, implements, and monitors program policies, procedures, and performance standards.

          Directs, through subordinate supervisors, the programmatic and fiscal monitoring audit activities including collection, evaluation and reporting of subrecipient annual audit and financial reports, IRS tax reporting and City tax verification; coordinates with other City departments, federal, state, and local agencies to ensure compliance with applicable regulations.

          Establishes reporting and evaluation systems or modifications to monitor staff performance and improve reviews of contracted agencies; directs training and outreach to funded agencies and affiliates on regulatory requirements and reporting.

          Develops and implements risk-based monitoring plans based on performance, funding, and capacity; ensures staff apply current regulatory guidance and U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) standards in review protocols and compliance procedures; coordinates with fiscal, legal, and program staff on enforcement to align audit and contract enforcement practices; advises leadership on high-risk areas and oversight improvements; manages systems for tracking audit findings, risk levels, and compliance.

          Makes recommendations on continuation of contracts and participates in contract reviews for continued funding; maintains systems for administering, monitoring and auditing Housing and Urban Development funded contracts.

          Serves as liaison with U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development to ensure compliance and integrity of departmental policies and programs; directs the investigation and resolution of complaints and incidents related to program operations and services.

          Approves, prepares, or issues audit reports upon conclusion of field audits and fiscal reviews; communicates results of final reports to department executives and agency officials, including scheduling and facilitating exit conferences; meets with department fiscal officials and attends meetings with City Finance officials for matters pertaining to department subrecipient monitoring activity.

 

 

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

•         principles, practices and procedures used in auditing agencies or organizations providing contracted services

•      principles of housing and community development, including affordable housing, homelessness prevention, neighborhood revitalization, and equitable development

•         federal, state, and local housing and community development funding programs, including grant programs and related regulatory guidelines

•          public-sector procurement and contracting processes, including Requests for    Proposals (RFPs), contract negotiation, and reporting program outcomes

•          uses and capabilities of computers and software applications related to auditing  grant management systems such as the IDIS

 

•         federal, state and local laws and regulations pertaining to subrecipient agency contracts

•         administrative principles, practices and procedures as they relate to subrecipient agency contracts and the operation of programs within the housing and community development field

•         requirements of federal and state audit standards

·                  Federal Community Development Block Grant and other federal or state housing guidelines

•         uses and capabilities of computers and software applications related to auditing and program evaluation

•         standard contract language and format as applied to federal and state audit requirements

•         principles and practices of contract and grant compliance monitoring

·                  City of Philadelphia subrecipient audit guide

•         the principles, practices, and procedures for preparing and reviewing budgets and financial reports for housing and community development agencies

 

    

ABILITY TO:

 

•         direct, through subordinate supervisors, staff engaged in program auditing, analysis and evaluation

•         develop and implement departmental program and fiscal requirements for provider contract agencies

•         effectuate subrecipient compliance with program evaluation service standards, auditing standards and reporting requirements and contract and/or grant stipulations

•         establish and maintain effective working relationships with departmental administrators, associates, contractor agency providers and representatives of federal, state and other governmental and quasi-governmental agencies

•         maintain and enhance manual and computerized evaluation, auditing and related tracking systems for the monitoring of contractual agreements

•         express ideas effectively both orally and in writing

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 03/26.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

          Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.

 

NOTE: Completion of an industry related certification or additional experience may substitute for the required education on a year for year basis.

 

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

          Two years of housing and community development experience planning, managing, or monitoring programs or contracts.

 

OR

 

          Two years of administrative, program analysis, or contract management experience.

 

OR

 

          Two years of experience auditing fiscal operations and records.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

          Two years as a Housing and Community Development Supervisor with the Department of Planning and Development which has included the monitoring of subrecipient government and non-profit agencies receiving federal, state, and local funding.

 

OR

 

          Two years of supervisory experience in contract administration, monitoring, or audit functions, including developing, coordinating, and evaluating contract activities.

 

OR

          Two years of supervisory experience directing financial or program/operational compliance audits of contracted or subrecipient agencies, including managing contract compliance reviews and evaluating audit findings.

 

          Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources that has included the specific experience described above.

 

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

          Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
