7K76
COMPUTER SERVICE TECHNICIAN I

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is technical work installing, maintaining, and repairing electronic data communication and data processing equipment in a medium to large automated data processing systems environment. An employee in this class is responsible for installing computer hardware, performing preventive maintenance, diagnosing causes of equipment failure, and performing minor repairs to computer equipment. Work is performed under the supervision of a technical or administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Installs data processing and data communication equipment such as display devices, personal computers, and printers; surveys facility for best location of equipment for user and ensures cable access and electrical line service; installs connecting cable to data processing equipment or makes connections to telephone service; sets up equipment according to manufacturers' instructions; tests terminal display devices, keyboards, and printer to ensure proper functioning.
  • Receives notification of equipment malfunctions; ascertains cause of problem; performs minor repair of data processing equipment; notifies higher level technician or vendor representative of more serious malfunctions; performs preventive maintenance to ensure system viability.
  • May maintain operational maintenance records, statistics, and data necessary for contract administration of lease, purchase, and maintenance agreements.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods, materials, tools, and practices used in the maintenance and repair of electronic data processing and data communication equipment.
  • the principles and theory of electronics and electricity as they relate to electronic data processing and data communication equipment.

SKILL IN:

  • the use of tools and equipment used in repairing and testing electronic data processing and data communication equipment.

ABILITY TO:

  • diagnose malfunctions and perform necessary maintenance and repair tasks to make computerized equipment operational.
  • read and interpret manufacturers' manuals.
  • read and understand schematic diagrams, blueprints, and sketches.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 11/1986.)
  • Completion of an associate degree program in data processing or computer technology or a related subject area at an accredited technical institute, college, or university.

  • One year of experience in the installation and maintenance of electronic data communications and data processing equipment, which has included the installation, set-up, initial testing, and repair of a variety of data communication and data processing equipment such as display devices, printers, RJE devices, and controllers.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to and during tenure of employment if required by the work assignment.
