CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2A63
LABOR STANDARDS EXAMINATION SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is labor standards compliance work interpreting and enforcing the Labor Standards Ordinance and determining if contractors are in compliance with this ordinance and other relevant laws and regulations. An employee in this class directs and coordinates the activities of a moderate-sized staff of employees engaged in conducting on-site employee interviews and inspections of city construction or rehabilitation projects and also participates in such work. Contact with departmental officials, construction contractors and their representatives and union officials is of major significance to the work. Work is performed under the general supervision of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Supervises the activities of a moderate-sized staff engaged in carrying out the provisions of the Labor Standards Ordinance such as interviewing construction employees at the construction site to determine if their payments are in accordance with established labor standards, and observing employment at work site to determine their correct work classification.
  • Conducts investigations of contractors performing city construction or rehabilitation projects to enforce the Labor Standards Ordinance; reviews payroll records and related business records submitted by mail to insure that contractors are paying employees prevailing rates; visits company offices and examines payroll journals, canceled checks, unemployment compensation reports, social security and withholding tax reports, job cost records and related documents to determine that specified prevailing wage rates have been paid for all building trades and related jobs; analyzes the costs of fringe benefits paid to non-union employees to insure that total cost of wages and fringe benefits conform to prevailing wage and benefits received by union employees; prepares work sheets and reports detailing findings.
  • Attempts to persuade employers in violation of compensation standards to rectify the situation; notifies appropriate department heads if employers do not comply; advises the commissioners of city departments on the likelihood of obtaining compliance; recommends the stoppage of payments and the barring of offenders from bidding on City projects; schedules and appears before the Labor Standards Board to testify on evidence gathered.
  • Meets with union officials and contractors to discuss alleged violations of Labor Standards Ordinance and to discuss alleged violations of collective bargaining agreements; attempts informal solution of disputes by suggesting alterations in working conditions and procedures before formal grievance procedures are instituted.
  • Assembles, maintains and updates schedules of prevailing federal wage and City wage rates, and working conditions for each occupational classification involved in city work based on current trades union contracts; maintains files on past work history of contractors engaged by the city.
  • Reviews pre-qualification contractor questionnaires to evaluate contractor's solvency; attends pre- bid and pre-construction meetings to explain and interpret the provisions of the Labor Standards Ordinance to construction firms.
  • Assigns investigator or examiner to audit individual contractors; reviews audit findings for violations.
  • Trains new enforcement inspectors in inspection procedures and practices; interprets the Labor Standards Ordinance for the inspectors and informs them of the significant provisions.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices and procedures of accounting.
  • regulations, laws and standards governing wages and benefits for employees of private firms under contract to the City.
  • standard audit procedures.
  • the principles and techniques of labor relations.

SKILL IN:

  • interviewing for the purpose of eliciting data from individuals.

ABILITY TO:

  • evaluate financial statements in order to determine conformance with labor standards.
  • work with contractors, union representatives and city officials in order to avoid or settle disputes regarding alleged violation of Labor Standards Ordinance and collective bargaining agreement.
  • present ideas effectively in oral and written form.
  • explain and interpret codes and regulations to associates, contractors, and union representatives.
  • plan, assign and review the work of enforcement personnel.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, contractors, the union and the public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/83.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in Accounting.
  • Three years of auditing experience examining the records of contractors engaged by the City to determine compliance with labor standards, laws and ordinances, two years of which has been above the trainee level.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
