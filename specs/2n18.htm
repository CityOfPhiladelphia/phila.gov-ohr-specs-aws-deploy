HEALTH PERSONNEL AND EMPLOYEE MEDICAL SERVICES DIRECTOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative work managing a City-wide industrial medicine program and directing a comprehensive personnel program for the Health Department. An employee in this class is responsible for evaluating and developing recommendations for policies, goals and objectives to be applied in program issues and problems and planning, organizing and directing the implementation of personnel and employee medical services. Contact with departmental and city administrators and officials, medical providers, union representatives and individual employees to explain program procedures and coordinate activities is a major aspect of the work. The employee in this class reports directly to the Health Commissioner.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, directs, coordinates and evaluates the activities and objectives of a departmental personnel program and City-wide medical services program; acts as major liaison in the coordination of administrative and programmatic activities within the department with related agencies; interprets and promulgates policies as they apply to departmental personnel and employee medical activities; submits recommendations for the resolution of problems and the development of new programs to obtain goals and objectives.
  • Directs, through subordinate administrators, a City-wide industrial medicine program relating to employee medical examinations and on-the-job injuries; conducts periodic reviews of employee health services activities; reviews and evaluates statistical and written reports prepared by subordinates; confers with departmental and other city officials to evaluate program effectiveness and to ascertain the need for modification of program objectives and procedures; develops program, budgetary and staffing revisions; plans, negotiates and implements contractual agreements for the provision of medical services by outside medical institutions; meets with medical personnel and contractors to discuss and resolve problems and revise services and scheduling in order to improve or maintain the quality of services; confers with City officials, the Personnel Director and departmental representatives to resolve problems and discuss revisions in medical standards and injury procedures; acts as departmental representative on committees for the review of policy issues such as workmen's compensation, employee health programs, disability cost control and safety programs; responds to inquiries and requests from departmental administrators, union officials and outside attorneys concerning medical rejections, medical policy, patient information, and duty status of individual employees; conducts medical panels to determine status of treatment and injuries.
  • Directs, through subordinate administrators, a comprehensive departmental personnel program which includes such functions as labor relations, employee discipline, training, safety, recruitment, affirmative action, and personnel record keeping; reviews and evaluates personnel programs to ensure that departmental needs and objectives are being met; reviews new policies and procedures developed by subordinates relating to the administration of personnel services; analyses or directs analysis of problem areas and makes recommendations for implementation of new services and functions to address personnel problems and long range objectives; consults with other departmental administrators on personnel related problems encountered within their divisions and recommends possible solutions or referrals.
  • Meets with top level departmental management to plan and review service programs and goals and provide input with respect to application and/or revision of personnel policies; reviews priorities established for commitment of resources in activities to meet departmental operational needs; reviews recommendations made by subordinate managers concerning proposals for new City-wide policies and procedures developed by central agencies to determine impact on personnel operations; serves as representative of the Commissioner to hear grievances and other cases appealed to the
  • Functions as departmental emergency operations coordinator; reviews and evaluates City-wide emergency plan and determines appropriate departmental responses and readiness requirements to carry out plan; develops recommendations for assignment of responsibilities to departmental personnel to insure provision of services in emergency or disaster situations; coordinates planning of departmental emergency procedures among division heads and with other City agencies.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of administration and management and their application in resolving a variety of operational and administrative problems.
  • the principles and practices of public personnel administration.
  • the methods, principles, and goals of industrial medicine as they pertain to the administration of an employee health services program.
  • the principles and practices of contract preparation and control.
  • current laws and regulations pertaining to employment connected medical evaluation and workmen's compensation requirements.
  • supervisory methods and practices.

ABILITY TO:

  • plan and coordinate the activities of technically specialized subordinates.
  • initiate and administer activities pertaining to personnel and employee health services which meet departmental and City-wide operational needs.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, administrative officials, and representatives of employee organizations and outside institutions.
  • express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/87.)
  • Possession of a master's degree from an accredited college or university in public, business or health care administration.
  • Six years of public personnel experience, two years of which shall have been at the administrative level in charge of the personnel program for a health services department and included the supervision of a staff engaged in technical personnel activities.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which had included a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
