TYPEWRITER REPAIR TECHNICIAN

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is typewriter repair and equipment management work involving the repair of manual, electric and electronic typewriters, dot-matrix printers and other related equipment, and the evaluation of department needs for and recommendations of purchases of specific typewriters. An employee in this class will also participate in the defining of specifications for typewriter service contracts and will be responsible for service contract management.

The work is performed under the supervision of an administrative superior, and involves some light physical effort.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Disassembles manual, electric and electronic typewriters, dot-matrix printers, and calculators with some mechanical components such as printing assemblies; determines malfunction and parts and equipment needed for adjustment or repair; replaces mechanical parts, electronic chips, circuit boards and other components and may repair circuit boards as needed; reassembles, calibrates and tests repaired devices; cleans, lubricates, adjusts and performs other maintenance on such equipment.
  • Operates an air compressor, grinder and buffer in repair of manual devices and the mechanical aspects of electric and electronic devices; operates electronic test equipment, soldering equipment and other tools for the repair of electronic and electric devices.
  • Analyzes usage and needs for units in the departments requesting typewriters to determine if manual, electric or electronic machines are appropriate; researches brochures, trade publications and catalogues and evaluates records of typewriter repairs to recommend purchase of specific models of typewriters and printing calculators; prepares purchase specifications for use by fiscal office staff; evaluates equipment to determine if it meets contract provisions.
  • Evaluates typewriters and other equipment to determine repairability; recommends purchase of new typewriters to replace irreparable ones; processes condemnation on irreparable devices and retains some for parts replacement purposes.
  • Prepares specifications for typewriter service contracts; determines which devices will be repair by contractors; evaluates performance of contractors and contacts them to expedite repairs or to discuss work quality problems; may recommend discontinuance of specific contractors.
  • Receives equipment repair requests; arranges for transport of new or repaired equipment; receives new equipment and checks for compliance with contract specifications; maintains records of equipment requestors, of repair of each device, and of contract service activity.
  • May maintain, adjust or repair typewriters and other equipment at their regular work locations; may drive a vehicle to make field calls or transport equipment needing repairs.
  • Keeps current on the latest developments in typewriters, dot-matrix printers and other equipment, and their servicing and repair; may receive training in electronic repair techniques or related matters.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods, materials, tools and practices necessary to the repair of manual, electric, and electronic typewriters, dot matrix printers and related equipment.
  • the makes and kinds of manual, electric and electronic typewriters and their usages and applications.

SKILL IN:

  • the care and use of tools, testing equipment and calibration equipment used in the servicing of manual, electric and electronic typewriters and dot-matrix printers.

ABILITY TO:

  • disassemble, repair, maintain and reassemble a variety of kinds and makes of manual, electric and electronic typewriters and dot matrix printers.
  • read electric and electronic schematics necessary to the repair of such typewriters and dot matrix printers.
  • provide data for the preparation of equipment and service specifications for purchase and service contracts.
  • follow oral and written instructions and to understand service manuals and testing equipment operation manuals.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/89.)
  • Education equivalent to the completion of the tenth school grade.

  • Three years of experience in the servicing and repair of manual, electric and electronic typewriters of various makes.

Or any equivalent combination of training or experience determined acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure in the class of Typewriter Repair Technician.
