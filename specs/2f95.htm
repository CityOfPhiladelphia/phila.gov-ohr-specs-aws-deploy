CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2F95
PRISONS INFORMATION SYSTEMS AND RESEARCH ANALYST

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is advanced and supervisory analytical work designing, implementing, and maintaining a computerized inmate recordkeeping system and performing other analytical administrative studies in the prisons system. The employee in this class supervises a small clerical and analytical staff in carrying out the research and development functions for the prisons and the development and implementation of information systems using computer and other automated technology. The employee reports to an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, designs, implements, and maintains the prisons' inmate recordkeeping system; discusses software and hardware packages and modifications with vendors; makes recommendations to prisons' management on hardware and software selection; surveys the utilization of hardware and software systems by other prisons for inmate recordkeeping; interviews prison managers and determines information needed to be stored by the recordkeeping system and the kinds of reports to be generated.
  • Plans and directs analytical studies of communications systems at the prisons; examines and directs the assessment of communications needs at the prisons; serves as liaison with the City's communications agency to facilitate the installation of necessary communication lines and equipment to provide expanded communications services
  • Prepares requests for proposals for professional and personal services; reviews proposals received; recommends selection of contracted services to stop level prison management; arranges for contract development by the Law Department; ensures that services rendered are in conformance with contract specifications; resolves disputes between prison staff and contractors.
  • Participates in the development of organizational goals for use in the budgetary process; develops some budgetary justifications for new programs; calculates the City funds requires to implement new programs; prepares charts and graphs to be used in budget presentations.
  • Prepares and supervises the preparation of outside funding for special projects; oversees and participate sin the preparation and submission of various reports on project progress required by grant agencies.
  • Plans and implements studies relating to inmate population and staff including demographic and stay duration studies; supervises and participate sin analytical studies of prisons operations; prepares inmate census information for federal and state agency use.
  • Attends prison executive staff meetings; participates in policy formation discussions; services on committees charged with investigating specific administrative problems.
  • Plans, organizes, and supervises the work of a small analytical and clerical staff; assigns and reviews work tasks for a small unit engaged in departmental analytical functions.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of utilizing computers for recordkeeping and management reporting and analysis in a penal institution or other human services organization.
  • the principles and practices of public administration as they relate to a penal institution or other human services agency.
  • the principles and practices of methods and procedures analysis, administrative control, work simplification, and form and record control.
  • the current uses and programming principles of one or more non-procedural languages and their application and solving managerial problems.
  • the current uses and capabilities of electronic data processing equipment.

ABILITY TO:

  • analyze and evaluate office and field production, flow of work, forms and record systems, staff utilization, and administrative efficiency and work performance in a penal or human services setting.
  • apply current non-procedural computer languages and microcomputer systems to improve agency operations.
  • supervise a small clerical and analytical staff engaged in carrying out analytical functions for a large municipal agency.
  • prepare comprehensive reports, charts, graphs, layouts, forms, and other media of information and instruction.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, officials and staff of the City's computer center, and representatives of consultant contractors.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/87.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
  • Four years of administrative experience analyzing or devising administrative methods, procedures, or operations, two years of which has been at the full performance level implementing and applying computer-based management information systems to improve operating efficiency and facilitate management reporting and analysis in a penal institution or human services agency.
Or any equivalent combination of training and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
