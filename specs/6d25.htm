CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

6D25
WATER SECURITY SERVICES MANAGER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative and technical work, directing, through subordinate supervisors, the provision of all security and investigative services for a large water utility. The employee is responsible for a comprehensive security services program, including computerized security systems, protecting all departmental facilities and moveable properties, maintaining a variety of security and conduct control programs, and conducting or directing investigations of criminal or civil complaints and backgrounds of contractors and employees. An important aspect of the work is coordination of activities with private security agencies and department administrators. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The employee must direct, through subordinate supervisors, a comprehensive security services program.
  2. Only one position may be allocated to this class.
  3. The position must be located in the Water Department.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs the provision of security for all Water Department facilities; deploys security personnel; integrates deployment of personnel with video and other electronic surveillance equipment; evaluates the security needs of all facilities; develops goals and objectives of security program; determines staff structure, deployment, and surveillance equipment required to meet objectives; evaluates effectiveness of program; directs the development and implementation of policies and procedures relating to physical security, internal and external investigations.
  • Directs the activities of staff through subordinate supervisors; directs the conduct of investigations; reviews periodic and special progress reports submitted by investigative staff; confers with subordinate supervisors regarding security of departmental property; sets performance standards for security and investigative personnel; selects, orients and trains new security personnel; evaluates their progress through consultation with subordinate supervisors, review of reports, and personal observation; trains staff in the operation of photographic, video and other specialized security and investigative equipment.
  • Evaluates security systems and other new equipment on the market and adapts such equipment to expand the security capability.
  • Develops programs and procedures intended to monitor the activities of department employees and contractors; develops standards for determining appropriate employee and contractor access to department facilities.
  • Initiates and coordinates investigations of civil and criminal complaints; performs background investigations of employees and contractors; directs investigations that are highly complex or sensitive in nature; oversees photographic and video surveillance; testifies in court as expert witness; directs preparation of evidence, statements and other technical support data for department legal counsel in pre- litigation; interviews complainants and suspects; collects and evaluates evidence; recommends continuation of investigation or notifies law enforcement officials of suspected violations.
  • Performs as liaison to the Court system and Law Enforcement in criminal/civil cases initiated by the judicial system against the Department and/or its employees; coordinates the dissemination of information as requested by the Courts or Law Enforcement; performs as liaison to the Inspector General's Office, investigating complaints involving the Water Department generated through the Inspector General's Office.
  • Directs activities of contracted private investigation personnel; assigns investigations to contracted personnel; negotiates terms of contracts.
  • Prepares operating budget proposal for the security program; monitors overtime expenditures; directs the preparation of work schedules.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • techniques used in physical, photographic and video surveillance.
  • technical security systems, their applications and limitations.
  • basic accounting principles and practices.
  • basic statistics.
  • the principles, practices, materials and equipment used in photography and related security work.
  • the techniques of collecting and examining physical evidence.
  • investigative methods and techniques applicable to a security services program.
  • supervisory methods and techniques as used in a security services program.
  • the laws, rules, and regulations relating to applicable civil and criminal cases.
  • the laws and rules of evidence and general principles of law and applicable rulings governing criminal offenses.
  • Water Department rules and regulations and criteria as they apply to bidding by private contractors.
  • the practices, methods and techniques involved in protecting and safeguarding against theft, vandalism and disruptive and illegal actions.
  • the security problems involved in protecting property against theft and vandalism.
  • security rules, regulations and procedures.
  • the laws of arrest.
  • supervisory and training methods and techniques.

SKILL IN:

  • obtaining information by interview, interrogations observation, and investigation.
  • the use and operation of various electronic surveillance devices.
  • the use of firearms.

ABILITY TO:

  • develop plans and policies, and to provide effective advice regarding investigation and security matters.
  • maintain and revise computerized file systems and security controls as applied to a comprehensive security services program.
  • cope with situations firmly, courteously, tactfully, and with respect for the rights of others.
  • establish and maintain professional confidence while performing investigations.
  • communicate effectively both orally and in writing.
  • successfully complete a firearms training program.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with City agencies, departmental authorities, law enforcement officials, and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/94.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university, which has included at least two courses in accounting, finance, or statistics.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE
  • Four years of experience performing specialized investigatory work which has included the use of photographic and video equipment; and the analysis and examination of evidence.

    OR

  • Four years of experience as a private investigator licensed in the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania, investigating civil complaints, criminal complaints and conducting surveillance.

AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

  • Two years of experience in municipal government supervising the provision of technical security services.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Water Security Services Manager.
  • Possession of a valid permit to carry firearms issued by the City of Philadelphia after successful completion of a firearms training program and during tenure of employment as Security Services Manager.
