CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2L27
PRISON BUSINESS MANAGER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative work supervising the business functions of the City's prison operation. The employee in this class supervises the preparation and monitoring of the prison's operating budget, the inmate welfare fund budget, and inmate commissary fund. The employee directs accounting, procurement, stores, and inmate accounts functions, as well as the management of a large food service operation for the prison inmate population. Included in the work is the responsibility for monitoring the fiscal elements of the prison industries program. Supervising a staff of technical and clerical employees and establishing effective working relationships with departmental administrators and other City officials are important in the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Supervises the preparation and monitoring of the prison's operating budget and inmate welfare fund budget; directs the preparation of budget estimates and the justification for needed budget increases; attends budget hearings and provides information and data to administrative officials concerning budget requests and figures.
  • Supervises the maintenance of fiscal records established to control expenditures; reviews periodic and special reports on the status of accounts and supervises the transfer of funds to meet anticipated needs.
  • Reviews requisitions, purchase orders, and invoices for propriety and conformance to budgetary requirements; supervises clerical and stores personnel engaged in keeping records, establishing inventory levels, and maintaining stores supplies; reviews warehouse procedures and inventory levels and makes recommendations for changes in the receiving and storing of stock; initiates studies concerning the standardization of supplies within the prisons and prepares recommendations for new purchases.
  • Plans and reviews the prison industries production schedule; determines the supply requirements of the prisons and whether trades shops have the capability to produce such goods in sufficient quantity and quality; directs the utilization of inmates within the prison trades shops and meets with other prison administrators to secure additional inmate help; consults with shop supervisors to determine type and amount of raw materials required and prepares cost analysis estimates of manufacturing efficiency; prepares production reports and makes recommendations concerning the establishment of new industry shops.
  • Directs the management of the prisons food service operations; oversees through subordinate supervisors the requisitioning of food, the planning of menus, and staffing of food service operations at the major prison institutions.
  • Assembles fiscal data and supporting information for use in the preparation of grant applications; meets with federal and state officials to develop procedures for implementing new programs.
  • Supervises the operation of the prison's and commissaries and the preparation of related financial statements.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • principles and practices of preparing and administering operating budgets in a municipal agency.
  • the principles and techniques of developing costs and performance statistics.
  • accounting principles.
  • administrative principles, practices, and methods.

ABILITY TO:

  • analyze departmental needs and make recommendations concerning related budget requests and administration.
  • supervise the work of a group of employees engaged in maintaining budgetary, inventory and related records.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with departmental and central agency officials.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 9/74.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
  • Three years of technical experience including one year at the journeyman level in any of the following areas: accounting, budget, personnel, or procurement.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
