CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2D04
PERSONAL PROPERTY EXAMINER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is analytical work at the full performance level examining personal property holdings to determine tax liability. An employee in this class audits the holdings of individuals, trusts, and estates to determine taxable and exempt personal property. Contact with taxpayers and their representatives to resolve problems is significant. Work is performed under the supervision of a technical superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Audits and analyzes tax returns to determine taxable and exempt holdings of personal property; audits decedents' trusts, trust accounts, and custodial estates to determine taxable and exempt personal property; analyzes federal and state income tax returns; performs call-in audits of estates and trust accounts; audits appeals, refund requests, and requests for exemptions; performs sampling audits to determine feasibility and cost-effectiveness of new taxing procedures.
  • Consults reference sources to determine exempt status of corporate and municipal bonds, consults stock and securities publications; brokerage houses, and state agencies to determine year-end values of intangible personal property; researches taxability of mutual fund portfolios; evaluates stock of closely held corporations to determine market value; determines evaluation of mortgages; calculates dividend and interest income; calculates taxpayers' capital gains and losses; calculates taxpayers' personal property holdings; determines tax liability based on value of holdings; determines residence of taxpayers and estates.
  • Confers with taxpayers, attorneys, accountants, and bank trust officials to secure information and discuss interpretation of tax laws; answers telephone inquiries from taxpayers; explains personal property tax and individual assessments to taxpayers and their representatives; reviews, researches, and resolves appeals of tax assessments; prepares allowance documents for appeals; may testify before the Tax Board at appeal hearings.
  • Oversees the work of training-level auditors and clerical employees engaged in processing assessments; attends staff meetings and training sessions; prepares reports on audits; maintains monthly audit figures; maintains records and files on taxpayer's accounts.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the Personal Property Tax Act, related legislation and relevant case law.
  • the techniques of personal property examining, auditing and assessment.
  • the Internal Revenue Code and the Pennsylvania Income Tax laws as they relate to the taxability of intangible personal property.
  • the arithmetic procedures used to determine the value of intangible personal property.
  • current developments in the areas of securities exchanges, decedents' estates, trusts, and business entities.

ABILITY TO:

  • detect errors in personal property assessments.
  • analyze corporate balance sheets and profit and loss statements.
  • establish effective working relationships with associates, government officials, and taxpayers and their representatives.
  • communicate effectively, both orally and in writing.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/1984.)

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
  • Five years of personal property assessing experience, three years of which have been assessing personal property for taxation purposes in an assigned geographic district.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum.

NOTE: Additional experience assessing or assisting in the assessment of personal property for taxation purposes may be substituted on a two-for-one basis for up to two years of college for the first test holding only.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
