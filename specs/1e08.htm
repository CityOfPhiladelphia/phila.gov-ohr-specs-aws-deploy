1E08
ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATIONS SPECIALIST


GENERAL DEFINITION

This is electronic communications project management work, planning developing and directing the installation of a computer driven 800 MHz simulcast trunked radio system, under the initial direction and use by the Fire Department for eventual utilization by all of the City's operating departments. The employee in this class is responsible for obtaining approval and subsequent acquisition of 30 800 MHZ radio frequency channels from the Federal Communica- tions Commission, insuring compliance with all communications regulatory agency mandates and developing data and voice transmission capability for the city's proposed mobile and fixed microwave radio network. Work also includes performing systems analysis and applications programming of various manual administrative processes and procedures for the Fire Department's Administrative Services Unit, through mini and personal computer devices. Conferring with the City's Communication's Committee, directing outside consultants, monitoring system and equipment installation for contract compliance and operating efficiency, interacting with vendors and managing other assigned communications projects are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the general direction of the Fire Department's Administrative Services Director.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The work must be performed under the administration of the Fire Department.
  2. Only one project management position can be allocated to this class.
  3. The employee must be responsible for the planning, development and installation of the Fire Department's phase of the Trunked Radio System.
  4. The employee must perform systems analysis and programming for the Fire Department's Administrative Services Unit.


TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Integrates the Fire Department's need for a mobile microwave radio transmission system with that of the other City operating departments; performs project management responsibilities for the City's proposed computer driven trunked radio dispatch system/management information network; confers with supervisor to determine objectives of the project, user requirements and time frames for project completion; collects information necessary to develop project implementation methodology, preliminary functional and/or systems specifications for approval and to plan the project; confers with users of the proposed communications system; determines how the system that is to be computerized works under the existing technology to determine the proposed design under new technology; prepares Gant and Critical Path charts to plan and schedule project management activities.
  • Prepares a need or cost benefit analysis; develops a preliminary program specification, taking all the collected data and information into consideration; drafts and writes request for proposal and processes it through the appropriate agencies; reviews proposals and presides at contractor and consultant question and answer sessions; makes recommendations for the selection of the most appropriate contractor and consultant; makes appropriate requests to government agencies for required forms and licenses; complies with Federal Communications Commission rules and regulations governing acquisition, operation and maintenance of radio communications equipment and the required number of MHZ radio frequency channels; ensures that all requirements covered in the proposals meet the FCC regulations; participates in the selection process by making recommendations to a city-wide communications committee on the selection of the most qualified contractor and consultant.
  • Chooses field transmitter sites and designates vendor to perform site propagation analyses; confers with consultant in the assessment of required communications hardware and software specifications; directs and monitors the activities of communications consultants and contractors; reviews their work; insures compliance with RFP and contractual agreement; prepares written reports detailing project progress, recommendations and proposed modifications for review by a city-wide communications committee made up of departmental personnel;. discusses and develops schedules for implementation of the phases of the communications project; monitors performance efficiency of the newly installed communication system's hardware and software components.
  • Confers with the selected consultant on communications system design and directs required modifications; presents preliminary results to supervisor and the City's Communications Committee for review; reviews communication specifications for equipment and computer systems prepared by consultants; discusses modifications to such specifications to insure conformance to departmental and municipal requirements; promotes the cooperation between utilities, consultants, contractors and vendors to ensure expeditious completion of the project; interprets communication laws and regulations as required; insures contractor understanding of compliance requirements, installation schedule and operational standards; inspects design and installation work in progress and upon completion; troubleshoots problems when necessary; reports on the progress of project to the appropriate administrative authorities, agencies and committees.
  • Keeps abreast of current developments in microwave communications technology, systems analysis, and data processing; reads technical journals; attends training sessions sponsored by the employer.
  • Monitors and regulates the installation and operating efficiency of communications project by selected contractor and confers with consultant on system and equipment modification; resolves project problems and disputes; insures selected vendors comply with required contract specifications.
  • Studies and analyzes existing departmental administrative procedures and processes; confers with users to gain understanding of their responsibilities, needs, and objectives; prepares work flow charts; designs database data files and screen layout and determines record number and size; installs, tests, evaluates, modifies and maintains the database; designs user access parameters to provide file security; writes back-up and recovery procedures to protect against loss of data; develops a preliminary program specification, taking all the collected data and information into consideration; codes program in appropriate language; tests programs; creates test data; checks input for accuracy and completeness; trouble shoots existing programs to identify problems; periodically meets with users to identify problems; writes narrative explanations of programs; prepares forms and documentation; conducts training sessions to familiarize departmental users with the capabilities of systems; periodically updates users on new procedures and additional capabilities; arranges training sessions when necessary; instructs users on applications and techniques of new or revised software packages.
  • Performs related work as required.


REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the theory and practice of systems analysis as applied to the development of an 800 MHZ computer driven trunked mobile and fixed municipal radio communication network.
  • smart trunking and digital addressing technology.
  • the theory and practice of systems design as applied to the development of an 800 MHZ computer driven trunked radio system which includes voice and data transmission capability.
  • operating systems for voice and data communications radio systems.
  • project management and scheduling practices using such methods as Gant chart and Critical Path techniques.
  • the techniques of applications programming.
  • the current uses, capabilities and configurations for a city wide municipal computer aided dispatch and management information network for a mobile and fixed radio system.
  • the operating systems for computer driven trunked radio communications systems.
  • the theory, methods, practices and techniques involved in the design, installation, operation and testing of computer driven trunked radio and data communications systems for a municipal operation.
  • the current technology in the field of microwave communications software involving data and voice transmission capability for a mobile and fixed radio network.
  • Federal Communications Commission rules and regulations governing the installation, operation and maintenance of computer driven trunked communications equipment for mobile and fixed radio networks with voice and data transmission capability.
  • the methods and techniques used in the evaluation and analysis of software, hardware and application systems to achieve an efficient computer driven trunked radio dispatch and management information network.

ABILITY TO:

  • determine the number of MHZ radio frequency channels required to meet the emergency radio data and voice transmission needs of the City's operating departments.
  • evaluate the City's current communication system and to make technically sound and cost effective recommendations for updating the current telecommunications system.
  • perform project management for improvement and upgrade to a city-wide mobile and fixed radio communications system, managing the planning, design, RFP preparation, installation and testing activities.
  • evaluate the configuration of radio communications system hardware and software and make appropriate recommendations.
  • monitor and enforce contract compliance with project plans, design and specifications.
  • present ideas and complex technical information clearly, both orally and in writing.
  • evaluate hardware and software for computer driven communication systems and make appropriate recommendations for a municipal operation.
  • prepare request for proposal documents.
  • prepare and evaluate specifications of communications systems and equipment.
  • evaluate the merits of project proposals from contractors and make sound recommendations.
  • complete work within the confines of a time schedule.
  • think logically and sequentially.
  • communicate computer and communications concepts to non-technical personnel.
  • interpret Federal Communication laws and regulations.
  • maintain effective working relationships with consultants, contractors, vendors, other departments, committees, governmental agencies and associates.


MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 6/1991.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university, including or supplemented by 18 semester hours of course work in computer science, decision science, information science or a closely related field and a certificate in Radio Engineering from an accredited college or technical school.

AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Two years performing system analysis and applications programming work for a mainframe, mini computer or PC computing operation.

AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

  • Three years experience at the full performance level in communications systems design, installation, operation and maintenance of a municipal radio communications network.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above and a Bachelors Degree as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
