LICENSES AND INSPECTIONS REGULATORY ENFORCEMENT MANAGER

Specialties
( Business Compliance ) ( Weights and Measures )

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is second-level supervisory inspection and code enforcement work. Employees in this class direct, through subordinate supervisors, a city-wide program of inspections, investigations, and related enforcement work to determine conformance to the provisions of state, and local statutes, ordinances, codes, regulations and standards governing commercial and business activities or commercial weighing and measuring devices. Contact with commercial and industrial representatives, officials of federal, state, and municipal agencies, and the general public is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • the position must perform second level supervisory inspection and code enforcement work in the area of specialization.
  • only one position will be allocated to a specialty.
  • the position must be located in the Department of Licenses and Inspections.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

Both Specialties

  • Directs, through subordinate supervisors, the activities of field personnel engaged in inspections, investigations, and related code enforcement work; confers with subordinate supervisors to resolve inspection and enforcement problems; develops strategies to obtain compliance; approves work schedules; directs special inspectional and enforcement programs; directs supervisory personnel regarding non-routine, sensitive, or complex inspections.
  • Explains nature of violations and code requirements; discusses findings and problems with business representatives, attorneys, and law enforcement officials; develops training programs; trains subordinates regarding inspectional methods and techniques, and the interpretation of applicable laws, regulations, and ordinances; oversees the activities of a clerical staff maintaining records and processing violations.
  • Conducts hearings for violators; allows specified time to correct violations; assigns inspectors to re-inspect to determine compliance.
  • Performs related work as required.
Business Compliance Specialty

  • Participates in the formulation of ordinances governing commercial and business activity; makes recommendations regarding rejection or approval for licenses relating to areas such as specific commercial activity on public streets, the use of commercial vehicles, the transportation of explosives throughout the city; meets with officials to plan strategies regarding vending operations during major stadium and related events; orders, through subordinate supervisors, the confiscation of merchandise or the cessation of operations; presents information and testimony at administrative or municipal court hearings concerning violations; coordinates with law enforcement and related agencies the cessation of commercial establishments which constitute a danger to public health, safety and welfare.
Weights and Measures

  • Participates in formulating new or revised weights and measures regulations; interprets laws, regulations, and ordinances pertaining to weighing, measuring, and counting devices, including pertinent aspects of weigh-master, motor fuel inspection, packaging and labeling, sale of commodities, unit price, open date, octane testing, national type evaluation and scanner legislation; attends conferences of weights and measures officials.
  • Writes purchase specifications for a variety of measures and testing apparatus; ensures that field testing apparatus is periodically calibrated for accuracy.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the provisions of codes, laws, titles, standards, regulations, and related ordinances applicable to the area of specialization.
  • methods and techniques of dealing with the public affected by applicable codes, regulations, and ordinances.
  • procedures utilized in the prosecution of violations of applicable codes, ordinances and statutes in the area of specialization.
  • field inspectional and investigative principles, practices, and techniques.
  • licensing requirements of a variety of business and commercial activities for the Business Compliance specialty.
  • approved measuring, weighing, and counting devices commonly used in commerce and industry, and the methods and equipment used to test and calibrate such devices for the Weights and Measures specialty.
  • the basic operations of a wide variety of weighing, measuring, and counting devices for the Weights and Measures specialty.
  • methods used to defraud the public by the use of short weights and measures for the Weights and Measures specialty.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
SKILL IN:

  • interpreting and enforcing applicable statutes, ordinances, and regulations impartially, firmly, and tactfully.
  • directing and coordinating the activities of field inspectional and enforcement personnel.
  • using a considerable degree of tact, diplomacy and judgment when dealing with the general public.
ABILITY TO:

  • plan, assign, analyze, and review the work of a technical and clerical staff engaged in inspectional, enforcement, and compliance activities.
  • maintain records and prepare a variety of reports.
  • reach and implement decisions firmly, tactfully, and impartially.
  • express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, commercial and industrial representatives, federal, state, and municipal officials, and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 7/95.)

EDUCATION

  • Equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE

Business Compliance

  • Two years of experience conducting inspections, investigations, and related enforcement activity to determine and effect compliance to ordinances and regulations governing commercial and business activity.

Weights and Measures

  • Two years of experience performing inspectional and enforcement work testing and certifying the accuracy of weighing, measuring, and counting devices used for commercial transactions.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

Business Compliance

  • Four years of experience supervising the work activities of a field staff engaged in conducting inspections and related enforcement work to determine and effect compliance to ordinances and regulations governing commercial and business activity.
Weights and Measures

  • Four years of experience supervising the work activities of a field staff engaged in performing inspectional and enforcement work testing and certifying the accuracy of weighing, measuring, and counting devices used for commercial and industrial transactions to determine and effect compliance to ordinances and regulations governing commercial and business activity.
PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES
  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as an L & I Regulatory Enforcement Manager.
