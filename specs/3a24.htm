3A24
CARTOGRAPHIC DRAFTING SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory map and graphics work. The employee in this class supervises a small staff of technicians engaged in the preparation of land maps and graphics work, and is responsible for quality control and adherence to contract requirements of graphic and printing services performed by vendors. Work includes responding to requests for the provision of mapping information from departmental staff, other city agencies, and the general public. Work is performed under the general supervision of a technical superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Confers with superior to define priorities, budgetary constraints and optimum methods for map and graphics projects; recommends appropriate scale, symbols and colors to depict data on maps and charts.
  • Assigns routine work to subordinates for the preparation of maps and graphics work; reviews work of subordinates to determine its conformance with cartographic standards and assignment specifications; answers questions and solves problems encountered by subordinates.
  • Reviews contract and assignment specifications to gain familiarity with them; reviews galley and page proofs of catalogs and pamphlets submitted by printing and graphics service vendors; examines the quality of paper, illustrations, print, photographs and type face for conformance with contract and assignment specifications and standards; rejects vendor submission if they do not meet standards and specifications; confers with vendor to discuss and resolve problems or alterations prior to run-off; informs subordinates and contractors of deadlines; monitors deadlines to maintain production schedules; may perform map or graphic work as required.
  • Serves as custodian of the agency's mapping, photo, and publication filing system; operates existing computer hardware and software in performance of work as necessary.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods, principles, practices, and drafting instruments used in map preparation.
  • the methods, principles, and practices used in the reduction and reproduction of maps and in the preparation of graphic materials.
  • the techniques used in interpreting aerial photographs.
  • techniques used in preparing materials for publication.
  • printing production requirements and limitations.

ABILITY TO:

  • supervise employees engaged in cartographic drafting.
  • plan and schedule work to meet pre-established deadlines.
  • read and interpret printing and graphic contract specifications.
  • perform accurate arithmetic and geometric computations in the preparation of maps and charts.
  • present data and statistics in graphic form.
  • prepare difficult technical maps.
  • communicate effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • understand and follow written and oral instructions.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/87.)
    EDUCATION
  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

    AND
    GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Four years of drafting experience which has involved preparing maps and street plans.

    AND
    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

  • Two years of experience preparing complex and detailed maps and performing layout and graphics work for publication.

Or any equivalent combination of training and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

NOTE:
Completion of two years of course work in Cartography or Civil Engineering Technology at an accredited college or university may be substituted for two years of the general experience required.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
