6F51
STREET LIGHTING INSPECTOR I

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is technical field work inspecting contracted street light installation and maintenance projects. An employee in this class is responsible for interpreting contract requirements and for informing contractors and supervisors of non-conformity with contractual agreements and construction violations. Employees in this class ensure that street light installation and maintenance projects are carried out according to plans and specifications. Work is performed under the supervision of a technical superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Inspects street light installations; reviews plans and specifications of new installations; inspects material to be installed to ensure that it meets specifications; tours job site to ensure that work is performed in accordance with plans and specifications; informs contractors and superiors when requirements are not being met; reinspects areas where violations are found to see that corrective action has been taken; keeps records of materials used and submits progress reports of work performed.
  • Inspects the work of contractors performing such preventive maintenance as bulb washing and replacement; checks bulbs; ensures that contractors fulfill the required amount of work.
  • Investigates complaints of inadequate or deficient street lighting and alley lights; prepares forms recommending additional lights when warranted.
  • Uses voltage and amperage meters to determine the presence of and amount of current on street lights; uses those meters to assist in solving electrical problems.
  • May attend pre-construction meetings designed to define contract terms.
  • Prepares reports detailing changes in lighting, inspections completed, and descriptions of contracted work done.
  • Prepares plot plans and mechanical drawings of street light conditions; takes samples of materials for laboratory testing.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • inspectional methods and procedures.
  • the practices and procedures involved in the installation and maintenance of street lights.
  • blue print, specification, and shop plan reading and interpretation.
ABILITY TO:

  • learn the principles of electricity as they apply to street lights and street lighting connections.
  • recognize shop plan, blue print and specification deviations, improper or faulty installation and maintenance, and recommend corrective actions.
  • determine the conformity of material quantities, and workmanship methods and quality with contract requirements, plans and specifications.
  • interpret, explain and enforce compliance with contract specifications firmly and tactfully.
  • express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, contractors, and others.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/86.)

  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • Two years of inspection or trades experience which has included reading and interpreting plans and specifications as a significant aspect of the work.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

NOTE:
Completion of six credits in post high school courses dealing with construction or engineering technology may be substituted for the experience requirement indicated above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Street Lighting Inspector I.
