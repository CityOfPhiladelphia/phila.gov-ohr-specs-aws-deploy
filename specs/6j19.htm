CUSTOMER CARE SUPERVISOR

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

          This is customer service work at the supervisory level in the Philadelphia Water Department Customer Contact Center or for the Philadelphia International Airport’s (PHL) Guest Experience unit. Employees in the Water Department Customer Contact Center supervise and provide direction to lower level customer service staff responsible for investigating and resolving complaints pertaining to utility billing, establishing payment agreements for residential and commercial accounts to resolve utility account issues, addressing service problems and communicating to customers about water and wastewater system problems. Employees in the Philadelphia International Airport’s (PHL) Guest Experience unit supervise and provide direction to lower-level customer service staff responsible for providing customer service, resolving in-person customer inquiries, reporting facility issues, and providing on-going follow up with airport staff and stakeholders until resolution to consistently improve guest experience throughout all seven terminals and Customs. Responsibility for assigning and reviewing work, evaluating performance, coaching and ongoing development of lower level employees is significant to the work. The work of this class differs from lower level classes in that employees are responsible for supervising operations, analyzing customer service metrics to ensure efficiency and compliance with departmental procedures and standards. Employees have significant contact with the general public and perform work under the direct supervision of an administrative or technical superior. 

           

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

 

1.    Employees in this class must perform customer service work at the supervisory level in the Water Department Customer Contact Center or in the Airport Guest Experience unit.

2.    No more than eight (8) positions will be allocated to this class in the Water Department.

3.    No more than eight (8) positions will be allocated to this class at Philadelphia International Airport.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

BOTH ASSIGNMENTS:

 

Supervises subordinate staff and provides technical direction on responding to inquiries; assigns work to employees and verifies the work of assigned employees for accuracy, proper work methods, techniques, and compliance with applicable standards and specifications; works with employees to correct deficiencies; implements performance management procedures; analyzes customer service metrics to develop of goals and objectives to improve customer service efficiency; assists management in developing policies and procedures and implements changes and improvements in customer service; collaborates with other supervisors and managers to drive continuous improvement.

Reviews responses to and may respond to complex inquiries in a courteous manner; researches unresolved customer problems and complaints to provide solutions.

Performs related work as required.

 

WATER CUSTOMER CONTACT CENTER ASSIGNMENT

 

Ensures utility billing is completed accurately and in a timely manner; explains departmental billing and collection policies and procedures to the public; communicates with customer regarding water and sewer emergency situations; responds to sensitive and emergency Water and Sewer service events.

Directs all employee training in customer service protocols including appropriate methods, procedures, and techniques; reviews training content, and daily activity schedules for all customer service staff; presents classroom training for new employees; monitors employee calls and provides ongoing coaching and development.

Uses various computer systems to manage utility customer accounts and make data changes and service requests; prepares reports of Contact Center operations and makes recommendations to superior for system improvements.

         

 

AIRPORT GUEST EXPERIENCE ASSIGNMENT

 

          Resolves guest complaints, facility issues and recurring legacy issues; documents issues and makes recommendations to managers; supervises information service teams and the Care Team employees in Customs; oversees all aspects of PHL’s therapy dog volunteer program.

          Welcomes guests in terminals; monitors subordinate performance levels and professionalism in assigned locations; creates monthly data reports of activities within assigned locations; creates schedules, trains, communicates daily priorities to and monitors the work of subordinates.

          Updates Airport’s database on comments, complaints, and questions and provides follow up with stakeholders to find complaint resolution.

Responds to guests’ calls regarding inquiries, guest experience complaints, and requests; interviews guests via telephone to gain an accurate account of the nature of the related inquiry or request.

Provides accurate processing information to guests arriving from international flights in customs as needed; assists with Airport special events and tours of airport facilities.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

BOTH ASSIGNMENTS

·       principles of training and performance management

·       methods, procedures, practices, and terminology used in billing and financial record keeping work

·       customer service training methods and techniques

·       supervisory methods and techniques

·       modern office procedures, methods and equipment including computers

·       computerized software applications and systems for billing and account management  

·       principles and practices of quality assurance metrics and reporting

·       business communication and basic report preparation

·       methods and techniques used in customer service

·       English usage, spelling, grammar, and punctuation

 

 

PWD CUSTOMER CONTACT CENTER ASSIGNMENT

 

·       principles and procedures of the Water Department Customer Contact Center

·       Water Revenue Bureau policies and procedures pertaining to water and sewer billings and associated codes and fees

·       Water Department rules and regulations pertaining to water and sewer services

·       Water Department operations, programs and services related to customer accounts

·       state and federal regulations that dictate the operational requirements of the Water Department, e.g., the Clean Water Act and Safe Drinking Water Act

 

SKILL IN:

 

·       investigating unresolved, complex customer inquiries and determining next steps for inquiry resolution

·       utilizing a computerized software application to track and edit customer inquiries directing staff with responsibility for interpreting and applying departmental policies and procedures and customer service techniques effectively

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·       supervise assigned staff

·       identify performance deficiencies and train lower level staff

·       coach and train lower level employees

·       monitor and direct development and test in proficiencies required for customer service tasks

·       understand the role of the Customer Care staff in departmental operations and collaborate with other agencies as needed

·       understand, interpret, and apply administrative and departmental policies and procedures

·       make recommendations for improvements in customer service procedures

·       exercise tact and judgment in responding to inquiries and resolving complaints and problems

·       perform mathematical computations rapidly and accurately

·       find and reconcile discrepancies in balancing accounts

·       operate and use modern office equipment including a computer and various software packages

·       communicate clearly and concisely, both orally and in writing

·       establish and maintain effective working relationships with department officials, associates and the public

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/24.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

          Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

 

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

OPTION 1:

 

          Two years of customer service experience in a high-volume utility call center.

 

OR

 

          Two years of customer service experience in an airport.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

OPTION 1: WATER CUSTOMER CONTACT ASSIGNMENT:

 

          Two years of customer service work at the full performance level in the Philadelphia Water Department Customer Contact Center.

 

OR

 

Option 2 FOR AIRPORT GUEST EXPERIENCE ASSIGNMENT:

 

          Two years of experience at the full performance level in the Airport’s Guest Experience unit as a Customer Care Specialist.

 

OR

 

          Two years of full performance level experience in Airport communication services or maintenance dispatch work.

 

OR

 

          Any equivalent combination of training and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources which has included the specific experience as listed above.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

          Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

AIRPORT GUEST EXPERIENCE ASSIGNMENT ONLY:

 

Must be able to pass and maintain Security Identification Display Area (SIDA) and Airfield Operations Area (AOA) clearance.
