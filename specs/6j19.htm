WATER CUSTOMER CARE SUPERVISOR

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is customer service work at the supervisory level in the Philadelphia Water Department Customer Contact Center. Employees in this class supervise and provide direction to lower level customer service staff responsible for investigating and resolving complaints pertaining to utility billing, establishing payment agreements for residential and commercial accounts to resolve utility account issues, addressing service problems and communicating to customers about water and wastewater system problems.  Responsibility for assigning and reviewing work, evaluating performance, coaching and ongoing development of lower level employees in the Customer Contact Center is significant to the work. The work of this class differs from lower level classes in that employees are responsible for supervising Customer Contact Center operations, analyzing customer service metrics to ensure efficiency and compliance with departmental procedures and standards, and responding to sensitive and emergency Water and Sewer service events. Employees have significant contact with the general public and other city departments and perform work under the direct supervision of a higher level administrative superior. 

             

ALLOCATING FACTORS:  (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

 

1.    Employees in this class must perform utility customer service work at the supervisory level in the Water Department Customer Contact Center.

2.    No more than eight (8) positions will be allocated to this class in the Water Department.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

Supervises subordinate Contact Center staff and provides technical direction on responding to billing and service  inquiries; assigns work to employees and verifies the work of assigned employees for accuracy, proper work methods, techniques, and compliance with applicable standards and specifications; ensures utility billing is completed accurately and in a timely manner; works with employees to correct deficiencies; implements performance management procedures; analyzes customer service metrics to develop of goals and objectives to improve customer service efficiency; assists management in developing Customer Contact Center policies and procedures and implements changes and improvements in customer service; collaborates with other supervisors and managers to drive continuous improvement.

Reviews responses to and may respond to complex public inquiries in a courteous manner; researches unresolved customer problems and complaints to provide solutions; explains departmental billing and collection policies and procedures to the public; communicates with customer regarding water and sewer emergency situations.

Directs all employee training in customer service protocols including appropriate methods, procedures, and techniques; reviews training content, and daily activity schedules for all customer service staff; presents classroom training for new employees; monitors employee calls and provides ongoing coaching and development.

Uses various computer systems to manage utility customer accounts and make data changes and service requests; prepares reports of Contact Center operations and makes recommendations to superior for system improvements.

            Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         principles and procedures of the Water Department Customer Contact Center

·         Water Revenue Bureau policies and procedures pertaining to water and sewer billings and associated codes and fees

·         Water Department rules and regulations pertaining to water and sewer services

·         Water Department operations, programs and services related to customer accounts

·         state and federal regulations that dictate the operational requirements of the Water Department, e.g., the Clean Water Act and Safe Drinking Water Act

·         principles of training and performance management

·         methods, procedures, practices, and terminology used in billing and financial record keeping work

·         customer service training methods and techniques

·         supervisory methods and techniques

·         modern office procedures, methods and equipment including computers

·         computerized software applications and systems for billing and account management  

·         principles and practices of quality assurance metrics and reporting

·         business communication and basic report preparation

·         methods and techniques used in customer service

·         English usage, spelling, grammar, and punctuation

 

SKILL IN:

 

·         investigating unresolved, complex customer inquiries and determining next steps for complex billing and service issues

·         utilizing a computerized software application to track and edit customer contact, accounts, and manage service orders

·         directing staff with responsibility for interpreting and applying Water Revenue Bureau policies and procedures and Customer Contact Center customer service techniques effectively

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·         supervise assigned Customer Contact Center staff

·         identify performance deficiencies and train lower level staff

·         coach and train lower level employees

·         monitor and direct development and test in proficiencies required for customer service tasks at Customer Contact Center

·         understand the role of the Customer Contact Center in departmental operations and collaborate with other agencies as needed

·         understand, interpret, and apply administrative and departmental policies and procedures

·         make recommendations for improvements in customer service procedures

·         exercise tact and judgment in responding to inquiries and resolving complaints and problems

·         perform mathematical computations rapidly and accurately

·         find and reconcile discrepancies in balancing accounts

·         operate and use modern office equipment including a computer and various software packages

·         communicate clearly and concisely, both orally and in writing

·         establish and maintain effective working relationships with department officials, associates and the public

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/20.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

 

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

            Two years of customer service experience in a high-volume utility call center.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

            Two years of customer service work at the full performance level in the Philadelphia Water Department Customer Contact Center.

 

OR

 

            Any equivalent combination of training and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources which has included the specific experience as listed above.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
