REVENUE COLLECTION ENFORCEMENT SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory collection enforcement work directing the activities of subordinates engaged in the collection of delinquent water revenue receivables through various judicial processes. Employees in this class supervise the preparation of cases for the Tax Revenue Board, the Court of Common Pleas and Municipal Court, oversee the conduct of appellate hearings, and direct the resolution of complex billing or payment matters. Contact with attorneys, court officials and the general public is significant to the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Supervises the conduct of hearings for water customers appealing billings, penalties, or prosecutorial action; supervises the filing of municipal and common pleas complaints against delinquent water customers; directs and instructs representatives regarding testimony in response to subpoenas.
  • Supervises the research of delinquent water and sewer accounts and the preparation of supporting documentation for cases to be adjudicated; reviews documentation prior to presentation of court case; confers with subordinates about court cases to ensure adherence to applicable laws and regulations; handles the more complex and difficult billing and collection disputes; confers with water customers and their representatives to explain technical aspects of regulations and policies.
  • Receives and reviews special collections and appeals requests from departmental administrators or city attorneys; confers with superior to determine scope and methodology for special revenues collection projects; confers with subordinates to ensure that court decisions are executed in conformance with legal administrative requirements; coordinates the preparation of reports; interprets laws, regulations, ordinances, and codes to subordinates and the general public; monitors the progress of enforcement activities by subordinates; acts as liaison with city attorneys regarding water revenue policies and procedures; trains subordinate enforcement representatives and customer collection representatives.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the laws, ordinances, regulations and policies pertaining to water revenue collection enforcement.
  • the clerical procedures necessary to prepare cases for court, to generally facilitate the work of the court, and to implement the decisions of the courts.
  • the methods and techniques used in billing and collection activities.
  • investigative methods and techniques.
  • the functions of various administrative boards or courts, such as Tax Review Board, and Municipal and Common Pleas Courts.
ABILITY TO:

  • supervise employees performing a variety of revenue collection enforcement activities, including providing court testimony and presiding over informal hearings.
  • interpret and apply ordinances, laws, rules and regulations as they pertain to water revenue enforcement in a fair and impartial manner.
  • enforce procedures for collection of delinquent water and sewer bills.
  • review decisions and recommendations concerning continuances, withdrawals, appeals, conditional fines, and settlements for conformance with departmental regulations and policies, and applicable laws and regulations.
  • express and present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, court representa- tives, city attorneys, departmental administrators, and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/89.)

  • Education equivalent to the completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • Six years of clerical experience, two years of which have involved specialized customer contact concerning the collection of delinquent water and sewer bills and the establishment of payment agreements, and one year of which has included the representation of the Water Revenue Bureau in court matters pertaining to delinquent water and sewer bills.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
