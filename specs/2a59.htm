CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2A59
ECONOMIC CRIME INVESTIGATION SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is financial analysis work supervising and participating in the examination of financial records and management reporting systems of both City agencies and private businesses to gather evidence in support of criminal investigations of economic crime. The employee in this class provides financial analysis for use in criminal investigations. The employee is also responsible for the design and implementation of computer-based information systems to facilitate case management and financial analysis. Supervision of employees engaged in the examination of financial records and contacts with representatives of businesses in the course of investigations are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs or participates in the review of preliminary data on criminal investigations of economic crimes to determine the type of records required for analysis; makes field trips to various business and organizations under investigation to survey record keeping systems; requests access to financial records through subpoena or search warrant process; participates in the seizure of records as required.
  • Instructs investigational staff on the type of material to be collected and the type of accounting entries and transactions indicative of fraud or other white collar crimes; reviews the materials gathered by investigational staff to establish a paper trail for criminal transactions; analyzes journals, vouchers and related financial documents to identify criminal transactions; reviews management information systems and operational procedures to determine the method of committing an economic crime within the accounting system.
  • Prepares reports detailing investigation findings; meets with attorneys and investigational staff to discuss how findings impact on criminal investigation; recommends procedures for explaining complex accounting systems and audit findings as evidence.
  • Testifies in court as to the audit procedure utilized and the findings of financial analyses; conducts training sessions to familiarize staff and users within the department with the capabilities of microcomputers and the use of microcomputers as investigative tools.
  • Conducts training sessions both in-house and outside the department on economic crime prevention.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the generally accepted principles and practices of accounting.
  • the principles, practices and procedures used in auditing agency operations.
  • governmental accounting.
  • public accounting.
  • modern administrative principles.
  • the uses and capabilities of electronic data processing equipment as applied to the auditing of fiscal operations, with particular emphasis on micro-computers and microcomputer applications.
  • the techniques for recognizing fraud in financial records.
  • the laws governing the conduct of criminal investigations.

ABILITY TO:

  • plan, supervise and review the work of investigators engaged in collecting data as evidence in financial investigations.
  • organize, edit and present clearly and concisely, orally and in writing, the results of financial investigations.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/88.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in accounting.
  • Five years of experience in the auditing of a variety of fiscal operations and records, two years of which have been at the full performance level in support of criminal investigations of economic crimes.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
