ADMINISTRATIVE ASSISTANT – CONFIDENTIAL

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is administrative staff work serving as an assistant to a senior level official or Human Resources Manager.  Positions perform a variety of assignments of a confidential nature in support of a Human Resources Manager, Department Director or Commissioner or Deputy Director or Commissioner.  Positions are distinguished from non-confidential positions by their access to information subject to use in collective bargaining or their close continuing relationship with a Department Director or Commissioner or a Deputy Director or Commissioner associated with collective bargaining.  Work may also include supervision of a small clerical or administrative support staff.  Assignments require familiarity with the functions of the agency in which the position is employed.  Interacting with departmental and divisional administrators and officials in other City departments and developing and recommending work standards and procedures are significant to the work. Work is performed under the supervision of a Human Resources Manager or senior level official.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS:     (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The position must perform a variety of assignments in support of a Departmental Human Resources Manager or a Department Director or Commissioner or Deputy Director or Commissioner.

 

2.    Only one position each will be allocated to a Human Resources Manager or a Department Director or Commissioner or Deputy Director or Deputy Commissioner.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK

 

Assists senior level official or Human Resources Manager in the fulfillment of their tasks; completes administrative details as assigned; may supervise clerical or administrative staff as required to perform administrative processes; conducts research on a variety of topics; gathers information for presentation to superior; prepares synopses of information; recommends course of action.

Performs research, and prepares reports and recommendations on topics related to the collective bargaining process; has access to information subject to use in collective bargaining; presents recommendations associated with collective bargaining to a Human Resources Manager, Department Director or Commissioner, or a Deputy Director or Commissioner.

Investigates specific administrative problems; reviews literature and obtains information on current practices related to job assignments; submits such information with possible solutions in either oral or written reports.

Studies and analyzes limited areas of operational procedures and the related use of personnel and equipment; evaluates efficiency of current operations and makes recommendations concerning their modification and improvement; prepares detailed reports of findings and recommendations; maintains records and files of reports and studies for use by a superior.

Serves in a liaison capacity with other departmental managers, subordinate office staff and extra-agency contacts; responds to queries directed to superior; routes mail and superior's instructions to appropriate parties; prepares written correspondence under own or superior's signature; establishes cooperative working relationships with other departments, agencies, vendors as required.

Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         supervisory practices and techniques if required by assignment

·         policies and practices in such areas as operations, budgeting, organization,

staffing, and procurement as required by departmental assignment.

·         organization and operations of City departments.

·         sources of information, methods, and techniques used in administrative research.

·         interactive computer terminal entry and retrieval procedures.

·         the uses and capacities of word processing and spreadsheet software programs

such as Microsoft Word and Excel.

·         business arithmetic

·         business English and spelling

·         research methods and data analysis

 

SKILL IN:

 

·         communicating ideas clearly and concisely

·         representing supervisor's interests to other staff.

 

ABILITY TO:

 

  • learn departmental functions, operations and organizations, especially as they apply to the function assigned.
  • interpret a variety of narrative and statistical reports.
  • make judgmental decisions where several courses of action are presented.
  • understand and follow written and oral instructions.
  • carry out assignments requiring the organization of material and development of procedures.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with representatives of community groups and civic organizations, associates, departmental and central agency officials, and the general public.
  • conduct studies and analyses to develop sound operational procedures
  • use a computer or terminal keyboard for data entry and retrieval of word processing and spreadsheet tasks.

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/15.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

Five years of advanced level clerical, administrative or executive secretarial experience at or above pay range 11 or EP 7.

 

OR

 

Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources that has included the specific experience described above.

 

NOTE:            An Associate's Degree in Business Administration or related field may be substituted for two years of experience.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
