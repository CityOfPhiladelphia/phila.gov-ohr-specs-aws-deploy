CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

7E53
WATER PUMPING STATIONS ASSISTANT CHIEF

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory work directing, through subordinate crew chiefs, preventive, predictive and corrective maintenance programs, emergency repair activities and operations for on of the three pumping subsections in the City's water, wastewater and stormwater conveyance system. An employee in this class is responsible for the development and implementation of preventive and predictive maintenance schedules and corrective maintenance plans to achieve efficiency objectives in the operation and availability of physically dispersed auxiliary equipment and other related water, wastewater and stormwater conveyance equipment and intake and storage facilities. Conferring with water operations personnel, contractors and public utilities officials in maintaining pumping operations during emergency and major maintenance situations is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the supervision of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, assigns and supervises, through subordinate crew chiefs, the work of a large staff engaged in the inspection, maintenance and repair of electrical and mechanical pumping and auxiliary equipment and controls at pumping stations, water, wastewater and stormwater treatment plants, reservoirs and water, stormwater and wastewater storage and intake facilities for one of three sections of the City's pumping system.
  • Plans, assigns and supervise the general upkeep of buildings and grounds at pumping stations, reservoirs, water and wastewater intake facilities and grounds surrounding elevated and underground storage tanks.
  • Plans, assigns and supervises the maintenance and repair of floating protective covers on reservoir water surfaces; plans, assigns and supervises preventive maintenance activities and installation of electrolysis equipment, electrically operated transmission valves and elevated and underground water and wastewater storage tanks located throughout the City; confers with crew chiefs and inspectors to resolve difficulties that may arise; confers with public utility officials, contractors and departmental personnel on the feasibility of transferring or shutting down pumps or station power lines to maintain operation during emergency and unusual situations.
  • Periodically tours facilities to inspect and observe the operation of the machinery and equipment; develops preventive and predictive maintenance schedules based on the availability of production levels of pumps and auxiliary equipment; advises crew chiefs of the procedures or activities to employ in cases of major break-downs of machinery or equipment; develops plans and specifications and supervises the construction and installation of new and revised mechanical/electrical auxiliary pumping equipment; prepares plans and specifications for alteration to pumping stations buildings and grounds.
  • Maintains inventories of supplies and materials; prepares purchase requisitions to replace stock when necessary and accounts for petty cash fund purchases.
  • Prepares initial budget request detailing justification for needed items or services; confers with supervisor on the appropriateness of requests.
  • Interviews and makes recommendations on the selection of new employees.
  • Participates in setting up and conducting courses of instruction for operating and maintenance personnel as required; conducts safety meetings as necessary.
  • Reviews and develops modifications in the maintenance and production record keeping systems; reviews pumping equipment efficiency and availability levels through current and historical data analyses; reviews and analyzes a variety of maintenance and production reports from subordinate crew chiefs to determine achievement of efficiency objectives; submits periodic maintenance and production reports for supervisory review.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices governing the operation and maintenance of pumping systems.
  • the principles governing the operation testing and maintenance of pumping equipment, motorized water transmission valves and protective reservoir covers and linings.
  • the tools, instruments and practices involved in the repair of pumping equipment and appurtenances.
  • hydraulic, mechanical and electrical principles as required in the operation and maintenance of a large pumping system.
  • the hazards and safety precautions pertaining to high voltage equipment operation and maintenance, protective reservoir covers and linings and elevated and underground storage tanks.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
  • administrative principles and practices which include assisting in the preparation of the operating budget and monitoring budget expenditures.
  • computer application and program modification as they relate to the maintenance and revision to computerized records of water pumping activities.
  • inventory maintenance and control principles.

ABILITY TO:

  • coordinate the operations of river pumping stations, including repairs, maintenance and reconstruction, for the greatest efficiency and economy.
  • inspect and evaluate pumping station operations and analyze operating reports.
  • assign and review the work of personnel engaged in the various aspects of pumping maintenance.
  • develop and implement emergency pumping schedules and procedures.
  • read and interpret blueprints and develop designs and specifications for new and revised pumping and auxiliary equipment and pumping stations buildings and grounds.
  • trouble-shoot break downs and devise methods for repairs.
  • detect malfunctions in equipment or its operation and to initiate the proper corrective measures.
  • maintain effective working relationships with departmental and public utility officials, associates and contractors.
  • maintain computerized records of pumping equipment maintenance, operations and personnel.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 8/90.)
    EDUCATION:
  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

    AND

    GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

  • Four years of experience in the maintenance and repair of large industrial pumping equipment.

    AND

    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

  • Two years of experience supervising a crew engaged in the maintenance and repair of large industrial pumping equipment.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to and during tenure of employment as a Water Pumping Stations Assistant Chief.
