CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2A98
ACCOUNTING ASSOCIATE

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is accounting work preparing entries to and maintaining accounting journals and ledgers and preparing financial statements and assigned portions of reports. Employees in this class may receive increasingly difficult accounting assignments in order to prepare them for accounting work at the full performance level.

Work is performed under the close supervision of an accounting supervisor. Assignments are stable in nature and are performed in accordance with standard accounting practices and general work instructions. Work affects the accuracy of accounting assignments and the employee provides information for use by management officials.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Post encumbrances, expenditures, revenues, and disbursements to general and subsidiary ledgers; makes adjusting entries; verifies fiscal data with original financial documents to locate and correct possible discrepancies; reconciles and balances accounts; prepares periodic financial statements.
  • Allocates costs to a variety of activities according to established procedures.
  • Prepares reports, or assigned portions of reports, on various phases of fiscal activities by compiling, computing, and comparing figures of various accounts; gathers data on past financial activities for use in the preparation of budgets.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • accounting principles and practices.

ABILITY TO:

  • maintain general and subsidiary ledgers and journals.
  • prepare periodic financial statements and reports.
  • learn, within a reasonable time, the accounting procedures of city departments.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 8/72.)
  • Completion of an associate degree or equivalent certificate program from an accredited college or university which has included at least 15 semester hours in accounting.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
