2M76
WATER OPERATIONS ADMINISTRATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative work directing the provision of administrative services for the water pollution control or water treatment programs. The employees in this class are responsible for the provision of such services as: operating budget preparation and control, financial planning and analysis, operational analysis, procurement of good and services, contract administration, and personnel planning and control for the water treatment or water pollution control programs. A significant aspect of the work is supervision of administrative and clerical personnel. The employees in this class are supervised by a Water Engineering Assistant Manager.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Coordinates the annual sewer fund or water operations budget preparation; confers with central budget agency officials and departmental fiscal personnel to determine funding limits and changes in budget preparation policies and procedures; gathers and analyzes data on revenues, expenditures and operations necessary for budget preparation; requests the elaboration of justification and production statistics from section supervisors; assembles and drafts budget documents; attends budget meetings and hearings to justify allocations and requests.
  • Maintains operating budgets by reviewing requested expenditures; recommends approval or disapproval of requested expenditures; prepares and reviews a variety of reports on the fiscal operations of programs; recommends the transfer of money between budgetary accounts.
  • Expedites emergency purchases, resolves delays in procurement processes by contacting central procurement agency, vendor or section chief.
  • Reviews requests from program personnel to establish new positions; suggests classifications for proposed positions; advises supervisors of appropriate personnel policies and procedures; explains departmental disciplinary procedures; acts as liaison between program personnel and departmental personnel office.
  • Recommends that analyses be made of program operations; conducts analyses of manpower utilization, equipment work hours, overtime usage, sick leave usage, payroll and cost accounting procedures; recommends changes in operations and procedures based on analysis of operations.
  • Coordinates the provision of support services to departmental administrative employees including photographic services and transportation arrangements; advises employees on a variety of administrative problems related to the operation of division programs.
  • Performs special analytical assignments of a non-recurring nature; makes recommenda- tions for implementation of new programs.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

  • Thorough knowledge of the principles and practices of public administration.
  • Thorough knowledge of the principles and practices of public finance as applied to the preparation and control of operating budgets.
  • Thorough knowledge of the principles and practices of scientific management and their application in solving a variety of operational and administrative problems, particularly as they relate to support services in a large public agency.
  • Considerable knowledge of the principles and practices of administrative analysis in office management.
  • Knowledge of personnel administration, practices and procedures.
  • Knowledge of the principles and practices of financial analysis.
  • Skill in evaluating the effectiveness of administrative functions and in making procedural changes to provide for more effective and efficient operations.
  • Ability to learn the regulations governing the operation of a water treatment facility.
  • Ability to learn the operational requirements of a treatment facility.
  • Ability to supervise the activities of a staff of administrative and clerical personnel.
  • Ability to analyze and evaluate a variety of facts and data.
  • Ability to establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, representatives of central agencies and the general public.
  • Ability to present ideas with a high degree of effectiveness, both orally and in writing.
  • Ability to recognize, analyze and make recommendations to resolve problems.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 11/1984.)

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
  • Four years of administrative experience, two years of which shall have been at the full performance level in an operating division of a water utility.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
