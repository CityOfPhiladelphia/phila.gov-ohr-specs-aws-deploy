CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2D40
REAL PROPERTY APPEALS SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory technical work directing the City's real estate appeals and exemptions program. The employee in this class evaluates taxpayer appeals of real estate assessments and applications for tax exemptions, and makes recommendations concerning settlement of appeals and granting of exemptions to the Board of Revision of Taxes. Supervision of subordinate technical personnel engaged in investigating appeals and clerical personnel engaged in processing applications is significant to the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administra- tive superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, assigns, and reviews the work of a staff of clerical personnel engaged in processing appeals of tax assessments and applications for exemption from tax liability; plans, assigns, and reviews the work of subordinate technical employees engaged in investigating appeals and exemptions; oversees the maintenance of files and dockets for appeals and exemptions; oversees the issuance of certificates of exemption and assessment revision; trains and instructs new employees.
  • Reviews appeals and exemption applications for accuracy and conformance to legislative standards; requests reports on properties concerned from real property evaluations; evaluates appeals, exemptions applications, and assessment reports; determines proper assessment values; makes recommendations to Board members regarding dispositions of appeals and granting of exemptions.
  • Explains basis of tax assessments to taxpayers and their representatives; explains legislative requirements for tax exemptions; informs taxpayers of information and documentation necessary to process appeals and exemptions; confers with taxpayers and their representatives in attempts to settle assessment appeals without formal Board hearings.
  • Schedules taxpayer appeals for hearing by the Board of Revision of Taxes; presents City's position to the Board at appeals hearings; ensures that taxpayers are notified of hearing data and Board's decision; oversees processing of decision into taxpayer's and evaluators' files; notifies Department of Revenue of effect of Board decisions of taxes to be collected.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • state and local legislation pertaining to exemptions from real property tax liability.
  • the laws and regulations relating to real property appraisal and assessment.
  • the methods and techniques of determining the fair market value of real property.
  • current real property values and trends in values.
  • the theory of values as applied to real property assessment.
  • supervisory methods.
  • assessment record maintenance procedures and standards.
  • building construction and rehabilitation methods and their costs.
  • the statistical techniques used to determine market value.
  • appeals and exemptions procedures.
ABILITY TO:

  • evaluate appeals and assessments and to negotiate settlements based on evaluation.
  • interpret building plans, leases, deeds, and income and expense statements.
  • communicate effective, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, subordinates, Board members, taxpayers, and their representatives.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/84.)

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university. Four years of experience appraising and assessing real property, one year of which has been at the full performance level.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
