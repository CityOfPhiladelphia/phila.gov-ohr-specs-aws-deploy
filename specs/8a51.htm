CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

8A51
NURSING HOME CUSTODIAL SERVICES DIRECTOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is second supervisory level custodial service work with responsibility for all custodial and housekeeping tasks for a large resident care facility. Work involves directing, through subordinate supervisors, the custodial maintenance of patient care areas, and ensuring the provision of adequate laundry and linen service as prescribed by state and federal regulations. Scheduling and coordinating the work of a large staff of custodial employees is significant to the work.

Work is performed under the general direction of an administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • The employee must supervise, through subordinate supervisors, all custodial and related activities in a large nursing home facility.
  • The position must be located in the Health Department.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, schedules, and coordinates the work of employees engaged in cleaning patient care areas, offices, and grounds of a nursing home complex; reviews and evaluates custodial services through personal inspections, periodic reports, and comments of other facility personnel; tours buildings and grounds noting the appearance and general conditions of windows, floors, ceilings, offices and other areas; confers with subordinate supervisors on any work schedule changes which may be necessary due to the conditions of an area, work schedule, or shift.
  • Directs and supervises activities related to the maintenance of grounds, such as mowing, cleaning, and hedge cutting; plans and directs the clearing of snow and ice from sidewalks, walkways, and plazas.
  • Analyzes laundry and linen supply services; ensures that an adequate amount of linen supplies are available; coordinates contracted linen and laundry services with the linen distribution requirements of the facility; oversees the distribution and collection of linen to patient care areas; contacts laundry service to resolve problems pertaining to late deliveries, shortages, or damaged linen; sends linen samples on a periodic basis to an outside testing laboratory to ensure quality of laundry service; initiates requisitions for the purchase of linen and related supplies for the nursing home facility; directs subordinates in taking inventory of existing linen stock and the preparation of new stock levels.
  • Ensures that all infectious waste is disposed of properly; plans for garbage and waste collection; attends meetings pertaining to the proper disposal of infectious waste; attends departmental meetings pertaining to environmental standards within the facility; ensures that all custodial and related operations conform to appropriate city, state, and federal regulations and standards.
  • Directs and supervises vector control activities; plans, assigns, and reviews the work of the vector control staff; receives and logs in rodent and other vector complaints, assigns staff to lay traps and bait.
  • Periodically inspects custodial supply areas for cleanliness, orderliness and adequacy of supplies; conducts inventory to determine supply consumption, estimate needs, and to prepare or direct the preparation of the requisitions to replenish needed supplies; maintains records and prepares reports relating to personnel, supplies, equipment and other day-to-day operations.
  • Oversees the in-service training of all custodial, linen service, and related personnel.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • scheduling, work standards, equipment, materials, methods and practices used in large scale custodial operations.
  • custodial services provided in a nursing home facility.
  • occupational hazards and safety precautions involved in the work.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
  • methods, materials, and practices used in the operation of a central linen supply service.

SKILL IN:

  • expressing ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.

ABILITY TO:

  • make accurate estimates of time, labor and material needs.
  • analyze and evaluate production, flow of work and staff utilization factors.
  • prepare work schedules designed to achieve maximum use and efficiency of the work force.
  • learn appropriate state and federal regulations pertaining to a nursing home facility.
  • train subordinates in custodial practices and procedures particularly as they pertain to safety precautions.
  • supervise and coordinate the activities of a large number of employees engaged in a variety of housekeeping functions.
  • learn proper methods of disposal regarding infectious waste.
  • direct safe disposal of infectious waste.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/1991.)
    EDUCATION:
  • Completion of the twelfth school grade.

    AND

    GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

  • Two years of custodial work experience.

    AND

    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

  • Two years supervising a group of employees engaged in custodial and related activities in a large health care or nursing home facility.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience and the completion of the twelfth school grade as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
