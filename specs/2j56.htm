LEGISLATIVE AND REGULATORY AFFAIRS MONITOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is legislative and regulatory affairs work monitoring local, state, regional and federal legislation and regulatory initiatives that impact or the policies, plans, and operations of a City's department on division. Work includes interpreting such legislation, ensuring departmental review, and initiatives. The employee in this class serves as liaison to departmental officials and City Council on legislative and regulatory matters, in drafting preliminary legislation, and coordinating its introduction and passage. Establishing and maintaining communication and working relationships with federal and state regulatory agency officials, and local, state, and federal legislators are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The position monitors local, state, regional and federal legislation and initiatives that impact upon the policies plans and operations of the department or division.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Monitors federal, state, regional and local governments for legislative and regulatory initiatives affecting city operations; elicits the concerns, needs and interests of large commercial interests, civic, and special interest groups and intra-governmental agencies; prepares written or oral reports interpreting and detailing relevant legislative and regulatory development for departmental review; proposes appropriate strategy for response to legislation and regulatory initiatives in order to influence and advance the interests of the city; advises departmental personnel on designated legislative and regulatory matters; reviews and critiques reports, speeches, administrative statements and testimony for departmental officials; may interface with the news media.
  • Prepares preliminary papers on key legislation and regulations at the state, federal, regional and local levels; organizes departmental policy groups to analyze and gather comments on proposed legislation; develops preliminary departmental responses to major long-term regulatory initiatives; may present the department's position as a member of national and state industry and intra-governmental groups.
  • Functions as liaison to city Council in relation to maintaining an information network; establishes and builds communications and working relationships with members of lobbying agencies at the federal, state, regional and local governmental levels; conducts regular briefing for legislators and their staff on issues of importance to the department; provides similar liaison with interagency and community groups explaining the department's legislative programs and objectives; establishes and maintains working relationships with state and federal regulatory agencies.
  • Assists in the development, organization and coordination of the activities of a regional advisory council; coordinates the presentation of current and proposed departmental plans, operations and policies to the advisory council; communicates the advisory council's recommendations to the Department; coordinates, monitors and assists in the preparation of the department's responses.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, precedents and practices of local, state, regional and federal legislation and regulations which affect the activities of city operations.
  • the operations, functions and structures of legislative bodies and regulatory agencies.
  • the methods and techniques of interpreting, analyzing and critiquing governmental legislation and regulations.
  • the principles and practices utilized in notifying interest groups and the public of proposed legislation and regulatory mandates.
  • methods, practices and techniques of monitoring governmental legislative and regulatory agendas and identifying those initiatives that impact upon local government.
  • the methods, techniques and practices of drafting and introducing local legislation to local governing bodies.
  • the methods, techniques and practices used in briefing legislators, regulatory officials and lobbying personnel at all levels of government on matters of importance to the department.

ABILITY TO:

  • simplify the test of governmental legislations and regulations for departmental comprehension and review.
  • function as liaison between the department and other governments and their officials.
  • develop position papers and strategies to advance the interests of the department with key governmental legislators, their support staff and lobbying officials, and to influence proposed legislation and regulations that affect the operations of the department.
  • advise the department on legislative and regulatory matters.
  • oversee the passage of local legislation.
  • represent the department on various local, state, regional and federal councils.
  • learn the operational activities of the department.
  • establish, build and maintain effective working relationships with local, state, regional and federal governmental officials and their staff, lobbying agencies, regulatory agencies, professional associates, other departments and the public.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/90.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE
  • Two years of administrative and legislative liaison experience.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:
  • One year of which has been monitoring or drafting proposed legislation and developing and implementing strategies for selected legislative issues.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
