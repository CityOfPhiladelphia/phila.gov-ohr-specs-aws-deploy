LAW ENFORCEMENT ANALYST SUPERVISOR

 

 GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is supervisory crime information management and intelligence analysis work directing a staff of lower-level analysts for the Philadelphia Police Department.  Employees in this class have responsibility for supervising and participating in technical work for the purpose of substantiating criminal patterns and/or trends and predictive analysis. Work includes correlating, integrating, and evaluating varying types of information from criminal or operational activities for the purpose of generating strategic, operational, tactical, or administrative level analysis reports. Work includes utilizing computer hardware and software associated with law enforcement information systems for reporting and analysis. Distinguishing factors between this and the lower level class in the series include responsibility for providing supervision of analysts, and the level of interaction with management and external law enforcement agency administrators. Employees work on assigned shifts in the Real-Time Crime Center assignment. Work is performed under the supervision of a technical superior.            

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS:

 

1.    Positions in this class must supervise crime information management, and intelligence analysis work performed by a staff of lower-level analysts.

2.    Positions in this class must be in the Philadelphia Police Department’s Intelligence Bureau.

3.    No more than 6 positions will be allocated to this class.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

ALL ASSIGNMENTS

 

Supervises lower level analysts and provides technical direction in crime and intelligence analysis; performs evaluation and analysis of the most complex data using standard qualitative and quantitative methods; prepares and compiles reports documenting results of findings; directs and participates in formal presentations of findings; collaborates with other technical personnel on projects; plans, and assigns lower-level analysts performing analysis using crime, economic and demographic data to project crime trends and patterns; reviews the work of lower level analysts, trains personnel on the technical aspects of crime and intelligence analysis and departmental procedures and sets time frames for the completion of assignments.

Directs and participates in planning intelligence or research questions and objectives for crime analysis products; collates data collection from computer systems, field observations and interviews; applies and provides technical direction to staff in appropriate analytical tradecraft, and develops and reviews output reports for analysis products; collects, collates, and analyzes data and information from various sources including police systems, surveillance cameras, confidential sources, public records, social media, and other open source systems.

Confers with departmental representatives; reviews and makes recommendations for policies, procedures, forms and systems used for data collection and analysis.

Collaborates with representatives from other government organizations to share information or coordinate intelligence and research activities on multiple simultaneous complex assignments.

Directs the design or modification of current and new information systems and programs using a variety of methods and techniques; tests new programs and interactive geospatial applications.

Directs other analysts in literature reviews and in the investigation of new analytical techniques in program analysis and evaluation; may attend conferences.

Performs related work as required.

 

REAL-TIME CRIME CENTER ASSIGNMENT

 

Supervises lower level analyst staff supporting the 24-hour Real-Time Crime Center’s (RTCC) operations through the analysis of 911 calls, radio, crime tips, and other sources; ensures response directly to Requests for Information from Philadelphia Police or external agency partners; ensures requests are redirected to the appropriate unit within the Intelligence Bureau.

Directs and participates in operational or tactical-level crime analysis or intelligence products to support real-time field operations.

 

OPERATIONS ASSIGNMENT

 

Directs and performs tactical-level crime analysis or develops intelligence products for field operations and the needs of the Police Captain; employees in this assignment are detailed to operational units such as Police Districts or Investigative Units.

Serves as analytical liaison between multiple operational units and the Intelligence Bureau.

 

INTELLIGENCE ASSIGNMENT

 

Directs and participates in studies activities relating to narcotics, human trafficking, cyber security, money laundering, gangs, crime, terrorism, or other national security threats.

Reviews strategic, operational, or tactical analytical reports; reviews criminal profiles to aid in connecting criminal organizations with their members.

 

RESEARCH ASSIGNMENT

           

Directs and performs quantitative analysis techniques, including geospatial analysis software, for the development of analytical products; conducts literature reviews and best practice research to identify innovative policing programs to recommend for implementation; works with program managers and unit commanders to establish performance measurement systems.

Performs comparative research on data compiled by law enforcement or intelligence agencies in other jurisdictions.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         supervisory principles and practices

·         principles and practices of project management

·         standard sources and uses of information in law enforcement

·         qualitative and quantitative analysis techniques

·         the principles and practices of statistical analysis

·         law enforcement and statistical research methodology for the research assignment

·         principles, methods and techniques for research, analysis and data collection

·         computer based information systems used for data gathering, organization, and analysis

·         the principles and practices of utilizing computers for management reporting and analysis

·         information system resources needed to design computerized data entry

·         query languages, report writers, interactive database programs, spreadsheets and/or application generators or other software related to intelligence analysis

·         database management, geospatial software, and other software packages to manage data and produce tables, maps, and graphic displays if required by assignment

 

SKILL IN:

 

·         using analytical techniques and scientific principles to evaluate data

·         writing a variety of narrative reports

·         directing staff performing crime analysis using economic and demographic data

·         reviewing projected crime trends and patterns


ABILITY TO:

 

·         supervise lower level staff

·         plan, assign and review a variety of analysis and narrative reports

·         coordinate program activity with personnel in order to achieve program objectives

·         prioritize work and ensure lower level staff meet deadlines

·         process and analyze data using query languages, report writers, interactive database programs, spreadsheets and/or application generators

·         direct qualitative studies for intelligence analysis

·         express ideas and facts clearly and effectively both orally and in writing

·         establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, management, and federal and state agency partners, governmental representatives, and the general public

     

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/21.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Completion of a bachelor’s degree program at an accredited college or university with major coursework in criminology, criminal justice, crime science, data science, geography, intelligence analysis, mathematics, statistics, or a closely related social science field.

 

AND

 

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

Three years of experience performing crime information management and intelligence analysis work for a law enforcement agency.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

            One year of experience at the project-leader level performing crime information management and intelligence analysis work or at the advanced performance level performing research and information management for a law enforcement agency.

 

NOTE:           Possession of a master's degree in one of the fields outlined above or a closely related social science field may substitute for one year of the general experience requirement.

 

OR

 

Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources which has included the completion of a bachelor’s degree at an accredited college or university as an educational minimum.
