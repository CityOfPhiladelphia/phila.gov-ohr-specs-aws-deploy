CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

1E20
MANAGEMENT OPERATIONS SYSTEMS ANALYST


GENERAL DEFINITION

This is analytical computing work in the City's Managing Director's Office, coordinating and facilitating the development of automated information systems for all of the City's operating departments. The employee in this class evaluates operating departments' proposed computer requirement proposals to determine their capacity to enhance productivity and management control and recommends approval or disapproval of the proposals. Work involves participating in departmental computer system development projects by coordinating and facilitating administrative and technical support functions, such as contract preparation, equipment purchasing and systems installation. Contact with the City's centralized finance, procurement and computing agencies to organize support functions is a significant aspect of the work. Work also includes supporting all the automated information needs specific to the City's Managing Director's Office. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • The position must have responsibility for analyzing and facilitating the data processing requirements and operations of City operating departments and supporting all of the data processing needs of the City's Managing Director's Office.
  • The position must be located in the City's Managing Director's Office.
  • Only one position will be allocated to this class.


TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Reviews and evaluates data processing and office automation requisitions for computer hardware, software and professional service contracts submitted by operating departments; recommends approval or disapproval of requests; confers with departmental personnel to elicit information on the nature of proposed computer operations; ascertains the capacity of proposed automated operations to improve operational productivity management control and cost effectiveness; recommends modifications to data processing proposals for better utilization of computerized capabilities; communicates extensively with data processing officials from the City's central computing center and from operating departments concerning the technical feasibility and appropriateness of computer proposals.
  • Participates in developing, enhancing and implementing PC- and mainframe based information systems for operating departments; coordinates and facilitates the provision of administrative and technical support functions for computer system development; serves as a liaison between operating departments and the City's centralized finance, procurement and computing agencies to organize support functions; participates in preparing contract specifications for computer equipment and for service contracts; evaluates vendors' capacities to provide adequate computerized equipment and services; makes recommendations on vendor selection; expedites the procurement of data processing equipment and services; works to design and implement automated operations; periodically serves as project manager for large scale automation projects.
  • Supports all data processing needs of the City's Managing Director's Office; integrates PC based automated management systems to agency operations to enhance efficiency; configures and maintains agency system hardware; updates agency system software as appropriate and ensures backup and restore systems are in operation; develops and maintains the integrity of agency systems' databases; trains agency employees on the utilization of computer systems; troubleshoots and isolates agency computer problems as they arise; maintains the security of agency computer systems.
  • Maintains records on various microcomputer software packages used by the Managing Director's Office; ensures that software is registered and arranges for the purchase of updates or new releases; reads relevant information on automated information systems to maintain currency of knowledge in the field; receives demonstration diskettes of new software packages and reviews and evaluates them for use by the management agency and operating departments; answers questions about software, hardware and computer operations from operating department users.
  • May supervise subordinate computer analytical personnel.
  • Performs related work as required.


REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the theory and practice of computing systems analysis, including analysis of software, hardware and applications systems to achieve efficient system utilization.
  • the theory and practice of computing systems design as it relates to improving an organization's operational efficiency.
  • the capabilities of a variety of software packages applicable to the operations of departments.
  • the principles and practices of utilizing computing hardware for management reporting and analysis in a large organization.
  • the current theory, uses, and capabilities of computer-based management information systems utilizing non-procedural languages and microcomputers.
  • the uses, capabilities and configurations of computing hardware, such as multi-central processing units, telecommunication networks, and supporting devices such as disk drives, controllers and printers.
  • the current uses and programming principles of one or more non-procedural languages.
  • data base theory.
  • the methods and techniques used in systems security.
  • principles and practices of operational analysis.
  • current and new developments and technology in the computing field.

ABILITY TO:

  • analyze and comprehend operational problems relating to the development of automated information systems.
  • learn the City's procurement system.
  • learn the applications of new software packages.
  • learn the organization and functions of the departments and agencies which comprise City government.
  • enlist the cooperation of officials of various agencies in implementing and facilitating the development and enhancement of automated information systems.
  • direct and/or coordinate the activities of a task force for the duration of a project.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • evaluate and modify proposals.
  • establish and maintain effective relationships with associates.


MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 2/92.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major coursework in computer science, decision science, information science or a closely related field.

AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Two years of full performance level experience performing computer programming and analysis or analyzing, designing and applying automated information systems to administrative and management operations in a large organization.

AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

  • Two years of the above experience at the advanced level with responsibility for providing analysis and/or application of PC based information systems to improve operational efficiency in a large organization.

Or any equivalent combination of experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included a bachelor's degree and specific experience as described above.

NOTE:
Completion of a master's degree program in computer science, decision science, information science or a closely related field may be substituted for one year of the general experience requirement.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
