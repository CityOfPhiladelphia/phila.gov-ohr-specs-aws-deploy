ACCOUNTING VERIFICATION AND PROCEDURES MANAGER

 

 

 

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

This is managerial level accounting work in the City's central accounting division directing the Financial Verification and Procedures Section. The employee in this class directs, through subordinate supervisors, the review and processing of all city contracts, as well as encumbrance and payment documents received from all City departments and agencies to ensure they are in accordance with applicable accounting standards and guidelines.  Work includes monitoring the payment process to ensure that vendors are paid on a timely basis and extensive contact with numerous City officials in other departments and agencies to resolve problems. Analyzing accounting practices as they relate to the City’s payment process to accelerate the process and increase efficiency is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the general direction of an administrative superior.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met in order for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.        The position must be responsible for directing the Accounting Verification and Procedures section of the Finance Department’s Accounting Division. 

2.        The position must be located in the Finance Department.

3.        The position must direct section operations through subordinate accounting or administrative supervisors.

4.        Only one position will be allocated to this class.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

Anticipates, evaluates and determines accounting or payment processing needs; sets priorities and deadlines; coordinates the work of personnel in assigned units; assures that accounts  and reports  are processed  in conformity  with professional  and institutional standards and guidelines for municipal accounting and reporting; implements and participates in the development of changes to accounting procedures as required; ensures that all financial data published from the City central accounting system is accurate and in accordance with promulgations issued by the Governmental Accounting Standards Board.

Manages the City's centralized processing of contracts, purchase orders, encumbrance documents, payment vouchers and vendor invoices in accordance with municipal accounting procedures; assures timely payment to vendors; reviews documents, certifies and reports the availability of funds for municipal contracts and purchases; confers with departmental officials to resolve errors or omissions in procedure or documentation; reviews and certifies accuracy of schedules and reports.

Develops Standard Accounting Procedures, Administrative Board rules or other necessary regulations related to citywide contract conformance, vendor payment processing, and employee reimbursements related to travel or other work-related expenses; ensures that all reimbursements are processed in accordance with relevant IRS tax code regulations.

Analyzes accounting practices as they relate to the City's payment process to accelerate the process and otherwise to increase efficiency; updates and automates processing procedures; develops and implements standard rules and regulations in the processing of contracts and financial payment transactions.

Implements modernization of the City's financial accounting processes or systems as it relates to section operations; serves as part of implementation committees in establishing new guidelines and procedures; develops recommended processes for the interfacing of relevant data between Finance and other departments connected to the system; confers with representatives of other departments connected to the system to coordinate interfacing of data.

Investigates complaints from other City departments and agencies and from vendors regarding the contracting and payment process and identifies source of problems and facilitates resolution.

Provides guidance and training to City management officials and serves in an expert capacity in providing information to various departments and agencies pertaining to Accounting Verification operations; attends meetings with other City officials to provide expert information and to meet departments' payment processing and contracting conformance needs.

Manages, directly or through subordinate supervisors, the provision of various fiscal and administrative services involving the verification and processing of all city contracts, payment requisitions and reimbursements submitted by City departments and agencies.

Develops and implements strategies to create a system of controls to mitigate the risk of vendor fraud; develops, reviews and updates control procedures to ensure they are relevant to current threats. 

Directs the operation, user access, online availability, and maintenance of the City’s Vendor Payment website; ensures accuracy of information in the database; collaborates with Office of Information and Technology for anticipated enhancement and expansion of capabilities. 

Participates in hiring, supervises and manages performance of subordinate staff; identifies training needs and recommends staff for training; ensures established procedures and policies are followed by subordinate staff.

Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES:

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

•                     the techniques, practices and proper applications of systems and procedures analysis in public accounting

•                     the principles, standards and practices of the accounting profession

•                     the standards, guidelines and requirements for governmental accounting as established by the Governmental Accounting Standards Board

•                     the principles and practices of public administration and their application to a large accounting operation

•                    supervisory methods and techniques

•                    verification and procedures for payment processing

 

SKILL IN:

 

•            applying accounting standards and guidelines

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·                     direct the work of a staff of professional and clerical personnel

·                     direct a large accounting program

·                     establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, external department officials and the public

·                     analyze accounting practices as they relate to the City’s payment and contracting process

·                     analyze negotiated contracts and purchase orders and ensure vendors are paid in conformance with contract terms and conditions

·                     direct management of accounts payable and payment processes

·                     present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing

·                     manage and complete projects effectively

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 9/18.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in Accounting.

 

AND

 

Option 1- Finance Department

 

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

Five years of post-bachelor's degree accounting or auditing experience.

 

AND

 

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

Two years of experience as an Accounting Section Supervisor or Accounting Information Systems Supervisor in the City’s central finance agency.

 

Option 2 - City Controller's Office  

 

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

Four years of post-bachelor's degree accounting or auditing experience.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

Three years as an Audit Supervisor in the City's central auditing agency.

 

Option 3 - All Other City Departments

 

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

Four years of post-bachelor's degree accounting or auditing experience.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

Three years as a Budget Officer 2, Contract Audit Manager, or Revenue Accounting Manager in a City agency.

 

Option 4 - Other Governmental Accounting Experience

 

EXPERIENCE:

 

Eleven years of post-bachelor's degree accounting or auditing experience for a government agency or for an accounting firm providing accounting or auditing services to a government agency, which has included four years of progressively responsible supervisory experience.

 

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources, which has included a bachelor's degree with major course work in accounting and the specific experience indicated above.

Audit Supervisor (2A43)

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REOUIREMENTS

 

Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
