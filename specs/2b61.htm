CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2B61
REFUND UNIT SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory work directing the activities of a staff of technical and clerical employees engaged in explaining refund petition procedures to petitioners and in reviewing and processing refunds for overpayments and incorrect payments received by City agencies. Of major significance to the work are the supervision through subordinate supervisors of a staff of clerical and technical employees and contact with the public to explain the refund process. Work is performed under the general supervision of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Supervises the activities of a staff engaged in explaining refund petition procedures to petitioners and in reviewing refund petitions and accounts receivable records to insure that the refund is in order and that the petitioner has no delinquent accounts with the City; reviews and initials refund petitions; reviews reports on unit productivity.
  • Supervises a clerical unit responsible for processing requests for duplicate receipts for real estate, personal property and water and sewer payments, for reviewing accounts receivable to identify current payments and for preparing duplicate receipts.
  • Resolves more difficult and sensitive refund requests; explains the procedures, ordinances and regulations governing the calculation of refunds to petitioners, their representatives and to City attorneys.
  • Develops new procedures to increase office efficiency, to expedite the payment of refunds and to insure that refunds are paid only after all delinquent accounts are closed.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • standard auditing procedures, especially as they apply to the analysis of accounts for refund purposes.
  • laws governing municipal revenue sources and of their application in determining tax liability and the appropriateness of refunds.
  • accounting principles and practices.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
ABILITY TO:

  • supervise the work of a group of clerical and technical employees engaged in the review of financial records for the purpose of determining the appropriateness of refunds.
  • review municipal accounts receivable records to determine the appropriateness of refund petitions.
  • establish and maintain effective work relationships with associates and the public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/85.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in accounting.
  • Four years of auditing or accounting experience which has involved the auditing of accounting records to compute tax liabilities or other charges due, two years of which has been at the full performance level, and one year of which has been supervising such work.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
