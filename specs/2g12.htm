HISTORIC PRESERVATION PLANNER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is planning and research work in the field of historic preservation. The employee in this class participates in historic site surveys, identifies cultural resources eligible for National and Philadelphia Registers, performs research, and prepares appropriate nomination forms. Work includes administrative tasks for the Commission's Committee on Historic Designation, design review, providing guidance on federal historic preservation tax incentives and making oral and written presentations. Work is performed under the supervision of the Historic Preservation Officer.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Conducts comprehensive historic site surveys within the City of Philadelphia to identify buildings, structures, sites, objects and districts eligible for the Philadelphia Register of Historic Places and the National Register of Historic Places; undertakes research in primary and secondary sources in various repositories on the history of cultural resources, neighborhoods and the City; prepares nomination forms for the Philadelphia and National Registers.
  • Makes presentations to and assumes responsibility for the administration of the Commission's Committee on Historic Designation; participates in the coordination of issues related to historic preservation planning and district designation with the Philadelphia City Planning Commission and other public and quasi-public planning and development agencies.
  • Participates with other staff members in review of plans submitted in conjunction with permit applications; provides basic information and assists developers and architects in the preparation of applications for historic preservation tax incentives.
  • Consults with City agencies and others on compliance with federal, state and local historic preservation legislation; meets with neighborhood organizations on the uses of historic preservation in community planning; delivers written and oral presentations to professionals and lay organizations on historic preservation and the work of the Commission; participates in the routine activities of the Commission.
  • Coordinates the publication of property owner manuals including overseeing the design, printing, photographing and chapter writing of the materials.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

    KNOWLEDGE OF:

    • historic preservation methods and principles
    • architectural history
    • historic site survey methodology
    • federal, state and local legislation, regulations and procedures as related to historic preservation
    • the National Register nomination process
    • basic planning precepts, methods and issues

    ABILITY TO:

    • undertake primary and secondary source research in history and architectural history
    • present data and concepts clearly and concisely, orally and in writing
    • establish and maintain effective working relationships with other governmental agencies, associates and the general public

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/05.)

    EDUCATION

    • Completion of a master's degree, or all requirements for that degree, at an accredited college or university in historic preservation, history, urban studies, architectural history, American studies, or city or urban planning.

    AND
    EXPERIENCE

    • One year of fulltime experience in historic preservation, which has included historic sites survey, preparation of documentation for designation of buildings, structures, sites, objects and districts, and preservation planning.

    OR

    Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
