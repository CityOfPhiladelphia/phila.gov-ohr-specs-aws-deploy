CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

1C01
FINANCIAL REPORT PROCESSOR


GENERAL DEFINITION

This is specialized clerical work preparing a variety of financial and statistical records and reports in the City's central financial agency. The employees in this class, on a daily basis, compile a variety of financial and statistical records and reports to be included in various segments of the city's Financial Annual Report, and process the overall city's Financial Annual Report in accordance with the G.A.S.B. guideline provided. Work includes designing format for inputting financial and statistical records and reports, and tracking and updating reports to insure their timely completion. The employees in this class prepare memos and letters for their superiors on issues regarding city's finances. Work is performed under the supervision of an accounting superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • Positions must work in the City's central financial agency.
  • Positions must compile a variety of financial and statistical records and reports to be included in various segments of the city's Financial Annual Report, process the overall city's Financial Annual Report, design format for inputting financial and statistical records and reports, and track and update reports to insure their timely completion.


TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Prepares a variety of financial and statistical records and reports to be used in various segments of the city's Financial Annual Report such as statements for bonded indebtedness, city employees' yearly balances for sick leave and vacation, and index code listing for changes in employee status; compiles various records and reports, using numeric machine, word processing equipment and various software applications; types exhibits, statements, schedules, journal entries and accounting procedures and directives in accordance with the G.A.S.B. guideline provided.
  • Tracks and updates financial records and reports; prepares requests for the release of financial records and reports by City agencies and departments, and by financial institutions involved with city finances; updates records and reports, using numeric machine, word processing equipment and various software applications.
  • Processes the overall city's Financial Annual Report; prints draft copy to be reviewed by City agencies and departments, and by financial institutions and independent auditors involved with city finances; proofs and distributes report to agencies concerned; finalizes report after review and approval by immediate superiors; forwards report to the in-house printing unit for duplication.
  • Prepares memos and letters for immediate superiors; answers telephone calls; takes messages and sets up appointments for superiors; attends meetings on financial report and document format changes; suggests format changes to improve appearance and readability; maintains files for financial and statistical records and reports, and for accounting practices and procedures such as purchase requisitions and petty cash disbursements.
  • Performs related work as required.


REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • standard office practices and equipment.
  • business English, spelling and arithmetic.
  • software applications.
  • formats used in the processing of financial reports and analyses, and financial and accounting documents.

SKILL IN:

  • the use of numeric machines, word processing equipment and personal computers.

ABILITY TO:

  • prepare a variety of financial and statistical records and reports in accordance with the G.A.S.B. guideline.
  • compile a variety of financial and statistical records and reports, using numeric machines, word processing equipment and personal computers.
  • understand and follow complex oral and written instructions.


MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/68.)

EDUCATION:

  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • Three years of experience as a typist, including two years of experience which has involved major emphasis on the typing of statistical reports employing many columns of figures.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS
  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
