STREET LIGHTING SUPERINTENDENT

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is administrative and technical work directing inspections of contracted street lighting construction and maintenance and overseeing the repair or replacement of street lighting equipment and foundations by City crews involved in city-wide program.  Coordinating the activities of contractors and City street lighting crews on an operational level is a significant aspect of the work.  The employee in this class is responsible for overseeing all activities at the Street Lighting shop including preparing the preliminary annual budget, making field investigations, serving as a technical back-up and directing the stores and vehicular operations.  Work is performed under the direction of a technical superior. 

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

            Oversees the work of subordinate supervisors as they plan, assign and review the work of technical staff engaged in checking the installation and maintenance of street lighting facilities of private contractors for conformance to specifications; oversees and plans, assigns and reviews, through subordinate supervisors, the work of street lighting crews engaged in installation, maintenance, and repair of street lighting equipment.

            Coordinates at the operational level the activities of various private contractors and utility companies involved in street lighting or equipment on poles in the public Right of Way; meets with state officials concerning the City's installation and maintenance of lighting on state roads within the City.

            Attends meetings with contractors to clarify and discuss contract specifications.

            Serves as a technical source in matters concerning street lighting inspections, maintenance and specifications.

            Prepares proposed specifications for street lighting equipment; estimates need for street lighting equipment; contacts vendors to expedite delivery; directs operations of stores area through subordinate storeskeeper; tests new equipment for conformance to specifications.

            Makes inspections in the field; conducts tests in the field to determine lighting needs; investigates complaints and makes recommendations; lays out proposed street light locations and relocations.

            Directs a clerical staff in preparing reports, payroll records and correspondence regarding work activities of the various units; schedules the inspection and maintenance of a fleet of vehicles used by the entire street lighting operations section.

            Plans monthly reports on the activities of the staff for technical superior; prepares accident damage claim reports; reports those claims in court when necessary; prepares the preliminary annual budget.

            Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

•           the practices and procedures involved in the installation, maintenance and repair of street lights

•           inspectional methods and procedures relating to electrical equipment

•           the principles and theories of electricity

•           supervisory methods and techniques

•           occupational hazards and safety precautions involved in street lighting work.

 

SKILL IN:

 

•           the operation of electrical testing equipment required in street lighting 

 

ABILITY TO:

 

•           establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, engineers, contractors, and officials of governmental agencies

•           interpret, explain, and enforce compliance with contract specifications firmly and tactfully

•           write specifications and make requisitions for street lighting supplies and equipment

•           express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 9/18.)

 

OPTION 1:

 

EDUCATION:

           

            Completion of a two-year program at a technical institute or an accredited college with major course work in electronic or electrical engineering technology.

 

AND

 

EXPERIENCE:

 

            Two years of supervisory experience in construction, installation, repair and maintenance of electronics or electrical equipment or systems or supervisory experience in the testing and inspection of construction projects involving street lighting or outdoor electrical work.

OR

 

OPTION 2:

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

 

AND

 

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

           

            Three years of experience in the installation, maintenance and repair of various types of street lighting or outdoor electrical equipment.

 

AND

 

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

           

            Two years of experience at the first supervisory level supervising the repair, maintenance, and installation of various types of street lighting fixtures OR one year of experience supervising a city-wide program of installing, repairing, and inspecting street lighting equipment.

 

OR

 

Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources.

 

City of Philadelphia Classes That Typically Meet the Experience Requirements:

 

·         Construction Projects Technician 3 (Electrical and Electronic Specialties Only) (3A19)

·         Street Lighting Supervisor (7K57)

·         Electronic Technician Group Leader (Street Electronic Systems Specialty Only) (7K68)

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

 

            Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania.
