LABOR AND WAGE COMPLIANCE SUPERVISOR

 

 

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is supervisory wage enforcement work directing the activities of inspectors engaged in payroll certification work of prevailing wages for contractors and subcontractors in the Department of Labor. Employees in the class are responsible for directing a staff of Wage Compliance Inspectors engaged in monitoring and reviewing certified payrolls for compliance and enforcement of Davis-Bacon Wages, Department of Labor and Industry Wages, and City of Philadelphia Prevailing Wages.  Work involves explaining regulations and provisions of the Davis-Bacon Act, American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009 (ARRA), and the Philadelphia Code.  Ensuring that staff are monitoring and enforcing contractors and subcontractors to be paid the prevailing wage rate and resolving the more complex certified payroll related problems are significant aspects of the work.  The employees in this class report to an administrative superior.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.    Positions must supervise subordinate Wage Compliance Inspectors in the Labor Standards Unit.

2.     Positions must be located in the Department of Labor.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

            Supervises a staff of Wage Compliance Investigators in the examination of prevailing wage contracts for city agencies for compliance in accordance with Davis Bacon Act, American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA), and The Philadelphia Code; conducts investigations of contractors and enforces Labor Standards Ordinance, Davis-Bacon Act and other related regulatory requirements for wage and labor; reviews payroll journals and ledgers of contractors to ensure prevailing wages and benefits.

            Creates and maintains spreadsheets and files to track discrepancies in payrolls and prevailing wage of contractors.

            Trains staff in regulations and policies and use of electronic certified payroll system and other computerized software programs; ensures updates regarding labor union practices and collective bargaining agreements are enforced.

            Performs related work as required.

 

REBUILD Assignment

 

            Performs the above-mentioned duties to monitor contracts for workforce diversity and prevailing wages for the City’s REBUILD initiative. 

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         regulatory requirements of Davis Bacon-Act, American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009 (ARRA), and Section 17-107 of The Philadelphia Code

·         labor union agreements and collective bargaining agreements

·         supervisory principles and techniques

·         regulations, policies and procedures relating to contracts and prevailing wages

·         electronic certified payroll systems

 

SKILL IN:

·         eliciting information

·         interacting with City officials and the public in issues pertaining to prevailing wage compliance

·         reviewing payroll records and violations identified by staff

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·         supervise employees engaged in monitoring certified payrolls for compliance with prevailing wages

·         interpret and explain regulations, policies and procedural guidelines for prevailing wages

·         create spreadsheets and track data

·         to meet deadlines consistently

·         use computer applications related to certified payroll processing

·         present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing

·         establish and maintain effective working relation­ships with associates and departmental officials

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experi­ence requirements as approved on 12/20.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Completion of two years of study at an accredited college or university.

 

AND

EXPERIENCE:

 

            Three years of experience as a Wage Compliance Inspector in the City of Philadelphia Mayor’s Office of Labor.

 

NOTE:  Two additional years of the experience can substitute for the required education.

 

OR

 

            Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Common­wealth of Pennsylvania.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the envi­ronmental conditions required of a position in this class.
