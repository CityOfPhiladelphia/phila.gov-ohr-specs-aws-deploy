AIRPORT COMMUNICATIONS CENTER MANAGER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is managerial telecommunications work directing, through subordinate supervisors, the activities of a communications center at a large hub international airport, which encompasses dispatch for Public Safety and Security personnel (Police, Fire, Airport Public Safety staff), passenger customer service and paging, and in-bound informational calls concerning the airport. The employee in this class is responsible for ensuring that the Communication Center represents a professional and courteous image to the traveling public in need of information and/or assistance, and the expedient response to potential emergencies through emergency dispatch. Establishing service delivery standards, policies and protocols in accordance with FAA and Airport rules and regulations, are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of the airport administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

  • The position must be located in the Communications Center of the Philadelphia International Airport
  • The position must direct the daily, 24-hour/ 7 day/week activities and operations of the Communication Center through subordinate supervisors working on an assigned shift.
  • Only one position will be allocated to this class.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

Plans, schedules, assigns and monitors the work activities of Communications Center Supervisors on an assigned shift; prepares weekly work schedules; plans and arranges duty coverage and overtime; keeps staff abreast of news impacting, travel, local events and airport events; prepares performance reports.

Manages the communications center; manages all planning, budgeting and staffing while ensuring communications center provides reliable, timely assistance to the traveling public; develops policies and procedures; independently makes quick, accurate and appropriate decisions regarding service recovery, staffing and management issues that arise; develops and implements contingency plans to ensure levels of customer service can be maintained during periods of unanticipated operational disruption; analyzes call volume and call trends to pro-actively identify communication needs/gaps; provides monthly reports for senior management on volume and trends

Develops and implements procedures and programs to ensure that communications center operations are conducted according to governmental and division regulations; coordinates communication activities with Airport Public Safety and Security, Operations, Police and Fire; reviews and evaluates existing and new communications systems; prepares reports pertaining to suitability for use and maintenance; analyzes emergency procedures involving the communications center.

Manages a call monitoring program for Communication Center Operators using telephony software; provides coaching and feedback to staff; effectively handles communications regarding emergency situations such as aircraft emergencies, fires, weather emergencies, etc.; comprehends and is able to implement protocol for the airport security plan, in accordance with FAA regulations; ensures all staff is knowledgeable of emergency protocol, and follows appropriate notification to the various public safety agencies.

Coordinates between staff and other Aviation departments to ensure that communication staff members convey accurate information to the airlines, internal staff and traveling public; ensures staff is kept abreast of news impacting travel, local events and airport events; creates and documents process flows and standard operating procedures that align with the airport customer service philosophy; continually simplifies, streamlines and recommends improvements to the overall efficiency of customer service.

Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

    KNOWLEDGE OF:

    • FAA and the Airport rules and regulations pertaining to airport security plan, emergency protocol and communications
    • practices, procedures and performance metrics involving airport communications and call center operations
    • a variety of communications equipment/parts and systems, to include automated call distributor and quality monitoring systems, and their uses
    • supervisory methods and techniques

    ABILITY TO:

    • plan, organize, direct, and monitor the activities of communications center staff, through subordinate supervisors
    • ensure timely and appropriate services to callers in a customer-service oriented manner
    • analyze performance trends and operational metrics
    • train communications center operators in new communications equipment and/or computerized equipment
    • prepare work schedules and assignments and provide adequate duty coverage and overtime
    • learn communication systems and understand technical documentation
    • make sound judgments in emergency situations and effectively coordinate airport communications in such incidents
    • prepare written reports of accidents, emergencies and other center activity records and reports
    • use computers and standard software for word processing, data base management and preparation of reports
    • speak and write effectively, with special emphasis on the expression of technical and procedural requirements
    • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, subordinates and representatives of other agencies and the general public

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 06/08.)

    EDUCATION

    Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

    AND
    GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

    Six years of airport communications experience at a large hub international airport providing a variety of telephone communication and information services to include responding to alerts, interacting with passengers through giving and eliciting information.

    AND
    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

    Two years of call center experience at a large hub airport supervising subordinate staff providing a variety of telephone communication and information services at a large or medium hub international airport facility.

    OR

    Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as an Airport Communications Center Manager. Must be able to pass and maintain SIDA (Security Identification Display Area) and AOA (Airfield Operations Area) clearance.
