4C32
PUBLIC HEALTH NURSE

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is public health nursing work teaching, demonstrating and providing nursing care and treatment of patients with diseases in their homes. Work includes motivating people to receive proper medical care and following physicians' orders.

Employees in this class conduct physical examinations, determine medical and personal histories of patients, and/or provide treatment and management of identified diseases. Work also includes utilizing medical standing orders or program protocols under the direction of a physician. Counseling patients and household members in proper hygiene and basic nursing practices to expedite recovery is significant to the work.

Work is performed under the general supervision of a nursing supervisor or administrative superior and under the technical supervision of a physician.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

  • This position must perform nursing work in the homes of patients.
  • This position performs duties in accordance with standing medical orders and program protocols.
  • These positions must be located in the Health Department.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Makes a variety of home visits to provide nursing care such as giving injections, baths, irrigating and dressing wounds, and to give health guidance which includes the teaching, advising and counseling of patients and relatives on the principles and techniques of home care; demonstrates and teaches nursing care to patient or responsible member of the family; visits patients' homes to see that they are following physicians' orders; analyzes emotional and social problems of patients and makes referrals to other agencies.
  • Interviews patients with communicable diseases to determine any contacts that they have had; explains necessary precautions for the prevention and transmission of the disease; urges continued treatment and periodic medical examination.
  • Instructs patients and/or family on proper methods and procedures for obtaining necessary specimens for laboratory examinations.
  • Demonstrates public health nursing techniques and procedures to nursing and medical students assigned to the district for field experience; demonstrates nursing techniques used for different cases to new public health nurses; reports on performance of students and nurses performance to nursing instructors and supervisors.
  • Prepares case records and reports on work performed; calls private and clinic physicians to clarify orders on patients.
  • Maintains necessary records and reports of service; takes medical histories; conducts long-term case management; tracks dates of immunization and other medical treatments.
  • Receives referrals to assess household contacts of pregnant women identified with hepatitis B infection; provides vaccine to asymptomatic household contacts; case-manages infants born to hepatitis B-infected women to ensure appropriate and timely administration of hepatitis B vaccine over 1st 6-12 months of life; draws serologic specimen from infant to determine protective level of hepatitis B vaccine series; administers additional doses of hepatitis B vaccine to infants if initial series is ineffective.
  • Counsels patients and contacts in regard to risk reduction to Hepatitis B and chronic diseases; provides one-to-one educational services for the patient regarding diagnosis and treatment.
  • Evaluates patients on follow-up visits to determine adequacy of treatments and presence of adverse reactions to medications; refers patients when indicated to medical, nursing, or community resources; orients and instructs other nursing staff in program protocols and clinic procedures.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

    KNOWLEDGE OF:

    • the principles, practices and techniques of nursing and their application to patient care
    • health principles, practices and techniques with respect to clinical and related services
    • principles and practices of public health nursing and of their application to specific disease and social conditions
    • resources available to persons in need of related health and social services
    • effects of illness on individuals and their home environmental conditions

    ABILITY TO:

    • manage a large caseload of patients
    • teach the principles of home nursing and disease prevention
    • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, patients, private physicians, families and representatives of other agencies
    • evaluate health histories and results of examinations, and identify medical deviations from the norm
    • maintain complete and accurate records of patient services
    • understand and follow oral and written instructions

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 09/01.)

    EDUCATION

    • Completion of a Bachelor's of Science in Nursing (BSN) degree from an accredited college or university.

    AND
    EXPERIENCE

    • One year of nursing experience doing community field nursing services.
PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Eligibility for a license as a registered nurse, as issued by the Pennsylvania State Board of Nursing Education and Licensure, at the time of application, and possession of such licensure as specified within the length of time by the Department.
