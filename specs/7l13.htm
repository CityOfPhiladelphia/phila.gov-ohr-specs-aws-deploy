CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

7L13
PHOTOGRAPHER SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is technical and supervisory work directing the activities of a city-wide photographic operation which provides a variety of photographic services and processes employee identification documents for City departments. The employee in this class assigns, schedules, and reviews the work of subordinate photographers. Contact with City attorneys and departmental administrators to ascertain their photographic requirements is significant to the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Receives and reviews requests for photographic services from City departments; discusses requirements with requesting personnel; determines most efficient method of completing projects; assigns and schedules photographic projects; instructs photographers in method of completing assignments; reviews completed work for conformance to photographic standards and job specifications; establishes priorities in accordance with requests from departmental administrators and attorneys.
  • Supervises subordinate photographers in taking and developing employee identification pictures; supervises and instructs in the use of an automated processor to develop pictures taken in the field; oversees the preparation and retention of duplicate prints; instructs photographers in the care and use of equipment and supplies.
  • Participates in work to demonstrate techniques and procedures; assists in the completion of more difficult assignments; makes routine repairs to photographic equipment.
  • Reviews specifications for the new equipment and materials; tests new equipment and materials to determine applicability to unit function; prepares recommendations for the purchase of equipment and materials; insures that an adequate inventory of equipment and material is maintained to support unit function; researches new photographic techniques.
  • Prepares and submits written reports of unit activities.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices, materials and equipment used in photography.
  • the principles, practices, materials and equipment used in developing black and white photographs.
  • the properties and characteristics of the color photographic process.
  • the process involved in taking and developing employee identification photographs.
  • the capabilities and applicability of photographic equipment as it relates to the unit function.

SKILL IN:

  • the care and use of photographic equipment as it relates to the unit function.
  • the care and use of photographic equipment.
  • performing technical photographic work.
  • operating an automated processor.

ABILITY TO:

  • learn supervisory methods and techniques.
  • test and evaluate photographic materials and equipment for applicability to unit functions.
  • maintain records and prepare reports on unit functions.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and other City officials.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/1977.)
  • Education equivalent to the completion of the twelfth school grade.

  • Four years of experience as a commercial, news or portrait photographer.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Photographer Supervisor.
