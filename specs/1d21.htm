CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

1D21
COMPUTER-PERIPHERAL EQUIPMENT OPERATOR


GENERAL DEFINITION

This is data processing work operating equipment that is ancillary to the central processing unit in a large scale mainframe with multiple CPU and multiple operating system environment. The work may also involve operating a computer console in addition to peripheral equipment in an electronic mini- computer or micro-computer environment. An employee in this class prepares a computer for program processing of keypunched cards, tape and disk materials, monitors the peripheral equipment for proper operation, and performs minor maintenance. In addition to these duties, the employee initiates computer operations and monitors the computer or microcomputer environment. Employees in mini-computer or microcomputer environments may install and replace terminal consoles and similar peripheral equipment. Work is performed under the supervision of a technical superior.


TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Reads job control sheet and other instructions before beginning assignments; obtains and places required keypunch program cards, magnetic tapes, or magnetic disks into card feeder or tape/disk drives; sets up printer with proper forms; adjusts print density and the optical printer chain if required by job.
  • Observes the functioning of machines during job runs and notes their registering of data; checks for keypunch cards out of sequence or damaged tape; studies machine operation instructions and manuals for possible solution to program breakdown or machine malfunction; adjusts printer, tape or disk drives, and other equipment to attempt correction of mechanical malfunction.
  • Monitors functioning of programs and computer equipment on processor console in a mini- computer or microcomputer environment; determines point of program failure or equipment malfunction; interprets generated console messages; may contact systems support technicians for appropriate operating system commands to effect systems or network recovery.
  • May temporarily operate and monitor operating systems console in a large scale mainframe with multiple CPU and multiple operating systems environment.
  • Adjusts controls on mini-computer console and other peripheral equipment to start job run; monitors processor consoles or microcomputer to receive program messages and commands; interprets program messages and commands or contacts system support technicians; operates console or microcomputer computer keyboard to respond to program messages and commands.
  • Assembles completed job runs for return to requesting authorities; prepares computer-generated notices for mailing collects and compiles processed cards and set-up sheets; returns magnetic tape to tape library; logs out completed jobs.
  • Performs minor maintenance to equipment such as cleaning tape drives, cleaning tape heads, cleaning printers, replacing ribbons, and vacuuming filters.
  • May install and replace terminal consoles and microprocessors in a mini-computer or microcomputer environment; may install and replace modems, keyboards, and electronic reading devices; may replace components on terminal consoles.
  • Performs related work as required.


REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the operation of current electronic computers and related equipment, including data communication, inquiry systems, terminals, and consoles.
  • job control language.
  • the techniques, methods, and practices used in obtaining maximum utilization of electronic computers and peripheral equipment.
  • computer capabilities.
  • the care and minor maintenance of computer equipment.

ABILITY TO:

  • learn JCL programming techniques as applied to computer operation.
  • learn the most effective methods of machine operations within program framework.
  • learn relevant operating systems such as DOS/VSE or equivalent.
  • learn the operating routines utilized for programs in a mini-computer or microcomputer environment.
  • understand and follow written and oral instructions.
  • learn to read, interpret and respond to systems messages in the operation of computer consoles and peripherals.


MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 7/86.)

  • Successful completion of high school or equivalent.
  • One year of experience in the operation of computers and/or peripheral equipment located in a computer equipment room, or two years of experience in the operation of a variety of electronic tabulating, keypunching, or specialized word processing equipment or performing specialized data services support work which has been supplemented by formal training in job control language and the operation of computer consoles and peripheral equipment.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment if required by the work assignment.
