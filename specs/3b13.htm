ELECTRICAL SERVICES ENGINEERING SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory electrical engineering work with responsibility for the electrical aspects of design and construction projects and the electrical maintenance and repair program at municipal facilities. The employee in this class functions as project manager for electrical design and construction projects and directs, through subordinate a supervisor, electricians engaged in maintaining electrical systems and equipment. Contact with consultants and contractors to resolve design and construction problems is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an engineering administrator.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Prepares requests for proposals for consultant services; reviews consultant proposals for accuracy and completeness; makes selection recommendations; review designs, plans and specifications prepared by consultants for compliance to departmental and professional standards; recommends revisions to plans and specifications; review shop drawings for accuracy and conformance with plans, specifications and designs; meets with consultant engineers to resolve design problems.
  • Conducts feasibility studies to determine the practicality of electrical engineering projects; prepares electrical engineering designs, plans and specifications; prepares contract documents; determines material and labor requirements and computes cost estimates for electrical construction projects.
  • Reviews design and construction schedules; coordinates design and construction activities of departmental and contracted personnel; prepares and/or reviews daily construction reports; reviews and recommends the approval of change orders; participates in the final inspection of projects; recommends authorization of payments.
  • Directs, through a subordinate supervisor, the activities of electricians and helpers engaged in the alteration, installation, maintenance and repair of electrical systems and equipment; participates in the review of tenant proposals for modifications to or construction of electrical systems and equipment; participates in the establishment of preventive maintenance schedule and ensures adherence to schedules; reviews reports prepared by subordinate staff; provides technical guidance as required.
  • Prepares annual operating budget for electrical services; prepares requests for maintenance and service contracts; initiates emergency purchase orders and purchase requisitions.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices and theories of electrical engineering.
  • electrical engineering methods and techniques involved in the design, installation, operation, and testing of a variety of electrical systems and equipment.
  • modern construction methods and procedures as applied to the electrical phases of construction projects.
  • the ordinances and regulations governing electrical installations.

ABILITY TO:

  • plan and direct, through a subordinate supervisor, the activities of electricians engaged in maintaining electrical systems and equipment.
  • resolve electrical design and construction problems.
  • interpret and apply regulatory codes affecting the work.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and n writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, contractors, and consultants.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 11/89.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in electrical engineering.
  • Four years of electrical engineering work, two years of which have been at the full performance level.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included completion of a bachelor's degree program as specified above as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
