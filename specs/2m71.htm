CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2M71
EMPLOYEE HEALTH SERVICES ADMINISTRATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is health administrative and supervisory work directing an assigned phase of the City's overall occupational health services system. Employees in this class are responsible for either the day-to-day administration of medical care contracts covering injured or disabled municipal employees, or for administering the daily operations of the city's medical evaluation unit. Employees in this class supervise medical, nursing, technical and support personnel providing direct services or utilization review. Work includes responsibility for evaluating public and occupational health issues which affect City employees and developing employee health programs. Significant aspects of the work include the review of existing and establishment of new protocols to ensure that medical treatment is administered in the most efficient and cost-effective manner, continuous review of staffing needs for contracted and non-contracted services, and resolution of disputes regarding medical decisions and charges. Work is performed under the direction of the employee health services director.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. These positions must be responsible for directing all activities of an assigned phase of the occupational health services system through medical, nursing and technical personnel.
  2. These positions must be located in the Health Department's Employee Health Services Division.
  3. Only two positions will be allocated to this class.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Coordinates and supervises the daily activities of the medical evaluation or occupational health services unit; supervises physicians, nurses, technical and clerical personnel responsible for the provision of a variety of health services to municipal employees.
  • Reviews existing procedures and protocols of the assigned unit in conjunction with medical personnel; makes changes to current practices and establishes new protocols in an effort to improve efficiency of operations and accommodate new functions.
  • Confers with departmental administrators, independent medical specialists, and other appropriate professionals and interprets and applies expert opinions and/or complex health science and medical standards which affect the development of programs and policies.
  • Meets with departmental representatives on a periodic basis; assesses the need for the development of new health-related policies and programs for City employees; determines the feasibility of implementing new programs by conducting cost studies, reviewing printed material and consulting with field experts as needed; makes recommendations to superiors upon completion of study.
  • Designates and schedules employees to be recipients of established programs which may include such procedures as training or diagnostic tests; ensures that staff is available to provide the necessary services; evaluates the effectiveness of new and current programs.
  • Reviews current and develops new policies regarding the dispensation of medical services to employees; consults City attorneys to ensure that current or planned actions of the units are legally sound.
  • Oversees the payment process for those employees receiving treatment outside the City's compensation system and for treatment outside of the system; negotiates with providers to maximize services to City employees and reduce costs; establishes guidelines to be followed for approval of payment; ensures that treatment and/or equipment is appropriate in accordance with patient's condition and that charge is not excessive; authorizes payment for approved charges through subordinate staff; reviews and investigates those charges determined to be inappropriate by staff members; makes final determination based on investigation and discussion with medical personnel.
  • Assesses the staffing needs of assigned unit; intitates the acquisition of contracted or non-contracted staff as needed; interviews and selects new staff members; prepares the fiscal budget for unit; initiates contracts as needed for consultant services.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the administration, policies, regulations and procedures governing employee medical care and disability compensation in a large municipal jurisdiction.
  • the physical requirements and associated risks of various occupations.
  • direct and ancillary patient care programs.
  • the principles and practices of methods and procedures analysis.
  • methods of health services delivery.
  • appropriate use of medical and nursing personnel.

SKILL IN:

  • negotiating with health care providers to resolve service delivery disputes and ensure cost-effective treatment.

ABILITY TO:

  • evaluate, analyze, modify and develop procedures to increase efficiency of operations.
  • effectively resolve disputes involving medical and non-medical personnel.
  • exercise judgment in the interpretation of City and departmental regulations related to occupational health procedures.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationship with medical personnel, departmental officials, subordinates and associates.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/92.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE
  • Three years of administrative experience in the operation of a clinic delivering employee health services or in the administration of a departmental personnel or occupational safety program which has involved the determination and disposition of employee service-connected disability claims.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
