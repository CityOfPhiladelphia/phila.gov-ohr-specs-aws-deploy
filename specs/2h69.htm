LABOR ADMINISTRATIVE SERVICES COORDINATOR

 

 

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

This is administrative work coordinating employee relations/EEO complaint processing, labor grievance and arbitration intake and processing, EEO policy compliance reporting, and EEO training in the Department of Labor.  This position also is responsible for maintaining records of training attendees and ensuring all supervisors and managers are scheduled for the necessary trainings in a timely manner. This position evaluates the need for additional training programs, monitors requests from City officials for specialized training for departments or groups of employees and serves in a liaison capacity with City employees and officials concerning training. An employee in this class has access to information subject to use in collective bargaining and a close continuing relationship with a Department Director or a Deputy Director associated with collective bargaining. Work is performed under the direction of the Labor Administrative Services Officer.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS:  (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.    The position must be responsible for coordinating citywide training for the Department of Labor.

 

2.    Only one position shall be allocated to this class in the Department of Labor.

 

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

Monitors and tracks EEO claims and labor grievance and arbitration cases from several sources; enters complaints and grievances in the case management system; ensures case owner is accurately identified; assesses initial claim information to capture and track within case management system; produces management level reports. 

Schedules all training sessions for the Labor Relations and Employee Relations units of the Department of Labor; contacts appropriate parties related to training requests, enrollment and attendance; compiles and evaluates data and information; advises employees and City officials about details of training.

Maintains investigative and grievance files, and responds to related requests for information from Law Department.

Maintains records of training attendees and ensures all supervisors and managers are scheduled for the necessary trainings in a timely manner.

Maintains records documenting compliance activities mandated by EEO policy such as mandatory training, departmental signage, and designation of departmental EEO contacts.

 

Analyzes the need for additional training programs and monitors and responds to requests from City officials for specialized training for departments or group; utilizes

computerized software packages to provide and evaluate statistical trends and narrative/informational reports related to training; uses spreadsheets to enter and manipulate data in order to produce technical information; makes determinations and recommendations based on report findings.

          Determines and creates appropriate evaluation tools for the training courses; recommends changes based on evaluation results; prepares quarterly reports concerning the progress and effectiveness of training efforts.

            Maintains historical bargaining records; performs research, and prepares reports on topics related to the collective bargaining process; has access to information subject to use in collective bargaining; presents reports and recommendations associated with collective bargaining to a Department Director or a Deputy Director.

            Performs related work as required.
