INFORMATION CENTER ANALYST

GENERAL DEFINITION

This an analytical and training support work at the advanced performance level implementing the effective and appropriate use of non-procedural computer languages, microcomputers, and office automation to address management and business problems. An employee in this class analyzes clients' management and business problems, determines the applicability of the use of technological tools, assists clients in selecting and using appropriate products, and provides access to systems and training support. Work is distinguished from the full performance level in that the employee coordinates and directs all aspects of complex projects within assigned client agencies, functions with minimal supervision, and is responsible for supervising subordinates providing information center services on a project basis. Establishing and maintaining effective working relationships with personnel in client departments and with technical staff is extremely significant to the work. Work is performed under the general direction of an administrative supervisor and in accordance with City management information standards.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Confers with representatives of client departments to explain functions of the Information Center and client responsibilities in using the Center; determines clients' needs for decision support; designs study of existing procedures and resources to determine sources of input data, analyses to be performed, and description and scheduling requirements for output.
  • Coordinates the activities of client agency and information center staff in evaluating available resources and selecting best technology to meet needs; may prepare specifications for the acquisition of necessary hardware and software; develops prototype applications for decision support; obtains approval for prototypes for office of information management technical staff; advises clients on strengths and weaknesses of applications; determines areas of duplication of existing systems outputs with regard to client requests; determines equipment and personnel resources required to use technology; adapts programs to improve efficiency.
  • Determines with client representatives staff training needs regarding use of terminals, microcomputers, and non-procedural programs; provides technical support through use of appropriate training materials and media.
  • Arranges client access to systems by providing administrative assistance in locating work stations, getting log-on identification, password, and authorization for access to and alteration of data files; coordinates client data use with technical staff to maintain data integrity and security; ensures economical use of systems resources; assists users in resolving problems encountered during installation of application.
  • Determines cost-effectiveness of clients' use of Information Center resources; conducts post-audits of clients' maintenance of procedures, controls, and security; participates in meetings and activities of formal Information Center users' group.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • modern management principles and practices relating to a business, governmental, or non-profit organization.
  • the principle and practices of data analysis for business functions information management.
  • the uses and capabilities of data processing equipment in solving management and business problems.
  • the uses and capabilities of non-procedural languages such as RAMIS, Execucalc, Easytrieve, SPSS, or their equivalents.

ABILITY TO:

  • analyze and comprehend administrative decision-making needs and to make cogent recommendations concerning use of computer resources for management analysis.
  • analyze and make recommendations concerning office automation and the use of microcomputers.
  • select and adapt available in-service training material and to conduct in-service training programs.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with client agency representatives and central agency technical staff.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 6/1985.)

EDUCATION:

  • Completion of a master's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in business administration, operations research, decision sciences, or other approved equivalent degree areas.
  • Two years of administrative experience which has been in a business, governmental, or non-profit organization researching and analyzing data to improve efficiency for management decision making where computerization is used as a tool, one year of which has been at the full performance level.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum.

NOTE: Additional years of experience researching and analyzing data to improve efficiency for management decision making where computerization is used as a tool for a business, governmental, or non-profit organization may be substituted on a year for year basis for the master's degree.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
