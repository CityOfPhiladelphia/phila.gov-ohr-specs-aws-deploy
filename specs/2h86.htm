GENERAL DEFINITION

 

This is wage enforcement work in the Labor Standards Unit of the Department of Labor.  Employees in this class are responsible for monitoring and reviewing certified payrolls and ensuring proper wage determinations are applied in the wage compliance specialty.  Employees in the Diversity Compliance specialty monitor and review minority participation to ensure diversity of workforce to meet specified goals.  Enforcing the Davis-Bacon Act, Department of Labor and Industry standards, and City of Philadelphia Prevailing Wage among employers is significant to the work. Work is performed under the supervision of an administrative superior. 

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class)

 

1.    Positions in this class must be responsible for the monitoring and enforcement of federal, state, and local wage regulations with employers.

2.     Positions must be located in the Department of Labor.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

All specialties

Assists in investigating and resolving any violations of labor standards; prepares reports and apprises senior officials of compliance issues.

Conducts telephone, in-person, and on-site interviews, when necessary, regarding compliance.

May assist in special projects related to labor standards compliance.

Performs related work as required

 

Wage Compliance Specialty

Reviews, examines, investigates, and monitors certified payrolls of contractors for city agencies; visits job sites to interview employees for compliance and enforcement of the Davis-Bacon Act, Department of Labor and Industry standards, and City of Philadelphia Prevailing Wage; keeps logs of sites visited; applies construction requirements, applicable codes, and federal laws of construction sites; identifies non-compliant contractors.

 

Workforce Diversity Compliance Specialty

 

            Identifies contracts that meet the requirements for workforce diversity; monitors and enforces workforce diversity compliance on City of Philadelphia prevailing wage contracts; monitors and ensures workforce diversity compliance on City projects that fall under Chapter 17-1600 of the Philadelphia Code; identifies contractors not in compliance and monitors prime contractors that are not reaching their goals. 

            Prepares reports for assigned Workforce Diversity plans related to minority participation on City-funded, Prevailing Wage Contracts in accordance with Chapter 17-1600 of the Philadelphia Code; gathers data on jobs that have met a total hour contract percentage to ensure they are on track to reach goals; identifies deficiencies and actions needed to achieve Workforce Diversity compliance; approves workforce plans on new contracts that show how diversity goals will be met. 

            Conducts telephone, in-person, and on-site interviews, when necessary, regarding compliance with Workforce Diversity Plans.

            May attend meetings regarding matters of Workforce Diversity within the City of Philadelphia.

            Performs related work as required.

Diversity Compliance Specialty

SKILL IN:

 

All specialties

·         interviewing and investigatory techniques

·         eliciting information

·         interacting with City officials and the public in issues pertaining to prevailing wage compliance

Diversity Compliance Specialty

·         documenting findings

 

ABILITY TO:

·         work both independently and collaboratively

·         meet deadlines and handle multiple projects consistently

·         interpret and explain regulations, policies, and procedural guidelines for prevailing wage contract compliance

·         express ideas orally and in writing effectively

EDUCATION:

All Specialties:

 

            Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

 

AND

EXPERIENCE:


Wage Compliance Specialty:

 

            Three years of administrative experience in the preparation and processing of payroll or contractual documents in a construction, legal, or social service agency.

 

OR

            Three years of inspectional experience which has involved eliciting information from the public.

 

Diversity Compliance Specialty:

 

Three years of administrative experience in an advocacy, legal, or social service agency.

OR

Any equivalent combination of education or experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources.
