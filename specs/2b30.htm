REVENUE EXAMINER TRAINEE

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is auditing work of a training nature developing knowledge, skills, and abilities in the examination of accounting and other business records for the purpose of determining tax liabilities. As experience is gained a progressively expanding range and complexity of tax auditing projects are assigned. Work is performed under the supervision of a technical superior. Assignments are stable in nature and are carried out in accordance with instructions and established routines. Employees are responsible for the performance of assigned audits or segments of such audits.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

Learns the techniques of and participates in the review of both computerized and manual accounts and other fiscal records of business firms and individuals; serves as member of a field audit team by verifying tax returns against accumulated data and recomputing tax liabilities.

Learns and applies standard accounting and federal tax filing procedures, directives, and guidelines for use in reviewing accounting source documents, categorizing accounts and financial transactions, summarizing financial data, and verifying the accuracy of tax filings.

Gathers and compiles manual and computer generated supporting detail for computing tax liabilities; prepares original and adjusted billings; enters billing information into computer files.

Receives instructions in the content, interpretation, and application of business and earnings tax ordinances and in the procedures of explaining such laws to taxpayers and their representatives; attends training sessions to learn of new or revised tax ordinances, recent court decisions, or changes in auditing policies and procedures; meets with members of the law department to participate in court proceedings as a witness for the City.

Uses standardized computer forms and format in preparing reports of findings for assigned phases of audits; attends exit conferences with taxpayers and their representatives to observe procedures followed by auditing staff in explaining tax ordinances and in resolving appeals.

Assists taxpayers in the preparation of all city business and individual tax returns by answering telephone inquiries concerning the city tax laws and regulations, and explaining how to properly file the city tax returns. This assistance also includes preparation of tax filings for the general public.

Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

    KNOWLEDGE OF:

    • accounting principles and practices
    • standard auditing procedures and of their application to the analysis of accounts for tax enforcement purposes

    ABILITY TO:

    • learn tax laws and their application in determining tax liability
    • learn the accounting systems used by business organizations,
    • analyze accounting records and financial statements for purposes of tax enforcement
    • prepare written audit reports
    • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and the public

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 06/09.)

    EDUCATION

    Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university, with a major in accounting or certification as a Certified Public Accountant by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
