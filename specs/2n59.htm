2N59
REVENUE ADMINISTRATIVE DIRECTOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative management work for the City's Department of Revenue. The employee in this class is responsible for planning, directing, and coordinating administrative services including budget and expenditure control functions, a personnel unit, the administrative analysis function, an extensive revenue mailing operation, and office services support, a stores function, and a management information system. Of major significance to the work is the directing of a large administrative staff through subordinate supervisors and planning and implementing divisional operations. The employee reports to the Revenue Commissioner.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans and directs administrative functions such as budget preparation, accounting, personnel, administrative analysis, and management information systems development; plans and directs the activities of a unit responsible for monitoring the quality of services provided by the operating divisions of the department and preparing departmental staff for review and inspection by regulatory agencies; develops administrative procedures and sets goals; analyzes and evaluates administrative problems to improve services and operations.
  • Confers with departmental managers concerning administrative services needs and requirements in relation to municipal and departmental policies, rules, and regulations, interprets policy determinations as they affect specific phases of the administrative program.
  • Attends staff conferences and participates in the development of departmental policy; consults with subordinate administrative officials on the application of policy to specific cases occurring in the administrative area of departmental operations; resolves all administrative matters which do not require the attention of the Commissioner.
  • Reviews operating and capital budget requests for conformance with budget policy, economy of operation, and needs of the department; makes recommendations for modifications; explains needs, figures, and justification of budget results before fiscal, management, and planning agencies; provides information to departmental head at hearings before City Council.
  • Ascertains by review of reports and financial statements the financial status of the department and ensures that funds are properly spent.
  • Reviews and approves purchase requisitions for major cost items and recommends approval or disapproval of items requiring a substantial amount of funds.
  • Directs analysis and planning studies be made of departmental operations; reviews recommendations for resolution of problems and discusses them with division heads; arranges for implementation of solution.
  • Plans and participates in the development and the implementation of specialized operations such as the election returns reporting process, an automated tax collection system, and the extensive revenue billing mail operation.
  • Prepares or supervises the preparation of reports governing administrative actions; brings salient facts pertinent to program progress to the attention of the Commissioner so that major decisions regarding them may be made.
  • Represents the department in meetings with other city agencies and state and federal officials; discusses departmental policies and procedures; participates with other division heads and the Commissioner in formulating and reviewing long and short term policy.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of public administration including such areas as budgeting, organizational development, and staffing.
  • the principles and practices of administrative organization and management and their application in solving a variety of operational and administrative problems.
  • the principles and practices of administrative and managerial analysis.
  • the principles and practices of public finances as applies to the preparation and control of operating and capital budgets.
  • supervisory methods and techniques, practices, policies, and procedures.

SKILL IN:

  • evaluating the effectiveness of administrative functions and programs and implementing changes to provide for more effective and efficient operations.

ABILITY TO:

  • plan, organize, direct and coordinate the activities of a large multidiscipline staff providing a variety of administrative services.
  • evaluate the quality and effectiveness of administrative functions.
  • analyze and evaluate complex organizational, administrative, and managerial problems as they apply to a large municipal human services agency.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, representatives of central agencies, and the general public.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/1988.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
  • Seven years of administrative experience, four years of which shall have included supervising budgetary, personnel, or administrative analysis functions for a large municipal department, one year of which shall have been directing the provision of all administrative services necessary to provide efficient operation for a department characterized by moderate complexity and diversity of its functions.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
