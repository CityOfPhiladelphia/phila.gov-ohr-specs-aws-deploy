CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

3H46
FORENSIC INSTRUMENTATION SPECIALIST

Specialties
( Electron Microscopy/ Infrared Analysis ) ( Mass Spectrometry )

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is specialized analytical chemistry work utilizing mass spectrometry, or electron microscopy and infrared analysis, and a variety of analytical instruments and techniques, for forensic purposes. The employee in this class specializes in either mass spectrometry or electron microscopy and infrared analysis and also supervises the activity of one or more subordinate chemists and/or technicians performing analysis of criminal evidence by means of chemical analysis instruments, and advises all chemists and technicians in the laboratory unit regarding the results and their interpretation. Contact with law enforcement and court officials, and laboratory employees, and giving testimony in court concerning results of analysis, are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the supervision of a technical supervisor.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

    Electron Microscopy/Infrared Analysis Specialty
  • Supervises or performs the analysis of evidence by Fourier transform infrared spectrometry and scanning electron microscopy, and other laboratory instrumentation as required.
  • Determines computer program selection for type and nature of evidence to be analyzed; activates computer programs by use of terminal keyboard to input command; observes screen for display of electronic scanner sample and for analytical graphs produced by the computer and scanning microscope; activates printer for hard copy of scanner image or graphs; interprets results by use of spectra manuals and experience to produce report; regularly calibrates microscopic equipment to insure accurate results; keeps abreast of development in electron scanning microscopy and related computer applications to develop possible forensic applications.
  • Prepares microscopic samples for analysis by Fourier transform infrared spectrophotometer; makes complex computer programs which control instrumentation and data preservation parameters; produces and evaluates print-outs with analytical patterns resulting from infrared analysis; interprets results by use of spectra manuals and analytical experience; regularly calibrates infrared equipment to insure accurate results; keeps abreast of developments in infrared analysis and related computer applications to develop possible forensic applications.

    Mass Spectrometry Specialty

  • Supervises or performs the analysis of such criminal evidence as suspected controlled substances, other drugs, precursors, by-products, and other compounds including diluents and poisons by use of the gas chromatograph/mass spectrometer, a gas chromatograph, a liquid chromatograph and infrared equipment; directs the preparation of test samples for instrument analysis; calibrates equipment to insure accurate results; interprets instrumentation results; searches and constructs computer-based libraries; identifies nature and quantities of substances; insures all analysis is ready for scheduled court sessions.

Both Specialties

  • Reviews test results of subordinates; advises chemists on types of chemical analysis to use to identify unusual substances; determine which of several analytical instruments and techniques should be used to check on results of standard analysis; diagnoses system malfunction and performs minor repairs.
  • Analyzes unit instrumentation needs; reviews available analysis equipment and systems; recommends equipment and/or system purchase or modification; prepares information for and presents to informal departmental committee on laboratory matters concerning improvement in techniques, equipment, methodology and procedures.
  • Reviews and studies current technical books and publications related to gas chromatography/mass spectrometric analysis or electron microscopy and infrared analysis; conducts analytical methods research to improve, develop and establish instrumental test procedures; prepares analytical methods reports on results of projects and analyses.
  • Prepares programs in specialized program language to improve or alter computer-assisted instrument selectivity for interpretation of results; researches appropriate available computer software to improve instrument interpretive capability.
  • Prepares written reports on results of projects and analyses.
  • Prepares analysis reports and exhibits for court testimony; testifies in court as to methodology and results of analysis; provides expert testimony on general or hypothetical questions and explains technical methods and procedures to non-technical court personnel.
  • Supervises the work of subordinate professionals and technician assigned to instrumentation work; trains such personnel in the theory and operation of instrumentation for the appropriate specialty.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, methods, and practices of analytical chemistry, especially as applied to forensic analysis.
  • analytical and organic chemistry.
  • the applications of chemical principles, methods, and practices in the analysis of samples for organic and inorganic compounds.
  • mathematics and statistics as they relate to chemistry.
  • analytical chemistry techniques utilizing standard laboratory equipment and materials.
  • the procedures for operating a gas chromatograph/mass spectrometer, for the appropriate specialty.
  • electron scanning microscopy and of instrumental infrared analysis for the appropriate specialty.
  • electronics, optics and vacuum technology as applied to forensic laboratory instrumentation.

SKILL IN:

  • the use and care of laboratory equipment.

ABILITY TO:

  • operate and accurately interpret results of a gas chromatograph/mass spectrometer; or electron scanning microscope and infrared instrument, for the appropriate specialty.
  • conduct research on chemical methods and techniques.
  • present expert testimony in court understandable to non-professional court participants.
  • read and interpret instrumentation schematics and service manuals.
  • learn the applications of data processing systems to chemical analyses.
  • learn to use specialized program language for use in interpretation of results.
  • prepare reports showing results of tests and analyses.
  • determine the proper analytical test or procedures for use in conducting chemical analysis.
  • instruct and advise subordinate chemists and technicians in forensic instrumentation work.
  • express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 9/87.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in chemistry.

  • Two years of experience performing chemical analyses of organic compounds, one year of which has included analysis of organic compounds with a gas chromatography/mass spectrometer, liquid chromatograph and other instrumentation equipment and techniques for the Mass Spectrometry specialty, or in the analysis of organic or non-organic compounds or materials with a scanning electron microscope and instrumental infrared analysis for the Electron Microscopy/Infrared Analysis specialty, for forensic analysis purposes.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included completion of a bachelor's degree program as specified above as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
