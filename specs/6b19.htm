FIRE SERVICE EMERGENCY MEDICAL TECHNICIAN - ADVANCED

(General)

                                                                                                            (Bilingual)

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

This is advanced level Emergency Medical Technician field work responding to emergency calls from the public to administer emergency medical care and provide safe transport to an accredited hospital emergency room or full-service medical facility.  Employees in this class serve as a first responder with responsibility for assessing patient condition and determining actions necessary for stabilization and administering medications and vaccines. Comforting patients, reassuring family and bystanders, and coordinating with the general public, fire and police authorities are of major significance to this work.  Duties are performed according to Pennsylvania standard emergency medical services for Advanced Emergency Medical Technician and related scope of practice and protocols.  This class is distinguished from the lower level class by treatment of airway ventilation and oxygenation, immobilization and by administration of medication and intravenous needles.  Employees in this class work on rotating shifts and are subject to adverse weather conditions, and work requires moderate physical effort.  Work is performed under the general supervision of an administrative or technical fire officer.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be

                                                allocated to this class.)

 

1.    Positions in this class must perform limited advanced life support work responding to emergency calls from the public in accordance with the Pennsylvania Emergency Medical Services Act Rules and Regulations requirements for Advanced Emergency Medical Technicians.

2.    Positions must be located in the Fire Department. 

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

Provides medical intervention and administers medications and vaccines; assists Fire Service Paramedics with providing intravenous injections of a variety of drugs based on radio-based hospital physican instructions or according to established life support practices and drug therapy protocol; uses equipment, resuscitators, and nasal and oral suction devices to treat respiratory problems and to improve oxygenation; assists Fire Service Paramedics during Advanced Life Support (ALS) emergency interventions; provides limited advanced life support techniques; evaluates the emergency situation; may contact physician for medical direction; administers proper emergency treatment in accordance with scope of practice for, departmental protocol and patient assessment.

Drives mobile unit to scene of emergency in response to call in accordance with department safety policies and dispatcher instructions; contacts fire units to provide equipment such as ladders or hydraulic power tools for use in extricating patients before or after treatment, or police units to provide crowd control or other assistance.

Administers basic life support and limited advanced life support to stabilize patient conditions; administers cardiopulmonary resuscitation techniques, oxygen equipment, and automated external defibrillator (AED) to treat cardiac arrest;

Observes vital signs of patients by checking respiration, pulse, blood pressure; questions patient to obtain information to determine nature of injury; examines body for fractures, wounds, signs of fever or symptoms; evaluates signs and decides on course of action; records and reports on patients condition or injury and treatment provided.

Uses personal protective equipment when interacting with patients; applies bandages; treats burns; uses a variety of splints to set suspected broken bones; treats for shock; may be exposed to bloodborne pathogens; may deliver children under emergency conditions and provide medical care to mother of child.

            Lifts and moves patient on stretcher; loads and unloads patient on litter into and out of ambulance.

Completes refresher training and instruction in new treatment techniques, protocol changes, and the use of new materials and equipment.

Completes individual reports on each treatment given, any medications administered, location and identity of patient and receiving hospital, and special problems encountered; requisitions materials and equipment as needed; completes all documentation as required; communicates unit status, updates, receiving hospital notifications, medical doctor consultation and resource requests via radio/phone/computer in accordance with departmental policy. 

Maintains equipment and supply stock in emergency vehicle; regularly cleans motor vehicle; performs first echelon maintenance on assigned emergency vehicle; performs assigned housekeeping duties in station.

Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         Pennsylvania Advanced Emergency Medical Technician practices

·         Pennsylvania Basic Life Support (BLS) and Advanced Life Support (ALS) treatment protocols within scope of practice

·         vital signs and symptoms of physical condition of persons requiring emergency treatment

·         procedures, practices, materials and techniques used in the emergency treatment of burns, wounds and other physical trauma

·         procedures, practices, materials and techniques used in the emergency treatment of cardiac problems and cardiac arrest, especially in the application of cardiopulmonary resuscitation techniques and automated external defibrillation

·         procedures, practices, materials and techniques used in the treatment of respiratory problems and respiratory arrest, especially in the application of cardiopulmonary resuscitation

·         procedures, practices, materials and techniques used in the emergency treatment of shock, childbirth deliveries, illness, stroke and drug abuse

·         rescue and evacuation principles, procedures and techniques

·         departmental operational procedures, directives, and safety practices

·         physical properties, characteristics and problems of common chemical hazards

·         ambulance mechanical equipment and routine preventative maintenance

 

SKILL IN:

 

·         administering subcutaneous or intramuscular injections

·         the use of medical equipment

·         the use of automated external defibrillation equipment

·         the application of bandages, splints, and stretcher-carrying techniques

·         the use of a variety of oxygen equipment

·         current Emergency Medical Technician techniques

·         reading maps and understanding Global Positioning System (GPS) data

·         assessing and controlling hazards present to self, victim and bystanders

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·         provide limited advanced life support techniques under the supervision of a Fire Service Paramedic

·         follow medical directions in the treatment of medical emergencies

·         think clearly and make critical decisions in stressful situations

·         understand and interpret established Pennsylvania Basic Life Support protocols

·         use medical equipment to administer proper basic life support and life saving measures

·         drive an emergency vehicle with caution in accordance with state regulations and departmental rules

·         complete required records and documents accurately concerning emergency treatment of patient

·         maintain vehicles, medical and communication equipment

·         think clearly and effectively in emergency situations

·         establish effective working relationships with other fire units, police personnel, medical personnel and the general public

·         speak fluently in the designated non-English language for the bilingual specialty

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE  (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 03/20.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

 

AND

CERTIFICATION:

 

            Possession of a current Emergency Medical Technician (EMT)- Advanced Certification issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania. 

 

 

ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENT FOR THE BILINGUAL SPECIALTY

 

Sufficient training and/or experience to be able to converse effectively in English and the designated non‑English language.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

 

Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as Fire Service Emergency Medical Technician-Advanced.

Employees in this class must have not been convicted of reckless driving or had a drivers license suspended due to use of drugs or alcohol or a moving traffic violation within two years prior to the last date for filing applications and at any time during tenure of employment as a Fire Service Emergency Medical Technician- Advanced.

Possession of certification by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania as an Emergency Medical Services Vehicle Operator at the time of application and during tenure of employment as a Fire Service Emergency Medical Technician-Advanced.

Possession of certification by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania as an Emergency Medical Technician- Advanced at the time of application and during tenure of employment as a Fire Service Emergency Medical Technician-Advanced. 

Successful completion of the required hours of Continuing Education Credits

every three years for recertification as an Emergency Medical Technician-Advanced during tenure of employment as a Fire Service Emergency Medical Technician-Advanced.
