CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

3A37
CABLE AREA MANAGER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is engineering contract compliance work monitoring cable television services in one of the City's four geographically assigned cable areas. An employee in this class monitors cable television contracts with private sector cable companies to insure compliance with contract specifications and adherence to FCC regulations and ordinances. Work may include project management activities in the installation and implementation of new equipment and technology and new cable company replacement equipment. A significant aspect of the work is participation in the operation of the City's two municipal cable television channels' production and broadcasting activities for public viewing. Work is performed under the supervision of a technical supervisor.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

  • The work must be performed in the Communications Division of the Public Property Department.
  • The position must direct the activities of and monitor contract compliance for one (1) of the City's cable company franchise agreements assigned to a geographic area of the city.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Oversees and maintains all franchise agreement requirements, including technical standards and customer service regulations; accepts, compiles and reviews all public complaints and investigates special problems in the field.
  • Corresponds at the management level with other departments and agencies, such as Law, Streets, City Council and Federal/State agencies.
  • Reviews plans and specifications for any prospective new installation; determines if plans provide adequate electrical current levels and power to area buildings; insures plans comply with contract specifications and engineering standards; contacts cable firms to indicate variances and problems and to suggest corrections or improvements; recommends the disapproval of plans that fail to meet specifications or standards.
  • Determines and applies Federal Communications Commission (FCC) regulations to comply with rate regulation of basic cable TV service, all associated equipment (converters, remote controls) and installation in one of the franchise areas; reviews financial documents submitted by cable operators which support their rate structures; analyzes them working with legal, accounting and consultant personnel to ascertain whether regulations have been met; responds to questions about and make interpretations of the legal, technical and consumer related aspects of these documents and policies to cable firm representatives, the general public, property owners and others; responds to complaints from residents, property owners and others concerning their rights under the Franchise and Federal Cable Act provisions within the department's jurisdiction. Familiarizes oneself with CATV franchise agreements, laws, ordinances, rules, regulations and policies concerning cable installations in the City; answers questions about and makes interpretations of technical and engineering aspects of these documents and policies to cable firm representatives, media representatives, property owners and others; receives complaints from residents, property owners and others concerning cable installation and operations problems or related services; prepares log entries and complaint documents and forwards latter to cable firms; monitors disposition of cable complaints.
  • May provide production services for the two City operated government access channels, including technical services, operation of equipment, liaison with City departments and City Council.
  • Recommends, installs, and operates television production equipment for the establishment of studios for the two government access channels 63 and 64.
  • Prepares periodic and special reports on work; may prepare maps or charts and take photos of areas or specific installation work, either for own area or entire project.
  • May review financial reports and engineering documents and provide technical, and advisory support to other projects, such as the subway TV surveillance project, if so assigned.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • electronic engineering theory and of the principles and practices of engineering as applied to the installation and operation of television cable.
  • the methods and techniques of engineering as applied in inspecting and testing materials used in public works cable television installation projects.
  • contract administration including scheduling and the preparation of contracts and change orders.
  • engineering mathematics as applied to cable television projects.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
  • legislation, regulations, policies and guidelines concerning City-wide TV cable installation.

ABILITY TO:

  • analyze cable franchise agreements and enforce regulations.
  • evaluate cable operations modifications for soundness of engineering features.
  • analyze installation and specification interpretation problems and to exercise sound judgment in their resolution.
  • analyze data and explain contract deviation to contractors and to resolve disputes firmly and tactfully.
  • review and analyze contractor's proposed work schedule.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, contractors, and others.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/95.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in electronic or electrical engineering.

    GENERAL

  • Two years of electronic, electrical or construction engineering experience.

    SPECIFIC

  • Two years of engineering experience at the full performance level designing, modifying designs of, or overseeing the installation of the electronic or electrical engineering phases of engineering projects.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience which has included completion of a two year program in an acceptable area of engineering or engineering technology as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
