PARK MANAGER I

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is work of a technical and administrative nature designed to broaden the employee's knowledges and skills in park management. As experience is gained, the incumbent becomes involved in projects which expand progressively in range and complexity in such park operations as administration, horticulture, arboriculture, grounds maintenance and recreation. As the employee reaches the full performance level he becomes eligible for promotion to the next level of park manager.

Work is performed under the supervision of an administrative services director. Assignments call for evaluative thinking and are carried out in accordance with standard administrative practices and procedures. Employees in this class perform interpretive and advisory services which form an important basis for actions taken by superiors in a variety of park activities.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Performs technical and administrative work in such areas as administration, horticulture, arboriculture, grounds maintenance and/or recreation park divisions on a planned rotating basis.
  • Obtains and evaluates facts concerning park operating procedures, practices and organizational structures; confers with operating unit supervisors on administrative and operational problems; prepares reports and recommendations for administrative superiors.
  • Prepares a variety of periodic and special reports which deal with budget requirements, personnel vacancies, work production and park usage and needs.
  • Assists in the development and implementation of park management procedures.
  • Prepares written instructions on the implementation of new procedures.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of park administration.
  • the principles and practices of horticulture.
  • the principles and practices of forestry.
  • the principles and practices of landscape architecture.
  • the park operations.
  • the techniques of planning, coordinating and supervising the work of technical and clerical staff.
ABILITY TO:

  • analyze facts and exercise sound judgments.
  • apply the principles of management in relation to coordinating and planning the flow of work of technical and clerical employees.
  • present ideas effectively both orally and in writing.
  • understand and follow oral and written instructions.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with employees.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/75.)

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program in an accredited college or university with major course work in park administration, horticulture, forestry, or landscape architecture.
  • One year of on-the-job training developing skills and gaining experience in such areas as administration, horticulture, arboriculture, grounds maintenance and/or recreation.
NOTE:

Completion of a master's degree program from an accredited college or university in one of the fields cited above may be substituted for the one year of experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
