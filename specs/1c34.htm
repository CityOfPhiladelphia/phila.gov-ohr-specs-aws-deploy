CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

1C34
TELEPHONE CONSOLE SYSTEM SUPERVISOR II


GENERAL DEFINITION

This is telephone console system work at the second supervisory level with responsibility for the operation of the City's central exchange telephone system. The employee in this class supervises, through subordinate supervisors, a large number of telephone console operators involved in the maintenance of a high degree of efficiency in a municipal telephone installation. Work is performed under the direction of a telephone services manager.


TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Assigns, schedules, and reviews, through subordinate supervisors, the work of a large group of telephone console operators; directs subordinate supervisors to update and make changes to extension number, beeper number and other data files; makes reassignments for and directs procedures for distributing critical information and handling calls during city emergencies; prepares subordinate supervisors to handle calls resulting from special events; schedules the working hours of operators and supervisors; sees that operators are providing rapid and accurate service; assists operators in answering questions by plugging in on line.
  • Instructs new employees in the operation of the particular console to which assigned; instructs new operators in the functions of the departments which are served by the console system; monitors system periodically to check on efficiency of operations; receives complaints about telephone service and takes measures to eliminate their cause.
  • Maintains records of all long-distance and toll calls and of action taken during fires and other emergencies.
  • Performs related work as required.


REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the operation of a telephone console installation.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.

SKILL IN:

  • the operation of a telephone console.

ABILITY TO:

  • secure the accurate and efficient operation of a large telephone console system.
  • speak clearly and distinctly.
  • plan standard operating procedures and to consult with subordinates on complex operating problems.
  • understand and follow oral and written instructions.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and telephone users.


MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/1987.)

  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • Three years of experience in the operation of a multiple-position telephone switchboard or a telephone console system, one year of which shall have involved responsibility for the supervision, on an assigned shift, of a large central switchboard or telephone console system serving a number of agencies, or supervision of the operation of a switchboard or telephone console system in a large institution.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
