CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

3E71
SLUDGE UTILIZATION MANAGER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative environmental sciences work directing the City's land-based sludge utilization program through a subordinate scientific supervisor and subordinate scientific and engineering personnel. An employee in this class is responsible for developing and administering various sludge based products in cost-effective and environmentally-accepted utilization programs. Evaluating new and alternative sludge utilization methods and their costs, preparing sludge utilization contracts, working closely with state and regulatory agencies, recommending modifications and improvements to proposed environmental regulations and attending public meetings and hearings to explain the sludge application process and legal requirements are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative supervisor.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans and directs the activities and implementation of the City's sludge utilization program; supervises a subordinate scientific supervisor engaged in research projects, and scientific personnel engaged in a compost marketing and landfill projects; assigns and supervises scientific and engineering personnel engaged in specific phases and special projects of sludge application and utilization programs; reviews and evaluates the results and impact of subordinates' sludge utilization research and development projects; determines whether subordinates' recommended utilization program alternatives are environmentally and economically sound; implements program development policies and procedures and sludge marketing strategies; provides administrative and technical assistance to subordinate; determines testing, analysis and sample collection methods and procedures to be used in sludge utilization site preparation; trains subordinate in new and revised site evaluation and preparation methods; reviews subordinates; written reports and annual, quarterly and monthly summaries.
  • Reviews and approves all contracts in sludge application agreements; reviews contractor qualifications; prepares and/or reviews contract specifications for other sludge hauling, marketing, strip mine reclamation, local utilization programs and other special projects; administers these contracts through subordinate personnel.
  • Visits and delegates visits to sludge utilization sites to determine and ensure the progress and quality to work; meets with contractors and makes corrections to problems encountered as a result of these visits; reviews new alternative sludge utilization methods for feasibility, dependability and cost effectiveness; conducts research into the positive and negative effects of sludge utilization on crops grown in sludge, the potential for soil or ground-water pollution, and other environmental concerns; prepares reports on research findings.
  • Meets with state and federal regulatory agencies to discuss regulations and compliance requirements; reviews proposed regulations, rules, polices and standards governing the proper use of sludge-based products; establishes monitoring procedures and strategies for compliance and work quality governing various sludge utilization contracts; insures prompt and adequate response to regulatory agency correspondence; directs preparation of sludge application permits; monitors reports mandated by local, state and federal regulatory agencies.
  • Attends public meetings, workshops and hearings on the City's behalf to explain the beneficial uses of sludge-based products; meets with township officials and residents to explain the sludge application process in an effort to obtain additional land for sludge application; represents the department at public hearings concerning permits and sludge application strategies; collects and prepares the sludge utilization program budget for supervisory review and monitors expenditures; prepares quarterly and annual reports on the progress of utilization activities.
  • Participates as a member of regional and national sludge utilization committees; attends conferences to keep abreast of the latest developments in sludge utilization; implements new ideas where possible.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the impact of methods of environmental analysis and planning as applicable to sludge utilization.
  • the practices, principles and methods of sludge processing, utilization and marketing.
  • federal and state rules, regulations and standards applicable to sludge application.
  • the current literature, research and trends in sludge utilization and programs.
  • contract and specification preparation as applied to sludge application agreements.
  • agronomy as applied to agriculture, soil preservation and sludge utilization and application.
  • environmental analysis, planning and research projects.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
  • the principles, practices and techniques of public speaking.

ABILITY TO:

  • conduct environmental analysis and planning.
  • apply principles of environmental planning and agronomy to the development of sludge utilization and projects.
  • establish and implement short and long-term sludge utilization program objective and polices.
  • develop, evaluate and improve sample collection procedures and methods of analysis of testing results.
  • plan, assign and direct the work of subordinate agronomists and engineers engaged in research, development, and evaluation of sludge application programs and sample collection and analysis of soil sample testing results.
  • determine, evaluate, develop and implement sludge utilization marketing strategies.
  • prepare and negotiate contracts related to sludge utilization agreements.
  • prepare comprehensive written and oral reports on marketing efforts.
  • express ideas effectively, both oral and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, consultants, governmental and regulatory agencies, community groups and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/90.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in agronomy or environmental studies.
  • Four years of experience performing environmental impact analysis and planning work relevant to water pollution control and utilization of wastewater by-products or performing agronomic work for a land-based sludge utilization program two years of which shall have been at the full performance level.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included completion of a bachelor's degree program as specified above as the educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment in this class, if required by work assignment.
