CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

7B28
SEWER MAINTENANCE SUPERINTENDENT

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative and technical work, directing a city-wide program of inspection, maintenance, and repair of sewer structures, inlets, flood and stream control facilities. Work includes responsibility for establishing policies for, and planning, an overall inspection and repair program, recommending priorities for contracted sewer construction or re-construction activities, and contacting public utility officials to coordinate work where subterranean utility structures are adjacent to sewer structures. A significant aspect of the work includes the responsibility for contacting the general public to resolve sensitive complaints. Work is performed under the direction of a technical superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The employee in this class must perform administrative and technical work directing, through subordinate supervisors, a program of inspection, maintenance, and repair of sewer structures, inlets, flood and stream control facilities.
  2. Only one position will be allocated to this class.
  3. This position must be located in the Water Department.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, directs and coordinates through subordinate supervisors the inspection, maintenance and repair of sewer structures, inlets, and flood and stream control facilities; reviews blueprints and plate plans of existing sewers and determines which sewers will be inspected.
  • Reviews supervisors' reports of work progress, work completion, personnel assigned and length of time needed to complete work; analyzes information gathered and takes action to improve deficiencies in the performance of personnel and equipment; conducts periodic work site inspections to determine adherence to departmental policies, work standards and safety practices; reviews inspection reports and makes field re-inspections to determine the need for the priority of sewer reconstruction or major repairs; recommends major sewer repair or reconstruction projects for performance by contractors.
  • Analyzes overall effectiveness of sewer maintenance program; sets annual goals and objectives designed to increase productivity, quality of work and occupational safety awareness; prepares annual operating budget estimates; prepares or supervises the preparation of annual unit activity reports; prepares or supervises the preparation of periodic activity reports, reports concerning emergency problems, and reports on complaints from the general public; determines supplies, materials, equipment and personnel needs.
  • Receives, reviews and assigns citizen complaints to subordinates for investigation and resolution; may investigate sensitive or recurring complaints; serves as a technical representative in law claims involving sewer and sewer maintenance; may testify in court regarding law claims involving sewers.
  • Confers with inter and intra-departmental officials and representatives of various utilities concerning the relationships of subterranean utility structures and sewer structures; recommends acceptable structural relocations.
  • Reviews and evaluates new machinery and equipment available for sewer inspections, repairs and cleaning; requests the purchase of new machinery and introduces new maintenance techniques; implements and enforces sewer maintenance safety program; designs and oversees the implementation of safety practices and procedures; trains personnel in policies and procedures of sewers maintenance and the equipment used.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods, practices, materials and equipment used in the inspection, maintenance and repair of sewer structures, inlets, and flood and stream control facilities.
  • the economic utilization of equipment and personnel.
  • the general physical layout, component structures, and appurtenances of the municipal sewer system.
  • the occupational hazards present and safety precautions used in sewer inspection, maintenance and repair work.
  • supervisory methods and techniques required in the direction of a large staff of clerical, supervisory and field personnel.
  • the preparation of the budget and monitoring of budget expenditures.

SKILL IN:

  • determining and rectifying deficiencies in the sewer system.
  • evaluating the effectiveness of administrative functions and in making procedural changes to provide for more effective and efficient operations.

ABILITY TO:

  • plan, implement and review, through subordinate supervisors, a city-wide program of inspection, maintenance and repair of sewer structures, inlets, and flood and stream control facilities.
  • determine by visual inspection the structural weakness, defects and repair needs of sewer structures.
  • analyze effectiveness of equipment, procedures, and personnel, and rectify deficiencies.
  • evaluate and implement new equipment, materials, and techniques relating to sewer maintenance.
  • specify and anticipate the need for personnel, materials, tools and equipment essential to sewer maintenance and repair.
  • make estimates of time, cost, labor and material.
  • interpret for subordinates the more complex sewer plans and blueprints.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and the general public.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • maintain records and prepare reports pertinent to sewer maintenance.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 8/95.)

EDUCATION

  • Equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE
  • Four years of experience in the inspection, repair, and maintenance of sewers.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE
  • Three years of experience supervising repair and maintenance of a district of the city's sewer system.
Or any equivalent combination of training and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included the specific experience requirements listed above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Sewer Maintenance Superintendent.
