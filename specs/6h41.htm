CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

6H41
PLUMBING PLAN EXAMINER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is code compliance work examining plumbing permit applications and construction or alteration plans for conformance with the City's plumbing code. Discussing plumbing plans and code requirements with applicants is a significant aspect of the work. Employees offer an interpretive service in the examination of assigned plumbing plans and blueprints as one phase of the city's code enforcement program. Work is performed under the general supervision of a technical superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Examines plot plans and blueprints of single and multi-family dwellings and commercial and industrial buildings for water, drainage, venting, and hazards of cross connections; ascertains that the required drainage sizing from roof areas, sanitary fixtures, floor and area drains, interceptors, and pumps are consistent with code requirements; checks for methods of sewage disposal, ground areas in unsewered districts, distance relation between potable water supplies, curb traps, fresh air inlets and other aspects for compliance with the plumbing code; prepares and issues permit approvals and related forms; calculates fee from established formulae.
  • Explains and interprets the Plumbing Code to registered master plumbers, contractors, property owners and other interested parties; makes suggestions and prepares sketches and diagrams for applicants to follow in order that they may adhere to the standards set by the plumbing code.
  • Prepares and maintains reports and records.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • plumbing codes as they apply to the examination of plumbing plans.
  • the methods and practices involved in installing, repairing and maintaining a variety of plumbing installations, equipment and appliances.
  • possible defects and faults in plumbing systems and appliances and of standard testing devices.
  • codes and safety standards governing building construction, housing, zoning, industrial waste, fire prevention and environmental health.
ABILITY TO:

  • read and interpret plumbing construction plans and specifications.
  • recognize improper or faulty plumbing installations and other hazardous conditions, and to recommend corrective action.
  • enforce codes and regulations firmly, tactfully and impartially.
  • prepare and maintain records and reports.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and the public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 10/74.)

  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • Seven years of experience in the plumbing trade, one year of which shall have been inspecting plumbing installations for conformance with safety and health standards or codes.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Proof of successful completion of the Master Plumber's Examination or possession of a Master Plumber's License as issued by the City of Philadelphia.
