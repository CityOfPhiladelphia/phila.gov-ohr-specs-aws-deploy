TRANSIT SIGNALS AND COMMUNICATIONS SPECIALIST

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is specialized construction engineering work supervising, through technical subordinates, the inspection of public works transit signals and communications construction projects to assure good workmanship and contractors' adherence to plans, specifications and contract requirements. The employee directs the work of inspection units under his supervision and provides engineering interpretations to resolve construction disputes. The work may involve supervision of contracted inspectors.

Work is performed under the direction of an engineering supervisor. Assignments require analytical thinking and are performed in accordance with accepted construction inspection and engineering standards and applicable regulations. Employees in this class participate with contractors, engineers and technicians to insure that all transit signals and communications construction projects are completed properly. Work may require light physical effort.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs the work of field units inspecting transit signals and communications construction projects; assigns projects to provide an equal distribution of work; develops an inspection schedule which allows for the most efficient utilization of time and which permits the observation of contractors' work at times defects would be most apparent.
  • Reviews daily, weekly and/or monthly reports governing inspection activities; reviews all activities and problems encountered; schedules meetings during the project to work out solutions to problems and interpret contract requirements.
  • Makes on-site inspections of all phases of the transit signals and communications construction projects while in progress to determine that specific phases of work are performed in accordance with predetermined outlines; interprets the plans and specifications for the contractor and determines whether the intent of the plans and specifications has been properly carried out; resolves disputes with contractors regarding the intent of the City on a specific phase in the contract requirement; confers with immediate supervisors to determine the appropriateness of proposed changes.
  • Investigates and resolves complaints from the general public, contractors and others pertaining to damages or inconvenience created by construction projects; meets with complainants and contractors to obtain details and examine the site of the damages; analyzes all data and makes recommendations to resolve the complaint.
  • Prepares and supervises the preparation of drawings, engineering designs, specifications, quantity calculations, costs estimates, engineering computations and related engineering work in the office as well as the field.
  • Prepares a variety of correspondence to contractors, engineering firms, officials of various City departments, and others regarding the status of transit signals and communications construction projects.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of electrical engineering as applied to the construction of transit signals and communications projects.
  • the methods and techniques of electrical engineering as applied to inspecting and testing materials used in transit signals and communications public works construction projects.
  • the nature and qualities of transit signals and communications construction materials and their uses.
  • contract administration including scheduling and the preparation of contracts and change orders.
  • engineering mathematics as applied to transit signals and communications construction.
  • supervisor methods and techniques.

ABILITY TO:

  • supervise field assistants and consultants in the inspection of transit signals and communications projects for conformance to contract requirements, plans and specifications.
  • evaluate construction modifications for soundness of engineering features and economic feasibility.
  • analyze construction and specification interpretation problems and to exercise sound judgment in the resolution.
  • analyze data and to explain contract deviations to contractors and to resolve disputes firmly and tactfully.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, contractors and others.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in electrical engineering.
  • Four years of electrical engineering or electrical inspection experience, two years of which has included electrical inspection and/or design of transit signals and communications systems and equipment.

Or any equivalent combination of acceptable education and experience which has included the completion of the twelfth school grade as an educational minimum.

NOTE:
Additional years of experience in the installation, repair, inspection and/or design of transit signals and communications systems and equipment may be substituted for the educational requirement on a one-to-one basis.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to, and during tenure of employment as a Transit Signals and Communications Specialist if required by work assignment.
