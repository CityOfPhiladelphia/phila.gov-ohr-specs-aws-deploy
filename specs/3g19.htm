3G19
PHYSICAL TESTING TECHNICIAN II

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is laboratory and field testing work at the full performance level testing various construction materials and consumer products for conformance to accepted materials standards and contract specifications. Work is distinguished from the next lower level in the series by the complexity and diversity of tests performed on a wide variety of material and consumer products at construction sites and manufacturing plants. Work is performed under the general supervision of a technical superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Receives a variety of construction materials and consumer goods for testing; reviews material specifications, the American Society for Testing Materials Standards and American Water Work Association Standards to determine appropriate techniques and equipment; weighs and measures specimens; performs a variety of tasks to prepare specimens to testing, including aging concrete cylinders, capping cylinders with capping compound, and sieving samples of aggregate; performs machining operations such as tapping and threading, drilling, grinding, polishing, cutting and shaping metals and other samples with power saws, drill presses, and grinders in order to fit the specimens to testing machines; advises the machine shop of other modifications required.
  • Prepares reports on test specimens including physical descriptions, test results and recommendations concerning materials.
  • Travels to test sites to perform required tests for corrosion control analysis on underground pipes; prepares reports and records of corrosion control field work.
  • Visits manufacturing and fabricating plants to inspect materials being purchased for use in city projects; confers with manufacturers to insure that test procedures meet prescribed standards; observes tests, including pressure testing of large valves and fittings and hydrostatic testing of pipes; informs manufacturer when materials fail to meet required specification; prepares reports detailing findings and recommendations.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • physical testing techniques, procedures and equipment.
  • the hazards and safety precautions of lifting and moving heavy materials.
  • the principles and methods of reading and interpreting shop drawings and blueprints.
  • the mathematical calculations and formulas required to evaluate test results.
  • the tools and equipment used to make routine repairs to mechanical equipment and in modifying test specimens and testing machines.
  • electricity and corrosion fundamentals, corrosion control methods and related equipment.

ABILITY TO:

  • demonstrate a variety of physical tests for lower level technicians.
  • operate, adjust and care for physical testing machinery.
  • make mathematical and algebraic calculations.
  • prepare accurate reports and records.
  • lift and move heavy objects.
  • learn techniques and procedures for testing fabricated parts and equipment at manufacturing plants.
  • read and interpret shop drawing and blueprints.
  • express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 7/87.)
  • Completion of a two year post high school program at a technical institute with major course work in building construction technology or civil engineering technology, or two years at an accredited college or university with major course work in civil, metallurgical, materials or mechanical engineering.

  • One year of experience testing the physical properties of materials.

Or any equivalent combination of training and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the completion of the twelfth school grade and at least one year of the training specified above as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Physical Testing Technician II if required by work assignment.
