CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

7D16
CUSTODIAL SERVICES SUPERINTENDENT

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is building custodial services supervisory work directing through subordinate supervisors the activities of custodial crews in the cleaning and custodial maintenance of several large city buildings. An employee in this class also directs the testing of and makes selections of custodial equipment, materials and supplies. Supervision of custodial crews and contact with representatives of custodial equipment and materials manufacturers are of significance to the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

  • Employees in this class must direct, through subordinate supervisors, the activities of custodial crews in the cleaning and custodial maintenance of several large city buildings.
  • Employees in this class must also direct the testing of and selection of custodial equipment, materials and supplies for use by subordinate staff.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Develops and implements a comprehensive custodial program designed to insure the cleanliness of assigned major buildings and adjacent grounds, taking into account such pertinent factors as pedestrian traffic, working schedule, visiting areas; usage/reviews equipment needs and current techniques of occupants to determine necessity and feasibility of modernization; plans and directs the clearing of snow and ice from sidewalks, walkways and plazas of assigned buildings.
  • Tours buildings and grounds noting the appearance and general condition of windows, floors, ceilings, offices and other areas; confers with subordinate supervisors on any work schedule changes that may be necessitated because of the condition of the area, work schedule, shift, etc.
  • Conducts training sessions for subordinate supervisory personnel instructing them on supervisory techniques, safety measures, squad cleaning procedures as well as the care and use of new materials and equipment.
  • Confers with sales representatives on the uses of custodial machinery and equipment; prepares reports listing the advantages and disadvantages of machinery and equipment retained on a trial basis; makes recommendations for the purchase or rejection of such machinery and equipment based on trial performance.
  • Periodically inspects custodial supply areas for cleanliness, orderliness and adequacy of materials and supplies; maintains log of inventories; makes unannounced visits to storage areas to compare actual inventory with book inventory; investigates and reports on discrepancies; reviews and approves or disapproves requisitions for custodial materials and supplies.
  • Interviews new employees instructing them on divisional rules and regulations; approves vacation schedule; recommends disciplinary actions.
  • Makes unannounced visits to work areas to inspect work both in progress and completed for conformance to standards; points out areas of non-conformance and makes re-inspections to see that corrective actions are taken.
  • Maintains records and prepares reports relating to personnel, supplies, equipment and other day-to-day operations.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

    KNOWLEDGE OF:

    • the modern materials, methods, equipment and practices used in custodial care of major buildings and adjacent grounds
    • the occupational hazards and safety precautions involved in the work
    • supervisory methods and techniques

    ABILITY TO:

    • make accurate estimates of time, labor and material needs
    • prepare work schedules designed to achieve maximum use and efficiency of work force
    • train subordinates in custodial practices and procedures particularly as they pertaining to safety precautions
    • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4702.)

    EDUCATION

    • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

    AND
    GENERAL EXPERIENCE

    • Two years of custodial work experience.

    AND
    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

    • Three years of experience supervising, through subordinate supervisors, the janitorial and housekeeping activities for assigned public buildings and surrounding grounds.

    OR

    Any equivalent combination training and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

NOTE:

The class (es) which typically meet(s) the training and experience requirements for the class is/are:

  • Custodial Work Supervisor 2
PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
