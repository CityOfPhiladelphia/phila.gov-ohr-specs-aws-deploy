CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

4J23
ASBESTOS CONTROL PROGRAM MANAGER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative air management work directing a municipal citywide asbestos control program in the City's Health Department. The employee in this class supervises the activities of a Industrial Hygienist engaged in establishing procedures for evaluation of asbestos testing procedures, training and safety programs for air management services, evaluating asbestos removal hazards as required under the City's Health Code, and providing technical assistance to the asbestos control public education campaign. The employee in this class also supervises a project coordinator responsible for the administration of the Asbestos Project Inspectors' training (API) course and, through subordinate supervisors, a staff engaged in certifying workers and businesses, reviewing permits and notifications, and conducting inspections and investigations of asbestos projects to ensure compliance with applicable codes and regulations in an effort to minimize asbestos health hazards throughout the City.

Work includes developing and implementing operational regulations and procedures for subordinate staff to conform with health codes, federal, state and local regulations, and the standards of the Environmental Protection Agency. Contact with various governmental agencies and consulting with contractors, consultants, and other asbestos workers to ensure compliance, evaluate operations, and to provide technical advice and direct, through off-site contract, the City contractor license and occupational certification programs are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

  • The position must direct the City's asbestos control program through subordinate asbestos control supervisors and contracted professional personnel.
  • This position must perform the work in the Health Department.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Develops programmatic policies and procedures concerning asbestos certification, inspection, and removal for the City in conformance with federal, state and local regulations and requirements; sets goals and objectives for the operations and professional staff; establishes priorities for work orders; develops new and revises current work methods to enhance efficiency in the areas of staff inspections and removal operations; evaluates recommended alternatives to standard procedures; assesses training needs in conjunction with the development of new and revised procedures.
  • Directs, through a technical expert, the review of the Department of Public Health's analytical procedures for evaluating asbestos testing methods; the evaluation of purported hazards under the Right to Refuse Hazardous Work clause in the City's Health Code relating to asbestos removal; the participation in the development and conduct of indoor air pollution programs; participation in directing emergency situations involving the release of hazardous and toxic air pollutants; and, assisting in the development of regulations, in accordance with City ordinances, that reflect improvements in asbestos abatement technology, methods, procedures, and accreditation programs.
  • Supervises, through subordinate supervisors, asbestos control project inspection and permit issuance; evaluates complaints and assures response within set time limits; assists in the resolution of complex problems; periodically reviews and approves recommendations of subordinates; may order evacuations in areas of confirmed or suspected contamination.
  • Supervises, through a project coordinator, the processing of applications for licensure from Asbestos Contractors, workers, supervisors, laboratories, investigators and training agencies; performing application follow up with the Department of Licenses and Inspections; managing the asbestos abatement hotline; serving as liaison with all program and administrative staff; maintaining course and candidates files; handling course related inquiries from candidates and others; and, developing and conducting a Public Education Campaign to disseminate information on air management issues to the general public and targeted groups as directed by the Department Public Health.
  • Ensures, through subordinates, that asbestos workers employed by contractors are certified prior to the issuance of permits; ensures the review of permits and notifications; develops and initiates procedures for license revocation of contractors and decertification of asbestos workers or firms whose work practices violate established policies; directs the establishment and implementation of criteria and procedures to evaluate the qualifications of analytical laboratories to perform asbestos sampling; supervises the evaluation and approval and disapproval of the certifications of laboratories employed for the purpose of asbestos testing.
  • Reviews and evaluates, along with other program administrators, violations cited by workers; sets penalties as deemed necessary; recommends legal action when necessary; may testify in court.
  • Directs the development and implementation, in accordance with regulations, licensing programs for contractors, consultants, laboratories and air monitoring firms involved in asbestos removal in the City for use by the public or workers in the field; speaks to the general public and private organizations giving general information concerning asbestos removal and explaining relevant federal regulations as well as the City's policies; confers with and advises asbestos contractors and workers, building/plant owners, staff members, and governmental agencies on problem cases; critiques in-house asbestos abatement programs set up by firms planning to establish their own abatement program.
  • Directs the planning, administration and instruction of the Asbestos Project Inspector certification course through a subordinate technical expert engaged in the review and improvements to the City's Asbestos Project Inspector Course and serving as primary faculty for the course; evaluation; development and the conducting of the asbestos worker training program, particularly air monitoring, respiratory protection and the qualifications of the course instructors; and, the provision of technical assistance to the development of the Asbestos Control Public Education Campaign; directs the development of the curriculum, focusing on technical content; develops and/or evaluates training programs for the certification of asbestos workers; ensures that all staff members receive proper training and that proper equipment is used; provides special on-the-job training as needed.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

    KNOWLEDGE OF:

    • the principles and practices of asbestos abatement
    • all applicable laws and regulations for asbestos abatement and associated activities of containment, removal and disposal
    • the applicable laws and regulations for the licensing and certification of asbestos workers
    • current developments and trends in the field of asbestos abatement
    • research methods and measurement techniques relating to the identification and removal of asbestos hazards
    • supervisory methods and techniques

    ABILITY TO:

    • direct a supervisory civil service staff and contracted off-site and on-site professional service personnel
    • resolve complex asbestos abatement problems
    • develop operational goals to achieve desired output
    • assign and direct a large variety of work projects
    • prepare clear, concise, and technically sound evaluation reports
    • express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing
    • establish and maintain effective relationships with associates, asbestos contractors and workers, firm owners and their representatives, governmental agencies, and members of the general public
    • interpret and implement ordinances and statutory requirements related to asbestos removal and control

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 8/99.)

    EDUCATION

    • Education equivalent to the completion of a Bachelor's degree program at an accredited college, university, or technical institute which has included at least twenty (20) semester hours of coursework in science, engineering, public health, environmental science or related field.

    AND

    GENERAL EXPERIENCE

    • Two years of experience in the asbestos abatement field.

    AND

    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

    • Two years of experience supervising inspectional and investigatory employees in an asbestos removal compliance program.

    OR

    Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that included at least twenty (20) semester hours of coursework in the areas stated above as an educational minimum and the specific experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
