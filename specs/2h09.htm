CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2H09
PERSONNEL MANAGER

Specialties
( Employee Relations Management ) ( Human Resources Management )

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative public personnel work directing either a city-wide Employee Relations or Human Resources Management Program in the City's central personnel agency. An employee in this class directs, through subordinate supervisors, several major personnel program functions, establishes program goals and content and assures that departmental needs for human resource services are appropriately met. Work is performed under the general direction of the Personnel Director and/or Deputy Director.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

Both Specialties

  • Plans and determines program objectives and goals; participates in the formulation of policies and procedures relating to the administration of the assigned human resources program function; makes recommendations to the Director and prepares studies for long range programs; reviews reports and evaluates program performance related to program goals and objectives.
  • Plans, organizes, directs and coordinates, through subordinate supervisors, the work of a group of technical specialists, analysts, and clerical staff; determines work priorities and staffing requirements; reviews completed work/projects for quality and conformance to prescribed standards; coordinates divisional activities and functions with other Personnel Department divisional units.
  • Identifies training needs; directs the establishment of training programs to keep subordinates abreast of new developments in the human resources field; works in cooperation with approved divisional initiatives developed by policy, planning and analysis staff.
  • Performs related work as required.
Employee Relations Management Specialty

  • Directs, through subordinate supervisors, the following central agency functions; secondary job placement; recruitment, affirmative action, employee benefits administration, labor relations, training and development, and such support services as required.
  • Directs, through subordinate supervisors, a city-wide recruitment program designed to obtain qualified candidates for employment in city government and to place in employment permanently and partially disabled employees; establishes working relationships with college and high school placement programs and trade groups.
  • Directs policy and strategy implementation through subordinate supervisors, appropriate to the achievement of labor relations program goals and objectives and the development of program practices and procedures; meets with City officials, union representatives and departmental administrators to resolve problems relating to contract administration and other labor relations issues; directs the provision of advice and consultation to city and departmental officials to resolve labor relations problems; assigns labor relations staff, as required, to the City's negotiating effort for the preparation of background studies on union issues and demands in conjunction with contract negotiations; directs the research and preparation of presentations concerning labor wage and benefit practices and trends; participates in contract negotiations with union organizations.
  • Directs, through subordinate supervisor, the research, planning, development and maintenance of the City's employee benefits program; resolves complex problems; directs the preparation of specifications for new benefits programs; negotiates with insurance carriers to obtain employee benefits programs acceptable to city officials; participates in the review and selection process; reviews bid proposals submitted and recommends/ advises city officials; directs, through subordinate supervisors, the city's unemployment compensation program; directs the periodic review of office operating procedures to expedite the processing of claims; ensures compliance with state regulations; confers with City officials and state employment security representatives; directs, through subordinate supervisors, employee secondary job placement and service-connected disability programs for permanent and partially disabled municipal employees; directs the evaluation of skills and limitations of disabled employees and their referral to departments for job placement.
  • Directs, through subordinate supervisors, the maintenance and administration of the city's affirmative action and equal employment opportunity programs; directs the investigation of job discrimination complaints from employees in the City's workforce; directs the completion and interpretation of work force affirmative action statistics; recommends personnel practice changes; insures the preparation and appropriate modifications to affirmative action plans, based upon federal and state guidelines, and facilitates their implementation.
  • Directs, through subordinate supervisors, the planning, evaluation and administration of the City's training and personnel development program; reviews and approves proposals of and modification to education and training policies and curricula; ensures the establishment and maintenance of a comprehensive management development program; directs the development of specialized training packages for departmental application; directs the coordination of divisional activities and the training and development function with other personnel and departmental programs.
Human Resources Management Specialty

  • Directs, through subordinate supervisors, the following agency functions: classification, compensation, organizational analysis, placement services, test development, test administration and related support services.
  • Directs, through subordinate supervisors, the activities of personnel analysts and specialists engaged in the maintenance of the job classification and pay programs.
  • Reviews recommendations for changes in the classification and pay plans; meets with departmental and union representatives to resolve classification and/or pay appeals; prepares and presents agenda of revisions to the classification and pay plans to the Civil Service Commission for its review; directs the processing of all approved items by the Civil Service Commission to the Administrative Board.
  • Directs, through subordinate supervisors, the maintenance of the pay plan, including the preparation of pay schedules for union and non-union represented employees; directs and may participate in pay evaluations for pay rate determination for new classes; resolves pay appeals for existing classes; directs through subordinate supervisors, studies to compare outside pay rates with those of comparable City civil service classes and studies to determine cost of class establishments and increases in the pay rate for job classes.
  • Directs, through subordinate supervisors, a merit based selection program; directs the development and implementation of hiring processes intended to secure the best available candidates for City employment; meets with departmental officials to develop hiring processes and procedures that meet departmental objectives; insures the development and implementation of selection protocols according to professional and technical standards for selection; directs the development and revision of processes and procedures to improve service delivery quality and efficiency; directs the production of selection plans and tests that are based on acceptable job analysis and that satisfy professional and legal guidelines; directs subordinates in the analysis of computing programs, systems and designs for improved automation of the employee selection processes.
  • Directs studies of departmental staffing and organization structure; directs the development and implementation of compensation, job classification, personnel selection, and other related training programs for in-house staff and departmental managers and personnelists.
  • Directs a variety of hiring process support functions, through subordinate supervisors, such as placement processing services, test administration, test scoring and other related support functions.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of public personnel and merit system administration.
  • the principles and practices of organizational administration and management.
  • current developments in the field of human resources administration.
  • the principles, practices, and methods used in program and organizational planning and analysis.
  • current developments in the field of classification, compensation, organizational analysis, job placement and test development and administration if required by work assignment.
  • current developments in the fields of job recruitment, affirmative action, benefits administration, labor relations and training and development, if required by work assignment.
  • long range planning principles, practices and techniques.
  • supervisory principles and practices.
ABILITY TO:

  • supervise and coordinate, through subordinate supervisors, the activities of a group of personnel analysts and specialists, engaged in Employee Relations or Human Resources management activities.
  • represent the assigned division on policy, technical and procedural matters.
  • apply supervisory principles and practices if required by work assignment.
  • evaluate program quality and effectiveness.
  • apply sound management practices in the administration of assigned human resource management program.
  • analyze a wide variety of complicated facts and to exercise a marked degree of independent judgment in arriving at conclusions and recommendations.
  • formulate new or alternative strategies to achieve organizational goals and objectives.
  • to perform organizational analysis, staffing studies, and other human resources quality assessment initiatives.
  • plan, organize and implement administrative and other ancillary support services to meet program goals.
  • present ideas with a high degree of effectiveness, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with a substantial range of individuals and groups, including associates, departmental and union officials, outside agencies and the public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/1994.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
Employee Relations Management Specialty

GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Four years of experience in public personnel work.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE
  • Three years of public personnel analyst experience in a supervisory capacity in either a labor relations, employee training and development, personnel administrative services, affirmative action/EEO or benefits administration program.
Human Resources Management Specialty

GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Four years of experience in public personnel work.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE
  • Three years of public personnel experience in a supervisory capacity directing a staff of personnel analysts performing city-wide examination or classification and compensation services for merit based employment program.

Or any combination of equivalent experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum and the specific experience as described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
