CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2A16
DEPUTY FINANCE DIRECTOR

Specialties
( Accounting Bureau ) ( Operating Bureau )

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is fiscal management work directing all activities of either the City's central operating budget agency or of the City's central accounting bureau through subordinate managers and supervisors. The employee is responsible for the implementation and review of City-wide policy and for setting overall goals, objectives and priorities within his/her area of responsibility. Significant to the work is the overall responsibility for implementing financial mandates set forth by the Director of Finance. Working with administrative, legislative and departmental officials to implement fiscal policies and testifying at City Council and before other boards and hearings, and providing information on the financial state of the City to media, legislators, government agents, and representatives of the financial and economic community are significant to the work. The employee reports to the Director of Finance.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • One position will direct all central finance agency accounting functions; one position will direct all operating budget functions.
  • Only one position in each specialty may be allocated to this class.
  • Work must be performed in the City's Central Finance Department.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

(Operating Budget)

  • Formulates and develops the City's operating budget goals, objectives and policies; plans, organizes, and directs the implementation of these budgetary goals, objectives, and priorities; directs the preparation, review and analysis of revenue estimates; establishes priorities and timetables for the formal preparation of the City's overall operating budget; confers with the Finance Director on the preparation and implementation of budgetary goals, objectives, and priorities; establishes priorities and budget; confers with the capital budget director to determine the impact of the City's capital budget on operating expenses; reviews budget requests and recommends inclusions, deletions and modifications; conducts hearings on budgets of city agencies; testifies in councilmanic and other hearings on budget requests and related matters.
  • Plans, organizes, directs, coordinates and reviews the activities of the City's central budget agency; directs, through the assistant director, the activities of a budget analyst staff in the examination, evaluation, and control of the City's operating budget and various aspects of the capital budget; establishes, develops and implements agency priorities, procedures and policies; reviews staff recommendations pertaining to transfer of funds and additional appropriations; reviews all personnel transactions and fund transfer requests; reviews reports and recommendations on major financial policy issues and problems; reviews results of special or recurring studies pertaining to the fiscal and related operations of City agencies; provides cost analyses of benefits and other contract awards for labor negotiation purposes.
  • Discusses the implementation of budgetary policy with departmental officials; explains and discusses budgetary policy changes; advises City administrators and City Council officials on caucus modifications; represents department at meetings with the media, fiscal and economic agency representatives, and state and federal legislators.
(Accounting Bureau)

  • Formulates and implements accounting policy directives and standards on a city-wide basis; assures that all accounting activities comply with general accepted accounting standards and ethics; develops and implements new and effective accounting processes and resolves the most complex and sensitive accounting problems.
  • Directs all activities of the City's centralized accounting bureau through subordinate managers; develops policies, goals, and objectives for the accounting bureau; commissions studies and selects audits, develops policies and plans to implement policies; reviews bureau activities and products to determine if goals and objectives have been achieved.
  • Directs City-wide accounting functions, including the computerized maintenance of all financial transactions and statements for City funds; oversees the preparation of the City's comprehensive Annual Report for the City of Philadelphia mandated by the City Charter within specified time frames; assures that the Annual Report complies with all government, accounting, and ethical standards and practices; oversees the preparation of complex financial statements such as the Supplemental Reports for Revenues and Obligations, Bonded Debt Report, and Schedule of Financial Assistance.
  • Establishes and implements policy and directives for all of the City's accounting transactions including payments, financial documents, purchase orders and determines that documents are properly recorded in compliance with accepted accounting standards and practices; meets with department heads, Controller, City Treasurer, vendors and others to establish the most efficient flow of financial documents to provide current and accurate information of the City's cash flow status.
  • Sets the City's policy for deciding whether to close out prior year documents and establishes schedules for when documents should arrive in the accounting bureau from departments; establishes cut off dates for documents.
  • Establishes the policy determining the flow of financial information to departments; evaluates and determines the best and most efficient format for providing that information.
  • Sits on the City's Payment Review Committee; determines which of the City's obligations can be met with cash on hand; sets priorities for meeting future obligations; negotiates with contracted vendors to prevent lawsuits from delayed payments for goods and services.
  • Serves as technical financial consultant to the Managing Director's Office and department heads on major financial issues such as the separation of Water and Aviation Fund from the General Fund; establishes proper safeguards to assure that appropriate issues and concerns are addressed.
  • Has overall responsibility for the on-going recordkeeping and contract auditing on all of the City's grants monies.
  • Provides overall direction for the processing of city payrolls in accordance with all City regulations, Civil Service Regulations, Administrative Board Rules and Union Contracts.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

(Operating Budget)

  • the principles of public finance and accounting as applied to the preparation and control of operating budgets.
  • economic and econometric principles as applied to the use of econometric models for forecasting revenues and expenditures.
  • administrative and managerial practices, policies, and procedures as applied to budget, grant administration, and analyses.
  • organizational, management and systems theory as applied to budget administration and analysis.
(Accounting Bureau)

  • the standards, guidelines and requirements for governmental accounting as promulgated by the Government Accounting Standards Boards.
  • the principles, standards and practices of the accounting profession.
  • generally accepted accounting principles, practices and procedures.
  • principles and practices of governmental accounting.
  • the principles and procedures used in the analysis, maintenance, development and installation of accounting systems, methods and procedures.
  • principles and practices of public administration and their application to a large centralized accounting operation.
  • financial statements and reports used in municipal accounting.
  • the principles and practices of modern management as applied to management problems in the accounting field.
  • the uses and application of computer systems to accounting, fiscal reporting and preparation of the City's payroll.
  • the principles, practices and techniques of accounting as they refer to municipal bond indebtedness and bond sales.
  • the application of computing equipment and software to the maintenance of financial records.
(Both Specialties)

  • supervisory practices and techniques.
  • the application of computing equipment and software to the maintenance of financial records.
SKILL IN:

(Operating Budget)

  • evaluating the effectiveness of budgetary programs and policies.
  • evaluating budget agency activities and functions and implementing changes to provide for more effective and efficient operations.
(Accounting Bureau)
  • examining and analyzing financial records.
  • preparing a variety of comprehensive accounting statements and reports.
ABILITY TO:

(Both Specialties)

  • enlist the cooperation of officials to implement policy and procedural changes.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • exercise judgment and discretion in applying and interpreting policies and procedures consistent with overall policy and objectives.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, departmental officials, and the public.
(Operating Budget)

  • plan, organize, direct and coordinate the activities of a staff of budget analysts in the evaluation and control of operating budgets.
  • analyze and comprehend organizational and procedural problems and budgetary recommendations based on such trends.
  • prepare and interpret a variety of financial reports and statements.
  • analyze and resolve complex budgetary problems and make sound recommendations consistent with budgetary principles and departmental policies.
(Accounting Bureau)

  • direct the work of a large staff of managerial, professional, technical, and clerical personnel.
  • administer a large centralized municipal accounting program.
  • establish and implement city-wide accounting policy.
  • develop and install standard and special accounting methods, procedures and forms.
  • resolve technical accounting problems and issues encountered in municipal accounting.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 9/92.)

Operating Budget Specialty

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in business administration, public administration, economics, finance, accounting or urban studies.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Seven (7) years of budget experience in a central government budget agency reviewing and analyzing budget requests.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE
  • Three (3) years in a supervisory capacity in the analysis of the day-to-day operations of a group of budget analysts within a central operating budget agency.
Accounting Bureau Specialty

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major coursework in accounting.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE
  • Three (3) years of accounting experience.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE
  • Three (3) years of supervisory accounting experience.
AND
  • Three (3) years of supervisory accounting experience supervising through subordinate accounting supervisors a major section of a central fiscal operation which has included accounting control and the issuance of accounting reports.
Both Specialties

Or any equivalent combination of experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
