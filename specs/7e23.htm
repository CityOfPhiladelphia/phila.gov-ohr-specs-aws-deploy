SOLID WASTE TRANSFER PLANT SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is second level supervisory work directing the operation, major maintenance and repair of a transfer plant. An employee in this class serves as first assistant to a transfer plant superintendent. Responsibility for planning, assigning and reviewing the work of a moderate number of operational and maintenance employees on various shifts, engaged in the operation, maintenance and repair of a transfer station is a significant aspect of the work. Work involves some disagreeable environmental aspects in conducting inspectional tours of the plant and some moderate physical effort in repair and maintenance activities. Work is performed under the general supervision of the plant superintendent.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, assigns and reviews the work of a crew of skilled, semi-skilled, and unskilled employees engaged in refuse disposal activities; furnishes special instructions for the operation, repair and maintenance of a transfer station; reassigns personnel to various work projects to avoid backlog and to insure the continuation of efficient refuse disposal activities.
  • Supervises and participates in the adjustment and repair to equipment and machinery used in a transfer station; makes adjustment and repairs to electric bridge cranes by adjusting brakes, replacing broken or frayed cables and repairing and replacing parts in control panels; repairs electric switch lines and power lines; repairs and adjusts drive wheels; repairs frayed and worn electric motor drive belts; continues repairs begun on previous shift.
  • Inspects all plant equipment and facilities for operating efficiency; notifies higher level plant supervisor of inoperative equipment; recommends need for major repair and/or maintenance work; lubricates motors and gears; checks the vertical and horizontal alignments using filler gauges, calipers, micrometers and other precision tools; disassembles mechanical equipment and inspects component parts for wear or damage; disconnects couplings from electric motors, removes inspection plates; reassembles unit using hand tools and checks completed work for proper alignment.
  • Makes adjustments and performs maintenance to hydraulically operated compaction equipment by checking fluid levels, hose lines, and electrical connections; repairs or replaces parts in the control panel; repairs and replaces electric motors; repairs hooks, sidings and other related compaction hardware; continues repairs begun on previous shift.
  • Directs cleaning and maintenance operations on weekends; insures that the plant and related equipment are in clean condition and properly operated so that air pollution or similar nuisances do not occur.
  • Determines pit levels to ascertain shift production and space available for the dumping of additional refuse; prepares requisitions and reports relating to plant operation; prepares reports of repair work accomplished during the shift; prepares and reviews employee time sheets.
  • Prepares routine reports on work projects and estimates of labor, time and materials.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods, practices, materials, tools and equipment used in the repair and maintenance of electrically or hydraulically operated mechanical equipment used at solid waste disposal facilities.
  • the occupational hazards and safety precautions involved in the maintenance of buildings and related facilities and in a variety of mechanical equipment used at transfer stations.
  • the operation and maintenance of a transfer station, including the overhead bridge cranes and hydraulically operated compaction equipment used at transfer stations.

SKILL IN:

  • the use and care of tools and equipment used in making repairs to a variety of mechanical equipment including overhead cranes and hydraulic equipment.

ABILITY TO:

  • supervise employees engaged in operation, maintenance and repair activities at transfer stations.
  • read meters and gauges and to interpret such readings.
  • maintain records pertaining to plant operations.
  • make estimates of time and materials.
  • read and interpret blueprints and sketches.
  • use an ammeter.
  • understand and follow oral and written instructions.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 7/87.)
    EDUCATION:
  • Education equivalent to completion of the tenth school grade.

    AND

    GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

  • Two years of experience in the maintenance of mechanical equipment found in an incinerator, transfer station, heavy-duty processing or materials handling plant, or used in high pressure gas, coal or oil-fueled steam boilers.

    AND

    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

  • Two years supervising activities performed by a group of skilled, semi-skilled or unskilled workers on an assigned shift.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Solid Waste Transfer Plant Supervisor if required by work assignment.
