HIRING SERVICES ASSOCIATE 2

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is public human resources work at the full performance level in a hiring team in the City's central human resources agency. The employee in this class works as part of a hiring team and is responsible for performing public human resource work proficiently in assessment and/or job design and compensation. The employee is expected to perform work in the function of their expertise with minimal supervision and to become proficient in both functional areas as they progress within this class. The employee builds on established relationships with city administrators, union officials, employees, and the public as required to achieve individual and departmental objectives.

CORE STANDARDS OF PERFORMANCE:

  • Performs assessment and/or job design and compensation assignments proficiently and autonomously; becomes cross-functional as experience is gained.
  • Performs assignments in other function with supervision.
  • Serves as a departmental resource in function with expertise.
  • Consistently meets department and professional quality standards when carrying out assigned tasks.
  • Consistently fulfills production requirements and commitments to team leader, colleagues, and clients.
  • Mentors associates at lower levels in area of functional expertise.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE

  • English grammar, spelling and oral communication.
  • Interviewing methods and practices.
  • Report writing methods and procedures.
  • Structure and functions of City departments.
  • Civil Service Regulations and Home Rule Charter.
  • Current developments in the field of human resources administration.
  • Hay method of pay evaluation, if required by assignment.
  • Job analysis.
  • Position classification, if required by assignment.
  • Statistical concepts and techniques appropriate to assignment.
  • Test development and administration, if required by assignment.
  • Standard computer software packages used for work processing, spreadsheets, and database management as well as Personnel Department specific information systems.
REQUIRED ABILITIES:

All Abilities Required for Hiring Associate I

Additional Abilities:

  1. Explain or justify decisions, conclusions, findings, or recommendations.
  2. Persuade others to take a particular course of action or to accept findings, recommendations, changes, or alternative viewpoints.
  3. Interpret and apply laws, regulations, policies, standards, or procedures to specific issues.
  4. Serve as a mentor to staff.
  5. Notify individuals or offices orally or in writing of decisions, problems, or further actions needed.
  6. Explain non-technical and technical or other complex information orally or in writing.
  7. Read and understand technical or other complex materials required for the job.
  8. Develop assessment tools (for example, written tests, training and experience evaluations, oral examinations).

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements approved on 4/99.)

    EDUCATION

    • Bachelor’s degree in human resources, industrial psychology, or a closely related field.

    AND

    GENERAL EXPERIENCE

    • One year of public human resources experience.

    AND

    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

    • One year of experience of public human resources work at the Hiring Associate I level.

NOTE:

A master's degree in human resources, industrial psychology, or closely related field may be substituted for the general experience listed above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of City human resource positions.
