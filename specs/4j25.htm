CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

4J25
AIR MANAGEMENT LICENSING SPECIALIST

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is specialized technical work reviewing and processing permit and license applications, and performing inspections associated with the City's air pollution control licensing program. The employee in this class directs the operational aspects of the air management licensing program. Contacts with license applicants and the coordination of license processing activities with other units in the air management program are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the general direction of a technical superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs the air pollution control installation permit and operating license program; oversees the processing of applications and record keeping for permits, and annual and indefinite licenses for equipment which may issue air contaminants; establishes and modifies clerical and billing procedures; prepares recommendations for changing the air management code with regard to permit and license qualifications.
  • Coordinates activities of licensing program with inspectional and engineering forces; reviews applications for completeness of technical information; recommends appropriate enforcement actions to achieve compliance with licensing requirements such as surveillance, tests, citation of violations, and legal action.
  • Contacts applicants to explain permit and licensing requirements, and supplies technical information to aid them in conforming to the air management code; assists companies in the proper completion of application forms.
  • Reviews permit and license applications, and performs field visits to inspect equipment on difficult licensing problems; makes recommendations concerning the approval of licenses for combustion, incineration and paint spraying equipment.
  • Inspects ventilation and carbon monoxide detection systems associated with enclosed garages.
  • Inspects comfort ventilation systems serving occupied spaces to investigate reports of contaminated indoor air.
  • Makes revisions to a computerized billing and coding system for storing information on licensed equipment.
  • May direct technical and clerical personnel engaged in license processing activities.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the theory of combustion and of the properties and characteristics of various fuels.
  • the operation and construction of a variety of fuel burning devices.
  • the Air Management Code and regulations as they apply to air pollution control and licensure.

ABILITY TO:

  • initiate, adapt and install clerical procedures and operations.
  • interpret licensing ordinances and regulations to the public.
  • identify mechanical problems and determine the sources of air contamination associated with mechanical ventilation equipment.
  • assign and direct a small group of technical and clerical personnel engaged in processing licenses on a project basis.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/74.)
  • Education equivalent to the completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • Four years of experience operating, repairing, or inspecting fuel burning or industrial process equipment and one year of technical field work in the inspection and investigation of air pollution sources to determine compliance with municipal codes and regulations.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as an Air Management Licensing Specialist.
