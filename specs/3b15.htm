TRANSIT ELECTRICAL ENGINEER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is electrical transit engineering work providing final review and approval of electrical and electronic designs, plans and specifications for transit projects of considerable scope and complexity. Work involves supervision of a small staff of engineering personnel engaged in electrical transit projects. Developing design criteria for consulting electrical engineers of a public transportation agency is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the general direction of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Establishes guidelines and the framework for consulting engineers in regards to the electrical requirements needed for transit projects; advises engineers of public transportation agencies on electrical requirements and considerations for subway and el systems.
  • Reviews, revises and approves plans and specifications prepared by consultants for new installations, alterations and additions to existing transit facilities to assure that they meet safety and power requirements as well as municipal, state and federal regulations.
  • Consults with design consultants and engineers of private transportation agencies to coordinate transit projects and maintain cost controls.
  • Supervises a small staff of engineering personnel engaged in designing and reviewing electrical plans for transit projects.
  • Designs electrical components for automatic fare collection, communication systems and alarms used on the city's transit system.
  • Provides technical electrical advice to engineers responsible for coordinating the construction of new large scale transit projects.
  • Directs the planning, design and preparation of electrical designs and specifictions for transit projects of limited scope.
  • Compiles data concerning electrical equipment and supplies needed to maintain and improve the transit system to be utilized in applying for federal funds for transit projects.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • engineering methods and practices used in the design, construction and alteration of transit power facilities and related electrical and electronic components.
  • the electrical equipment and components of subway, el, rail and trolley systems.
  • the use of alternating and direct currents as they relate to transit and railway systems.
  • transit power and distribution systems.
  • electrical plans and specifications for transit projects.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
  • wayside and other transit signal systems.
  • current developments in the field of public transportation.

ABILITY TO:

  • interpret and apply regulatory codes affecting the work.
  • adapt new electrical engineering principles and environmental considerations to existing transit facilities.
  • represent the department in conferences, hearings, and meetings to deal tactfully and effectively with associates, engineering and others.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/75.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in electrical engineering.
  • Six years of electrical engineering experience, two years of which has been in a supervisory engineering capacity directing the preparaton and review of desings, plans, and specifictions for the electrical aspects of transit design and construction projects.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university in an acceptable area of engineering as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Registration as a professional engineer with the Pennsylvania State Registration Board for Professional Engineers prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Transit Electrical Engineer.
