CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

4C61
UTILIZATION REVIEW NURSE

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is specialized nursing work reviewing and coordinating the provision of medical, rehabilitative services and related care from multiple site contracted medical care providers to municipal employees who have received service-connected injuries. An employee in this class ensures that medical care received is in conformance with the applicable Civil Service regulations, departmental, medical, and fiscal guidelines, and is consistent with accepted medical practices. Contact with contracted health care medical and administrative personnel, departmental officials, municipal employees and/or their representatives is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the supervision of an administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The position must perform utilization review and coordinate the provision of medical services for employees who have received service connected injuries through multiple site contracted medical care providers.
  2. The position must be located in the Occupational Health Services Division, Department of Health.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)
  • Coordinates the provision of medical services for municipal employees who have received service- connected injuries through multiple site, contracted medical care providers: contact the appropriate departmental safety office regarding questionable referrals.
  • Reviews treatment plans submitted by contracted providers; determines if treatment prescribed is consistent with injuries determined to be job-related; contacts the provider for clarification and/or to stop treatment; reviews modifications to treatment plans; approves or disapproves accordingly.
  • Approves requests for surgery, therapy, treatment and assignment of rehabilitative consultant; confers with appropriate superior regarding out-of-system treatment, specialized diagnostic testing, physiotherapy, and ordering durable medical goods from non-contracted providers.
  • Explains and interprets City policies and regulations regarding treatment to injured employees to medical, administrative and other related personnel; makes recommendations to the Medical Director regarding the services rendered by contracted providers; participates in discussions pertaining to permanent and partial disability determinations.
  • Prepares statistical medical reports pertaining to patient status; addresses complex billing problems; ensures that payments due are consistent with injuries that have been determined to be work related; provides assistance to patients who have questions or problems with recommended medical care or treatment; serves as the contact person for departmental representatives who have questions regarding the status and prognosis of employees.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • nursing theory principles, practices, and techniques.
  • current principles, practices, methods, literature, and new developments in the field of occupational health.
  • the systems and techniques of comprehensive patient care in an occupational medical setting.
  • the appropriate use of professional personnel in various health disciplines and the ability to analyze functions accordingly.
  • utilization review methods and techniques as they relate to patient care and treatment.

SKILL IN:

  • monitoring and evaluating the quality and effectiveness of services performed by contracted medical providers.
  • coordinating the provision of occupational health care services at various clinics, hospitals, rehabilitative service facilities, and diagnostic centers.

ABILITY TO:

  • to analyze patient information and to make appropriate recommendations to health care professionals regarding continued treatment.
  • interpret Civil Service regulations, departmental policies and medical standards relevant to the provision of occupational health services.
  • prepare and maintain medical and statistical reports.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with medical and administrative staff, contracted medical and administrative personnel, departmental officials, employees and/or their representatives and associates.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 6/1993.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in nursing.

GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Two years of nursing experience in an industrial medicine or ambulatory health care program.

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

  • One year of experience in utilization review or patient health care evaluation.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included the specific experience as described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a license as a registered nurse issued by the Pennsylvania State Board of Nurse Examiners prior to appointment.
