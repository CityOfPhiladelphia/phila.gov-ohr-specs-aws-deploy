JUVENILE JUSTICE ADMINISTRATIVE SERVICES MANAGER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative work directing the fiscal, stores, inventory control, payroll, procurement and other administrative functions for the Division| of Juvenile Justice Services. The employee in this class supervises directly, and through subordinates, a staff of fiscal, technical, clerical and stores employees engaged in budget preparation and control, expenditure analysis, requisition tracking, payroll preparation, procurement of goods and services, inventory maintenance and related administrative activities. Work includes management information system development and interfacing with the central personnel agency in regard to disciplinary, grievance and other personnel matters. Participating in the formulation of administrative policies and procedures is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the direction of the Executive Director, Division of Juvenile Justice Services.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • This one position class must be located in the Division of Juvenile Justice Services of the Department of Human Services.
  • The employee must direct all administrative functions for the Division of Juvenile Justice Services.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Supervises directly, and through subordinates, a staff of fiscal, technical, clerical, and stores employees engaged in providing administrative services in support of Juvenile Justice Division activities.
  • Supervises and participates in budget preparation and expenditure analysis; reviews budget estimates of projected operating expenditures for upcoming fiscal year; prepares justifications for required capital programs; provides information and data to administrative officials concerning budget requests and figures; analyzes service provider contract expendi- tures; projects future contract costs; makes recommendations for contract amendments based on expenditure analysis.
  • Directs the maintenance of accounting records and the preparation of reports on account status; periodically reviews account status; directs subordinates to prepare requests for fund transfers to meet anticipated needs.
  • Reviews and approves requisitions, purchase orders, and invoices for propriety and conformance with budgetary requirements; establishes inventory levels; directs, through a subordinate supervisor, stores workers engaged in receiving, storing and issuing supplies; supervises the maintenance of inventory records.
  • Directs, through a subordinate supervisor, clerical employees engaged in preparing payroll and maintaining leave balances; represents division at union meetings held to discuss employee grievances, work assignments and other personnel problems; discusses procedures and need for disciplinary action with departmental supervisors; directs the preparation of papers to initiate such action; discusses needs for change in organizational or staffing patterns with director.
  • Assists the director in the formulation and implementation of new administrative programs, policies and procedures; serves as a member of the division's management team.
  • Meets with service providers and vendors to discuss contract provisions, services and methods of reimbursement; participates in negotiation of purchase of service contracts.
  • Identifies departmental information management needs; confers with the City's central computing agency to determine resources applicable to the divisions needs; coordinates the acquisition and installation of hardware and software; oversees the maintenance of computer-- based information systems.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • The principles and practices of budget administration in a municipal agency.
  • The principles and practices of administrative and financial analysis.
  • Administrative and managerial practices, policies and procedures, especially as they apply to programs related to juvenile justice services.
  • municipal procurement policies, practices and procedures.
  • the principles and practices of management as they relate to personnel, payroll, procurement, stores and fiscal operations.
  • The uses and capabilities of computer equipment and management information systems.
  • operations, functions and goals of a juvenile justice system.
  • Administrative requirements of a juvenile justice system.
ABILITY TO:

  • Plan, direct, organize and coordinate the work of fiscal, technical, and clerical employees engaged in fiscal, personnel, stores and procurement activities.
  • Evaluate the quality and effectiveness of administrative functions
  • Analyze departmental needs and make recommendations concerning budget, personnel, information management, and other administrative areas.
  • Learn the uses and capabilities of microcomputers and microcomputer applications
  • Learn the operational requirements of a youth detention facility.
  • Learn the philosophy and objectives underlying juvenile justice services.
  • Express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • Exercise sound judgment and discretion in applying and interpreting policies and procedures.
  • Establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, vendors, and central agency officials.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 6/91.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree at an accredited college or university.
AND GENERAL EXPERIENCE
  • Two years of technical or administrative experience.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:
  • Three years of administrative experience in a juvenile justice or children and youth services agency providing departmental or divisional administrative services in any one of the following areas: budgeting, accounting, personnel, or procurement.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which includes the specific experience and possession of a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
