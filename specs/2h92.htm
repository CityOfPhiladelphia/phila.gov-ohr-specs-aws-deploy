GENERAL DEFINITION

 

This is entry level employee and labor relations work in the Philadelphia Department of Labor. Employees in the Labor Relations Assignment research, analyze, and assist in the interpretation of labor contracts and make recommendations pertaining to grievances, and disputes. Employees in the Employee Relations assignment assist in investigating employee relations, discrimination, and Sexual Harassment complaints and provide recommendations pertaining to departmental actions to address the complaints. An employee in this class may perform work of one or more assignments, acquires knowledge of all areas of the Department of Labor responsibilities, and has access to information subject to use in collective bargaining. Analysis, research, and investigations are significant aspects of the job. Employees report to a higher-level administrator.

 

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.    Positions in the class shall only be allocated to Department of Labor.

2.    Positions in this class must perform employee or labor relations work.

 

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

 

BOTH ASSIGNMENTS:

 

Reads, and actively participates in interpreting and applying Civil Service Regulations, Home Rule Charter, collective bargaining agreements, interest arbitration awards and federal, state and local legislation and regulations related to employment discrimination and union represented employees.

Performs related work as required.

 

ASSIGNMENT –LABOR RELATIONS

 

Provides interpretation, advice, and guidance pertaining to labor contracts, laws, and regulations concerning labor/management issues to department officials. Serves as point of contact to departmental leadership, human resources representatives, and union officials on labor/management issues, and participates in departmental labor management meetings. Researches the history of collective bargaining agreements, interest arbitration awards governing wages, and hours, terms, conditions of employment for represented employees and provides limited analysis. Researches disputes and assists in preparing narrative reports detailing the positions of management and labor. Assists in the preparation, prepares exhibits, attends, and collaborates in the arbitration of issues ranging from basic labor contract language, routine disciplinary action, and the negotiation and settlement of grievances.

Assists in conducting city-wide training on labor contracts and labor relations best practices. Provides assistance to the City's negotiating team during contract negotiations and interest arbitration hearings.

 

 

ASSIGNMENT – Employee Relations

 

Assists with employee relations matters including but not limited to the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) Reasonable Accommodation Process, and the Family Medical Leave Act (FMLA) and receives complaints of job discrimination prohibited by law. Assists in investigating employee relations and Sexual Harassment complaints by interviewing parties and witnesses, requesting and reviewing documents and evidence, analyzing facts and allegations, and writing investigative reports. Counsels complainants in regard to civil service processes and assists departments with procedures and recommendations pertaining to departmental actions and reviews and monitors departmental affirmative action plans for conformance with prescribed standards.

Prepares, or assists in the preparation of federally mandated statistical reports on the city workforce and assists in conducting city-wide employee relations related training.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

  • Labor and employment in the public sector
  • Relevant federal legislation, including ADA, Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA), FMLA, Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), Title Seven of the Civil Rights Act, and the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA)
  • Use of personal computers and relevant software packages and applications comparable to WORD, EXCEL, ACCESS and PowerPoint
  • English grammar, spelling and oral communication
  • Report writing methods and procedures

 

SKILL IN:

 

·       Soliciting information from relevant parties

·       Narrative report writing

·       Public speaking

·       Problem solving

 

ABILITY TO:

 

  • Interact effectively with peers, superiors, department managers, and employees and union officials
  • Adapt to change and stress in the work environment and manage competing demands
  • Explain or justify decisions, conclusions, findings, and recommendations
  • Persuade others to take a particular course of action or to accept findings, recommendations, changes, or alternative viewpoints
  • Interpret and apply laws, regulations, policies, standards, or procedures to specific issues
  • Understand and explain technical or other complex information orally and in writing
  • Read and understand technical or other complex materials required for the job
  • Establish and maintain effective working relationships with managers, peers, employees, and union officials
  • Gather and analyze information
  • Communicate effectively, both orally and in writing

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/24.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

Completion of a bachelor’s degree program at an accredited college or university.

 

NOTE: Additional experience or completion of an industry related certification may substitute for the required education on a year for year basis.

 

AND EXPERIENCE:

 

One year of employee or labor relations experience.

 

NOTE: Completion of an industry related Master’s Degree or Juris Doctorate from an accredited college or university may substitute for the experience.

 

OR

 

            Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

              Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
