CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

4A32
PHARMACIST II

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative and supervisory pharmaceutical work planning, coordinating, and evaluating the pharmaceutical services conducted in the City Health Department. Work is performed under the general direction of a technical superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs subordinate pharmacists in the dispensing of medication in the central pharmacy or district health centers; makes assignments to insure adequate coverage of facilities; determines all procedures to be followed in the storage and dispensing of medication; provides technical guidance to pharmacist on the interpretation of physicians' medication orders, formulating of drugs and the interaction between medication prescribed for patients; consults on problems encountered by the pharmacist pertaining to record control, reordering of medications, and administrative procedures.
  • Recommends generic equivalents for medications; reviews requests by physicians for items not listed in department Formulary; supervises drug regimen review performed by lower level pharmacists; insures that adequate records are maintained pertaining to activities and distribution of medications; insures that adequate safety and storage procedures are followed within pharmacy facilities; reviews inventory and records of all supplies; determines quantity of supplies to be prepackaged based on usage rates.
  • Directs lower level pharmacists in the submission and review of requests for reimbursement to third party providers.
  • Furnishes information concerning pharmaceuticals to health program personnel; prepares recommendations for change in pharmacy procedure.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of pharmacy and pharmacology.
  • federal and state laws, regulating the practices of pharmacy and the reporting of dangerous drugs.
  • the generic and trade names for a wide variety of drugs.
  • pharmaceutical drug and pharmaceutical supply requisitioning methods.

ABILITY TO:

  • prepare pharmaceutical compounds in both small and large volumes.
  • maintain accurate records and to prepare reports of quantities ordered, used and inventoried.
  • schedule, supervise and review the performance of pharmacists in City-wide health service programs.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and other health program personnel.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/82.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in pharmacy.
  • Two years of experience in the practice of pharmacy, one year of which shall have been at a hospital or clinic.
NOTE:
Completion of a master's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in pharmacy may be substituted for one year of the indicated general experience requirement.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a license as a registered Pharmacist as issued by the Pennsylvania State Board of Pharmacy prior to and during tenure of employment as a Pharmacist II.
