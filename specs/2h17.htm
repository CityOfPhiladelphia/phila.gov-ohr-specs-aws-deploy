HIRING SERVICES ASSOCIATE TRAINEE

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is public human resources work at the trainee level in a hiring team in the City's central human resources agency. In this class, the employee develops expertise in essential functions of public human resource work, including assessment and/or job design and compensation. The employee is part of a hiring team and works under the supervision of a team leader or higher-level associate in handling human resources assignments of increasing complexity and difficulty. The employee establishes relationships with city administrators, union officials, employees, and the public as required to achieve individual and departmental objectives.

CORE STANDARDS OF PERFORMANCE:

  • Develops working knowledge of city organizational structure.
  • Develops working knowledge of the various hiring functions.
  • Works under the supervision of a team leader or higher-level associate.
  • Actively acquires knowledge, abilities and competencies specified for performance at the Hiring Associate I level.
  • Learns established procedures required to complete tasks associated with assessment and/or job design and compensation; applies these procedures under supervision of team leader or higher level associate.
  • Consistently meets department and professional quality standards when carrying out assigned tasks.
  • Consistently fulfills production requirements and commitments to team leader, colleagues, and clients.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE

  • English grammar, spelling and oral communication.
  • Basic human resources principles and practices.
  • Report writing methods and procedures.
  • Standard computer software packages used for word processing, spreadsheets, and database management.

REQUIRED ABILITIES:

  1. Search for and extract information from files, documents, reports, publications, recordings, or other materials.
  2. Compose simple correspondence or other written work (for example, memoranda, form letters).
  3. Question, interview, or confer with others to obtain or verify information.
  4. Observe individuals, activities, or operations to obtain or verify information.
  5. Analyze or interpret data or other information.
  6. Review and analyze forms, applications, documents, or other information to make determinations.
  7. Acquire and maintain a working knowledge of relevant laws, regulations, policies, standards, or procedures.
  8. Promote or develop and maintain good working relationships with key individuals or groups.
  9. Collaborate with others or works on teams to accomplish work-related activities.
  10. Inform supervisor or other official of issues or problems.
  11. Attend classroom training, workshops, or seminars.
  12. Attend briefings, meetings, conferences, or hearings.
  13. Participate in on-the-job training and developmental assignments.
  14. Maintain security of assets or confidentiality of information (for example, equipment, cash, computer access).
  15. Use computer systems or applications to access, create, edit, print, send, retrieve, or manipulate data, files, or other information.
  16. Collect, compile and organize information.
  17. Read and understand non-technical materials.
  18. Contact others orally and in writing to obtain information.
  19. Use prescribed formulas, schedules, tables, or procedures to calculate or check values or numbers.
  20. Discuss results, problems, plans, suggestions, terms or conditions with others.
