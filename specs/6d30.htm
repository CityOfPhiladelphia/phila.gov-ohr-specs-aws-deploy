CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

6D30
AIRPORT FIELD ATTENDANT

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is airport grounds work patrolling and inspecting the landing areas of a municipal airport. Work involves responsibility for collecting landing fees and service charges from non-tenant aircraft that land at the municipal airport.

Work is performed under the close supervision of an airport superintendent. Assignments are stable in nature and are carried out in accordance with instructions and established routines. Work involves some disagreeable aspects.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Patrols airport runways, taxiways and ramps to inspect for the presence of hazards such as improperly working lights, broken paving, debris, fuel or oil spillage, or ice and snow on airfield; removes debris and other obstructions; erects barricades and places blinking lights or flags around ditches or other hazards; directs pedestrians around emergency or danger areas; contacts superintendent and traffic control tower to report any unsafe conditions.
  • Directs airplanes to parking sites during inclement weather; records the number of persons getting on and off non-scheduled flights, as well as, the type of aircraft, the weights of cargo and other fee determinants; explains airport rules and regulations to pilots; records and reports all violations; collects landing and service fees from pilots.
  • Patrols perimeter of airport checking all gates and entry points; patrols restricted areas and challenges any individuals present who are not displaying proper identification; records and reports any violations of airport regulations.
  • Escorts contractors' vehicles to and from areaways and concourses; acts as guide during emergency periods escorting vehicles and personnel from gate to scene of fire or accident; conducts snow and ice removal equipment on and off airfield.
  • Records, in the absence of stores manager, the disbursement of gasoline form the airport storage area to city-owned vehicles.
  • Collects monies from coin-operated machines such as toilets and paper cup dispensers; collects monies for baggage removed from lockers, and for lost and broken locker keys.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • learn federal, state and local rules, regulations and procedures governing activities and movement on the landing area of a large airport.
  • make arithmetic computations quickly and accurately.
  • act calmly, quickly and effectively under emergency conditions.
  • maintain routine records and to prepare reports.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and the public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 2/69.)
  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • One year of experience in airport maintenance or operations, including laboring or custodial work.

Or any equivalent combination of acceptable education and experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as an Airport Field Attendant.
  • Eligibility for a restricted radiotelephone operator's license issued by the Federal Communications Commission and possession of such licensure within 30 days following appointment.
