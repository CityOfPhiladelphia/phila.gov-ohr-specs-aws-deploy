CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

5A42
CHILDREN AND YOUTH SERVICES OPERATIONS DIRECTOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative management work directing the provision of social services to children and youth. The employee in this class directs large social work sections responsible for intake, protective services, continuing services, and related functions. The employee directs through subordinate managers the implementation of children and youth service plans and programs. The employee is also responsible for directing program analysis and evaluation and the use of statistical and financial analysis to justify program needs. Supervising through subordinate managers a large multidiscipline staff, meeting with appropriate private and public officials, and coordinating the activities of private service provider agencies with program goals are of major importance in the work. The employee reports to the Deputy Human Services Commissioner, children and Youth Services. The employee in this class has responsibility for the implementation of social services programs for children and youth and ensuring compliance with appropriate legislation and departmental policy.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, assigns, reviews and evaluates through subordinate managers the activities of a large staff of managerial, social service, clerical, and administrative personnel engaged in providing direct and purchased social services for children and youth; analyzes operational and managerial problems; initiates organizational and/or operational changes to resolve these problems; establishes staff performance objectives and controls; devises and approves in-service and outside training programs for staff; evaluates programs to assure effective use of personnel; interprets rules and analyzes staff requirements for budgetary allocations; reviews reports on program progress; develops technical statistical analyses of program needs; performs cost benefits and fiscal analysis of costs related to the program.
  • Directs through subordinate managers the centralized intake of children and youth for social services, the provision of continuing social services, and protective services for neglected and abused children and youth; organizes and directs the overall management of the major components of the children and youth welfare services program; establishes goals and objectives within the framework of the program's overall plan and departmental policy; confers with social services staff to develop plans, priorities, and policies to ensure the coordination of all program components and services rendered; issuers directives and policy statements to implement new and revised programs and to effect internal reorganization.
  • Participates in negotiating contracts and agreements with private agencies for the purchase of care for dependent, neglected, and abused children; coordinates the activities of private, non-profit agencies with goals and objectives of programs; directs of participates in committees, conferences, and meetings with public and private service agencies to develop more effective programs for children and youth; ensures compliance with appropriate federal, state, and local standards; evaluative criteria; develops agency budget request and justification using fiscal and programmatic analysis.
  • Attends a variety of meetings for the formulation and coordination of overall program objectives; confers with appropriate federal, state, local officials; meets with advisory board members to define program needs and objectives; provides staff services to the board.
  • Interprets the department's social service program to the community and the press through conferences with individuals and groups and through addresses to various groups and organizations.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of social service administration especially as it applies to children and youth programs.
  • the philosophy and objectives underlying social services for children and youth.
  • the principles, practices, and procedures of public administration.
  • statistical analysis, budgeting, and fiscal analysis.
  • the functions, resources, and management of public and a private agencies caring for children.
  • current developments in the field of children and youth welfare.
  • the legal provisions and regulations applicable to the delivery of social services to children and youth.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
SKILL IN:

  • evaluating the effectiveness of administrative and operational programs and in implementing changes to provide for more effective and efficient operations.
ABILITY TO:

  • organize and direct effectively the activities of a large multi-discipline staff providing a variety of social services to children and youth.
  • coordinate program activities in a manner that is designed to meet objectives and goals.
  • exercise sound judgment and discretion in applying and interpreting policies and procedures and in making decisions consistent with policies and objectives.
  • express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effectively working relationships with associates, public and private officials, civic groups, community groups, the judiciary, and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 11/84.)

EDUCATION:

  • Completion of a master's degree program in social service, social work, or social work administration at an accredited college or university.
EXPERIENCE:

  • Seven years of experience in the field of human services, three years of which shall have been administering through subordinate supervisors a program in a large children and youth welfare agency or directing the overall program in a smaller agency employing a social work staff.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
