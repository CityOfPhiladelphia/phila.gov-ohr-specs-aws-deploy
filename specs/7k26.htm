CABLE SERVICES SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory communications cable service work directing a group of trades and labor employees providing overhead and underground communication cable maintenance and related services for a number of City departments. The employee in this class plans and assigns work, through subordinate trades supervisors, for a combined overhead/underground cable service unit. Work is performed under the general supervision of a technical communications supervisor.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, assigns and reviews through subordinate group leaders, line workers, cable splicers, trades helpers and others engaged in the installation and maintenance of overhead and underground cables used for lights, CCTV and microwave systems, alarms, police and fire signal boxes and other wire communication facilities; schedules work of subordinates and determines work priority.
  • Visits proposed work sites where major projects are contemplated to gather information necessary for planning the best method to be employed for implementing the proposed installation or maintenance of assignments; reviews labor, time and material estimates made by subordinates in work planning, techniques and practices; submits plans to a technical superior for final approval.
  • Tours work area on an unscheduled basis in order to observe work both in process and completed for conformance to instructions or specifications; advises group leaders of area of non-conformance and observes to see that corrective actions are taken; advises subordinates on problem matters; interprets plans and specifications.
  • Reviews technical diagrams and plans listing communication instruments servicing an assigned area and determines where changes should be made with respect to the kind and size of cable used, the location of terminals and junction boxes, cross connections to existing cables and other matters connected with the construction of underground facilities; inspects plans submitted by utility companies or contractors involving relocation or construction of underground or overhead cable facilities to insure continuity of city services; coordinates cable work with City department, contractors and utilities.
  • Reviews requests and arranges for installation of temporary lighting and power facilities for special events and emergency activities; inspects plans submitted by utility companies and contractors involving relocation or construction of underground and/or overhead line facilities to insure continuity of city services.
  • Performs or assigns to subordinate group leaders the training of new employees; insures training in and the provisions for work safety.
  • Requisitions materials, tools, and equipment essential to the installation and repair of overhead and underground communication facilities; maintains records and reports of crews' activities.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the standard tools, methods, practices and testing devices of the overhead line worker and cable splicer trade.
  • the principles governing the operation of overhead and underground communication systems.
  • the occupational hazards and safety precautions in overhead and underground construction activities.
  • the principles and practices involved in overhead and underground construction activities.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.

ABILITY TO:

  • plan, assign and review the work of a large number of skilled and semi-skilled trades workers.
  • solve problems or unusual difficulties and complexity pertaining to cable repair and installation.
  • plan new construction projects and to requisition materials, tools and equipment essential to the repair and maintenance of overhead and underground communications facilities.
  • interpret and work from technical diagrams and plans.
  • keep time, material and work records.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/86.)
  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

  • Seven years of experience in the overhead line worker or cable splicer trade, three years of which has been at the full performance level, one year of which has been in a supervisory capacity as a group leader.

Or any equivalent combination of training and experience determined acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment in this class.
