AIR MONITORING TECHNICIAN

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is full performance repair, maintenance and calibration work on electronic and electro-chemical air monitoring instruments. Work may include assignment to shifts during periods of intensive air sampling and requires some physical effort and exposure to outside conditions. Work is performed under the general supervision of a technical superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • Positions in this class must be located in the Air Management Services Unit of the Health Department.
  • Positions in this class must perform repair, maintenance and calibration work on air monitoring instruments.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Examines and monitors data printouts to detect equipment irregularities; tours monitoring stations; checks, services, calibrates, adjusts and repairs ozone and carbon monoxide analyzers, particulate tape samplers, telephone line interfaces, signal conditioners and other electronic, electro-mechanical and mechanical equipment associated with the component parts of air monitoring systems.
  • Uses oscilloscopes, chart recorders, decibel meters, digital volt meters, spirometers, wet and dry air flow meters, and other test equipment.
  • Interprets and uses electronic circuit diagrams and instrument maintenance and repair manuals.
  • Keeps logs of maintenance work performed.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the basic principles of electronics and electricity and their application to air monitoring instrumentation.
  • standard electronic and electrical test equipment
  • the purposes and functions of analog and digital electronic instruments and instrument systems.
  • the procedures involved in troubleshooting, repairing, calibrating and maintaining electronic and electro-mechanical equipment.
  • the use of electronic circuit diagrams and mechanical blueprints.

SKILL IN:

  • the use and care of tools and equipment used in the repair, service and calibration of air monitoring instruments and instrumentation systems.

ABILITY TO:

  • repair, maintain and calibrate electronic, electro-chemical, and mechanical air monitoring instruments and instrument systems.
  • diagnose malfunctions in electric, electro-mechanical, and mechanical air monitoring instruments and instrumentation systems.
  • apply various tests and test devices to facilitate the repair, maintenance and calibration of air monitoring instruments and instrumentation systems.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/1996.)
    EDUCATION:
  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • Completion of an instructional program of at least one year's duration with major course work in practical electronics at an accredited technical school or institute.

    AND

    EXPERIENCE:

  • One year of experience in the repair, maintenance and calibration of electronic instruments and/or instrumentation systems.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS:

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES:

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to and during tenure of employment as an Air Monitoring Technician.
