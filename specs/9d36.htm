PARKS STEWARDSHIP ADMINISTRATOR

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is supervisory parks programming work directing, through subordinate staff, comprehensive park usage and stewardship programs on a city-wide basis.  Employees in this class plan, coordinate, and evaluate environmental stewardship programs and initiatives that directly engages volunteers in environmental restoration and park beautification projects.  Establishing and maintaining effective working relationships with Parks Friends groups, community organizations, civic associations, small businesses, corporations, Business Improvement Districts, city agencies and other stakeholders in reclamation and revitalization projects for park areas are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

  1. The position in this class must have responsibility for managing a comprehensive park usage and stewardship program on a citywide basis.
  2. The position in this class must be located in the Parks and Recreation Department.
  3. Only one position will be allocated to this class.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

            Directs a comprehensive park usage and stewardship program on a city-wide basis through subordinate staff; manages and collaborate on park projects across divisions in the department, with other City agencies and with outside partners.

            Manages Parks Service Programs, Park Friends Network, Friends and Community Group Permit, and plans annual events; plans and creates new stewardship programs in collaboration with Natural Lands team focusing on environmental restoration.

            Manages daily communications and timely response to neighborhood park concerns; maintains active correspondence with park volunteers and partners to address their concerns.           

            Assists in preparation of the stewardship team budget and work plans; develops strategic partnerships and acts as the departmental representative to community groups; develops short and long term goals for the stewardship programs; monitors program progress to achieve goals.

            Participates in the selection and hiring of staff, evaluates staff for performance; provides coaching and technical direction to staff.  

            Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         the organization, planning and scheduling of park programs

·         techniques of volunteer and community group relations involved in directing activities at parks and recreational facilities

·         principles and techniques of parks preservation and restoration program administration and management

·         supervisory methods and techniques

·         park stewardship best practices

 

SKILL IN:

·         planning, implementing and managing volunteer or community projects and events

·         effective conflict resolution approaches

·         customer service best practices

·         community engagement for departmental recreation initiatives

·         intercultural competence

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·         organize, oversee, integrate, and evaluate large and varied stewardship programs on a community wide basis

·         recruit, orient, evaluate, and retain volunteers on a district wide basis

·         evaluate program quality and effectiveness

·         establish and maintain effective working relationships with program officials, associates, neighborhoods, and civic groups, representatives of other City agencies, and other stakeholders

·         present ideas effectively both orally and in writing
