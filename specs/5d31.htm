5D31
EMPLOYMENT AND TRAINING PROGRAM ANALYST I

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative and technical work at the full performance level planning, evaluating and coordinating programs and systems of controls for governmental and private agencies providing services under the Comprehensive Employment and Training Act. Contact with officials of public and private agencies providing employment and training services to advise and resolve programmatic problems is a significant aspects of the work.

Work is performed under the general supervision of a technical superior. Assignments require evaluative thinking and are carried out in accordance with accepted program standards and regulations. An employee in this class provides advisory and consultative services for others in the planning and implementation of employment and training programs.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Researches a variety of source material on employment and training programs and labor market conditions including federal regulations, governmental and business publications, reports from other jurisdictions, and reports on local programs to develop recommendations for new programs and for revised program standards; contacts representatives of public and private agencies to gather data on operational procedures and standards; relates labor market information to the development of employment and training programs; evaluates the feasibility, cost effectiveness and appropriateness of proposed standards and programs; makes recommendations to unit supervisor.
  • Evaluates grant proposals from public and private agencies to provide employment and training services; reviews proposals and operational data on similar programs and appropriate federal regulations and guidelines; recommends approval or disapproval of applications based on feasibility of program and on conformance to federal regulations and agency guidelines.
  • Analyzes community resources to identify potential grant participants; outlines program goals, guidelines and restrictions to representatives of community agencies; interfaces with personnel from organizational development, planning and operational units to aid and encourage program development by community groups.
  • Analyzes and evaluates the impact of proposed new programs and legislation on agency operations; projects staffing needs, reporting requirements and procedural changes necessitated by new legislation in the area of employment and training programs; prepares reports detailing proposed impact of participation in new program on agency operations; communicates agency policy and procedures to sub-grant agencies.
  • Reviews contract for content; visits service provider site to monitor performance and adherence to federal regulations; interviews staff to elicit information on programs; makes formal evaluations of programs; recommends changes if problem conditions exist; provides technical assistance in programmatic-related areas.
  • Comments and prepares reports of evaluations; confers with program officials to analyze evaluations.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • current legislation and funding mechanisms as they apply to human service programs including employment and training programs.
  • manpower planning techniques, evaluation methods and research practices.
  • the administrative principles and practices particularly as they pertain to the evaluation and planning of employment and training programs.
  • the practices and procedures used in the examination of fiscal documents and records.
  • current research methodology in the area of program planning and evaluation.
ABILITY TO:

  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and officials of public and private social service agencies.
  • evaluate program quality and effectiveness and to prepare recommendations based on evaluations.
  • interpret research findings into program planning.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 9/80.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
EXPERIENCE

  • One year of training level experience planning, developing and implementing human services, employment or training programs.
Or any equivalent combination of acceptable education and experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
