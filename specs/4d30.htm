4D30
MEDICAL SPECIALIST, MATERNAL AND INFANT CARE PROJECT

Specialties
( Obstetrics & Gynecology ) ( Pediatrics )

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is highly responsible professional medical work in the field of specialization indicated by the subtitle.

An employee in this class provides consultant services in the appropriate medical specialty to appropriate hospital personnel, the project director and the administrative staff. Significant aspects of work include evaluating patient services provided by contracting hospitals in district health centers as well as in the out- patient and in-patient department of the hospital, recommending and participating in the formulation of, and implementing changes.

Work is performed under the general direction of a medical superior. Assignments require analytical thinking and are carried out in accordance with the accepted practices and policies governing the maternal and infant care project. The employees participate with other medical personnel in directing the development of activities in the area of specialization as it pertains to the overall project.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

Both Specialties

  • Serves as a consultant to the project director regarding all problems pertaining to the maternity and infant care project as indicated by the area of specialization.
  • Visits clinics to observe the standards of care; confers with the senior medical officials indicated by the area of specialization regarding the services being provided by hospital personnel included in the program, and with the district health director regarding the clerical, nursing, dental and ancillary services provided.
  • Supervises the coordination and integration of the particular phase of the program indicated by the area of specialization to insure optimum service.
  • Interprets program policies and procedures and confers with the appropriate officials to see that they are implemented.
  • Confers with the appropriate hospital and clinic officials, on a consultant or advisory basis on project rules and regulations; evaluates the various aspects of the care for project patients or pertaining to the project in the area of specialization; keeps abreast of on-going research projects within hospitals in which pregnant patients may be included, in order to determine whether any research pattern hinges on the quality of care provided.
  • Reports to the project director and recommends changes in policies and procedures where indicated.
  • Confers with the appropriate clinic and officials to insure implementation of approved changes.
  • Participates in the planning, development and expansion of the project by formulating technical standards for initiating and increasing care, and developing patterns and policies for follow-up care, and in evaluating statistical reports of services provided.
  • Receives, reviews and analyzes requests from hospitals for authorization to provide additional patient care services and approves or disapproves them based on knowledge of the project indicated in the area of specialization.
  • Conducts detailed investigations on new or complex aspects of the work to insure proper execution or solution as specified in the work of the project.
  • Confers with and interprets for professional societies and other community groups specific goals of the project.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices and objectives of preventive medicine particularly as they pertain to the area of specialization.
  • the literature, current developments and research methods in the area of specialization.
  • the organization and functions of hospital departments and of the general policies and procedures of hospitals.
  • medical jurisprudence.
ABILITY TO:

  • serve as a consultant and advisor in the field of specialization to departmental heads in participating hospitals.
  • exercise a high degree of judgment.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with professional persons in other medical and paramedical disciplines providing direct care services to project patients.
  • express ideas, both orally and in writing.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 9/72.)

  • Graduation from an accredited school of medicine, with a degree of Doctor of Medicine, and completion of an internship in an approved hospital.
OR
  • Graduation from an accredited school of osteopathy, with a degree of Doctor of Osteopathy, and completion of an internship in an approved hospital.
  • Possession of a certificate in Obstetrics and Gynecology or Pediatrics as issued by the appropriate board of specialization.
  • Four years of experience beyond that required for certification in the specialty, including two years of experience as a chief of an obstetric or pediatric in-patient service in a hospital approved for training of residents in the field of specialization and also including a recent holding of a teaching appointment at the rank of associate in an approved school of medicine.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to meet approved minimal physical and medical standards.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a license to practice medicine or osteopathy in the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania issued by the State Board of Medical Education and Licensure or the State Board of Osteopathic Examiners, or possession of such licensure within one year after appointment.
