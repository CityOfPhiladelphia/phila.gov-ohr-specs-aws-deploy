 LABOR PROTECTIONS ENGAGEMENT SPECIALIST

(Bilingual)

(General)

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is administrative and field work serving as a business and community liaison and providing training and outreach on worker protection laws enforced by the City of Philadelphia Department of Labor. Employees in this class develop and conduct trainings on labor laws to the business community and community organizations.  Employees in this class may also conduct investigative work, conducting compliance investigations, delivering written findings, and issuing violations when applicable under all laws related to wage and benefits administered by the City of Philadelphia Department of Labor. Employees are responsible for investigating and responding to both external complaints and complaints originating from City employees. Work includes attending public events, identifying business leaders, and assisting in developing and implementing outreach strategy for the department.  Employees may work evenings and weekends. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior. 

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS:  (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.         Employees in this class must perform liaison work and training regarding labor laws.

2.         Positions must be located in the Philadelphia Department of Labor.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

BILINGUAL SPECIALTY

            Performs the duties shown below with members of the English language speaking and designated non-English language speaking populations. 

BOTH SPECIALTIES

           

            Assists in developing outreach strategy for respective organizations; interacts with commercial enterprises, business groups, community groups and stakeholders to build capacity surrounding department initiatives and overall mission; attends events and speaks on panels; performs door to door outreach such as handing out flyers; collaborates with diverse partner organizations; receives requests and questions from organizations regarding worker protection laws.

            Assists in developing labor law training programs, researches and evaluates relevant and topical labor protection material, assists in developing curriculum design; conducts training courses to diverse organizations on labor laws.

            Monitors, maintains records and tracks data relating to paid sick leave, wage theft, wrongful discharge of parking employees, Fair Workweek, and Domestic Worker’s Bill of Rights and laws related to work place protections during a pandemic or similar public emergency; interprets data surrounding labor laws in order to assist in updating of training programs; prepares written reports on projects or activities; maintains contact with various business and/or community organizations; assists with intake for complaints.

            Responds to and resolves complaints related to paid sick leave, wage theft, wrongful discharge of parking employees, Fair Workweek, and Domestic Worker’s Bill of Rights; investigates complaints; interviews various employees and managers to gather facts of the case and other pertinent information. In the case of complaints from City employees, reviews of timesheets and internal HR files; interviews other City employees and managers as necessary to gather facts of the case and other pertinent information.

            Issues determinations on investigations; levies fines and violations when appropriate and applicable under the law; issues notices to complainants and businesses regarding case determinations; maintains and updates case data and records in Excel or other electronic databases.

            Performs related work as required.

 

BUSINESS OUTREACH ASSIGNMENT

 

            Performs the above duties specifically related to business and business professional organizations; speaks to business leaders and human resources units and business organizations to inform them of labor laws and departmental programs and to encourage compliance; assists in answering questions from employers regarding worker protection law compliance; performs extensive and targeted outreach to support the Department of Labor’s business engagement initiatives and mission.

 

COMMUNITY ORGANIZATIONS ASSIGNMENT

 

            Performs the above duties specifically related to community groups and organizations; speaks to community groups, nonprofits, and service organizations to inform them of labor laws and departmental programs and to encourage compliance; trains staff of civic and community groups on labor laws; promotes awareness of labor laws to marginalized populations and the advocacy community; performs extensive and targeted outreach to support the Department of Labor’s community engagement initiatives and mission.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         the principles and practices of developing and conducting training programs for labor  protection topics

·         engaging business and community organizations

·         current federal, state, and local laws and regulations, and court rulings related to labor laws involving worker protection

·         investigative methods and techniques

·         the problems encountered in establishing and conducting training and outreach and possible approaches to their solution

·         functions, organization, and role of local government as it pertains to worker protection enforcement

·         computer systems and software including Microsoft Office and commonly used applications

 

 

SKILL IN:

 

·         speaking effectively to groups

·         training business leaders and HR or community groups in complex labor laws

·         effectively communicating complex rules and procedures to the public and to businesses

·         presenting labor law material to a diverse group of business and community audiences

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·         learn departmental rules and regulations

·         effectively conduct classroom or on-the-job training programs related to labor laws

·         assess areas regarding wage compliance and community and business training needs

·         assisting in developing training programs

·         maintain, analyze and interpret data regarding labor laws

·         investigate complaints and inquiries relating to labor laws and issue letters of findings

·         establish and maintain effective working relationships with the business community, departmental personnel, other city agencies, diverse community groups, and the general public

·         present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing

·         disseminate information about labor laws

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/20.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.

 

AND

 

EXPERIENCE:

 

            Two years of experience providing outreach, training, for a government or non-profit organization, which has included six months of experience performing training and outreach on worker protection laws in the Mayor’s Office of Labor.  

 

OR

 

            Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources, which includes a bachelor’s degree as an education minimum.

 

ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENT FOR THE BILINGUAL SPECIALTY

Sufficient training and/or experience to be able to understand and speak fluently and clearly in English and the designated non-English language.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

 

            Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Common­wealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment, if required by work assignment.
