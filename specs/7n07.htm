                                         GREENHOUSE/NURSERY ATTENDANT

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is skilled gardening work propagating and cultivating a variety of flowers, shrubs, trees, and other plants as well as maintaining and managing native plant nursery.  An employee in this position is responsible for the proper temperature control of the greenhouse and plant nursery.  Work is performed under the close supervision of a technical supervisor.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class)

 

1.    The positions must be responsible for propagating and cultivating a variety of flowers, shrubs, trees and other plants.

2.    Positions in this class must perform the work in the Department of Parks and Recreation.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

            Maintains greenhouse and nursery as a functioning propagation facility, including buildings, hoop houses and cold frames; propagates plants by budding, layering, or dividing in a greenhouse or cold frames; cultivates plants by watering, spraying various mixtures of insecticides, applying fertilizers, and regulating temperatures by ventilation and heating controls; maintains live-stake, and Missouri Gravel bare-root beds for the growth of alternative stock types for partner use; maintains all plants stored on site.

            Makes soil mixes; makes cuttings; plants, transplants and prunes a wide variety of flowers, shrubs, trees and other plants in the greenhouse and nursery.

Develops nursery maintenance and propagation activities based on annual goals established by the Urban Forestry and Ecosystem Management Division, and the Office of Watersheds of the Philadelphia Water Department

Maintains and manages nursery equipment and supplies

            Operates hand and power tools and equipment such as vacuum sweepers, snow blowers, hedge trimmers, sprayers, soil mixers, rakes, shovels and hoes.

            Organizes seed collection efforts; processes, records and stores collected seeds; keeps detailed records of inventory of seeds, plants, equipment, tools and supplies; maintain detailed records of seed provenance.        

            Operates a Cushman or van to transport floral displays to other areas.

            Engages community through providing educational programs to school groups or individual visitors; aids volunteer and intern groups in special projects.

            Participates in planting floral displays.

            Participates in the maintenance of the grounds surrounding related facilities; maintains nursery equipment and supplies.

            Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         methods and materials used in propagating, cultivating and transplanting stock in greenhouses and nurseries.

·         proper seed collection and handling techniques

·         flowers, shrubs and trees which grow well in an urban environment.

·         the common insects, fungi, and diseases which affect flowers, shrubs and trees

·         integrated pest management methods

·         greenhouse and nursery facility operations

 

SKILL IN:

 

·         the budding, layering and dividing of plants.

·         the method of propagating plants.

·         the use and care of gardening tools and equipment.

·         the handling of seeds

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·         apply growth regulators, herbicides, fungicides and fertilizers.

·         propagate, cultivate and force grow a variety of greenhouse and nursery stock.

·         transplant, trim and pot plants.

·         manage the greenhouse and nursery facilities, including irrigation and heating system

·         plan, direct and coordinate departmental propagation program

·         manage multiple tasks simultaneously, and work flexible hours

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/16.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Education equivalent to the completion of the twelfth school grade. 

 

AND

 

            Successful completion of forty (40) hours of training approved by the Department of Parks and Recreation or 30 college credits from an accredited college or university in grounds maintenance, nursery, arboriculture or related field.

AND

 

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

            Three years of grounds or horticultural experience in a large urban park system.

 

            Or any equivalent combination of training and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

 

            Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment in this class as required by work assignment.
