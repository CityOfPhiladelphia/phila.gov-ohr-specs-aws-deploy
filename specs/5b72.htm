CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

5B72
YOUTH PLACEMENT COUNSELOR SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory placement and counseling work supervising a small group of placement counselors on an assigned shift in a community based shelter program. An employee in this class supervises counselors engaged in the placement and monitoring of court-referred youths in group and foster homes providing a variety of services as an alternative to secured incarceration. The unit's responsibilities also include providing emergency and interim placement for neglected, dependent, and abused children and youth referred by social workers in the City's children and youth services agency. Supervising counselors and meeting with child care agency representatives, probation officers, and officials from Family Court to discuss the progress and problems of children and youth and to ensure that proper care is provided are important in the work. Employees in this class report to a Youth Emergency Placement Supervisor.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Supervises the activities of an assigned shift of counselors interviewing, screening, and providing placement services for youths as an alterative to incarceration or as emergency or interim social service placements; assigns and explains tasks to counselors; develops procedures to improve placement activities; reviews background history reports for content and appropriate recommendations; reviews and approves placement determinations.
  • Discusses placement of youths into particular facilities with subordinate counselors; discusses with counselors the results of placement interviews and other pertinent information; may conduct placement interviews; orients youth to facilities' rules and regulations, purpose of the program, and type of behavior expected; contacts youth's parents or guardians to inform them of location, program contacts, and visiting procedures; prepares justifications for court review rejecting referrals from community-based shelter.
  • Supervises the review of weekly adjustment reports on each youth in placement to monitor progress in placement; supervises and participates in agency visits to investigate incidents; reviews gathered information on incidents, complaints, and problems; supervises counselors in the provision of crisis intervention; meets with director of child care agencies to resolve individual behavioral and medical problems; approves interagency transfers to youths.
  • Monitors and supervises the monitoring of contracted agencies for conformance with contract terms; periodically reviews the quality and effectiveness of services received from contract providers; aids unit head in devising procedures for subordinates to evaluate services provided and progress of youths participating in the program; reviews and approves agency requests for payment; consults with private agencies in the field to compare and evaluate services received; reports on services available to unit supervisor.
  • Trains and orients subordinates in acceptable counseling techniques; instructs counselors in applicable rules, regulations, and policies; checks work of subordinates for conformance to procedures and specific instructions; may supervise unit court representatives and provide direction in conveying information to court officials; schedules leave time and tours of duty; prepares performance reports; recommends disciplinary action if necessary.
  • Compiles information used in the preparation of weekly progress and informational reports; organizes information for inclusion in the annual report.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods, practices, and techniques of interviewing, especially as applied to interviewing children and youth.
  • the principles and practices of protective and custodial care as applied to delinquent, neglected, abused, and dependent youth.
  • the behavior patterns and problems encountered in dealing with delinquent and/or dependent youths.
  • the social and emotional factors that result in institutionalization of youths.
  • community and social resources available for delinquent youths.
  • the organization and operation of Family Court.

ABILITY TO:

  • supervise a small group of placement counselors in providing placement services to youths.
  • learn the principles and practices of placement of delinquent youths in appropriate group or foster homes.
  • train subordinates in interviewing techniques and conducting interviews.
  • evaluate performance of various agencies for conformance to negotiated contracts.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with youths and their families, agency officials, social workers, and associates.
  • determine the appropriate type of program and facility for various types of youths referred to the program.
  • follow written and oral instructions.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 11/1979.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of two years of study at an accredited college or university which has included course work in sociology, psychology, mental health and/or related fields.

EXPERIENCE

  • Four years of paid experience counseling, teaching, or supervising groups of delinquent and/or dependent youths, one year of which has been placing youths in a program which provides recreational, educational, counseling, and health services in group or foster homes.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience acceptable to the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
