CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

4C49
DIABETES NURSE SPECIALIST

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is specialized nursing work involving consultation, assessment and evaluation of diabetes related services provided on a City-wide basis. The employee in this class assesses, plans, implements and evaluates diabetes related educational programs and provides consultative services to ensure effective program implementation. Contacts with hospitals, physicians, nurses, other health-care professionals and community groups and the provision of educational services are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of a Nursing Director.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Visits medical facilities to determine the need for diabetes related services; confers with health care professionals regarding the need for implementation of patient and/or professional diabetes education services or the improvement of existing program; assists in the development and implementation of diabetes related educational program; provides with hospitals and health care agencies educational materials related to patient education.
  • Plans, develops and conducts diabetes educational program for health care professionals and community groups; provides diabetes research information to hospitals, health care agencies, physicians and nurses; participates with other diabetes service agencies in the development of educational materials.
  • Plans and conducts training sessions for departmental nurses and health care professionals to update their knowledge in the area of diabetes control; assists departmental health care professionals in the incorporation of diabetes educational components into existing programs.
  • Assesses diabetes services, treatment and patient education programs provided by public, private and volunteer agencies; obtains diabetes related data through surveys of physicians, hospitals and community agencies; analyzes information obtained through surveys to determine areas requiring diabetes educational services; plans and implements new programs as required.
  • Participates as an active member and attends meetings held by diabetes support groups, committees and task forces.
  • Compiles and maintains resource file of services available to persons with diabetes; responds to inquiries from hospitals, physicians, nurses and the general public regarding diabetes services.
  • Maintains records of work performed; submits periodic activity reports to the state diabetes control program.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices and techniques of nursing and their application in a community health setting.
  • health principles, practices and techniques with respect to clinical and related services.
  • the pathology, treatment and control of diabetes.
  • the theories of education and their application.
  • the laws and regulations governing nursing practice.
  • available resources and current developments in the area of diabetes control.
  • the methods and techniques of disseminating educational information to health care professionals and community groups.
ABILITY TO:

  • assess the training needs of hospitals, health centers, and health care agencies related to diabetes control.
  • plan, develop and conduct training sessions in the area of diabetes control.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • maintain records relating to program activities.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, representatives of public and private health care agencies, community groups and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 2/88.)

  • Completion of a master's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in nursing, nursing education or a closely related area.
  • Two years of nursing experience.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Eligibility for license as a registered nurse issued by the Pennsylvania State Board of Nursing Examiners at the of application and possession of such licensure within the time limits specified by the department.

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Diabetes Nurse Specialist.
