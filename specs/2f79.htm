CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2F79
MENTAL HEALTH LEGAL ASSISTANT

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is technical work interpreting and utilizing the Mental Health Procedures Act as it relates to the criminal justice system. The employee in this class reviews psychiatric evaluations prepared by court appointed psychiatrists and recommends action to be taken in the case. A significant aspect of the work is presenting the commonwealth's position in mental health matters to the court. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative supervisor.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. Performs technical work interpreting and utilizing the Mental Health Procedures Act.

  2. Presents the commonwealth's position on mental health matters to a court.

  3. Position is located in the Trial Division of the District Attorney's Office.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Reviews psychiatric evaluations received from the psychiatric unit of the courts concerning individuals who have come in contact with the criminal justice system; reviews criminal charges, present offense, and prior record of defendant for whom examination was ordered to determine if a mental health commitment appears necessary; meets with unit chief of Trial Division to discuss mental health procedures and implications in relation to specific criminal cases; presents commonwealth's position in mental health matters to the prosecution, defense and courts.
  • Receives and evaluates reports from mental health institutions on committed defendants; assures that evaluation reports are completed and available to the court for the upcoming listing of the case.
  • Advises the court of the proper procedure for commitment and the various mental health options at the pre-trial, sentencing and post-trial levels.
  • Outlines mental health history of defendant to the assistant district attorney; recommends various mental health institutions for commitment purposes.
  • Acts as liaison in obtaining psychiatric testimony for presentation of the commonwealth's case.
  • Marks trial folder and unit mental health file as to the disposition of the case; notifies trial listing clerks of status of next court listing and place of defendants commitment.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices, and programs in the field of criminal justice.
  • the Mental Health Procedures Act.
  • the English language and spelling.
  • the criminal justice system as it applies to prosecuting mental health cases.
  • current developments in the field of mental health as they relate to the prosecution of the mentally ill.
  • the various types of treatment facilities for mentally ill defendants.
  • common legal concepts and terms.
  • the legal procedures involved in recommending commitment of the mentally ill.

ABILITY TO:

  • interpret and explain the Mental Health Procedures Act.
  • determine if a mental health commitment is necessary.
  • recommend the proper mental health institution for commitment.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, mental health institutions, judicial officials, and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 9/81.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university, including or supplemented by courses in legal practices and procedures.
  • Two years of experience providing technical staff assistance to attorneys charged with the prosecution of individuals accused of violating the criminal penal code.
Or any equivalent combination of acceptable education and experience which has included completion of a bachelor's degree program as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
