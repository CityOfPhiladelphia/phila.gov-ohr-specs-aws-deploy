CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

3A44
HIGHWAY PROJECTS COORDINATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is inspectional and technical work with responsibility for planning the resurfacing of city streets by city forces or private contractors. Work also includes coordinating the construction and reconstruction of driveways, alleyways and retaining walls and the preparation of plans and specifications to effect needed improvements. Establishing effective working relationships with district engineers on resurfacing programs and informing city officials and property owners of inspection results and costs necessary for corrective action on alleyways and driveways are significant aspects of the work.

Work is performed under the general supervision of an administrative and technical supervisor. Assignments require the use of evaluative thinking and are carried out in accordance with accepted construction standards and applicable departmental regulations. The employee in this class advises higher level departmental officials in the formulation and implementation of a city-wide program of street resurfacing and alleyway, driveway and retaining wall construction and reconstruction.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Receives and reviews lists of all proposed streets to be resurfaced by city forces or by private contractors; checks computations for proposed work; evaluates condition of street; approves or rearranges suggested priorities; obtains preliminary clearance of streets scheduled for resurfacing with all utilities; revises resurfacing priorities based on schedules of work to be performed by utilities; prepares contract packages; makes final clearance; submits for approval to chief highway engineer.
  • Prepares specifications, including labor and material cost estimates for contract advertising and subsequent bidding procedures; keeps control cost figures; insures all work performed as ordered or contracted.
  • Receives, reviews and replies to complaints regarding unsafe conditions of alleys, driveways and retaining walls; orders inspections; determines nature and extent of complaint; personally investigates those complaints of a more serious nature; determines party or parties responsible for paying for repairs; reports on the status of complaints, types of improvements needed, and the persons responsible for bearing the cost of repairs.
  • Confers with health department representatives regarding condemnation proceedings; coordinates with the various district engineers, surveyors and regulators, or the city bridge engineer regarding preparation and service to property owners of cost estimates, notices of condemnation, and notices to proceed; schedules community meetings and represents the department to explain the departmental policy for methods of assessment and construction procedures.
  • Initiates and reviews project plans and confers with the district engineers and/or surveyors relative to changes which may be required; supervises the inspector or inspectors assigned to the project; makes on-site inspections and determines that specific phases of the work are performed in accordance with plans and specifications; interprets and determines that the intent of the plans are being carried out; resolves misinterpretations of plans and/or specifications; makes minor changes in plans when required by existing field conditions; makes final inspection and processes bills for recommended payment.
  • May assist the construction engineer regarding city-state highway construction projects; reviews plans for proposed projects and submits changes as necessary; attends pre-bid and/or pre-construction meetings to review all aspects of construction projects; answers complaints pertaining to damage, inconvenience or nuisance created by construction operations.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the materials, methods, equipment and practices used in the construction and alteration of streets, sidewalks, curbs and driveways.
  • contractual practices relative to scheduling, and the preparation of contracts, building and materials specifications and city ordinances pertaining to the work.
  • inspection techniques related to the work.
  • the various types of conditions of streets and their corrective measures needed within the area of street resurfacing.
  • the physical characteristics and approved testing methods of construction materials.
  • elementary algebra and the methods of computing area and volume.

ABILITY TO:

  • evaluate the physical condition of streets and determine their priority of repair.
  • read and interpret plans, blueprints, and specifications to recognize deviations, improper or faulty construction and other hazardous conditions created during the construction process and to recommend corrective actions.
  • make accurate arithmetic computations.
  • prepare and maintain cost repairs and estimates.
  • explain deviations and to enforce compliance with contract specifications and other requirements firmly and tactfully.
  • determine the conformity of materials quantities and workmanship methods and quality with contract requirements, plans and specifications.
  • read and interpret general contract plans and specifications pertaining to the work.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, contractors and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/75.)
  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • Six years of experience in the technical inspection of street construction and repair work, two years of which has been supervising employees engaged in such work.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Highway Projects Coordinator.
