CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

1D18
MAGNETIC MEDIA LIBRARIAN

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is data processing support work maintaining a library of magnetic data storage media containing computer readable information. An employee in this class works on an assigned shift in the central computer agency, distributing, collecting and maintaining control of magnetic media and/or cartridges. Creating and maintaining computerized and manual records concerning disposition of media is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the supervision of the Magnetic Media Librarian Supervisor.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • The employee must work on an assigned shift maintaining a library of magnetic data storage media.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Pulls media and cartridges from library and delivers to computer room upon request of operations personnel; ensures that computer room shift is issued sufficient scratch media to run scheduled job; controls flow of media; keeps record of location and disposition of media; maintains inventory of all media in library; releases media to other units, departments, and outside agencies when required; documents all releases of media.
  • Creates and updates computerized records containing cataloging information for each new tapes or cartridges using tape or cartridge management software; catalogs the content, history and other pertinent data for each tape or cartridge; maintains cross-referenced files to facilitate locating desired information.
  • Maintains tape security and backup schedule; prepares media for shipment to offsite storage; stores appropriate media in security vault; rotates tapes and cartridges according to set schedule in the absence of supervisor.
  • Cleans tapes and cartridges periodically to remove residue that can cause mechanical distortion or errors; inspects and evaluates used media to determine if they are in satisfactory condition for continued use; tests media using tape evaluator and records defects or worn areas; rejects media for use if they do not meet pre- established defect standards; inspects newly purchased magnetic media for reel warpage, irregular winding, and scratches; tests random sample of purchase for conformity to specifications.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • computer operations as they apply to the use of magnetic media as an auxiliary data storage medium.
  • the proper methods and practices employed in the care and handling of magnetic media.
  • cataloging methods and procedures.

SKILL IN:

  • entering data on computer terminal keyboard.

ABILITY TO:

  • learn the operation and features of tape management systems such as CA-DYNAM-T and CA1.
  • learn non-procedural program languages such as Easytrieve.
  • search computerized and manual records to locate tapes and cartridges.
  • conduct effective media maintenance and repair programs.
  • understand and execute written and oral instructions.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and representatives of other City departments.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/88 .)

EDUCATION

  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
EXPERIENCE

  • One year of clerical or key entry experience in a data processing environment which has included the use and handling of magnetic media.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
