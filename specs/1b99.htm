COURT CLERK II


GENERAL DEFINITION

This is advanced level specialized clerical work in processing and expediting the flow of a variety of court cases. Work differs from that of the next lower class in the degree of responsibility for the assurance of the correctness of court proceedings and decisions. A significant aspect of the work involves dealing with judges, attorneys, welfare agencies, defendants, and plaintiffs to gather all necessary data for facilitating courtroom work.

Work is performed under the general supervision of an administrative superior. Assignments are performed in accordance with legal and judicial regulations and precedents and well defined policies. The employee advises court officials on correct procedures as it affects the processing of cases.


TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Prepares for hearings or trials by assembling pertinent legal records and other information, including previous appeals, rulings and other pertinent documents; prepares forms and work sheets for court sessions showing case number, names of petitioners of plaintiffs, defendants, witnesses, and attorneys; notifies all parties concerned of trial or hearing date and place of such trial or hearing; issues subpoenas and warrants upon legal order of the court to assure presence in court of all parties to the hearing or trial.
  • Records rulings and decisions of the court on bills of indictment of petitions and orders, trial lists and court documents; completes forms which may be required while court is in session such as waiver of jury trial, bench warrants, wage attachments and commitment forms.
  • Processes a variety of forms to implement commitment, discharge, bail, bond, transfer, appearance in court, payment of fine and other decisions and rulings of the court; issues certificates of election of behalf of the court.
  • Arranges for trial of cases which have been deferred by notifying lawyers, sheriff, and bondsmen of the date of trial.
  • Supervises the work of subordinate Court Clerks by making assignments and by observing work progress in the office or bondroom; plans, assigns and supervises the work of a clerical staff.
  • Performs related work as required.


REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the operation and procedure of criminal and quasi-criminal courts.
  • the clerical procedures necessary to prepare cases for court, to implement decisions of the court, and to generally facilitate the work of the courts.
  • criminal law, with particular reference to sentences established for various offenses.

ABILITY TO:
  • learn, within a reasonable period of time, the purpose, functions, and scope of the court concerned.
  • prepare and process a considerable variety of legal documents.
  • supervise the work of employees engaged in specialized clerical activities facilitating work of the criminal courts.
  • follow complex oral and written instructions.
  • keep records and prepare reports.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with judicial authorities, lawyers, superiors, subordinates, and the general public.


MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.)

  • Education equivalent to the completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • Four years of clerical experience, two years of which shall have been in criminal and quasi-criminal courts of record, processing legal and judicial documents.

Or any equivalent combination of acceptable training and experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
