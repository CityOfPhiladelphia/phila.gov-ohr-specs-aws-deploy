                          MUSEUM COLLECTIONS AND EXHIBITIONS CURATOR

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is supervisory museum curatorial work managing the preservation, development, care and access to the artifactual collections pertaining to Philadelphia regional history, primarily through exhibitions, related publications, public presentations and providing support for marketing and development initiatives.  Initiating collection development and preservation projects for the historic Atwater Kent Museum building and organizing and coordinating exhibition development and production are significant to the work.  An employee in this class also oversees the acquisition, registration, storage, care, and conservation of historical artifacts at the museum and at the local off-site storage facility.  Work is performed under the supervision of the Executive Director.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be present for a position to be allocated to this class)

 

1.    The employee in this class must supervise the preservation, development, care and access to the artifactual collections and coordinate exhibition development and production.

2.    This position must be located at the Atwater Kent Museum and at the local off-site storage facility.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

            Plans and implements on-going evaluation of the collection; identifies gaps in collection and establishes new collection areas; works with donors and dealers to improve the quality of the collection; oversees monitoring of environmental conditions and conducts periodic reviews of collection care issues; makes recommendations for improvements in collection care; supervises staff in the improvement of collection-related cataloging, documentation and reporting; supervises guest curators; identifies and remedies building and facilities needs that impact the proper care and exhibition of collection.

            Acquires artifacts for museum's permanent collection; evaluates offers to donate artifacts to museum; makes recommendations on artifacts to accept; researches, writes and prepares exhibition catalogues of artifacts; conducts periodic inventories of artifacts; ensures routine care and maintenance of artifacts on exhibition; identifies, prioritizes, and recommends restoration or conservation of artifacts; ensures necessary insurance for artifacts is obtained.

            Researches themes and concepts for exhibits; develops and organizes exhibition schedule; contacts public and private collectors to identify potential loans or donations for exhibitions; provides direction to staff and consultants to prepare exhibit content, information and related publications; oversees design and installation and integrates interactive technology within exhibition designs; directs the activities  of exhibition designers and installers; ensures exhibitions are presented with the highest regard for the visitor experience, content, aesthetics, and care of the collection; directs training for docents for  tours and lectures about exhibits; interacts with Education unit staff in development of exhibitions; gives interviews to the media when requested; arranges for return of artifacts after presentation of exhibits.

            Prepares annual budget and formulates goals and objectives for Collections unit; oversees the development of collection management databases and online access to the collection; assists in locating funding sources to fund exhibitions and collection purchases; assists with preparation of grants and prepares exhibitions and curatorial related final reports; prepares reports and schedules meetings for the Collection Committee; serves as liaison with other city departments.

            Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

           American history with particular emphasis on the local history of Philadelphia

           standard museum administration and collection practices, procedures, methods, and techniques

           the literature and research sources in the field of American history

           exhibition development and production

·                      budgetary principles and practices

·                      supervisory principles and practices

 

SKILL IN:

 

·             conducting research

·             using technology to develop interactive exhibitions

·                      museum collections planning and management

 

ABILITY TO:

 

           supervise staff and contractors

           exercise judgment and discretion in screening difficult objects for acquisition and restoration

           express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.

           establish and maintain effective working relationships with museum staff, professional associates, donors, committee members, private collectors, and the general public.

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE  (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/14.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Completion of a Masters degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in history, American studies, humanities, historical administration, public history, museum studies, art history, or a related field.

 

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

            Three years of experience maintaining historic collections or performing curatorial, registration, or exhibition work for a museum.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

            Two years of experience planning, developing and implementing the design and installation of exhibits for a museum.

 

OR

 

            Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources which has included the Master's degree with major coursework in the areas described above and the specific experience.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
