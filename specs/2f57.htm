LICENSES AND INSPECTIONS QUALITY ASSURANCE OFFICE DIRECTOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is quality assurance and review work at the managerial level in the City agency responsible for enforcing the City's building, housing, fire, plumbing, electrical, zoning, licensing and related codes. A position in this class is responsible for assuring the systematic and thorough review of departmental functions, operations, procedures, and episodic issues to increase the department's efficiency, effectiveness, and overall integrity and quality of efforts. Development and implementation of standards intended to improve the efficiency and integrity of the department work force is an important aspect of the work. Work includes the responsibility of assuring all employees, including supervisors and managers, are audited to insure adherence to Departmental standards of efficiency. The employee has responsibility for implementing an integrity plan for all employees and performs as the department's Integrity Officer coordinating with the Inspector General. The employee directs the development and maintenance of a Comprehensive Training Plan. The employee works under the direction of the Commissioner.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Develops and drafts a formal integrity plan; develops goals and objectives for the quality assurance program; develops programs and procedures to monitor activities within the department; determines staff structure and deployment, budgetary requirements, equipment needs and other resources; maintains adherence to Departmental Integrity Plan for all employees on matters of integrity and procedures for filing complaints and adherence to code of ethics, charter requirements and Departmental roles.
  • Receives complaints or allegations of misconduct from individuals or organizations both within and outside of the department; initiates and coordinates investigations; directs preparation of evidence, statements and other technical support data for department legal counsel, Inspector General's Office and outside law enforcement agencies; collects and evaluates evidence; documents findings and compiles reports on all activities and recommendations; recommends continuation of effort or referral to law enforcement or other officials for subsequent action.
  • Assures, through an employee audit program, that all managers and supervisors review the activities of subordinates periodically to determine adherence to Departmental performance standards; evaluates such standards through consultation, review of reports and observation of work practices.
  • Coordinates efforts with, and serves as primary departmental liaison to, the Inspector General's Office; works with representatives of other civil and criminal investigative and/or analytical bodies, providing information and assistance to ensure complete and thorough investigations; completes reports on all such coordinated activities and issues.
  • Oversees the analysis of standard operating practices pertaining to permit applications and plans review, license issuance and methods and quality of code compliance inspection in assigned departmental units or functional assignments; analyzes such practices for opportunities for potential misuse or abuse, identifying sources and/or causes of same; administers the development of revisions to procedures to increase thoroughness, accuracy and credibility; coordinates such revisions with department management and technical personnel; complies reports on activities and recommended solutions.
  • Administers the development and implementation of employee training programs designed to educate employees on work quality, departmental regulations and techniques for dealing with contractors, businesses and the general public; directs the training of employees on quality assurance and ethical matters and allegations of misconduct.
  • Oversees, department wide, the planning, implementing, and evaluating of training and staff development; supervises the development and implementation of a comprehensive plan for all department employees including material and technical matters; supervises the implementation of the Training Assistance Program to improve customer relations.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the goals, objectives, and techniques of quality assurance and review work, particularly as they pertain to maintaining the integrity of regulatory code enforcement.
  • departmental systems, goals, processes and procedures.
  • the goals and objectives of administrative investigation work.
  • the principles and practices of work flows, layout and procedural and practices of work flows, layout and procedural analysis.
  • the philosophy, purpose, and function of regulatory codes.
  • administration, organization, and management.
  • organization development and techniques.
  • methods and techniques used in training and development.

SKILL IN:

  • presenting factual evidence and drawing logical, objective findings and conclusions from such evidence.
  • recommending sound and effective means to address and/or resolve issues of administrative and criminal wrong-doing and inappropriate activity.
  • obtaining and documenting detailed information through interviews, records review and observation.

ABILITY TO:

  • develop programs and standards which respond to problems detected during analysis.
  • plan, assign and review the work of administrative and technical personnel.
  • analyze and comprehend organizational and procedural problems involving work production, work flow, forms, records systems, and staff utilization.
  • prepare and interpret a variety of reports, charts, layouts, forms, handbooks, and other materials.
  • enlist the cooperation of officials of various agencies in implementing policy and procedural changes.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and representatives of related organizations.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 6/1990.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.

  • Five years of administrative experience, at least four years of which shall have been in:
    • administrative code experience directing through subordinate supervisors a major section of the City's overall licensing and inspection program.

Or any equivalent combination of training and experience which has been determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
