RESIDENT CARE SERVICES MANAGER

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is management level resident care work directing, through subordinate supervisors, a large staff providing a variety of resident care services and overseeing the effective coordination of information with other units to ensure communication of residents’ needs.  The employee in this class is responsible for managing resident care services for a large personal care home for aged and indigent homeless adults and adults with mental and behavioral health issues.  Counseling and instructing patients in aspects of personal care and following standing orders for medical care are a significant aspect of the work. Work also includes supervision of an institutional laundry operation.  Work is performed under the general supervision of an administrative superior.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

  • The employee must direct, through subordinates supervisors, technical staff engaged in the provision of a variety of direct resident care services at Riverview home.
  • Only one position will be allocated to this class in the Office of Supportive Housing.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

Supervises resident care supervisors and oversees daily activities of resident care staff subordinate health care personnel; supervises work by observation and review of reports and evaluates work performance; confers with subordinate staff and residents on problems encountered in the application of personal care practices and gives recommendations for their solution; makes shift assignments and ensures minimum staff coverage; ensures that direct care staff adhere to directives identified in the resident’s support plan while providing quality daily care in accordance with applicable laws and regulations.

Develops and establishes work policies; develops work procedures and routines and prepares written guidelines for staff; trains and instructs subordinate personnel in the administrative and clinical protocol to be followed in the resident care program; may provide nursing services according to standing orders as needed; trains staff to identify and address the challenges of providing direct care to older adults with dementia, cognitive functioning and other mental health issues;  trains staff to recognize symptoms and side effects of psychotropic and pain management medications; develops and reports plans of correction to the PA Department of Public Welfare; investigates claims and complaints of residents.

            Reviews laundry operations regularly; discusses problems and proposed improvements with laundry employees; develops laundry service policy as it impacts resident services.

            Holds regular meetings to promulgate changes and analyze problems; interviews prospective employees and recommends for hiring; participates in and/or directs the orientation and training of new employees and supervisors; meets with and discusses problems and work situations with union representatives.

            Analyzes resident needs and activities to determine areas for service improvement or development; determines admission or discharge of residents and change in level of care of residents; coordinates with social service, volunteer service and health service units for the provision of services to residents; recommends the development and/or intensification of services for residents as needed; maintains computerized files; supervises the maintenance of records and medical treatments and related services as completed by subordinates; ensures that adequate follow-up is performed; ensures that residents are not engaging in any activities that may cause harm to themselves or others; oversees scheduling and transportation to medical appointments and other activities identified in support plan.

            Selects and orders resident clothing and hygiene articles and institution furnishings; develops and ensures enforcement of supply dispensing procedures; coordinates the cleaning and maintenance of residences and removal/treatment of hazards to residents with maintenance and custodial staffs; ensures compliance with state, federal and local health and safety laws, regulations and guidelines concerning resident care.

            Prepares reports on unit activities; reviews and analyzes reports and logs submitted by others regarding showering and meal attendance; reviews menus to determine if items are listed appropriately on the menu for each meal; reviews daily log of condition of the facility and reports of any repairs or issues that need attention; reviews reports on residents; meets with and advises residents on residential issues and behavioral problems; investigates complaints of injury and health referrals by residents; reports plans of correction directly to PA Department of Public Welfare.

            Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

•           the principles and techniques of residential care

•           housekeeping and sanitation requirements for personal care facilities

•           federal, state and local health and safety laws, regulations and guidelines concerning personal care facilities

•           personal care home resident rights

•           clinical guidelines and their practical application in the provision of adult personal care

•           principles and techniques of staff development and sensitization to the emotional and social needs of residents

•           administrative principles and procedures for personal care homes

•           supervisory methods and techniques

 

SKILL IN:

 

•           Microsoft Word and Microsoft Powerpoint

•           managing the coordination of personal care and medical care of residents

 

ABILITY TO:

 

•           direct and train a large staff of supervisors and aides providing residential care, and employees providing laundry service

•           learn and apply codes and regulations governing the provision of personal care services

•           maintain records and prepare comprehensive reports

•           direct the training of employees in residential care work

•           successfully complete yearly training required by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania

·                      direct the coordination of the provision of a variety of resident care services associated with resident support plans

·                      evaluate and resolve problems related to the administration of resident care services

·                      establish and maintain effective working relationships with residents, staff and associates

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE  (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 11/13.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Graduation from an accredited school of nursing.

 

AND

 

            Currently licensed as a Registered Nurse by the State Board of Nursing for the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania. 

 

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

            Three years of experience providing resident care services in a hospital, nursing home, assisted living facility, or personal care home.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

            Two years of experience supervising direct care staff in a hospital, nursing home, assisted living facility, or personal care home.

 

OR

 

            Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources which has included current licensure as a Registered Nurse by the State Board of Nursing for the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania and the specific experience.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATIONS

 

            Possession of a proper class motor vehicle operator’s license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania at the time of appointment to the Resident Care Services Manager class. 

            Certification as a Personal Care Home Administrator as issued by the Pennsylvania Department of Public Welfare within one year of appointment and during tenure as a Resident Care Services Manager.
