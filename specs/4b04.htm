SENIOR MEDICAL ASSISTANT/ EHR SPECIALIST

 

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

           This is advanced level patient care and electronic health record (EHR) support work in an Ambulatory Health Services (AHS) facility.  An employee in this class is a multi-skilled practitioner who implements and supports the electronic health record initiative by performing technical services and providing training and support.  Work includes troubleshooting electronic health record system issues received by phone and in person from end users and traveling to different health center sites.  Employees in the class also assist in implementing physicians’ orders and provide clinical services and procedures necessary to provide comprehensive health care services to patients.

            Significant to the work is applying knowledge of Ambulatory Health Services’ workflows and the clinical and technical concepts required to support the electronic health records department-wide.  This position is assigned to a central work location, however, is required to travel to different health center locations. Work is performed under the supervision of an administrative superior.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.    Positions in this class must work in the Health Department.

2.    Positions must provide a variety of electronic health record support services directly to end users.

3.    No more than two positions shall be allocated to the class.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

            Responds to inquiries and acts as a first-level response providing support for all aspects of the electronic health records to end users; optimizes electronic health record workflows; logs and maintains details of support activities using tracking systems; obtains the support of higher level technical staff when necessary.

Performs Medical Assistant duties such as preparing patients for examination, administering tests, obtaining samples, and maintaining records; performs clinical work operating specialized medical equipment; interacts with patients; organizes supplies and equipment and the cleaning of equipment and patient areas.

Performs related work as required.

           

 REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         the function of electronic health records and wireless operations

·         computer software commonly used in City health centers  

·         clinical records and patient care

·         the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA)

·         electrocardiography, audiometric testing, autoclaves, microscopes,                      sphygmomanometers and office machines

·         medical assistance practice

·         the basic principles of chemistry

·         the principles, methods, materials, equipment and techniques used to perform     laboratory analysis

·         the principles, methods and techniques used to administer injections to adults and pediatric patients

·         the safety precautions required in laboratory work including universal precautions

 

SKILL IN:

 

·         the use of electronic health records to address organizational needs

·         electronic information processing, computer applications and other software that integrate and/or interface with electronic health records

·         data entry and retrieval

·         provide advice, guidance, and both individual and classroom training to a variety of electronic health record users

·         customer service regarding issues related to the electronic health records

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·         identify problems and resolve issues in a timely manner

·         travel to different Ambulatory Health Services’ locations as needed

·         learn department application software packages

·         critically assess workflow gaps and identify solutions

·         deliver health software training for both individuals and groups of users

·         establish and maintain effective working relationships with departmental                         

associates          

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 10/20.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Education equivalent to the completion of the twelfth school grade and completion of didactic work and graduation from an accredited Medical Assistant Training program.

 

AND

EXPERIENCE:

           

            Two years of clinical experience in AHS as a Medical Assistant in the Health Department, six months of which has included varied end user support experience in a comprehensive EHR program.

 

AND CERTIFICATION:

 

Certification in ONE of the following:

 

            Registered Medical Assistant (RMA) from American Medical Technology;

 

OR

 

            Certified Medical Assistant (CMA) from American Association of Medical Assistants;

 

OR

 

            National Certified Medical Assistant (NCMA) from the National Center for Competency Testing;

 

OR

 

            Nationally Registered Certified Medical Assistant (NAHP) from the National Association for Health Professionals

 

OR

 

            Certified Clinical Medical Assistant (CCMA) from the National Healthcareer Association.

 

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

            Possession of valid certification as a Medical Assistant at the time of application, appointment and during tenure of employment as a Senior Medical Assistant.

            Certification as a Medical Administrative Specialist by the American Medical Technologist Association within six months of appointment and during tenure of employment.

            Possession of valid certification in CPR at the time of appointment and during tenure of employment as a Senior Medical Assistant.
