CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

4D01
PHYSICIAN I

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is general duty professional medical work in the diagnosis of disease and in the care and treatment of patients. Work is that of a general practitioner in scope.

Work is performed under the direction of a higher level physician. Assignments require evaluative thinking and are carried out in accordance with precedents and accepted medical standards. The employee is responsible for diagnosing and treating a variety of illnesses and injuries.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Conducts physical examinations, obtains medical histories, diagnoses causes of illness and injury, prescribes and administers treatment, designates nursing care; recommends hospitaliza- tion, requests follow-up treatment; uses laboratory studies, x-rays and other techniques in diagnosing and confirming the presence of disease; confers with consulting physicians and nurses concerning the treatment of patients; recommends patients to appropriate medical specialists.
  • Performs minor surgery; administers flu, tetanus, polio and other immunization vaccines.
  • Determines that proper public health procedures are adhered to by the patients and staff of the institution to prevent the occurrence of epidemics in the institution.
  • Makes recommendations concerning the physical condition of applicants for city employment; provides city employees and victims of fires, accidents and other disasters with emergency medical treatment; determines whether persons charged with the illegal operation of motor vehicles are under the influence of liquor; examines suspects charged with being narcotic addicts; appears in court to testify as an expert medical witness.
  • Keeps records and prepares reports concerning assigned cases.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of general medicine and surgery including the diagnosis and treatment of a variety of diseases and injuries.
  • current developments in the field of general medicine and surgery.
  • the principles and practices of preventive medicine.
  • medical jurisprudence.
ABILITY TO:

  • examine patients, and diagnose disease and to prescribe and administer necessary treatment.
  • interpret and use the results of x-rays and laboratory tests to confirm clinical findings and to aid in the diagnosis of disease.
  • gain the confidence and cooperation of patients and to establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 10/72.)

  • Graduation from an accredited school of medicine with a degree of Doctor of Medicine and completion of an internship at an approved hospital.
OR
  • Graduation from an accredited school of osteopathy with a Degree of Doctor of Osteopathy, and completion of an internship in an approved hospital.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to meet the physical and medical standards established for this class by the Municipal Medical Dispensary, approved by the City of Philadelphia Personnel Department and described on Form 73-111, Special Medical and Physical Standards.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid license to practice medicine or osteopathy in the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania issued by the State Board of Medical Education and Licensure or the State Board of Osteopathic Examiners prior to appointment.
