INFORMATION SYSTEMS AUDITING MANAGER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative information systems work directing the provision of all computer services in the city's central auditing department. The employee in this class directs, through a subordinate supervisor, a small staff engaged in performing computer assisted auditing projects, and manages and coordinates reviews of financial and non-financial EDP systems, departmental LAN administration and hardware/software procurement and maintenance. Work includes establishing guidelines and procedures for departmental computer analysis/design projects and internal control reviews of the city's computerized systems. Contact with City agencies and departments, and MOIS technical staff to discuss and resolve issues pertaining to computer projects is significant to the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

  • This is a one-position class located in the city's central auditing agency.
  • The position must direct all computer services in the city's central auditing agency.
  • The position must direct a small staff engaged in performing computer assisted auditing projects and services of city financial and non-financial EDP systems.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs, through a subordinate supervisor, a small staff engaged in performing computer program analysis/design projects of the city's mainframe computer systems; ensures that user needs and program objectives are met; directs the review of an existing system specifications to determine current design needs and equipment; directs the development of preliminary program specifications; reviews data and information collected; ensures that program logic is designed to meet specifications and that program is coded in appropriate language; determines the size and number of program files and records; ensures that program is tested and inputs checked for accuracy and completeness.
  • Directs the examining of system-wide financial and related records; trains new employees in the use of electronic data processing equipment, flow charting conventions, and the applications and programming techniques of major software packages used in auditing; monitors work in progress and evaluates performance of supervisory staff.
  • Confers with departmental administrators to determine computer assistance needs; evaluates requests for feasibility; writes or directs the writing of applications program to address specific auditing needs; establishes time tables; schedules assignments; reviews prior year's work documentation and determines degree of change necessary; reviews audit projects for areas of possible computerization and recommends computer applications; selects appropriate hardware and software to perform desired analysis.
  • Confers with officials and staff of the City's central computing agency; arranges for access of electronically stored media; reviews files stored on magnetic tape, analyzing probability of future usage and recommending retention or disposal; arranges for the duplication, storage and disposal of archived tapes; oversees installation of computer hardware and software, including local area networks; oversees scheduling, programming and maintenance of departmental personal computer/mini-computer/local area network systems; serves as a member of the city-wide Network Task Force; oversees data base maintenance.
  • Develops new programs to simulate and test system-wide financial and personnel data-processing systems; develops programs to provide computer assisted alternatives to manual procedures; recommends adoption of new procedures.
  • Reviews technical updates to current software, new software specifications and changes in system-wide data processing procedures; studies technological advances and recommends purchase of new software packages; designs continuing education programs in software packages and personal computers; coordinates usage by departmental personnel, of computers and peripheral equipment.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices and procedures used in the development, infrastructure, hardware, software, operations and maintenance of local area networks and other computerized information systems.
  • the capabilities of electronic data processing equipment, including personal computers.
  • the conventions used in flow-charting.
  • the applications and programming techniques of query and other major software packages.
  • the methods and techniques used in protecting computer systems security.
  • supervisory principles and practices.
  • modern administrative principles and practices.
  • one or more computer operating systems.
  • one or more fourth generation computer programming language.

ABILITY TO:

  • plan, organize and direct the activities of a technical staff engaged in the development and administration of computerized information system projects.
  • analyze and resolve complex computing and system development problems.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and representatives of agencies being audited.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 6/96.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in computer science or a related field.

    AND
    GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Three years of information management systems experience performing computer program analysis/design projects.

    OR

  • Three years of information systems auditing experience.

    AND
    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

  • Two years of supervisory experience directing computer program analysis/design projects or audits and internal control reviews of computerized information systems.

Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum.

NOTE:
Possession of a bachelor's degree in accounting can be substituted for the education requirements above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
