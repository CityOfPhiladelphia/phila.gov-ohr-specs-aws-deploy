CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

9D22
RECREATION STADIUM DIRECTOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is recreation management work at the deputy commissioner level directing all operations of the City's multi-purpose stadium complex including surrounding parking facilities and ancillary practice facilities for major league football co-tenant. The employee in this class directs, through subordinate supervisors, a large staff of administrative, technical, and trades personnel responsible for maintenance of the stadium's physical plant, and for planning and coordinating of sporting, entertainment and other social and recreational events. Work includes responsibility for administering contracts with vendors, negotiating and implementing lease provisions with tenants, and formulating capital program objectives. Establishing stadium policies and procedures and contact with representatives of the media, community groups, and government are significant aspects of the work.

Work is performed under the general direction of the Recreation Commissioner.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The employee in this class must be responsible for directing all aspects of the City's stadium complex.
  2. This position must report to the Recreation Commissioner.
  3. Only one position will be allocated to this class.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs all aspects of stadium operations, including special event planning, event supervision, post-event analysis and critique; coordinates with other city agencies, such as the Police, Streets and Fire Departments, for provision of services; administers contracts for the provision of services by vendors.
  • Plans, assigns and reviews, through subordinate supervisors, the work of a large staff of trades, administrative, and technical personnel engaged in maintaining the engineering, buildings and grounds, and other aspects of stadium administration; determines staffing needs; oversees the interviewing, hiring, and promotion of employees; confers with union representatives regarding disciplinary problems; directs the formulation and preparation of budgetary documents, including forecasting operating and capital expenditures and estimated receipt of revenues; reviews and approves the requisitioning and receipt of supplies and equipment.
  • Determines objectives of capital improvement program; tours facilities to ascertain operational efficiency of the physical plant and to confer with subordinate supervisors on matters relating to the provision of services to tenants and spectators; meets with tenants to arrange for sporting and other events at the facilities, to avoid scheduling conflicts, and to assure that city and private work forces are available to erect and dismantle grandstands and to convert playing field surfaces for various sporting events.
  • Formulates policies and procedures governing the use of the facilities; negotiates terms of leases; arranges contracts with groups providing such services as catering, parking, security, and janitorial concessions; continuously monitors concessionaires to assure the adequacy of services being provided to tenants and the public and to assure that the city's share of revenues is received and recorded; initiates corrective action where necessary.
  • Initiates and maintains a comprehensive program to promote and publicize the facilities; develops and implements plans for seeking other events that are suitable for the facilities; works with national and local news media to arrange interviews and press conferences; confers with representatives of other governmental agencies such as the convention and tourist bureau and the office of the city representative to oversee the preparation and dissemination of informational material to the press, radio, and television; advises prospective tenants on matters relating to the rental of facilities; formalizes commitments for leasing or rental.
  • Confers with other city officials to coordinate the regulation of traffic and crowd control during sporting and other events being held at the facilities, to review bids for the purchase of goods and services, and to prepare leases and contracts; attends meetings with advertising agencies to advise them of established policies concerning the promotion of sponsors' products.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • principles and practices of facility-stadium/arena management.
  • principles and practices of preparing and administering operating and capital budgets in a multi-purpose stadium complex.
  • administration, management and coordination principles and techniques as they relate to the operation of a large stadium complex used for sporting and other events.
  • principles and practices of administrative and operational analysis.
  • the form and content of leases and contracts as they relate to the rental of facilities and concessions.
  • the methods and techniques of disseminating informational and promotional material to the public and the facilities and media available for this purpose.
  • appropriate use of personnel to maximize efficiency and productivity.
  • principles and practices of contract preparation and administration.
  • management planning and control strategies.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.

SKILL IN:

  • evaluating the effectiveness of administrative and technical functions and making procedural changes to provide more efficient and effective operations.
  • coordinating the activities of the organization with intra-and extra-departmental agencies.
  • promoting entertainment, sporting, and related events to a broad spectrum of the regional community.

ABILITY TO:

  • develop operational goals to achieve desired results.
  • communicate operational strategies and objectives to employees.
  • coordinate material and human resources to implement goals.
  • prepare cost estimates on materials and personnel needed to perform required tasks.
  • plan, organize, and implement administrative and other ancillary support services to meet program goals.
  • establish effective working relationships with departmental officials, industrial and commercial firms, community groups, representatives of the media, and the general public.
  • prepare and present effective written and oral reports to outline findings and conclusions.
  • effectively represent the Recreation Commissioner at meetings and conferences.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/1993.)
  1. EDUCATION:
    Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in public administration, business administration, finance or a related field.

    AND

  2. GENERAL EXPERIENCE:
    Five years of managerial level experience in a multi-purpose stadium complex or in a recreation program.

    AND

  3. SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:
    Three years of recreation management experience planning, coordinating and evaluating all operations of a multi-purpose stadium complex which is used for a variety of sporting, entertainment and other events.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
