CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

1B07
DATA EXAMINATION CLERK


GENERAL DEFINITION

This is clerical work in the control of data processing output to assure accuracy and completeness. The employee verifies and reconciles output data by reviewing and comparing it to source material, reports, and other documents.

Work is performed under the supervision of an administrative superior. Assignments are normally stable in nature and are carried out in accordance with standard rules and instructions with supervisory guidance continuously available. The employee performs a control function in the data processing operation.


TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Reviews and reconciles the number and amount of payroll and pension checks issued by the city and the deductions taken by various agencies.
  • Reviews and reconciles pay reports sent to the deducting agencies showing the totals deducted for each agency.
  • Prepares an adjustment voucher when an error is discovered.
  • Keeps running totals of budgetary records for city departments.
  • Prepares periodic reports to show the taxable gross of all payroll and pension checks and the amounts deducted by the various agencies for the quarter-to-date and year-to-date.
  • Performs related work as required.


REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • office practices and procedures as applied to clerical and machine procedures and practices in a large scale computer operation.
  • arithmetic.
  • the principles and practices of bookkeeping.
  • methods and procedures analysis as applied to clerical procedures and practices.

SKILL IN:

  • making varied arithmetic computations with speed and accuracy.
  • the operation of office machines as required by work assignments.

ABILITY TO:

  • maintain data processing control records.
  • understand and follow complex oral or written instructions.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with departmental officials and associates.


MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.)

  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • Two years of experience in clerical work, one year of which has been performing clerical procedures requiring the use of judgment in selecting alternative courses of action.

Or any equivalent combination of acceptable education and experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
