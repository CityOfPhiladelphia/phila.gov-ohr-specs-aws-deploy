CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

5D61
REFUGEE EMPLOYMENT SUPPORT SPECIALIST

Specialties
( Bilingual ) (General )

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is specialized clerical work providing a variety of support services for an agency providing employment counseling and placement assistance for refugees currently receiving public assistance or who are in need of employment. The employee in this class also interviews clients to determine education levels and work history, and reviews and processes requests for employment on an as needed basis. Receiving visitors,giving out program information, and encouraging clients to accept jobs, are significant aspects of the work. Work is perform under the direction of a technical superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

BILINGUAL SPECIALTY

  • Performs the duties below with members of a designated non-english language speaking community.
BOTH SPECIALTIES

  • Receives telephone messages and relays information; receives visitors and directs to proper destination; gives out standard program information; receives and logs correspondence and forms; assembles forms and correspondence upon request; complies summary of posting log;
  • Sorts and delivers mail; sorts, fills out and fills a variety of routine forms; operates office equipment for which no prior training or experience is required.
  • Interviews refugees to determine clients previous contact with the program, work experience, job skills,abilities and educational background;determines client employment preference; arranges for clients to attend training sessions; schedules and/or coordinates client interviews with prospective employments; assists clients in completing applications; explains purpose and goals of program to prospective clients and employers; assists clients in acclimating to jobs; acts as central contact point for scheduling client appointments; maintains client's files both manual and computerized.
  • Types memos and forms.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the English language and spelling
  • arithmetic.
  • office practices and procedures.
  • typewriter and computer keyboards.

SKILL IN:

  • interviewing.
ABILITY TO:

  • learn the methods, practices and techniques of interviewing.
  • learn the principles and techniques of employment counseling.
  • learn the methods and techniques of preparing employment resumes.
  • learn the sources of employment and vocational training in the metropolitan area.
  • learn regulations regarding specific clerical procedures.
  • identify job skills, interests, abilities, and educational background and work history through the process of interviewing.
  • identify client training needs.
  • maintain departmental clerical records and prepare report forms.
  • keep records of clients and to track them through the employment seeking and interview process.
  • speak, read, and write in a specified foreign language and the English language fluently for the bilingual specialty.
  • work effectively with clients, employers and contracted trainers.
  • present ideas effectively both orally and in writing.
  • follow written and oral instructions.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/89.)

BOTH SPECIALTIES

  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • One year of paid experience performing routine clerical tasks in an agency designed to resolve social or employment problems.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

BILINGUAL SPECIALTY

  • Sufficient training and/or experience to be able to understand and speak fluently and clearly in English and a designated foreign language.
PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
