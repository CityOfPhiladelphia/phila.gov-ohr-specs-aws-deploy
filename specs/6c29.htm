LAW ENFORCEMENT ANALYST MANAGER

 

 GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is crime information management and intelligence analysis work at the managerial level directing law enforcement analysis activities through subordinate supervisors for the Philadelphia Police Department. Employees in this class have responsibility for planning and designing analytical projects and directing employees researching, collecting data and performing intelligence analysis using law enforcement information systems to monitor trends, patterns, and changes in crime, and demographic factors, which affect quality and delivery of service to the community and other City agencies. Duties include the responsibility for the management, design, implementation and maintenance of the information systems necessary for analysts to perform their work. Work is performed under the supervision of an administrative superior. Work includes correlating, integrating, and evaluating varying types of information from criminal or operational activities for the purpose of generating strategic, operational, tactical, or administrative level analysis reports. Work includes utilizing computer hardware and software for reporting and analysis.  

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS:

 

1.    Positions in this class must direct, through subordinate supervisors, the daily activities of Law Enforcement Analysts for an assigned section of the Intelligence Bureau.

2.    Positions in this class must be in the Philadelphia Police Department’s Intelligence Bureau.

3.    No more than 3 positions will be allocated to this class.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

Directs, through subordinate supervisors, analysts performing intelligence analysis using crime, economic and demographic data to project crime trends and patterns; plans, delegates and prioritizes work for a unit of analysts and support staff; sets deadlines and performance metrics; monitors production standards and capabilities and ensures the maintenance of adequate quality and quantity of work; may direct the 24-hour Real-Time Crime Center’s (RTCC) operations through the monitoring of 911 calls, radio, crime tips, and other sources.

Reviews and researches established policies of funding sources to determine the impact on funding, operations and information systems; may prepare formal reply to funding source providing comments and recommendations; clarifies and translates state policy into operational procedures; monitors data collection and information reporting methods to ensure that state and/or federal requirements are being met by the office and provider agency staff.

Designs analysis projects in such areas as police service delivery, information collection, information management, and information validation; and directs extensive data analysis using quantitative or and qualitative methods; collaborates with high-level management from the department and other government organizations to share information or coordinate intelligence & research activities.

Reviews existing databases and management information systems to determine if unit goals as well as state reporting requirements are met; determines if informational needs are being satisfied.

Directs the design or modification of current and new information systems and programs based on operational needs.

Prepares reports of findings which include written and graphic descriptions; provides training for staff in conjunction with operational changes; develops manuals, handbooks and other instructional material as needed; acts as departmental expert within the department on issues which impact intelligence analysis, research, evaluation and information management; attends conferences and reviews periodicals in the field to direct the implementation of new techniques in program analysis and evaluation.

Researches grant opportunities and prepares grant proposals for the funding of intelligence analysis and information management projects; coordinates with other unit managers to determine appropriate budget amounts.

Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         supervisory principles, methods and practices

·         standard sources and uses of information in law enforcement

·         application of qualitative and quantitative analysis techniques to advance departmental strategy for intelligence analysis

·         law enforcement and statistical research methodology

·         City’s budgeting processes and procedures

·         principles, practices and techniques of project planning and management

·         principles, methods and techniques for research, analysis and data collection

·         design and modification of computer-based information systems used for data gathering, organization, and analysis

·         the principles and practices of utilizing computers for management reporting and analysis

·         information system resources needed to design computerized data entry

·         query languages, report writers, interactive database programs, spreadsheets and/or application generators or other software as necessary

·         database management, geospatial software, and software packages to manage data and produce tables, maps, and graphic displays

 

SKILL IN:

 

·         managing intelligence analysis functions and staff

·         using analytical techniques and scientific principles to evaluate data

·         reviewing a variety of narrative reports

·         directing staff performing crime analysis using economic and demographic data

·         reviewing crime trends and patterns


ABILITY TO:

 

·         direct the activities of a staff of subordinates

·         plan, direct and implement programs

·         prepare grant proposals and develop policy

·         write a variety of narrative reports

·         prioritize work and meet deadlines

·         process and analyze data using query languages, report writers, interactive database programs, spreadsheets and/or application generators

·         direct qualitative studies for intelligence analysis

·         express ideas and facts clearly and effectively both orally and in writing

·         establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, management, federal and state agency partners, governmental representatives, and the general public

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/21.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Completion of a bachelor’s degree program at an accredited college or university in a closely related field.

 

AND

 

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

Four years of experience performing criminal investigative research or information management and analysis work for a law enforcement agency, intelligence agency, government or nonprofit organization, research institution, or an industry-related private sector consulting firm, two years of which have been at the full-performance level.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

            Two years of experience as Law Enforcement Analyst Supervisor.

 

NOTE:           Possession of a master's degree in one of the fields outlined above or a closely related social science field may substitute for one year of the general experience requirement.

 

OR

 

Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources which has included the completion of a bachelor’s degree at an accredited college or university as an educational minimum and the specific experience.          

 

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

 

            Must be able to pass, prior to appointment, a local, state and national criminal background check including a fingerprint-based check as required by the Federal Bureau of Investigation's (FBI) Criminal Justice Information System (CJIS) Security Policy.
