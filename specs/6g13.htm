CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

6G13
COMMERCIAL AND INDUSTRIAL FIRE INSPECTION SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative and technical work supervising employees engaged in the city-wide inspection of commercial and industrial buildings for compliance with fire prevention codes and related ordinances, regulations and standards. Contacts with owners, building managers, realtors, tenants and contractors in the resolution of code interpretation and application in order to obtain compliance are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the general supervision of an administrative manager.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • The positions must supervise employees engaged in the city-wide inspection of commercial and industrial buildings for compliance with fire prevention codes and related, ordinance, regulations and standards.
  • The positions must be located in the Department of Licenses and Inspections.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Supervises lower-level inspectional personnel engaged in fire prevention code compliance activities; meets with building owners, managers and contractors to resolve problems pertaining to the elimination of potential or actual violations; arranges time schedules for elimination of violations; attempts to persuade owners and managers to make beneficial changes to satisfy the minimum requirements of the fire prevention code; conducts administrative hearings with violators; coordinates inspections with intra and interdepartmental units, and other governmental agencies.
  • Analyzes operations to assure that work is being performed in accordance with prescribed policies and practices; prepares materials for non-compliance prosecution and appears in court as a witness for the department; prepares pertinent written and computerized reports and records; recommends revision of policies and procedures.
  • Supervises technical personnel engaged in the review of applications for fire service permits; interprets fire prevention code for the requirement of the permit/license and insures that all prerequisite licenses and certifications have been received prior to the issuance of the permit; directs and may perform special inspections for conformance to the Philadelphia Prevention codes, reference codes such as the Building Officials and Code Administrators (BOCA), and standards such as the National Fire Protection Association Standards.
  • Assigns the work to a staff of inspectors engaged in inspecting commercial and industrial buildings and sites for compliance with the fire prevention and related codes; reviews completed inspection reports for accuracy and validity of conclusions; discusses questionable cases with inspectors; advises these subordinates on the more difficult applications of the pertinent codes and ordinances; refers questionable cases to supervisor.
  • Supervises clerical personnel maintaining and maintains records of fire protection systems which have been tested and certified by approved fire protection system contractors; assigns programmed hazardous materials site inspections and performs inspections for commercial and industrial properties containing hazardous materials; assigns and performs inspections of above-ground and underground storage tanks; provides inspectors pertinent information concerning hazardous materials sites including previous inspection reports and lists of chemicals and substances used or stored; reviews inspection reports for violations and recommends remedial action; contacts property owners/managers to obtain compliance.
  • Trains and instructs lower level personnel in the proper procedures necessary to perform hazardous materials sites inspections; participates as a responder and information resource to incidents of chemical releases and spills; attends training sessions on fire prevention codes and related issues.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the fire prevention codes, ordinances, regulations and standards as they apply to the inspection of commercial and industrial establishments.
  • inspectional methods and techniques.
  • the accepted standards of fire safety as they apply to commercial and industrial establishments as promulgated by the National Fire Protection Association, National board of Fire Underwriters, Building Officials and Code Administrators (BOCA) and similar associations.
  • the provisions of federal, state and local laws, regulations and codes pertaining to the handling and the safety of hazardous materials.
  • basic industrial processes and practices with emphasis on the handling and storage of hazardous chemicals, flammable or combustible liquids or solids, corrosive liquids, oxidizing materials or toxic substances.

SKILL IN:

  • recognizing deviations from the provision of applicable fire prevention codes, ordinances, regulations and standards.
  • obtaining information by interview, observation and investigation.

ABILITY TO:

  • direct the work of lower-level staff.
  • enforce codes, ordinances and regulations firmly, tactfully, and impartially.
  • prepare reports and maintain routine records.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and the public.
  • identify hazardous material substances and enforce recommendations for proper handling, storage and use.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/97.)

    EDUCATION:
  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

AND

    GENERAL EXPERIENCE:
  • Three years of field inspectional or investigational experience.

AND

    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:
  • Two years of experience at the full performance level enforcing provisions of the fire prevention codes as they relate to commercial and industrial establishments.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Certification by the Environmental Protection Agency at the First Responder Operations Level prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Commercial and Industrial Fire Inspection Supervisor.
  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to and during tenure of employment in the Commercial and Industrial Fire Inspection Supervisor class.
