7B01
WATER OPERATIONS REPAIR HELPER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is semiskilled work assisting a higher level repair worker in various aspects of water operations repair and maintenance. An employee in this class assists in the performance of first-line maintenance and repair of segments of city-wide water operations systems. Work involves conditions which can be only partially controlled, and strenuous physical effort must be exerted in the performance of duties. Employees in this class may be required to work evenings, weekends, and holidays as assignments require. Work is performed under the close supervision of a technical supervisor.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

Breaks street surfaces with a pneumatic drill; spades dirt with a shovel to reach damaged water distribution area and clears the damaged area of all obstacles; hands repair worker needed tools, supplies and materials and assists him or her as necessary during the actual repair work to receive the training required to qualify for water operations repair worker or an equivalent class; backfills holes with dirt and tamps for levelness; sweeps work area with a broom and assists in the loading and unloading of all supplies, materials and equipment; may notify customers of possible shutdowns or disruption of service.

Enters interceptor chambers, siphon valve chambers, wet wells and the attached sewers under the direction of a supervisor or lead worker; assists in the performance of maintenance and repairs; removes debris, greases fittings, and lubricates control devices.

Learns to uses hand tools and power equipment to make repairs such as replacing broken chains, adjusting tide gates and breaking up large items blocking wastewater flow; assists in the use of manual, hydraulic, and mechanically operated sewer and wet well cleaning equipment.

Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

    KNOWLEDGE OF:

    • The care and use of tools used in the work, such as pneumatic drills
    • the hazards and safety precautions involved in the work.

    SKILL IN:

    • the care and use of tools used in the work, including the pneumatic drill.

    ABILITY TO:

    • perform tasks requiring mechanical aptitude
    • learn and understand the hazards involved and safety measures employed in performing water operations maintenance and repair work
    • understand and follow oral instruction
    • perform heavy manual labor often under adverse climatic conditions and for extended periods of time.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 10/08.)

    EDUCATION

    Education equivalent to the completion of the eighth school grade.

    AND
    GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

    One year of recent field laboring experience.

    Note: Completion of a City of Philadelphia approved training program in the use of small hand tools and the operation of the pneumatic drill may be substituted for the one year of laboring experience requirement.

    OR

    Any equivalent combination of acceptable education and experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

Ability to meet approved minimal physical and medical standards.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Water Distribution Repair Helper.
