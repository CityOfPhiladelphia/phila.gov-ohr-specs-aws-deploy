CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

5B28
JUVENILE JUSTICE PLANNING AND RESEARCH SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory youth service work in the research, planning and development of systems, policies and procedures relating to the juvenile justice system. The employee in this class participates in the development of goals and plans for the Juvenile Justice Services Division and studies and evaluates pre-trial and post-adjudication commitment and placement practices and policies for delinquent youth. Supervision of lower level analysts and contact with representatives of other agencies involved in juvenile justice administration including the courts, the prosecutor's office and state and federal juvenile justice and human service agencies are major aspects of the work. The employee reports directly to the Executive Director, Juvenile Justice Services Division.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. This is a one position class on the Juvenile Justice Services Division, Department of Human Services.

  2. The employee must supervise lower level analysts.

  3. The employee must be involved in strategic planning, research and analysis for the Juvenile Justice Services Division.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans and develops goals, policies and procedures relating to juvenile justice administration for the City's Human Services agency; evaluates practices of related agencies as they impact on department resources and funding; recommends strategies to enhance inter-agency cooperation and implement measures to increase effectiveness and improve quality of programs; studies and evaluates pre-trial and post-adjudication commitment and placement practices and policies; recommends modifications to existing operations.
  • Participates in the preparation of the Division's annual report and development of the Division's three year plan, and annual updates to the plan, for the state; researches and prepares reports on program initiatives; compiles supporting data and statistics to be included in plan; analyses and develops budget costs for implementation of programs and new approaches dealing with delinquent youths; prepares justifications for new programs for appropriate funding and regulatory agencies and associated juvenile justice entities.
  • Reviews pending and proposed legislation affecting juvenile justice system administration and evaluates impact on agency operations;
  • Works with departmental staff to develop and implement automated information systems pertaining to financial and client service tracking for delinquent youth; determine database and reporting needs; consults with Division managers to implement reporting methods and techniques.
  • Serves on appropriate committees to review and coordinate implementation of program proposals; participates in developing program budgets, locating funding sources and drafting applications for funding.
  • Supervises a staff of analysts engaged in performing program monitoring, research and evaluation.
  • Meets with officials and representatives of other agencies including the courts, prosecutor's office, state and federal juvenile justice and human service agencies to discuss, interpret and confer on agency policies, procedures and requirements.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and common practices of the administration of a large juvenile justice system.
  • the principles, practices, and techniques of planning especially as they apply to the operation of a large juvenile justice system.
  • current literature, research, and developments in the area of criminal justice administration, especially as they apply to juvenile justice administration.
  • local community, civic, and social organizations and governmental agencies concerned with juvenile justice administration.
  • report writing principles and practices.

ABILITY TO:

  • carry out assignments requiring obtaining of facts, organization of material, and evaluation of data obtained in the preparation of recommendations.
  • quickly assimilate and comprehend the functions, programs and goals of a large human services agency.
  • serve effectively in a liaison and coordinating capacity between a large human services agency and local, state, and federal agencies concerned with juvenile justice administration.
  • prepare and present comprehensive written and oral reports and speeches.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with representatives of city, state, and federal agencies concerned with juvenile justice administration as well as interested private groups and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 6/91.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a master's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in social work, social work administration, criminal justice, public administration or a related field.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE
  • Two years of experience in the youth services field.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:
  • Two years of either program analysis experience at the full performance level in the area of juvenile justice services or administrative experience involving the care and custody of delinquent youth.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which includes the specific experience as indicated and a master's degree as an educational minimum

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
