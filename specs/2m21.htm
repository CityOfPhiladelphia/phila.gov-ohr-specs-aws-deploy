HEALTH FACILITIES AND SUPPORT SERVICES DIRECTOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is facilities management work directing, through subordinate supervisors, the provision of all facilities maintenance and related support services for the Health Department. The employee in this class is responsible for directing a building maintenance and repairs program, building security and custodial services, groundskeeping, related capital program projects implementation, and the provision of other administrative support services for the Department's public buildings and facilities. Work includes such support services as capital budget preparation, implementation and administration, departmental telecommunications services, vehicle fleet operation, messenger services, and directing and monitoring the activities of outside consultants and contractors engaged in capital improvement programs for the department's public buildings and facilities. Coordinating a large diversified maintenance and administrative staff, providing efficient maintenance support to the Philadelphia Nursing Home (PNH) facility's 24-hour a day operation, and complying with the State life safety codes and licensing requirements are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of a Deputy Health Commissioner.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. This one position class must be in the Health Department and report to a Deputy Health Commissioner.
  2. This position must be responsible for all maintenance and capital improvements for the public facilities in the Health Department, including the Medical Examiner's Office and PNH.
  3. This position must be responsible for ensuring that the PNH facilities comply with the life safety codes and licensing regulations.
  4. This position must be responsible for the implementation of the capital budget program for the Department's facilities.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Collects and compiles data for capital budget preparation; meets with division heads to determine capital program needs; inspects facilities to ascertain capital program needs and develop projections; prepares budget figures and justifications; provides information and data to administrative officials concerning budget requests and figures; prepares reports and summaries detailing capital program expenditures.
  • Directs the department's building maintenance and repair program; tours facilities to ascertain the general state of repair and determine where repairs are most needed; refers major construction or renovation of existing facilities to Public Property for appropriateness of design, location, and effectiveness; establishes priorities on repair projects; confers with contractors to resolve problems; monitors progress of construction projects; submits budget modification requests as necessary.
  • Ensures that the facilities operation at the nursing home runs smoothly and uninterrupted; ascertains the major renovations and repairs needed in the nursing home; oversees the operation of the power plant; directs through subordinate supervisors the procedures for compliance with nursing home codes and regulations; prepares the facilities for inspections by the state licensing agencies.
  • Assigns analysis and planning studies of departmental operations; directs information management studies to determine the feasibility, cost, and effectiveness of telecommunication and management information systems; directs the department's energy conservation program; implements sufficient security measures for the department's facilities.
  • Directs, through subordinate supervisors, the provision of custodial services to health centers and other departmental installations on a continuous basis; dispatches custodial crews to various facilities on an emergency basis.
  • Confers with division heads and other departmental officials to assess priorities and devise appropriate schedules to perform building services; resolves problems between departmental personnel and subordinates.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of administrative and process management and their application to the resolution of a variety of operational and administrative problems inherent in facilities management or building maintenance activities.
  • maintenance and scheduling procedures essential to the upkeep of large buildings, groups of buildings, and other structural facilities.
  • the principles and practices of administrative and managerial analysis as applied to the management and maintenance of a complex of building facilities.
  • the principles and practices of public finance as applied to the preparation of the capital budget for a building maintenance or facilities management operation.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
  • security measures and techniques to protect facilities.
  • the city and state laws and regulations applicable to the maintenance of public buildings.
  • the city and state life safety codes and regulations for maintenance and repair of nursing home facilities.
  • current developments in the field of building maintenance.
SKILL IN:

  • the preparation and implementation of a capital budget program.
  • process and administrative analysis as it pertains to facilities management.
  • dealing with representatives of other city departments in order to expedite materials and services required.
  • working with outside contractors, architects and builders to facilitate projects.
ABILITY TO:

  • plan, organize, and direct the activities of a multi-disciplined staff engaged in a large scale facilities maintenance and management program.
  • evaluate quality and effectiveness of selective administrative functions.
  • coordinate administrative functions with facilities management activities to provide maximum efficiency of building operations.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, vendors, representatives of other departments and the general public.
  • learn departmental operations and to preside over related administrative functions to meet the needs of the department.
  • keep abreast of new developments and regulations with regards to nursing home facilities.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/91.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major coursework in business management, public administration, organizational management or mechanical engineering.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE
  • Four years providing administrative, technical or operational analysis, or supervising a building maintenance or facilities management operation.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE
  • Two years supervising a building maintenance program through subordinate supervisors for a multi-facilities complex that has included some aspect of capital budget preparation, analysis and implementation.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which includes a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
