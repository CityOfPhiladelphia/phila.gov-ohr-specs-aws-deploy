CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

1D06
DATA ENTRY SECTION MANAGER


GENERAL DEFINITION

This is second supervisory level work directing the activities of a large staff of data entry personnel engaged in converting alpha numeric data to computer-readable form on a computer terminal in a computing operations support environment. The employee in this class is responsible for prioritizing and scheduling data entry work, establishing and maintaining timetables and deadlines for completion of work, and determining staffing requirements. Work includes monitoring production levels, and devising ways to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of data entry operations. Meeting with users and systems development staff to set priorities and deadlines, to determine data formats, and to process project elements is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the general direction of a technical superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The position must direct through subordinate supervisors employees performing off line key to disc, key to tape and on line data entry in a computing operation or support environment.


TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, assigns and reviews, through subordinate supervisors, the work of a large staff of data entry personnel; receives and reviews requests from users for data entry work; confers with users to determine priorities and deadlines; schedules work to optimize production and use of equipment; distributes and/or reassigns work among units to meet deadlines; determines staffing levels necessary to complete projected work; authorizes hiring of temporary employees and makes unit assignments.
  • Confers with systems development personnel and end users to determine source document and data entry form design to facilitate data capture and data entry operations; reviews source documents to determine allocation of entry fields to increase efficiency of data entry process; designs data entry formats and programs to capture desired data; instructs subordinate unit supervisors in proper data entry machine programming to record data according to format.
  • Meets with data processing personnel and reviews proposed projects to determine data entry needs of projects and to integrate data entry elements with other project elements; maintains contact with users to anticipate new assignments and maintain production schedules.
  • Directs the maintenance of document control systems, tape status logs, unit production records, project time records and data entry procedures manuals; monitors and reports on computer downtime and repair costs; reviews new data entry equipment and software, and recommends purchase; orders and maintains supplies of blank data entry cards, magnetic tapes and other necessary materials.
  • Performs related work as required.


REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods, standards, practices and procedures of data entry operations as an integral part of data processing operations.
  • principles and methods used to obtain maximum use of data entry equipment and most efficient use of personnel.
  • supervisory principles, practices and techniques, especially as they apply to high volume, clerical transactions.
  • current developments in data entry practices and equipment.
  • the capabilities and applications of data processing equipment.
  • general office practices and procedures.

ABILITY TO:

  • analyze work trends and to predict workload.
  • analyze work requests and determine operator hours required.
  • schedule work and adhere to deadlines.
  • analyze production levels and other statistics and apply them to suggest improvements in operations.
  • design data entry forms and formats for maximum efficiency in data capture.
  • communicate effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish effective working relationships with subordinates, associates and user departments.


MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/88.)

EDUCATION

  • Education equivalent to the completion of the twelfth school grade.

AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Three years of experience operating alpha numeric data entry machines.

AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

  • Two years of experience supervising employees performing off-line key to disc, key to tape and on line data entry in a computing operations or support environment.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

NOTE:
Completion of an Associate's Degree with major course work in Data Processing may be substituted for up to two years of the general experience required.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
