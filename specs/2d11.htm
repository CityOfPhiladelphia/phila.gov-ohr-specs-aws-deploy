CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2D11
PERSONAL PROPERTY ASSESSOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is responsible field and office work assessing personal property for purposes of taxation. An employee in this class supervises a lower level assessor and participates in determining the value of personal property for all accounts within an assigned geographic district.

Work is performed under the general supervision of a personal property assessing supervisor. Assignments are stable in nature and are carried out in accordance with accepted assessing standards and applicable departmental regulations. Employees in this class participate in carrying out the total personal property assessment program.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs and participates in preparing personal property tax forms for an assigned district of the city; approves the deletion of inactive accounts or accounts terminated because of sale of personal property or death of taxpayer; supervises the mailing of flyers to persons newly arrived in the district or to other potential new accounts.
  • Reviews returns to determine taxability and assessed value of personal property reported; determines necessity for calling taxpayers for additional information; prepares estimated assessment for non-filers; confers with taxpayers, their attorneys, accountants, and banking institution representatives; makes field investigations as required; supervises a lower level assessor engaged in reviewing returns, determining assessed value of personal property, and related duties.
  • Prepares notices to taxpayers whose accounts are overdue; maintains records of number of accounts in district and adds and deletes accounts throughout the years based on information supplied by the Register of Wills, Prothonotary, Records Department, or other official agencies.
  • Confers with members of brokerage houses to determine taxability and value of stocks and bonds and with mortgage companies to determine the value of mortgages; submits periodic statistical reports of accounts added, deleted, or transferred in the assigned district.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices, and methods, and techniques of personal property evaluation for assessment purposes.
  • the legal requirements covering the assessment of personal property under Pennsylvania Law.
  • the sources and interpretation of information used for determining the value of stocks and bonds for assessment purposes.
  • the types of personal property commonly exempt from taxation.
ABILITY TO:

  • supervise the work of a lower level assessor engaged in maintaining accounts, determining the value of personal property, and in related duties.
  • determine the factors influencing the value of personal property for taxation purposes.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with taxpayers, their representa- tives, associates, and the public.
  • make arithmetic calculations accurately and quickly.
  • maintain records and prepare reports.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/67.)

  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • Four years experience participating in the assessment of personal property for taxation purposes which has included the calculation of the amount of personal property tax due.
Or any equivalent combination of acceptable training and experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
