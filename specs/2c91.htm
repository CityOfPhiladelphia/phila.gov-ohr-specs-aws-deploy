HUMAN SERVICES SUPPORT SERVICES ADMINISTRATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative work directing various administrative support services functions, through subordinate supervisors, for the Human Services Department. The employee in this class directs and coordinates the provision of specific support functions, including data processing, revenue generation, accounts payable, information processing and records maintenance. Work includes planning and developing projects and programs designed to report on and forecast agency activities, functions and projections. Supervising, through subordinate supervisors, a large staff of professional, technical and clerical employees and establishing effective working relationships with agency administrators, other City officials and vendors are significant aspects of the work. The employee works under the direction of a Deputy Commissioner.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • the position must be located in the Human Services Department.
  • the position must be responsible for various administrative support functions, including data processing, revenue generation, accounts payable, information processing and records maintenance.
  • this is a one-position class.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs, through a subordinate supervisor, the administrative aspects of coordinating, designing, developing and implementing various automated data processing systems and programs; confers with the data processing supervisor in prioritizing and coordinating computer services projects; meets with departmental operations personnel to elicit their automated needs and concerns; works in attempting to meet the automated needs of operational staff.
  • Directs, through a subordinate supervisor, the processing of monthly payments to contracted services and dependent and delinquent youth payments to foster parents; develops and maintains agency financial systems in accordance with City and state accounting requirements; interacts with foster parents and agencies involving payments and funding levels; prepares monthly expenditure reports for agency administrators specifying expenditures to date, work in process and projected expenditures; reviews annual and special audits of agency operations and finances; compiles information and prepares reports to be used in agency response to audits.
  • Directs, through a subordinate supervisor, the units designated to be a district office of the Commonwealth's County Assistance Board and responsible for determining eligibility of social services for outside/non-City funding, including medical assistance, federal IV-E, supplemental security income, social security and parental support; ensures eligibility determinations comply with federal and state eligibility guidelines; performs on-line updates of both state and City medical assistance electronic data bases; distributes medical assistance cards; reviews medical bills to ensure maximum third party payments; directs, through a subordinate supervisor, the preparation of various reimbursement invoices including Title IV-E and medicaid services.
  • Directs, through a subordinate supervisor, the management of case records for the agency; clears, opens and closes case records; notifies social service staff of upcoming court appearances for children in their caseloads; interacts with outside callers requesting information on cases and worker assignments.
  • Directs, through a subordinate supervisor, the unit responsible for typing/word processing of materials for case records, data input of updates to management information system, and maintenance of the department's voice mail and digital dictation systems.
  • Monitors the operations of subordinate units to ensure they are at optimum performance; implements procedural and process changes in subordinate units to improve efficiency; organizes work groups for cross-communication purposes.
  • Participates in special projects with other high-level managers/administrators on the implementation of departmental policy and processes,the formulation of department strategy and the resolution of specific problems.
  • Works with contracted agencies, as assigned, in developing systems and processes with the objective of improving and enhancing administrative efficiency.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of public administration.
  • administrative and managerial practices, policies and procedures.
  • federal and state public assistance eligibility requirements.
  • modern management techniques, processes and procedures.

SKILL IN:

  • effectively delegating assignments in consideration of staff capabilities and structure.
  • problem-solving among organizational levels to achieve mutual objectives.
  • effectively communicating ideas clearly and concisely, both orally and in writing.

ABILITY TO:

  • exercise sound judgment in administering, reviewing and evaluating subordinate operations.
  • analyze and evaluate complex administrative and managerial problems.
  • evaluate the quality and effectiveness of administrative functions.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, City officials and vendors.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/94.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in business administration or accounting.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE
  • Three years of administrative experience.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE
  • Two years of experience supervising an administrative support function for a large organization.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
