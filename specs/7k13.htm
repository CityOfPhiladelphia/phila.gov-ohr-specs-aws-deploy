CABLE SPLICING GROUP LEADER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory and skilled cable splicing installation and maintenance work. An employee supervises crews of tradesman and helpers engaged in the installation, maintenance and repair of city-owned underground multiple conductor cables. Work also entails supervising tradesmen and helpers engaged in minor maintenance and temporary repair work of overhead cables directly servicing major departmental emergency communications systems. An employee participates in the more complex and difficult phases of job assignments.

Work is performed under the general supervision of a technical superior. Assignments require evaluative thinking and are performed in accordance with accepted cable splicing standards and applicable electrical regulations. The employee has controlling responsibility for the completion of assigned tasks in providing departmental services for other city agencies on a city-wide basis. Working conditions involve some disagreeable aspects and light physical effort.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Makes estimates of time and materials on work assignments; orders required materials and supplies; determines work procedures and assigns work to a moderate-sized group of tradesmen and helpers, reviews work in progress and upon completion for conformance to job requests and standard trade practices; drives to various work locations to spot-check work in progress and upon completion for conformance to work standards; provides technical advice for the more difficult phases of work assignments and ascertains that work is performed under safe working conditions.
  • Lays, rods, splices and wipes cables in accordance with accepted trade methods; connects cables in junction boxes or terminal racks; erects or removes fire and police box poles and shell casings using a jack hammer, digging bars and shovels and runs and connects terminal cables to such shell casings.
  • Issues materials and supplies to tradesmen and helpers for work assignments.
  • Makes required reports.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods, materials, tools, practices and testing instruments of the cable splicer trade.
  • the occupational hazards and safety precautions of the cable splicer trade.
  • the principles and theory of electricity.

SKILL IN:

  • the wiping of lead or plastic sleeve joints.
  • the care and use of tools, materials, equipment and testing instruments of the cable splicer trade.

ABILITY TO:

  • assign and review the work of a moderate-sized group of tradesmen and helpers.
  • test, diagnose and locate and resolve problems encountered in the installation and maintenance of multiple conductor cables.
  • make estimates of time, labor and materials.
  • work from plans and maps.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.)
  • Education equivalent to completion of the tenth school grade.

  • Six years of experience in the electrical trade, two years of which have been at the journeyman-level in the laying, roding, splicing and wiping underground multiple conductor cables.

Or any equivalent combination of acceptable training and experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to and during tenure of employment as a Cable Splicer Group Leader.
