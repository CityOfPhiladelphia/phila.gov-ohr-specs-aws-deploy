OVERHEAD ELECTRICAL LINE GROUP LEADER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is line work at the group leader level supervising a small crew of linemen and helpers engaged in the installation, maintenance and repair of city-owned overhead communication cables and wires.

Work is performed under the general supervision of an administrative superior. Assignments call for analytical thinking and work is performed in accordance with standard trade practices and general work instructions. The employee participates with other trades group leader in carrying out the various phases of a city-wide communication program.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, assigns and supervises the work of a small crew of full performance linemen and helpers engaged in the installation, maintenance and repair of city-owned overhead communication cables and wires.
  • Accompanies line crew to work locations, inspects job sites and assigns individual crew members specific tasks; reviews work in process and advises crew members on the steps to employ in resolving the most difficult phases of line work, particularly as it applies to the installation of cables and wires for police and fire boxes and the arrangements of electrical equipment to be employed for special events; reviews completed work assignments for adherence to work standards.
  • Instructs subordinate crew members on the safety precautions to be followed in the performance of work assignments; visually examines safety equipment before it is issued to crew members for use.
  • Keeps daily records of time, equipment and materials expended on work assignments.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the standard tools, materials, testing devices and methods essential to the maintenance and repair of overhead wire systems and appurtenances.
  • the occupational hazards and standard safety devices of the lineman's trade.
  • the principles and theory of electricity.

SKILL IN:

  • the use of pliers, knives, wrenches, connectors, test sets, jumpers, climbing spurs, safety belts and other lineman's tools and equipment.

ABILITY TO:

  • locate and determine causes of malfunctioning in overhead electrical wire systems and to instruct others in the complex repair assignments.
  • supervise and instruct full performance linemen and helpers.
  • supervise and use prescribed safety equipment.
  • work from and interpret sketches and plans.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.)
  • Education equipment to completion of the twelfth school grade.

  • Six years of experience in the lineman trade, two years of which has been at the full performance level.

Or any equivalent combination of acceptable training and experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to and during tenure of employment as a Overhead Electrical Line Group Leader.
