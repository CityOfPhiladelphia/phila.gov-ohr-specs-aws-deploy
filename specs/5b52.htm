CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

5B52
JUVENILE JUSTICE COMMUNITY SERVICES DIRECTOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative youth services work directing, through subordinate supervisors, community based detention services, disposition planning, shared case management, program monitoring, and prevention program development for the Division of Juvenile Justice Services. The employee in this class is responsible for planning, developing and evaluating a comprehensive plan for specialized residential social service programs to serve the needs of delinquent youths after adjudication by the courts. Work includes participating in the development of unit goals and objectives and evaluating program proposals submitted by service providers to ensure conformance to established goals and objectives. Contact with court officials and service providers is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the direction of the Executive Director, Juvenile Justice Services Division.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • This is a one position class located in the Division of Juvenile Justice Services of the Department of Human Services.
  • The employee must direct, through a subordinate administrator, community-based detention programs for delinquent youths.
  • The employee must direct, through subordinate supervisors, disposition planning, shared case management, program monitoring, and prevention program development.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs, through a subordinate administrator, a staff of social workers and counselors engaged in providing emergency placement, custody, and counseling services to clients in group, foster, or in-home detention programs.
  • Directs, through subordinate supervisors, the program monitoring, prevention program development, disposition planning and shared case management functions for the Division of Juvenile Justice Services; ensures that federal and state financial reimbursement applications are completed in a timely manner; plans, organizes and directs program operations; reviews reports on program progress and evaluates conformance to established standards.
  • Plans, develops, administers, and evaluates a comprehensive service plan for post-adjudication residential care to serve the needs of delinquent youths; confers with probation officers and social service agency officials to identify the needs of youths; evaluates program proposals submitted by service providers; establishes performance standards for contracted agencies; establishes methods to evaluate contracted services; establishes procedures to inform judges of all new services available in the juvenile justice system.
  • Directs a support community outreach program providing primary delinquent prevention services, on a city-wide basis; ensures the support and coordination of various community agencies as part of the program.
  • Directs the preparation of reports on juvenile justice service programs; analyzes reports to ensure adherence to performance standards as well as conformance to established goals and objectives.
  • Administers contracts for juvenile justice residential care services; monitors and evaluates the utilization of contracted services; determines trends in the utilization of services; recommends the amendment of contracts based on utilization analysis; makes recommendations for future budgetary allocations based on past utilization of contracted services; directs the monitoring and evaluation of provider agencies, ensuring adherence to contracted terms and conditions and analyzing service trends .
  • Meets with service providers on a quarterly basis to plan and coordinate services for delinquent youths; participates in the formulation of youth program objectives and division policies and procedures; participates in the development of section operating budget.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of social service administration especially as applied to the care and custody of delinquent youths and youths with family or emotional problems.
  • all phases of modern detention and protective care methods related to the field of temporary youth care.
  • the community and social resources available for delinquent youths.
  • social and emotional factors which result in the institutionalization of youths.
  • the principles, practices, methods, techniques, and current developments in the field of delinquent youth care.
  • the organization and operation of the juvenile justice system.
  • the principles, practices, and procedures applicable to youth service program evaluation.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.

ABILITY TO:

  • direct, through subordinate supervisors, the activities of a staff engaged in performing a variety of activities related to the provision of social services to adolescents and delinquent youths.
  • determine the capability of prospective service providers to deliver proposed services.
  • evaluate program quality and effectiveness.
  • develop a comprehensive service plan to address the needs of delinquent youths after adjudication by the courts.
  • administer and analyze contracts for juvenile justice services.
  • prepare and analyze reports pertaining to juvenile justice programs.
  • express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, service providers, and representatives of city and state agencies concerned with juvenile justice administration.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 2/94.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a master's degree program at an accredited college or university, with major course work in social work, sociology, counseling, or psychology.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE
  • Three years of youth counseling or social work experience working with youths.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE
  • Four years of experience in a supervisory capacity, two years of which have been administering, through subordinate supervisors, the provision of emergency placement, custody, and counseling services to youths in group, foster or in-home detention programs, or administering a support community outreach program and one year administering contracts including program development and implementation.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which includes the specific experience and possession of a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
