CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

7C33
POWER RODDER OPERATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is skilled work in the operation and maintenance of power rodding equipment to install new cable in and to clear obstructions from underground communication systems conduits. Work is performed under the supervision of a technical superior, involves conditions which can only be partially controlled, and requires moderate to heavy physical effort.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Drives a two and one-half ton power rodder truck or a two and one-half ton truck with cable reel trailer to job site; maneuvers truck and/or trailer into position over adjacent manholes; sets up warning flags and flasher to alert passing motorist.
  • Enters manholes; inspects manholes for cave-ins, weak or broken ladders, leaks and other dangerous conditions; operates hydraulically-driven submersible pump to drain flooded manholes; erects temporary patchwork of wooden plants to shore up minor leaks; notifies appropriate department of location and nature of conditions requiring repair.
  • Attaches rod lead-in hose to underground conduit and to power rodding apparatus; manipulates control lever to actuate lateral movement of rod through duct; pushes lever to rotate rod to clear blockages; controls reverse movement of rod to pull cable through conduit; monitors hydraulic pressure gauges during rodding operation and adjusts to optimum pressure by turning control knobs.
  • Attaches specialized bit to tip of rod with wrench; re-activates rod to remove obstructions in conduit such as tree roots, rocks, etc.
  • Manipulates control levers on cable trailer to feed cable into adjacent manhole; attaches cable to free end of rod; pushes levers to keep cable slack while it is pulled through the duct; manually rods short sections of conduit.
  • Checks air in tires and fluid levels daily on truck, rodder and cable trailer; performs first echelon maintenance and makes minor running repairs.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the component parts and operating principles of hydraulic power rodding equipment and cable reel trailer.
  • the general operating and maintenance principles of automotive equipment.
  • the occupational hazards and safety precautions involved in underground rodding and cable pulling work.
  • the traffic laws of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania.

SKILL IN:

  • the safe and efficient operation of power rodding and cable pulling equipment.

ABILITY TO:

  • service power rodding and related equipment and to make minor running repairs and adjustments.
  • understand and carry out written and oral instructions.
  • perform heavy manual labor for prolonged periods under adverse environmental conditions.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/89.)
    EDUCATION:
  • Education equivalent to the completion of the tenth school grade.

    AND

    GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

  • Two years of experience in the installation and maintenance of underground communications system cable.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to the scheduled examination and during tenure of employment in the class.
