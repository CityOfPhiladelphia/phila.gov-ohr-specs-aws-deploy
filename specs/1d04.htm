CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

1D04
DATA ENTRY SUPERVISOR


GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory level data entry work. An employee in this class is responsible for supervising a group of data entry key-to-disc machine operators in the rapid and accurate production of work within time frames established by the departments' unit manager. An employee in this class attends meetings with users and programmers in which they make recommendations regarding program control designs and data storage strategies. Work includes creating and modifying program control formats for data entry and storage computer systems. Performing data entry assignments during peak load periods and for special project assignments is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the supervision of an administra- tive superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The position must supervise employees performing off line key to disc, key to tape and on line data entry in a departmental alpha numeric data entry unit.
  2. The position must supervise from four to seven subordinates engaged in alpha-numeric data entry work using the keyboard of a computer terminal device.


TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Receives source documents from departmental units for data entry assignments; spot-checks documents to determine if all required information has been supplied; obtains additional information required to complete documents; prepares data entry instructions for subordinate operators and explains in detail the program formats and designated data entry fields; assigns priorities to each project according to deadlines established by departmental or data processing personnel.
  • Assigns alpha-numeric key-to-disc data entry and verification work to subordinate operations; spot-checks work progress to determine status of each project; answers questions of subordinates concerning data entry problems; checks the accuracy of completed work; prepares personnel, production, and control records.
  • Operates electronic disc-to-tape computer terminal and processor to transfer data on magnetic disc to computer tape.
  • Confers with programmer analysts and departmental officials to determine form layouts and data entry programs which will facilitate more rapid and accurate data entry.
  • Instructs new employees in the operations of key entry and verification equipment; instructs all employees in methods and procedures used in various key entry projects.
  • Operates electronic key-to-disc machines during peak load periods and on special projects.
  • Performs related work as required.


REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the operation of alpha-numeric electronic key-to-disc machines.
  • the methods and practices used in the entry and verification of coded and uncoded information in connection with electronic recording of statistical and accounting data.
  • the preparation of data entry programs.
  • program formatting for key-to-disc machines.
  • general office practices and procedures.

SKILL IN:

  • keying and entering alpha-numeric data accurately and rapidly, using a computer terminal keyboard.

ABILITY TO:

  • perform complex alpha-numeric operations rapidly and accurately.
  • plan, assign, and supervise the work of data entry operators to achieve effective utilization of personnel and machine resources.
  • maintain personnel, production and control records.
  • read small printed words and numbers rapidly and accurately.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates.


MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/1991.)

EDUCATION:

  • Education equivalent to the completion of the twelfth school grade.

AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Eighteen months of experience operating alpha-numeric data entry machines.

AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE
  • Eighteen months experience operating alpha-numeric data entry machines at the full performance level.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
