UTILITY LEGISLATIVE AFFAIRS SPECIALIST

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

            This is legislative and regulatory affairs advisory and liaison work at the full performance level. Employees in the class monitor local, state, regional and federal legislation and regulatory initiatives that impact the policies, plans and operations of the Water Department.  Work includes analyzing and reviewing such legislation for departmental review and recommending position statements and strategies to respond to and influence key legislation and regulatory initiatives.  Establishing and maintaining communication and working relationships with local, state and federal regulatory agency officials as well as the general public is a significant aspect of the work.  Work is performed under the general supervision of an administrative or technical superior.

 

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS:  (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.         Positions in this class must perform full performance legislative and regulatory affairs work.

2.         Positions must be responsible for analyzing and providing recommendations on local, state, regional and federal legislation and initiatives that impact upon the policies, plans and operations of the Water department.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

            Analyzes local, state and federal proposals and legislation which affect the programs or operations of the Water Department; recommends actions to ensure compliance with laws; prepares a variety of manuals, policy statements and procedures to facilitate understanding of departmental and city regulations and requirements; analyzes comprehensive reports and correspondence, outlining recommendations for changes to policy; presents recommendations for change to supervisor and executive staff.

            Performs research and prepares reports and recommendations on federal, state, regional and local governments for legislative and regulatory initiatives affecting departmental concerns, needs and interests of civic and special interest groups and intra- governmental agencies; prepares written or oral reports about relevant legislative and regulatory developments.

            Performs research and makes presentations to interested groups on various programs and their impact; facilitates meetings of internal and external groups and stakeholders; participates in committees and task forces established to analyze and review policy and legislation; develops and implements corrective actions.

            Assists in the development and implementation of a legislative strategy based on insight developed through contact with staff; assists in the planning and execution of ongoing strategic local, state and federal legislation and policy; attends and prepares reports related to hearings, mark-ups, and floor debates related to issues about the Water Department

            Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         the principles and practices of local, state, regional and federal legislation and regulations which affect major public utilities

·         legislative decision-making process

·         regulations, contracts, laws, ordinances and procedures governing utility decision making

·         the operations, functions and structures of legislative bodies and regulatory agencies

·         the methods and techniques of interpreting, analyzing and critiquing governmental legislation and regulations

·         legislative policies and principles

·         principles of statistical analysis

·         principles of policy and procedure development

·         office practices, procedures and equipment

·         the principles and practices utilized in notifying interest groups and the public of proposed legislation and regulatory mandates

·         the methods, practices and techniques of monitoring governmental legislative and regulatory agendas

·         the methods, techniques and practices of drafting local legislation for local governing bodies

·         current trends and best practices related to legislative processes

 

SKILL IN:

·         use of personal computers

·         narrative report and legislative writing

·         applying a variety of analytical techniques in gathering, analyzing and evaluating complex data and developing recommendations to provide to executive staff

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·         evaluate the effectiveness of current legislation and policy

·         interpret and implement a variety of Water Department policies and guidelines

·         create text of governmental legislation and regulation

·         review position papers and strategies to advance the interests of the department with key governmental legislators and their support staff and lobbying officials

·         analyze complex legislation, policy, and departmental regulations

·         write clear and concise reports

·         develop and manage lobbying contracts

·         organize and manage an advisory council within the local community

·         present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing

·         make recommendations on legislative policy and rule making

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE  (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/23.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Completion of a bachelors degree program at an accredited college or university with major coursework in political science, environmental studies, government affairs, public administration, business administration, city planning, community planning, communication, media studies, public affairs or a closely related field.

 

AND

EXPERIENCE:

 

Two years of experience monitoring or drafting government legislation and regulatory initiatives and liaising with public officials or the general public for a major public utility or company.

           

OR

 

            Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources that has included completion of a bachelor's degree program as an educational minimum.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
