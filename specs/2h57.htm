CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2H57
HUMAN RESOURCES ASSOCIATE 2

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is public human resources work at the full performance level in one of the City’s operating departments. Employees in this class perform a variety of human resources activities administering a comprehensive human resources program, encompassing diverse human resources functions. Work at this level is distinguished from lower levels by the degree of independence exercised, level of involvement and the ability to resolve problems and reach technical decisions. A significant aspect of this work is establishing and maintaining effective working relationships with departmental personnel, union officials, associates and Central Personnel staff. Work may also include supervision of clerical operations in support of the services provided. Work is performed under the direction of a higher level manager.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

  • Employees in this class must perform work in either a variety of areas including classification and pay, regulatory compliance, workforce planning, recruitment and selection, safety administration, training, labor relations or specialize in a particular area.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Analyzes and determines need for well-defined policies; ensures that departmental policies and procedures are communicated and followed by employees, supervisors and administrators; prepares periodic reports on department personnel matters by compiling, analyzing and interpreting information.
  • Performs related work as required.

Performs the following duties as primary assignment in a major operating department or in combination for small or moderate-sized departments:

Classification and Pay

  • Evaluates requests received from department employees for changes in classification or pay; prepares justifications for proposed changes and communicates information to designated liaison in Central Personnel; presents proposed classification and pay changes detailing rationale, including changes in state and/or federal regulations and determines impact on the department; recommends alternatives to central agencies and departmental employees regarding classification and pay matters; reviews class specifications to determine accuracy and conformance to department needs; proposes appropriate revisions to class specifications.
  • Assembles and recommends courses of action on compensation cases based on available information and ensures that policies and procedures are followed; coordinates departmental action on appeals.
  • Oversees payroll activities, if required by work assignment.

Regulatory Compliance

  • Reviews present personnel policies and procedures to determine if they conform to regulatory requirements, such as FMLA, ADA and EEO; advises complainant; gathers information; discusses and recommends courses of action based on gathered information; administers affirmative action policies; determines if employees are eligible for accommodations; identifies, evaluates and ensures accommodations are provided; obtains secondary medical opinions.

Workforce Planning

  • Prepares or participates in preparation of department’s annual workforce plan; meets with managers to determine operational needs; reviews budget to ascertain funding available; determines the need for examinations based on projected vacancies or operational needs; recommends examination type, and helps gather information needed for examinations; reviews and communicates information supplied by departmental experts; resolves problems regarding admittance and rejection of candidates; recommends alternatives to staff examiner regarding problems associated with examination.

Recruitment and Selection

  • Analyzes recruitment needs based on operational considerations; recommends and justifies needs for residency waivers; may assist Central Personnel in review of applications.
  • Requests names of eligibles from appropriate eligible lists from the Central Personnel agency; contacts, schedules and interviews applicants for employment; explains job duties; verifies prospective hires’ education and work history; obtains and calls references; may recommend eligibles for positions or the removal of eligibles from certification or eligible lists; discusses with supervisors and administrators the qualifications of prospective employees.
  • Identifies problems and/or needs as they pertain to filling vacancies in workforce; works with higher level associates, managers and central agencies to determine remedial plans of action; attends recruitment meetings and participates in career fairs.
  • Participates in the development of procedures and regulations for the processing and placement of employees in special employment programs; determines staffing distribution; monitors procedures and regulations to ensure conformance to prescribed procedures; resolves problems between departmental officials and special employment program agency.

Safety Administration

  • Reviews injury reports for accuracy; determines nature of accidents; interacts with third party administrators and centralized agencies; administers workers’ compensation departmental program; tracks and documents data and information; testifies in court regarding matters of workers’ compensation; makes recommendations to improve safety; participates in committees; facilitates coordination of CDL random drug testing program.

Labor Relations (Discipline, Performance Management)

  • Develops solutions to problems between employees and management; deals with union representatives on problems concerning employee rights, benefits, privileges and obligations; advises department administrators and supervisors on labor practices; interprets the effect of bargaining agreement on operational policies and procedures; ensures that performance reports are filed in timely fashion; meets with supervisors and subordinates to discuss performance report appeals; ensures that actions taken are appropriate and in compliance with labor agreements.
  • Informs department officials and employees of pertinent departmental and city-wide policies and obtains documentation needed for disciplinary cases; develops and interprets Civil Service Regulations and procedures; recommends appropriate disciplinary action; confers with union officials regarding the type and degree of required action; prepares legal counsel responses to appeals; attends hearings; testifies and prepares reports.

Training

  • Informs employees of training programs and courses available for participation; arranges with training agencies for specific training courses and coordinates department participation; reviews departmental needs for training; assembles and instructs basic training programs; determines eligibility of employees.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

    KNOWLEDGE OF:

    • city policies, practices and Civil Service Regulations affecting the certification, selection, probation and rejection of employees
    • city and departmental policies, procedures and past practices relating to employee rights, benefits, privileges and obligations
    • practices and procedures of employee workers’ or unemployment compensation in a civil service system
    • Civil Service Regulations, union contract provisions and prevailing practices used in the administration of a disciplinary program
    • Civil Service Regulations and policies pertinent to compensation and conditions of alternative work assignments, such as out-of-class pay, temporary promotions and relief appointments
    • interviewing methods and practices
    • practices and procedures as they relate to selection and classification
    • current best practices in human resources management
    • English grammar, spelling and punctuation
    • federal laws and regulations, such as ADA, EEO and FMLA
    • uses and applications of personal computers and current standard software packages used for word processing, data base manipulation and preparation of spreadsheets
    • practices, procedures, policies and regulations relating to payroll processing, if required by work assignment

    SKILL IN:

    • eliciting information
    • report writing

    ABILITY TO:

    • implement human resources programs
    • explain and interpret Civil Service Regulations, departmental procedures and policies in the various personnel functions
    • resolve complaints, problems and grievances between individuals, departments and supervisors
    • establish and maintain effective working relationships with union officials, employees, department officials, supervisors and central agencies
    • gather and analyze information
    • supervise a clerical staff working in support of human resources functions
    • coordinate the needs of departmental officials with central agencies
    • communicate effectively, both orally and in writing

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 10/05.)

    EDUCATION

    • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.

    AND
    GENERAL EXPERIENCE

    • Two years of experience in public human resources work.

    NOTE:

    A master's degree in human resources management or PHR Certification may be substituted for one year of the required General Experience.

    OR

    Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department, which includes possession of a bachelor's degree from an accredited college or university as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
