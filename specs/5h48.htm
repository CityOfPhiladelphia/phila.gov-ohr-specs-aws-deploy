DEPUTY SHERIFF CORPORAL

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

          This is law enforcement work directing the work activities of a group of uniformed and civilian personnel in a specialized sheriff’s unit on an assigned shift. Positions in this class are responsible for ensuring the processing and maintenance of the Sheriff’s Office and City records. Contact with law enforcement officials and the public is a significant part of this job.

Assignments require the use of evaluative thinking and are carried out in accordance with standard Sheriff’s Office policies and procedures and general work instructions. An employee in this class participates with others in the efficient operation of an assigned organizational unit. Work involves conditions that can be only partially controlled.  Work is performed under the supervision of a higher-level deputy sheriff.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

            Directs the work activities of a small group of uniformed and civilian personnel in a specialized sheriff’s unit or in the operations office/desk and/or cell room of an assigned unit or post; reviews the nature and content of all communications received by email, computer systems, radio, mail and messenger service, and directs those communications accordingly.          Approves transportation of prisoners; instructs personnel in the proper procedures involved in the slating, processing, and detention of all persons arrested; schedules and coordinates the transfer and delivery of prisoners for identification, investigations, court hearings, and medical treatments; as assigned, may participate in investigations and arrest procedures.

            Instructs uniformed and civilian staff in the preparation and distribution of reports on office or unit activities, incidents, and individuals and platoon shift assignments; reviews work for completeness, accuracy, and neatness; makes recommendations for correction of errors; guides subordinates in the preparation and issuance of writs and other legal documents within the purview of routine Sheriff’s Office document processing.

            Ensures that pertinent information, activities and communications are recorded on the daily logs and load sheets and that all interested authorized personnel receive copies; receives and routes all inquiries, complaints, and requests for Sheriff’s services made by citizens in person or by telephone and instructs Sheriff’s personnel in their proper dispositions; notifies superior officers, according to current directives, of all unusual or emergency situations occurring in the offices, field or court facility; arranges for other city departments, utility companies, and other law enforcement agencies to be notified of complaints and emergency conditions that may require their services.

            Arranges for the necessary services and repairs for Sheriff’s vehicles on a preventive maintenance  and emergency basis; requisitions and maintains supplies for the office or other units; receives, reviews, and maintains all reference materials and directives; reviews files and records and instructs personnel in their proper maintenance in accordance with current directives; maintains attendance records of all personnel in the office, unit or post.

           

            Conducts regular inspections of detention areas to determine cleanliness and the physical condition of prisoners; inspects the rooms and facilities of the sheriff’s offices and units and grounds and instructs personnel in their proper maintenance.

            Reviews schedules of court hearings held in the courts; dispatches vehicles to prisons to transport prisoners to hearings; makes appropriate notifications for scheduled court appearances; assembles case folders; oversees the custody of prisoners during court hearings; updates case folders by noting the disposition of the hearing as directed by court officials.

            Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

•    writs and court orders.

•    modern law enforcement practices and methods.

•    controlling laws and ordinances, particularly the laws of arrest and criminal procedure and civil procedure.

•    the geography of the city.

•    the rules and regulations of the Sheriff’s office pertaining to operations desk activities.

•    the forms used and departmental procedures applicable to Sheriff’s office operations rooms, Sheriff’s units and civil enforcement.

•    the processing of protection-from-abuse orders, weapon relinquishment and gun permits.

•    first-aid principles and practices.

•    the rules and regulations and process of re-slating a prisoner on a new arrest.

•    Procedures,  processes and documentation relating to inter-agency protocols, communication, and prisoner arrest and processing.

 

SKILL IN:

 

•    the use and care of firearms, including confiscation and safe and secure storage.

 

ABILITY TO:

 

•    oversee the work of a group of sworn and civilian personnel engaged in the administration, communication, and detention activities in a specialized unit or court facility for the Sheriff’s Office

•    analyze situations quickly and objectively and to determine the proper course of action to be taken.

•    obtain information through communication.

•    react quickly and calmly in emergency situations.

•    speak and write clearly and effectively.

•    understand and carry out oral and written instructions.

•    establish and maintain effective working relationships with other deputies and the public.

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards that will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 11/21.)

EDUCATION:

Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

AND
EXPERIENCE:

 

Two years of experience as a Deputy Sheriff.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

 

Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a  Deputy Sheriff Corporal.

 

Possession of current Act II certification prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a  Deputy Sheriff Corporal.
