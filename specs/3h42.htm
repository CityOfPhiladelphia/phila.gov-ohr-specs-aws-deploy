CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

3H42
CRIMINALIST

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is investigational laboratory work supervising and participating in the chemical, biochemical, instrumental, microscopic and macroscopic examination of physical evidence in support of criminal investigations. The employee in this class develops new techniques and methods for analysis and identification of a great variety of physical evidence and keeps abreast of and evaluates the efficacy of new methods and techniques developed by others. The management and supervision of a staff of subordinate chemists, technicians and clerical personnel, and the presentation of expert testimony in criminal court are of major significance to the work.

The employee reports to the laboratory director. The work requires analytical thinking and the selection of methods and techniques from a variety of scientific disciplines and specialties such as chemistry, microbiology, serology and physics. The investigational laboratory services provided by this employee form the basis for the resolution of criminal investigations.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, assigns and reviews the work of chemists and laboratory technicians performing comparison tests involving analyses and microscopic examinations of materials such as hair, clothing fibers, skin, glass, paints, paper, gunpowder residues and other organic and inorganic substances found on the suspect or victim with those materials found at the crime scene; directs the performance of blood alcohol level determinations, and analysis of hemolyzed blood by absorption, elution and other techniques in support of the Medical Examiner's Office.
  • Supervises the conduct of tests performed to determine the presence of semen on wet and dry specimens and the performance of serological analyses to identify blood and blood stains according to origin and blood groupings; oversees analysis of contraband alcohol evidence, and the conducting of chemical analysis in support of firearms identification activities; reviews comparisons of plaster and moulage casts and photographs of impressions and marks with shoes, tires, instruments and other objects; directs the examination of evidence with a wide variety of specialized instruments; oversees operation of a scanning electron microscope and energy- dispersive x-ray system for analysis for some controlled substances.
  • Participates in the performance of more complex comparisons and analyses and conducts the applications of chromatographic and spectrometric techniques to criminalistic problems; contacts a variety of public and private agencies to gather information on various analytical techniques and to solicit samples as testing standards; prepares reports of results of test, indicating probability of crime or violation of statutes or ordinances.
  • Conducts research experiments to develop modern, more efficient and more exact criminal laboratory methods, techniques and procedures; reads national and international professional journals and reports concerning developments in forensic analysis; tests and evaluates new methods, techniques and procedures to certify their efficacy and feasibility for use in police criminalistic work.
  • May make field trips to various crime scenes to supervise the collection of and analysis of evidence in complex or sensitive investigations.
  • Appears in court to testify as an expert witness in civil and criminal cases to present evidence brought out by laboratory tests and examinations; explains forensic techniques and theory for current or hypothetical cases; provides justification through analysis of evidence for probable cause basis needed for warrant issuing; presides over Civilian Board of Inquiry convened to examine police disciplinary cases.
  • Prepares budget for criminalistic section of crime laboratory and oversees other administrative functions applicable to section; prepares grant applications for the purchase of specialized laboratory equipment; reviews journals, research papers and vendor's information to develop grant applications; prepares reports on grant funded studies.
  • Develops and conducts formal training of detectives, police supervisory personnel, police recruits, civilian crime investigators, interns, nurses and other civilian personnel at hospitals, City agencies and elsewhere in forensic matters, principally on the proper collection of evidence; oversees and instructs college forensic interns and provides Master degree accreditation in laboratory practicum.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices and techniques used to prepare, examine and analyze investigational laboratory specimens.
  • the principles, methods and techniques involved in applying the disciplines of chemistry, physics and biology to tests performed.
  • the use of modern instrumentation in the areas of microscopy, spectrophotometry, spectrography and chromatography as applied to tests performed.
  • the rules of evidence and the methods used in presenting evidence in court.
ABILITY TO:

  • perform laboratory tests in accordance with prescribed crimninalistics procedures and to make accurate observations and analyses of test results.
  • adapt established criminalistic laboratory methods to meet specific problems.
  • manage scientific and technical personnel performing criminalist work.
  • write and interpret scientifically accurate and comprehensive reports of tests conducted.
  • prepare and maintain budgetary and related records and reports of tests conducted.
  • effectively present information in court.
  • develop and conduct training for college interns, police personnel and civilian personnel involved in forensic activities.
  • instruct chemists and technicians in investigational laboratory techniques and analysis.
  • develop new forensic methods, techniques, and procedures and to evaluate those developed by others for their efficacy.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/86.)

  • Completion of a master's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in chemistry, biochemistry, biology or criminalistics.
  • investigational laboratory work at the full performance level on a wide variety of organic and inorganic materials and interpreting tests results.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included a master's degree as stated above as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
