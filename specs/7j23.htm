WATER TREATMENT PLANT SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is second supervisory level work directing all aspects of operations and maintenance activities in one of the City's water treatment plants. An employee in this class plans, assigns and coordinates, through subordinate supervisors, operations personnel engaged in monitoring, adjusting and regulating a computer controlled water treatment process and trades, skilled and semiskilled personnel performing responsive and preventive maintenance on plant process equipment, buildings, systems and grounds areas.

Work involves the review and modification of plant process equipment construction and rehabilitation plans and specifications, the evaluation of water treatment process equipment to make acquisition recommendations, and the planning of a comprehensive preventive maintenance program for the plant. Employees perform a variety of administrative tasks associated with plant operations and maintenance functions. Ensuring the proper and efficient operation and maintenance of a digital electronically computerized water treatment monitoring and controlling system is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the general direction of an engineering superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • The position must be located at one of the City's water treatment plants.
  • The position must report directly to a Sanitary Engineer IV (Plant Manager).
  • The position must be responsible for a water treatment plant preventive and corrective maintenance program and a 24 hour a day, seven days a week water treatment plant operations function.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY) )

  • Plans, assigns and reviews plant operations activities through supervisors; develops and defines procedures, instructions and standards for subordinate operations personnel to follow during assigned shifts; responds to emergency plant operations problems requiring immediate attention during all plant operational hours; evaluates water treatment processing problems and determines proper corrective measures; makes periodic trips to the plant at night and during weekends to evaluate subordinate operator's working performance; ensures the effective and efficient operation of the plant; tours plant and surrounding areas daily to observe the operational efficiency of machinery and equipment and the physical appearance of water at various stages in the water treatment process; receives chemical lab analysis reports and confers with chemical technicians to identify daily water treatment chemical dosage needs, such as increases or decreases in ammonia, ferric, fluoride and sodium chloride; instructs subordinate operators to increase or decrease chemical dosage levels in accordance with needs identified through lab analysis; schedules subordinate operators' shifts; ensures all monitoring, controlling and regulating activities are recorded during assigned shifts.
  • Plans, assigns, reviews and coordinates, through subordinate supervisors, preventive and corrective maintenance activities on plant process equipment, including mechanical and electrical equipment, and hydraulic pneumatic, electrical and analog and digital electronic instrumentation; directs, through subordinate supervisors, preventive and responsive maintenance of plant buildings, structures and grounds areas; develops and administers comprehensive annual preventive maintenance schedules for plant process equipment, buildings, systems, structures and grounds areas; plans, reviews and coordinates, through subordinate supervisors the preventive and corrective maintenance activities of a microprocessor based computer control system; confers with operations personnel and reviews preventive maintenance reports identifying equipment malfunctioning; prioritizes preventive and corrective maintenance while ensuring minimum downtime of critical equipment; ensures compliance with established schedules and work, safety and efficiency standards; tours maintenance and repair work sites and inspects work in progress and completed to identify problems and discrepancies in the work performed; instructs subordinates in the proper corrective measures; may direct maintenance of water reservoirs.
  • Monitors computer operations to ensure the effective and efficient operation of a computer automated processing system; contacts servicing vendor to correct problems with complex computerized hardware and systems and applications software; designs and configures process loop sequences for analog and digital electronically automated instrumentation operations; programs process loop sequences in the main computer to system interface field instrumentation process operations with the computer systems, including inputs, signals outputs and locations; saves and files programmed data at regular intervals to prevent data loss; utilizes a CRT keyboard to retrieve and program data.
  • Reviews and modifies water treatment plant process equipment rehabilitation and construction design plans and specifications; prepares plans and specifications for small scale construction and rehabilitation projects; reviews vendor presentations of new mechanical and electrical equipment and various instrumentation and tests to determine whether they will improve plant operational efficiency; makes recommendations to the plant manager on the acquisition of new mechanical and electrical equipment and various instrumentation.
  • Confers with plant administrative personnel to review budgetary needs for the following fiscal year; prepares cost estimates for materials, supplies, equipment and personnel for budgetary purposes; prepares and reviews requirements contracts; schedules vendors to perform required maintenance, repair and services; contacts equipment and materials vendor specialists to develop technical solutions to equipment repair problems.
  • Monitors inventory levels for materials, supplies and equipment necessary for the operation of the plant; prepares purchase requisitions for needed materials, supplies and equipment; receives water treatment chemical inventory reports, estimates weekly consumption requirements and places re-orders as necessary.
  • Develops training and safety programs in conjunction with subordinate group leaders; schedules and conducts training sessions; conducts hiring interviews and makes selection decisions; chairs disciplinary hearings and makes disciplinary recommendations; prepares written reports summarizing maintenance activities and overall plant conditions.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the practices, methods, materials and tools used in the operation and maintenance of water treatment process mechanical and electrical equipment and hydraulic, pneumatic, electrical and electronic instrumentation.
  • the electrical trade and the principles and theory of electricity.
  • the ordinances and regulations governing electrical installations.
  • the basic principles involved in the design and configuration of digital electronic process loops.
  • the operations involved in troubleshooting, repairing, calibrating and maintaining digital electronic instrumentation and process loops.
  • the occupational hazards and safety precautions required in the maintenance of electrically, electronically, hydraulically, and pneumatically powered machinery and instrumentation at a water treatment plant, including working around harmful and toxic chemicals such as chlorine and ammonia and high voltage electrical equipment.
  • the methods, practices, materials and tools used in building and grounds maintenance.
  • the use and operation of a water treatment plant.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
  • the application of electronic data processing equipment and software to water treatment process operations equipment.

SKILL IN:

  • troubleshooting problems and malfunctions in the mechanical, electrical, electronic, hydraulic pneumatic components of water treatment plant process equipment.
  • the utilization of tools and equipment used in the mechanical, electrical and instrumentation trades.
  • the use of computer applications utilized to monitor and control a water treatment plant operation.

ABILITY TO:

  • direct through subordinate supervisors the activities of operations, trades, semi-skilled and unskilled personnel.
  • detect and analyze defects in electrical and mechanical equipment and various instrumentation and to assign the appropriate skilled personnel to repair such equipment.
  • evaluate and prepare cost estimates of water treatment plant materials, supplies and equipment for budgetary purposes.
  • read and interpret technical sketches, diagrams, blueprints and specifications related to the operation and maintenance of water treatment plant process equipment.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates.
  • attend and complete a three to four week training program on the utilization of computer applications which operate a water treatment plant and the configuration of digital electronic process loops.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/91.)
    EDUCATION:
  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

    AND

    GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

  • Four years of experience in the electrical trade or in the repair, maintenance and calibration of electrically, hydraulically, pneumatically and electronically activated instrumentation or in the maintenance and repair of heavy industrial machinery and mechanical equipment.

    AND

    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

  • Two years of experience supervising full performance trades workers engaged in the maintenance and repair of water treatment plant equipment and/or instrumentation.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Water Treatment Plant Supervisor.
