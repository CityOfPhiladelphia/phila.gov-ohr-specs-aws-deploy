AIRPORT PAVEMENTS AND GROUNDS SUPERINTENDENT

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative airport maintenance work directing comprehensive maintenance and repair programs for all external areas of the City's two municipal airports. The employee in this class directs, through subordinate supervisors, the activities of employees engaged in maintaining and repairing airfields, runways ramps, roadways, and grounds. Work involves directing a work order system to monitor completion of projects and advising administrative superiors on airfield and street maintenance and construction projects. Contact with engineering personnel, contractors, and building, electrical and automotive maintenance staff to coordinate maintenance and repair activities is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

Plans, prioritizes, assigns, reviews, and coordinates, through subordinate trades and airport maintenance supervisors, the work of a large staff of skilled and semiskilled trades, maintenance and laboring personnel and equipment operators performing a variety of airfield, ramp, roadway, and grounds maintenance and repair functions; assigns priorities for work completion in accordance with operational needs and administrative directives; prepares schedules for ongoing preventive maintenance of airfields, ramps, taxiways, the airport road system, sidewalks, and all non paved grounds; directs and schedules all refuse collection and disposal; inspects work sites to determine composition of work crews; analyzes and evaluates crew performance in relationship to established timetable and FAA and departmental airfield, pavements, and grounds standards.

Reviews airfield, roadways, and grounds work orders to determine priority and feasibility; estimates timetables, material and personnel costs for work projects; discusses with appropriate agency or supervisor the required operational adjustments to accommodate the work; anticipates problems and recommends modifications or alternative solutions to provide minimal impact on airport operations or ongoing construction projects; coordinates maintenance and repair activities with building, electrical and automotive services staffs to ensure that sufficient equipment is available and properly maintained to accomplish the work.

Directs the landscaping, planting and upkeep of all grassy areas on airport grounds; ensures the erosion control measures are taken; ensures that the condition of all paved and adjoining areas meets Federal Airport Certification requirements; remains knowledgeable about imposed F.A.A. requirements pertaining to airport external maintenance and repair work and implements changes when appropriate.

Prepares requisitions for materials, equipment and supplies; provides technical information required to prepare specifications for maintenance and repair contracts; oversees work performed under service contracts.

Directs the operation of a large and diverse fleet of light and heavy vehicles and ensures they receive maintenance when appropriate.

Prepares and submits annual budgetary requirements; maintains records and generates reports on work order progress, completion, staff requirements, costs and impact on airport certification status.

Works with airport engineering personnel and federal and state environmental officials to achieve engineering and environmental requirements regarding external maintenance of the airport.

Directs the provision of support services in the containment of hazardous materials.

May direct the activities of a large crew of equipment operators and/or contracted personnel engaged in the removal of snow from airfields and airport road systems.

Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

    KNOWLEDGE OF:

    • the practices, methods, materials, tools, and equipment used in the maintenance and repair of airport pavements and grounds
    • maintenance and scheduling procedures essential to the upkeep of large paved and landscaped areas, especially as they relate to Federal Airport Certification standards
    • federal and state environmental regulations and requirements as they relate to the maintenance of large paved and landscaped areas at an airport
    • the cost of labor, materials and time necessary to complete a variety of pavements and grounds projects
    • the occupational hazards and safety precautions involved in repairs to runways and roadways, and the maintenance of both landscaped and non landscaped grounds
    • administrative, record keeping, and report writing procedures as they relate to administering a comprehensive airfield and grounds maintenance and repair program and compliance with Federal Standards
    • supervisory methods and techniques

    ABILITY TO:

    • plan, organize, direct, and coordinate the activities of skilled and semi skilled trades workers and equipment operators engaged in performing a variety of maintenance services
    • assign priorities to projects based on Federal Airport Certification requirements and airport operating needs and standards
    • evaluate the quality and effectiveness of the Airport airfield, roadway and grounds maintenance programs with regard to Federal Airport Certification standards and operational needs
    • estimate timetables and materials costs for maintenance and repair projects.
    • prepare preventive maintenance schedules
    • requisition and specify materials, tools, equipment and supplies essential to the maintenance of Airport pavements and grounds
    • interpret and utilize technical sketches and plans
    • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, subordinates, and representatives from other agencies

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/08.)

    EDUCATION

    Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

    AND
    GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

    Six years of experience in operations or maintenance at a large airport.

    AND
    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

    Two years of experience supervising a variety of personnel including full performance trades workers engaged in airport building and/or grounds maintenance work.

    OR

    Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources that has included the specific experience indicated above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as an Airport Pavements and Grounds Superintendent.
