3B51
ENVIRONMENTAL ENGINEER I

Specialties
( Air Management ) ( Industrial Hygiene )

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is environmental engineering work investigating environmental health hazards such as air pollution sources, noise pollution sources, occupational hazards, sources of radiation and other potential environmental hazards to determine the extent of pollutant emissions and of compliance with regulations. The employee is given progressively more difficult field and/or office assignments and is expected to use progressively more independent judgment in planning work details and making technical decisions.

Work is performed under the supervision of a technical superior. Assignments require evaluative thinking and are performed in accordance with environmental engineering practices and general work instructions. Employees in this class provide interpretive and advisory services to technical superiors which lead to the completion of engineering projects.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

Air Management Specialty

  • Investigates air pollution control problems at industrial sites; performs tests on incinerators, stacks, ventilation systems, and plant equipment to determine type and quantity of pollutants being emitted.

Industrial Hygiene Specialty

  • Investigates industrial plants, medical offices, and other sites and establishments; for environmental health hazards, performs tests to determine existence and extent of occupational hazards, noise pollution, radiation hazards or other health hazards.

Both Specialties

  • Estimates extent of problems, and the sampling procedures and equipment required; reviews technical literature; sets up, calibrates and maintains a variety of physical and chemical testing equipment; takes readings and makes calculations to evaluate results; prepares reports on observations and analysis of findings; makes recommendations on less complex problems.
  • Reviews permit applications, blueprints and designs of plant layout and equipment to assure compliance with standards and regulations.
  • Meets with industrial officials to advise them of departmental programs and policies, and to resolve violations.
  • May testify in legal proceedings and speak before public and private groups.
  • Corresponds with industrial and chemical producers to acquire data on the composition of materials used in industrial plants; evaluates the data and the method of processing the materials to determine if a health hazard exists.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • engineering theory, principles and practices as applied to the area of specialty.
  • laws and regulations as applied to the area of specialty.
  • engineering mathematics and mathematical tables used in the area of specialty.
  • chemistry or biology as related to environmental health hazards in the area of specialty.
  • the principles and proper usage of a variety of testing, measuring and control devices used in the area of specialty.

ABILITY TO:

  • analyze mechanical and physical equipment and plant processes.
  • make surveys and secure complete and accurate data.
  • prepare concise and comprehensive reports.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/76.)

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in chemical, civil, electrical, mechanical or sanitary engineering.
  • One year of engineering experience in the specialty area.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience that has included the completion of a bachelor's degree program in an acceptable area of engineering as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as an Environmental Engineer I if required by work assignment.
