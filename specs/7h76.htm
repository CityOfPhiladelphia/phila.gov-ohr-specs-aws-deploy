CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

7H76
STADIUM EVENTS COORDINATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is events coordination work directing the preparation and conversion of the stadium complex for a wide variety of athletic and entertainment events. Work includes responsibility for showing facilities to prospective users to determine the required services and available facilities and coordinating these services with overall stadium operations. Contact with tenants and lessees to promote effective relationships is of major significant to the work. Work is performed under the general supervision of an administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class. )

  • the position must direct the preparation and conversion of the stadium complex for a wide variety of athletic and entertainment events.
  • the position must be located in the Recreation Department.
  • only one position may be allocated to this class.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Coordinates the work of employees to provide services to lessees and tenants; schedules and coordinates requisite services before, during and after events at the stadium complex; provides contracted and requested services such as marking athletic fields, assigning and preparing locker rooms, painting and decorating artificial turf and grass fields, erecting and removing goal posts, and installing and removing windows and platforms from boxes for all stadium complex events; insures HVAC, electrical and plumbing systems are operable; schedules maintenance as required.
  • Establishes and maintains good working relationships with prospective users of the stadium complex; maintains good relationships between stadium management and tenants; accompanies prospective clients on tours of stadium complex to showcase the facilities and to determine required services including special decorations.
  • Directs crews of employees performing emergency HVAC, electrical, plumbing and welding repairs during athletic and entertainment events; coordinates event crews' activities with managers of athletic teams and of entertainment events.
  • Reviews schedules of events to determine types of stadium conversions required between events; plans and directs field conversion including large numbers of laboring, equipment operation, and skilled and unskilled trades personnel, materials needed to reline playing fields, to remove and store scoreboards and to revise seating configurations; supervises the activities of conversion crews engaged in preparing the stadium facilities on an around-the-clock basis.
  • Assists in planning and coordinating special city events at the stadium complex; coordinates activities with other city agencies including the City Representative's Office and the Public Property, Streets, Water, Police and Recreation Departments.
  • Plans, assigns and reviews all contractor cleaning work at the stadium complex; supervises the care of grass, trees and shrubs in landscaped areas of the stadium complex; directs and supervises snow and ice removal from streets, sidewalks, walkways, seating areas and playing fields.
  • Maintains contact with the stadium concessionaires to coordinate activities and solve any problems.
  • Meets with representatives of the community surrounding the stadium complex to discuss problems caused by stadium operations; resolves complaints relative to traffic flow or noise of maintenance operations by contacting appropriate supervisors.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the physical requirements of various types of sports and other special shows, activities or events which are presented in stadium facilities.
  • the methods, techniques, materials and equipment used in maintaining and converting the playing fields.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
  • the principles and practices of stadium operations as they relate to the preparation and care of stadium facilities.
  • the functions of various governmental departments and agencies as they apply to stadium operations.
  • the methods, techniques and equipment used in general grounds keeping and maintenance.

ABILITY TO:

  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with lessees, associates and the public.
  • represent stadium management at meetings and conferences.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/95.)
    EDUCATION:
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.

    AND

    GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

  • Two years of experience coordinating indoor or outdoor maintenance activities for a stadium or arena.

    AND

    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

  • One year of experience supervising trades and/or laboring personnel engaged in maintenance activities for a stadium or arena.

Or any equivalent combination of acceptable education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
