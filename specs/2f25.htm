CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2F25
FISCAL ANALYST I

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is fiscal analysis work at the intermediate level conducting feasibility studies and research into long range, city-wide fiscal programs. Employees in this class perform an interpretative service in the long range capital and operating programs of the city. A significant aspect of the work is reviewing capital budget expenditures incurred in the acquisition and construction stages of self-sustaining capital projects. Work is performed under the general supervision of a higher level fiscal analyst.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Reviews capital budget expenditures incurred in the acquisition and construction stages of self- sustaining capital projects; computes interest to be charged to such projects on funds expended in advance of bond sales; computes debt service costs during construction for purposes of capitalizing such costs.
  • Reviews and prepares cash flow projections; forecasts bond sales in relation thereto and debt service to be incurred on such sales.
  • Compiles and analyzes the City's cash and investment positions including bank collateralization of City funds on deposit; attends and participates in meetings of local banking officials and bond dealers.
  • Analyzes financial reports of city-related quasi-governmental agencies; prepares reports on financial interrelationships between the City and such agencies; monitors grant transactions and expenditures to determine amounts due from other governmental entities; review requests for payments submitted by quasi-governmental agencies to ascertain that funds requested have been budgeted and that requesting agencies have conformed to contractual requirements.
  • Gathers information for and prepares reports required to qualify for participation in the distribution by the Commonwealth of realty taxes paid by utilities.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of municipal fiscal administration.
  • the principles and practices of public administration with particular reference to governmental finance, accounting, and budgeting.
  • research methods, techniques, and standard source of information as they relate to finance administration.
  • methods and techniques used in capital and operating budget formulation and administration.
  • the principles and practices of city and regional planning.

ABILITY TO:

  • analyze problems relating to fiscal administration and long range budgetary planning.
  • apply research methods and techniques to a variety of studies and problems.
  • express ideas effectively in oral, written and graphic form.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with legislative and executive officials and associates and members of the financial community.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/1982.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE
  • One year of experience performing research in and analyzing long range fiscal plans and programs or budgets.

    OR

  • One year of experience in the preparation or analysis of the final financial statements which reflect the activity applicable to such plans and programs or budgets.

    OR

  • One year of experience in finance or accounting.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE
  • One year in the analysis of the cash and investment positions of a large governmental jurisdiction.

    OR

  • One year in the analysis and control of funds to determine amounts due from other governmental and quasi-governmental entities.

Or any equivalent combination of experience which has included the specific experience and which has been determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
