AIRPORT SUSTAINABILTY MANAGER

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is managerial environmental planning work directing the sustainability program for a large hub international airport. The employee in this class directs, through a subordinate supervisor, the activities of a staff engaged in airport and environmental planning work in the implementation, monitoring, and reporting for the Division of Aviation’s sustainability program. The employee is responsible for implementing and overseeing all sustainability-related strategy, goals, policies, and plans and aligning them with the capital improvement program. Establishing and maintaining effective working relationships with airport staff, tenants, consultants, contractors, airline representatives, customers, community groups, and the general public are significant to the work. Work is performed under the general direction of a Deputy Director of Aviation.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS : (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.    The position must direct all sustainability programs for the Division of Aviation through a subordinate supervisor.

2.    Only one position shall be allocated to this class in the Commerce Department’s Division of Aviation.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

            Manages, through a subordinate supervisor, the daily activities of multi-disciplinary technical staff performing airport and environmental planning work in the oversight, implementation, monitoring, and reporting for the Division of Aviation’s sustainability program; provides technical  direction and management of consultant services in support of the sustainability program; defines goals and develops policies related to the sustainability program; plans short-term and long-term projects and programs to foster sustainability; evaluates programs and makes recommendations for improvement; directs research for best practices in sustainability and resiliency; prepares and approves reports for executive management.

Leads sustainability activities with management, airline personnel, and airport tenants on a sustainability committee to ensure business, environmental, and societal interests interconnect; coordinates with other airport staff, contractors, and airport tenants to develop and implement sustainability projects with minimal disruption to airport operations; coordinates with airport communication teams to communicate sustainability program on the website, social media, in presentations and at special events; identifies and establishes partnerships in the community related to sustainability; represents the sustainability program to elected officials, outside agencies, and the community; explains, justifies, and defends programs, policies, and activities; negotiates and resolves significant and controversial issues pertaining to sustainability and resiliency; develops and maintains recognition programs such as a green business recognition program for airport staff, stakeholders and tenants.   

            Utilizes tools to collect, measure, track and analyze environmental performance metrics such as direct and indirect energy consumed per passenger, water usage, reclaimed water usage, waste, cumulative greenhouse gas emission reductions and renewable electricity consumed at both airports operated by the Division of Aviation; writes reports and gives presentations to executive leadership on the sustainability program.

            Directs the development, implementation and execution of processes or checklists to incorporate sustainability criteria and recommendations into the capital improvement program; oversees the incorporation of sustainability in the design of contract specifications, contract provisions, engineering projects, and other capital improvement projects.  

            Participates in the hiring and staffing of the sustainability unit; prepares performance evaluations; addresses and responds to employee concerns. 

            Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

•           the principles, methods, and practices used in developing and administering sustainability programs at commercial airports

•           principles and practices of airport operations

•           research methods and techniques utilized in planning for a sustainability program

•           Federal, state, and local statutes and regulations governing commercial airports as they relate to environmental planning and sustainability

•           supervisory principles and practices

•           municipal procurement contracting practices and procedures

•           principles of and information systems related to project management such as PERT or CPM

 

SKILL IN:

 

•           organization and time management to achieve sustainability-related goals and alignment with the capital improvement program

•           collecting and analyzing quantitative data to create performance metrics identified in planning documents as they relate to energy, climate resiliency and adaptation, waste, water, air quality, noise and other focus areas

•               prioritizing, implementing and managing sustainability projects within scope, time and budgetary constraints

•               demonstrating leadership and innovating thinking in the development of policies and programs that promote an environmentally sustainable large hub airport

•               planning and directing various public information presentations or special events related to sustainability

 

ABILITY TO:

 

•               direct a sustainability program for a large hub airport

•               identify and establish partnerships in the community to advance the airport’s sustainability efforts  

•               evaluate and make recommendations for airport sustainability plans for soundness of environmental planning and engineering principles and for economic feasibility

•               apply modern management and administrative principles to airport sustainability activities

•               lead strategic change initiatives related to sustainability

•               prepare capital and operating budget requests

•               develop public interest and participation in airport sustainability programs

•           establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, representatives of airlines, concessionaires, and tenants

•           express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 10/20.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in architecture, aviation management, engineering, environmental science, sustainability, transportation, urban planning or a closely related field.

 

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE

 

            Seven years of experience in architecture, engineering, environmental science, sustainability, planning, airport management or operations.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

 

Two years of supervisory experience directing sustainability related programs for a large university, public agency or hub airport.

 

OR

 

            Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources that has included completion of a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum and the specific experience.
