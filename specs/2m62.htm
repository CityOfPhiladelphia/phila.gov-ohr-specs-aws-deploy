PARK MANAGER II

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is park management work at the full performance level, reviewing, analyzing, modifying and/or developing systems and procedures to resolve operating problems or to improve productivity. Work involves conducting studies in such areas as park administration, horticulture, arboriculture, grounds maintenance recreation and park operations, and may include supervision of a specific function within a district. Establishing and maintaining effective working relationships with departmental and city officials, vendors, and the general public is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Conducts interviews with administrative and supervisory personnel concerning park operating procedures and organizational structures; observes work methods to determine deficiencies in working operations; prepares flow and organizational charts describing procedures and organization; analyzes findings and prepares recommendations to revise existing procedures; develops new procedures to increase efficiency and expedite work flow; monitors implementation of new operating procedures to determine their effectiveness and to insure that they are achieving desired results.
  • Gathers data for the purpose of developing maintenance management systems for park districts or a particular geographic area; conducts cost effectiveness studies comparing equipment, manpower and scheduling requirements; determines impact of manual, mechanical and horticultural methods on performance and maintenance standards.
  • May supervise a specific function within a park district.
  • Evaluates the impact of general public and park users on park procedures and operations; formulates recommendations and prepares reports detailing methods for improving park operations through expanded park user programs.
  • Conducts technical research for the quantification of park land acreage; reviews all geographic source data for accuracy and conformity to park specifications; determines status of park land and physical maintenance responsibility.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of park administration.
  • park operations.
  • the techniques of planning, coordinating and supervising the work of technical and clerical employees.
  • the principles and practices of horticulture.
  • the principles and practices of forestry.
  • the principles and practices of landscape architecture.
ABILITY TO:

  • analyze facts and exercise sound judgment.
  • apply the principles of management in relation to coordinating and planning the flow of work of technical and clerical employees.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • understand and follow oral and written instructions.
  • supervise.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with employees and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 9/83.)

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in park administration, horticulture, forestry or landscape architecture and,
  • Two years of experience developing skills and gaining experience in park management.
PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
