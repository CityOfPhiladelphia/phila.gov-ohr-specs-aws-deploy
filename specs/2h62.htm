GENERAL DEFINITION

This is advanced performance level employee and labor relations work in the Philadelphia Department of Labor. Employees in the Labor Relations Assignment administer all phases of grievance processing for all City Unions. Employees in the Employee Relations Assignment investigate the most complex claims of discrimination, harassment, retaliation or violations of other labor laws and regulations. Positions in this class act as a principal contact between Department of Labor, City departments, and municipal labor unions and advise management level officials on EEO policy and provide recommendations. Analysis and research are a significant part of the job. An employee in this class has access to information subject to use in collective bargaining and a close continuing relationship with a Department Director or a Deputy Director associated with collective bargaining. Positions in this class serve as a lead worker on assigned projects and assignments. Employees report to a higher level administrative or technical superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

1.    Positions in this class must only be allocated to the Department of Labor.

2.    Positions in this class must perform complex employee and labor relations work.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

BOTH ASSIGNMENTS:

Conducts research, reads, and actively participates in interpreting and applying Civil Service Regulations, Home Rule Charter, collective bargaining agreements, interest arbitration awards and federal, state, and local legislation and regulations related to the work. Prepares reports and prepares and delivers presentations to a variety of groups.

Actively participates in making recommendations for recruitment and promotional goals and participates in recruitment efforts.

Performs related work as required.

 

LABOR RELATIONS ASSIGNMENT

 

          Acts as principal contact between the Department of Labor, other departments, and a major municipal labor union or union local; administers all phases of grievance processing for all City unions; provides staff assistance to the City's negotiating team during contract negotiations and interest arbitration hearings, including preparing and delivering presentations in support of the City’s proposals; represents the City in the hearing, negotiation and settlement of grievances, including arbitration; performs and leads lower level staff in preparing exhibits, and collaborating in the arbitration of issues ranging from basic labor contract language, routine disciplinary action, and the negotiation and settlement of grievances.

          Provides interpretation of labor contracts, laws, and regulations to department officials; functions as a subject matter expert on labor contracts, provides advice and guidance concerning labor/management issues to department leadership and officials; participates in departmental labor management meetings.

          Creates reports on grievance and arbitration cases from case management database and analyzes trends, and recommends strategies and areas of improvement; analyzes labor data and trends to identify areas of improvement and recommends proactive measures.

          Conducts and creates city-wide training on labor contracts and labor relations best practices.

 

EMPLOYEE RELATIONS ASSIGNMENT

          Analyzes federal, state and local laws relevant to the equal employment compliance program of the City; reviews collective bargaining agreements for all Labor unions and interest arbitration awards governing the wages, hours and terms and conditions of employment for represented employees; investigates employee relations concerning complains of discrimination, harassment, and retaliation; performs and leads lower level staff in interviewing complainants, supervisors, managers, witnesses and subject matter experts

          Writes investigation plans to include outlining questions, generating document requests, identifying witnesses; schedules witness interviews and creates interviewing methodology; gathers relevant and supporting documentation and evidence; conducts other duties necessary to comply with EEO regulations and internal policies; counsels complainants in regard to civil service and merit system process procedures and departmental actions.

          Facilitates communication and mediates meetings; conducts investigatory interviews; researches disputes, maintain files and documents of supporting evidence for investigation and prepares exhibits for grievance arbitrations and assists in the presentation of EEO cases; researches topics related to EEO and other employee relations matters; creates and conducts citywide EEO and employee relations policies; functions as a subject matter expert on city-wide EEO policy.

          Identifies training needs and gives EEO trainings to HR community; recommends and conducts training programs for supervisory and managerial level employees on the City’s EEO policy, employee relations and labor relations programs.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS, AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

·       The use of Microsoft Office/Access, other information/data management systems and standard computerized software

·       Investigatory practices and procedures

·       Administrative processes as the relate to EEO

·       Report writing

·       Research and analysis

·       Relevant federal, state, and local legislation including ADA, ADEA, FMLA, Title VII of the Civil Rights Act

·       Labor contracts and collective bargaining agreement

SKILL IN:

·       Interviewing methods and practices

·       Narrative report writing

·       Written and oral communication

  • Evaluating the effectiveness of employee and labor relations programs
  • Conflict resolution
  • Public speaking
  • Problem-solving among organizational levels to achieve mutual labor objectives

 

ABILITY TO:

·       Explain and provide guidance on laws, regulations, policies, standards or procedures

·       Give presentations and briefings to peers, superiors, department managers, employees and union officials

  • Persuade others to take a particular course of action or to accept findings, recommendations, changes, or alternative viewpoints
  • Establish and maintain effective working relationships with managers, peers, employees, and union officials

·       Adjust priorities when appropriate to conduct interviews

·       Maintain confidentiality

·       Analyze and synthesize complex or diverse information

·       Establish and maintain constructive and effective working relationships with the departments, unions, and employees

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience stan­dards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/24.)

EDUCATION

          Completion of a bachelor’s degree program at an accredited college or university.

NOTE:  Additional general or specific experience or completion of an industry related certification may substitute for the education on a year for year basis

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE

          Two years of employee or labor relations experience.

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

          One year of experience performing technical employee relations compliance work as a primary and recurring duty which must have included any of the following:

·       Creating, implementing and interpreting EEO policies that promote EEO, or designing and delivering EEO training programs, or conducting investigations into EEO related complaints

OR

·       Handling complex employee disputes and conflicts and finding mutually agreeable solutions, or conducting employee relations investigations and making recommendations for corrective action

           OR

·        Negotiating the settlement of grievances

 

NOTE: Completion of an industry related Master’s Degree or Juris Doctorate from an accredited college or university may substitute for one year of the general experience.

 

OR

          Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources which includes the specific experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

 

            Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Common­wealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment, if required by work assignment.
