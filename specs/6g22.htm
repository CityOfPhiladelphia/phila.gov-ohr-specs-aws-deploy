6G22
ELECTRICAL CONSTRUCTION INSPECTOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is journeyman level field work inspecting complex electrical installations. Work includes examining and interpreting complex diagrams and specifications for electrical systems of buildings under construction or undergoing major alterations of their electrical systems. Work is performed under the general supervision of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Inspects the installation, overhaul or repair of complex electrical equipment such as pumps, compressors, switching gear, load control equipment, surveillance systems, air conditioning, lighting and power equipment; inspects the installation or renovation of electrical systems at various public facilities and transit lines; examines electrical installations and equipment for conformance to plans and specifications; locates and diagnoses faults and confers with contractors to point out deficiencies and to advise on means of achieving compliance; confers with engineers; other inspectors, architects and utility company personnel to inspect equipment installations and plans; tests equipment; observes tests of equipment conducted by consultants; meets with representatives of contractors and manufacturers to discuss warranties.
  • Examines plans, wiring diagrams and specifications for the installation or alteration of electrical systems to determine their conformance to codes, regulations and related ordinances; checks presence and type of fire alarm and emergency lighting systems; contacts owners or electrical contractors to explain violations; approves changes or modifications of plans or orders the preparation of new electrical plans in cases where revisions are necessary.
  • Completes inspection reports describing conditions found during field investigations; maintains daily log of jobs in progress; cites violations of pertinent codes; makes recommendat- ions for specific actions to take in order to correct violations and improper conditions; makes follow-up inspections to insure that corrective action is taken.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods, materials, practices and principles used in the installation, maintenance and repair of electrical equipment and appurtenances, including those involving hazardous situations.
  • the possible defects and faults in electrical wiring and appurtenances, of the standard testing devices used in their detection, and of effective corrective measures.
  • the codes, regulations and related ordinances governing electrical installations and appurtenances.
  • inspectional methods and techniques.

ABILITY TO:

  • read and interpret complex wiring diagrams and specifications for electrical installations.
  • enforce regulations firmly, tactfully and impartially.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and the public.
  • maintain records and prepare reports.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/1977.)
  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

  • Seven years of experience in the electrical trade, five years of which shall have been in the installation or repair of electrical systems utilizing switchgear and relays and powered by at least 440 volts, reading and interpreting wiring diagrams, and installing, maintaining or repairing hazardous wiring and equipment.

Or any equivalent combination of acceptable training and experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as an Electrical Construction Inspector if required by work assignment.
