3B55
AIR MANAGEMENT STAFF ENGINEER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is environmental engineering work in a staff capacity researching specific aspects of the air management program for the purpose of developing new and improved methods and procedures. Conferring with a wide range of departmental, industrial and governmental officials as well as directing the activities of supportive personnel on these projects are significant aspects of work.

Work is performed with general direction from a higher level engineer. Assignments require analytical thinking and are performed in accordance with functional precedents, practices and policies. An employee in this class completes projects designed to improve the accomplishment of air management objectives and goals.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Receives oral or written assignments of specific air management projects; conducts in- depth investigations of existing practices or procedures and the effect that they have on the air management program both from a general and specific standpoint; develops new or revised methods and procedures designed to improve or correct air management standards and submits them to supervisors for review and implementation.
  • Confers with a wide range of industrial administrative officials as well as those of state, local and federal governments on current and anticipated air management program changes; notes their reaction to proposed changes and recommends revision or modifications based on such findings.
  • Prepares reports of assigned projects indicating progress to date; attends staff meetings with superiors to discuss possible solutions to problems encountered.
  • May supervise a small number of supportive technical employees assigned for a specific project.
  • Meets with and advises air management personnel on newly approved air management programs and policies; answers questions on program implementation.
  • May meet with specialized groups and members of the general public to discuss the details and provisions of a specialized program.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • engineering theory, principles and practices as applied to air pollution control.
  • the laws and regulations applicable to air pollution control.
  • engineering mathematics and mathematical tables used in environmental engineering.
  • the sources of air management engineering information and of current developments in the field.
  • research methods and techniques.

ABILITY TO:

  • read and interpret engineering plans, designs and specifications for technical feasibility.
  • prepare clear, concise, and technically sound engineering reports.
  • plan, assign and supervise the work of a small group of technical and engineering personnel if required by work assignment.
  • establish and maintain effective work relationships with associates, engineers and members of the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience requirements that will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/74.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in chemical, civil, electrical, mechanical or sanitary engineering.
  • Four years of engineering experience in air management work, two years of which shall have been at the full performance level.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience which shall have included completion of a bachelor's degree program in an acceptable area of engineering as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to meet the physical and medical standards approved for this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Eligibility for a valid motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and possession of such licensure within thirty days, and during tenure of employment as an Air Management Staff Engineer if required by work assignment.
