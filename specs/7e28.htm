SOLID WASTE DISPOSAL MANAGER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative work directing the operation, maintenance and repair of the City's transfer stations, and managing its operational relationship with private contracted disposal facilities. The employee in this class develops plans for and directs operational and maintenance procedures to ensure effective and efficient transfer plant operations. Supervision of a building maintenance and repair force, and consultation with area supervisors on production, and repair problems are of major significance to the work. Work involves some disagreeable environmental aspects in conducting inspectional tours of plant facilities. Work is performed under the direction of the Sanitation Operations Administrator.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs the operational, maintenance and repair activities of the city's transfer stations and service buildings by analyzing production reports and making visits to the various facilities to observe operations, maintenance and repair activities; monitors pit levels and revises route schedules to increase dumping efficiency and to reduce the waiting time for trash compactors; informs district supervisors of revised dumping schedules; coordinates revised dumping schedules during emergency situations; redirects trash overflow to various landfill sites; monitors amounts going to each landfill.
  • Coordinates with private disposal contractors for available facilities and capacity; coordinates and directs the routing of sanitation trucks to disposal facilities; negotiates any operational problems that may arise at contracted facilities and resolves problems with municipal operations and union representatives; identifies any long term trends with private contractors and makes appropriate recommendations to superiors to cope with them.
  • Tours transfer stations to inspect and observe the operation of equipment; prepares a preventive maintenance schedule based on findings to maintain equipment at operational levels; instructs plant superintendents of procedures or activities to employ in cases of major breakdowns to equipment; administers landfill agreements; explains contract requirements to landfill officials; assists superiors in initiating contract stipulations.
  • Reviews materials and supply costs of refuse transfer plants for previous and current year and prepares budget for the upcoming year based on anticipated maintenance and repair needs.
  • Reviews requisitions and either approves or disapproves based on transfer plant operations; directs, through a subordinate stores supervisor, the operation of a central stores facility; inspects stores inventories to ensure the most efficient use of materials and supplies.
  • Reviews and recommends the size and the composition of crews needed to make major repairs to transfer stations; consults with area supervisors to develop priorities of work and coordinates major repair activities to ensure maximum effectiveness.
  • Plans, assigns and reviews, through subordinate building maintenance supervisors, the work of a diverse group of trades personnel engaged in providing maintenance to departmental facilities.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices governing the operation and maintenance of transfer stations including overhead bridge cranes and hydraulically operated compaction equipment.
  • occupational hazards and safety precautions involved in the maintenance of the various facilities and mechanical equipment used in transfer stations.
  • the methods, practices, materials, equipment and tools used in the maintenance and repair of transfer plant machinery, equipment and buildings.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
  • administrative methods and techniques required to direct and coordinate the activities of a solid waste disposal plant.
  • current developments in the field of refuse disposals.
  • the operations of landfill sites.

ABILITY TO:

  • coordinate the operations of all municipal disposal transfer plants including repair and maintenance for the greatest efficiency and economy.
  • coordinate the availability of private disposal facilities with municipal operations.
  • supervise employees engaged in the operation, maintenance and repair activities of refuse transfer plants.
  • analyze breakdowns and advise on best method for repairs.
  • detect defects in equipment or its operation and to initiate the proper corrective measures.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, private contractors, and union representatives.
  • represent the department in conferences, hearings, and meetings concerning waste disposal problems.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 6/1987.)
  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

  • Eight years of experience in the operation and maintenance of refuse disposal plants, six years of which have been in a supervisory capacity, including four years in charge of the overall operation and maintenance of a solid waste disposal plant.

  • Or completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in engineering.

  • Four years of engineering experience, two years of which have been above the full performance level with responsibility for the contract administration of major solid waste disposal facilities.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Solid Waste Disposal Manager.
