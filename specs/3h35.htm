3H35
WATER ADMINISTRATIVE CHEMIST

Specialties
( Organics ) ( Inorganics )

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative chemistry work planning, directing and coordinating, through subordinate supervisors, a major phase of the analytical chemistry function of the Water Department's Bureau of Laboratory Services. An employee in this class develops, evaluates and determines the methodologies, personnel, and equipment required to achieve compliance with local, state, and federal rules and regulations for inorganic or organic compound measurement in drinking water, wastewater, sludge and industrial wastes. Contact with representatives of government agencies, industrial plants, and community groups is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

Both Specialties

  • Directs a major phase of the analytical chemistry function of the Bureau of Laboratory Services; plans, assigns, and reviews, through subordinate supervisors, the work of scientific and technical personnel engaged in sampling, monitoring, testing, and evaluating the chemical quality of drinking water in the water distribution system, water from rivers, raw and processed wastewater, sludge and industrial wastes, and researching new and improved water and wastewater treatment processes; reviews analytical programs, activities, and equipment for efficiency and productivity, and recommends and initiates improvements; reviews quality assurance parameters and insures compliance; prepares operating budget proposals for the unit; determines staffing levels and equipment needs; recommends which services can be performed more efficiently by contracted private laboratories; evaluates work performed by contractors. Provides technical guidance during complex testing; designs and directs the design of specialized apparatus to meet specific testing requirements; designs and reviews the design of equipment specifications; evaluates and reviews the evaluation of new equipment and makes recommendations regarding purchase of equipment and contracted services.
  • Coordinates testing with other laboratory units within the Department and in other departments; manages special sampling and analysis projects required for regulatory compliance and regulatory development; approves and assigns special projects.
  • Provides technical assistance to the administration and other agencies in the areas of water, wastewater, sludge, and industrial waste; represents the Department in administrative and legal proceedings involving organic and inorganic chemistry measurement and analysis; makes presentations before civic, industrial, and community groups on environmental testing issues.
Organics Specialty Only

  • Directs, through a subordinate supervisor, the analysis of aquatic biological conditions in water, wastewater, and sludge; reviews new regulatory requirements in organic chemistry; designs and directs the design of new analytical methodologies to meet regulatory changes; performs cost effectiveness evaluations regarding internal performance of required testing and contracted services.
Inorganics Specialty Only

  • Directs, through professional and technical subordinate supervisors, the provision of on- site laboratory services to Wastewater Treatment Plant facilities; ensures scientific review of routine work performed by on-site technicians; monitors Treatment Plant compliance with laboratory findings; directs provision of scientific consultative services to Plant Engineering staff.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

Organics Specialty Only

  • principles and practices of analytical organic chemistry applicable to water, wastewater, and sludge.
  • the literature, research findings, and trends in organic chemistry.
  • the principles and practices of biological analysis of water, wastewater, and sludge.
Inorganics Specialty Only

  • the principles and practices of analytical inorganic chemistry applicable to water, wastewater, and sludge.
  • water pollution control, sludge processing and water treatment plant operations, processes, facilities, and equipment.
Both Specialties

  • modern analytical chemistry methodology and instrumentation as applied to area of specialization.
  • the research and sampling methods and techniques applicable to water and wastewater.
  • current federal and state rules, regulations, and standards applicable to the analysis and treatment of water and wastewater as applied to area of specialization.
  • modern research and administrative methods and techniques required to direct and coordinate a major phase of a laboratory function.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
  • budget preparation.
  • the hazard and safety practices in the laboratory and in field sampling.
  • the computer software utilized in scientific applications.
ABILITY TO:

  • coordinate administrative functions with scientific activities to provide maximum efficiency of operations.
  • resolve scientific and administrative problems.
  • train personnel in various aspects of analytical science, technology, and procedures.
  • express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, governmental officials, plant representatives, and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/89.)

Organics Specialty Only

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in chemistry. Six years of analytical chemistry experience conducting scientific evaluations of water and/or wastewater, three years of which has been evaluating water and/or wastewater for organic compounds, two years of which has been supervising such work.
NOTE:
A master's degree in chemistry or environmental science may be substituted for two years of the general experience.

Inorganics Specialty Only

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in chemistry or chemical engineering. Six years of analytical chemistry experience conducting scientific evaluation of water and/or wastewater, two years of which has been supervising such work, or supervising quality assurance of such work.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included a bachelor's degree as specified above as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES
  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Water Administrative Chemist if required by work assignment.
