RECORD SERVICES MANAGER

General Definition

  • This is administrative work directing the Record's Department Photographic and Reprographic Services Division and overseeing the operations of the City Archives and Records Storage Units. The employee in this class supervises a staff of photographers, and directs through a subordinate supervisor, the activities of printing press operators and photographic scanner operators. A significant aspect of the work includes budget preparation and the on going monitoring and control of the Photographic and Reprographic Services Division’s operating budget. Work includes serving as point of contact with City agencies, departments, contractors and vendors concerning reproduction specifications and standards, equipment maintenance and repairs, special projects, and management of the citywide identification card system. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior and involves light physical exertion, contact with printing and cleansing solutions and exposure to a noisy and dusty environment.

Allocating Factors:

(The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)
  • This is a one position class located in the Records Department.
  • The employee in this class must direct the Record's Department Photographic and Reprographic Services Division and oversee the operations of the City Archives and Records Storage Units.
  • The position must be responsible for the preparation and control of the operating budget for the Photographic and Reprographic Services Division.

Typical Examples Of Work (Illustrative Only)

  • Directs, through a subordinate supervisor, the activities of subordinate staff; reviews requests for photographic and reprographic services from various City agencies and departments; discusses arrangements with requesting agencies and departments; collects and compiles data for preliminary budget preparation; interacts with representatives of various city agencies to maintain an electronic forms management system of job requests over CityNet; follows up work to ascertain that goals and schedules are met; modifies work procedures to achieve more efficient photographic and reprographic services operations.
  • Supervises a staff of photographers engaged in documenting city engineering and renovation projects, security projects, special events, historical buildings and responding to requests for photographs from the general public.
  • Oversees the operations of the City Archives and Records Storage Units; acts as a liaison between the building management office to ensure the facility is in compliance with safety regulations; works with the City’s Risk Management Office to document damage related incidents; attends periodic meetings involving lost control inspections of the facility by the city’s insurer; manages the service contracts for the maintenance and safety of all stored documents; creates and maintains an emergency response plan; provides staff training on the emergency response plan.
  • Determines, in conjunction with agency representatives and the department's analysts, the best methods of production depending on intended use of funds available; confers with agency representatives and the department's analysts to discuss the anticipated budget and material needs in view of proposed production schedules; develops schedules to ensure timely delivery of the division’s services; reviews completed work for conformance to instructions and quality standards.
  • Determines priorities and schedules special projects involving photographic production; maintains most effective utilization of personnel and machines based on workload and machine capabilities; determines the proper utilization of vendor and proprietary software in the production of photographic and reprographic services; maintains libraries of reprographic and forms design templates and software.
  • Manages projects related to incorporating photographic and reprographic services into an integrated citywide plan; works with external agencies and cultural institutions to coordinate special projects and exhibits.
  • Schedules and assigns work to a subordinate supervisor.
  • Reviews work of a subordinate supervisor for conformance to established policies and standards.
  • Requisitions materials and supplies; develops requests for proposals; maintains production and cost records; prepares reports concerning production, and project costs; prepare reports supporting planning, research, analysis, purchases, contract oversight and budget for the division.
  • Makes recommendations for effective use of funds and technology for services to the operating department and other city agencies.
  • Reviews developments in technology and software including applications related to data security, disaster recovery and internet technology to properly plan for ongoing operations of the division.
  • Trains subordinate personnel and representatives from various city agencies on new and existing equipment and technology.
  • Conducts disciplinary hearings for staff and subordinate supervisors.
  • Operates reprographic equipment as required; mixes chemical solutions; cleans and makes minor adjustments and repairs to equipment; ensures that safety precautions are observed regarding chemicals, noise and dirt/dust.
  • Performs related work as required.

Required Knowledge, Skills And Abilities

Knowledge Of:

  • the principles, practices and procedures of reprographics as they relate to the areas of photography, offset duplicating and digital photocopy work
  • the operation of direct positive copying, collating, scanning and associated equipment, and duplicating
  • the uses and adaptabilities of a variety of photographic reproduction machines
  • the various kinds of paper, ink and chemicals used in photography, duplicating, and direct positive photocopying processes
  • administrative and organization practices that improve overall efficiency; the principles of strategic planning
  • supervisory methods and practices
  • inventory control practices
  • the principles, practices, materials and equipment used in a photographic services unit
  • software and technology as they apply to the field of reprographics and photography; data management principles pertaining to disaster recovery and data security; tools and techniques used in converting historical and unit data into internet applications
  • the hazards of the equipment, chemicals, and exhaust gases as well as work site dangers of both the photography and central duplicating units
  • the safety precautions and air purification and exhaust systems necessary to protect the health and welfare of employees
  • the maintenance of and minor repairs necessary for photographic reproduction, duplicating, blueprint and whiteprint equipment

Ability To:

  • oversee production and records of work progress
  • order and maintain materials, supplies, services and contracts based on present and anticipated work load
  • evaluate software and technology as they apply to the field of reprographics and photography and make recommendations to management based on cost, efficiency and compatibility with existing systems; participate in deployment of applications to the internet to distribute information to the public
  • troubleshoot and diagnose malfunctions and perform necessary minor maintenance and repair tasks or refer problems to contracted vendors for resolution to keep photographic and reprographic equipment operational
  • provide cost estimates to city agencies for chargeable work
  • supervise effectively
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, vendors and officials of various city departments
  • recognize unsafe or hazardous working conditions and implement safety precautions as needed
  • prepare and maintain an emergency response plan
  • provide staff training on emergency response plan
  • prepare and maintain records, reports and inventory
  • prepare and monitor the Division’s operating budget
  • coordinate special projects and exhibits
  • train personnel on new and existing equipment and technology
  • provide cost benefit analysis for new equipment and services

Minimum Acceptable Training And Experience

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 9/11.)

Education

  • Completion of a bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or university in business administration, printing, photography, micrographics, engineering or a closely related field. AND

General Experience

  • Three years of experience operating a variety of photographic and/or reprographic equipment and related accessories. AND

Specific Experience

  • Two years of supervisory experience in photographic and/or reprographic services operations.

OR

  • any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources which has included the specific experience described above.

NOTE:

Additional years of specific experience may be substituted for the education requirement on a year for year basis.
Physical And Medical Requirements
  1. Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
