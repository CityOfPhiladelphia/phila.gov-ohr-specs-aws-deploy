CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

3G41
CRIMINALISTICS TECHNICIAN I

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is forensic laboratory work of limited scope and difficulty performing microscopic and macroscopic examinations of organic and inorganic substances for the purpose of identifying, typing and comparing most biological evidence involved in criminal cases. This class is distinguished from that of the next higher level in that employees work under more direct supervision and do not perform the full scope of examinations. Work expands in scope and approaches the full performance level as employees gain experience in the work. Work is performed under the supervision of a technical superior, and involves some disagreeable aspects.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Conducts tests to determine presence of semen on wet and dry specimens by preparing stain mixture and adding chemical reagents, and noting formation of crystals and color changes; stains and examines semen under microscope.
  • Prepares extract from suspected dry blood stain; mixes and adds proper chemical reagents; notes color changes; conducts controlled precipitin test to differentiate between human and animal blood; examines blood stains microscopically and tests blood to determine type and group.
  • Compares certain trace materials and other organic and inorganic materials found on suspect or victim with those materials found at the crime scene.
  • Learns characteristics of hair to determine origin by microscopic examinations; learns other techniques of criminalistic examinations.
  • Prepares reports of results of laboratory tests conducted, indicating probability of crime or violation of statutes or ordinances.
  • May learn a limited range of chemical testing procedures and techniques for use in the analysis of controlled substances evidence.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the practices, procedures, and techniques used to prepare, examine, and analyze forensic laboratory specimens.
  • the principles, methods and techniques involved in applying the disciplines of chemistry and biology to tests performed.
  • the use of modern instrumentation in the areas of microscopy and electrophoresis as applied to tests performed.
  • the rules of evidence and the methods used in presenting evidence in court.
ABILITY TO:

  • perform laboratory tests in accordance with prescribed criminalistics procedures and to make accurate observations and analyses of test results.
  • write and interpret scientifically accurate and comprehensive reports of tests conducted.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/1974.)

  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade supplemented by
  • One year of experience performing forensic laboratory work on a variety of physical evidence related to civil and criminal litigation, and interpreting tests results.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined acceptable by the Personnel Department which shall have included the education requirements as stated above as the educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
