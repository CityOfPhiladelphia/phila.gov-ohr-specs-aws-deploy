VIDEO PRODUCTION SPECIALIST

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

This is technical video production work with responsibility for all aspects of media production and distribution for digital, online and broadcast delivery platforms for the Philadelphia Police Department or for the Philadelphia International Airport. Employees in this class are responsible for providing technical and creative direction to staff responsible for video editing, camera operation and production. An employee in this class plans, coordinates, scripts, produces, edits and directs intricate single and multi-camera shoots for the production of public service, promotional, training and recruitment videos. Ensuring all phases from planning to post-production are completed within established timelines and budgets, managing content development and collaborating with audio visual, social media, graphic design and public affairs staff is significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the general direction of an administrative supervisor.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

  1. Employees in this class must perform technical video production work with responsibility for all aspects of television and media production and distribution for digital, online and broadcast media delivery platforms.
  2. Employees in this class are responsible for providing technical and creative direction to staff responsible for video editing, camera operation and production.
  3. Positions in this class must be located in the Philadelphia Police Department or at the Philadelphia International Airport.
  4. Only one position will be allocated to the class in each department

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

            Provides technical and creative direction to staff engaged in video shooting and production of videos for promotional, training, public service and recruitment purposes; directs staff in the use of video production equipment and the production of videos; coordinates and manages all aspects of video production including, writing scripts, scheduling client/subject consultations and pre-production and budget meetings; meets with department leadership to ensure material is aligned with overall goals and to discuss product outcomes; identifies stories that involve the department and community members to strengthen community connections and support aviation or public safety; assesses the scope and complexity of assignments; identifies budget for productions and makes recommendations to supervisor; coordinates with project team members; ensures all phases from initial planning to post-production are completed within established timelines and budgets.

            Determines and executes video concept and storyboard, art direction and audio design; serves as the editorial lead for department video and web stories; oversees the production of video programs to highlight departmental accomplishments, projects and events; formulates edit decision lists; conducts location surveys, develops shooting schedules, and directs all multi and single camera shoots; monitors and improves processes to ensure the timely completion and high quality execution of projects; works collaboratively with video editors and shooters on final product; selects raw footage, digitizes media (video, photos, music, dialog and sound effects), creates graphics, renders effects, and edits sequences; finalizes video content for digital and internet media delivery platforms not limited to web platforms, YouTube, City operated Government Access Channels and commercial channels.

            Operates audio and video equipment as necessary such as cameras, switchers, microphones, audio mixers, videotape recorders/players, edit controllers, character generators and lighting instruments; oversees and plans studio and field set design and lighting to meet production needs.

            Performs research to resolve and coordinate department-wide expectations with production requirements.

            Interacts with department stakeholders, businesses, community groups and community members; collaborates with social media, graphic design and public affairs staff in completion of video production.

            Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

  • writing techniques for video and multimedia stories
  • professional standard interview techniques
  • media production, communication, and dissemination techniques and methods
  • production and post-production processes
  • principles and practices of video production techniques, language, equipment and applications
  • methods and techniques for producing studio and field shoots
  • management principles involved in strategic planning and resource allocation related to efficient video production
  • project management principles and techniques
  • structure and content of the English language
  • operation of video production equipment
  • video editing principles, production methods, equipment and techniques
  • video editing software such as Final Cut
  • web video platforms such as Vimeo and YouTube

 

SKILL IN:

 

  • news style story writing
  • guest and interview booking
  • creative storytelling ability
  • the operation of field video equipment
  • video editing and scriptwriting
  • operating Mac OS X and PC equipment, software, and terminology related to the work

 

ABILITY TO:

 

  • identify and research stories that support various organizational themes, strategies and initiatives
  • recognize critical visual, auditory and editorial elements needed for a solid video story
  • write television news style video stories and multimedia stories for web
  • collaborate with graphic design, audio visual and public affairs staff to meet goals
  • edit video/audio recordings and still images and create finished products suited to their intended purpose
  • operate field video/audio and photographic equipment to secure images for finished projects
  • oversee and provide creative technical direction to video production staff
  • communicate information and ideas orally and in writing
  • ensure all phases from initial planning to post production are completed within established timelines and budgets
  • work cooperatively and productively with a variety of individuals in the planning, design and execution of video and multimedia projects
  • work independently with minimal technical direction

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 8/16.)

 

EDUCATION:

           

            Completion of a Bachelor’s degree at an accredited college or university in Mass Communications, Public Relations, Radio/TV Broadcasting, Digital Filmmaking, Video Production or a closely related field.

 

AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

           

            Two years of experience performing video production work for a television news, public affairs, creative services or film production company.

 

AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

           

            Two years of full time technical video production experience in field or segment producing, in a television broadcast market or a film production company, which has included responsibility for script writing and video editing.

 

OR

 

            Any equivalent combination of acceptable education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources which has included the completion of a bachelor's degree program in the areas indicated above.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

 

            The position at the Philadelphia International Airport must be able to pass and maintain SIDA (Security Identification Display Area) and AOA (Airfield Operations Area) clearance.
