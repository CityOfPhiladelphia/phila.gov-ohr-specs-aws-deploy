JUNIOR LAW ENFORCEMENT ANALYST

 

 GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is crime information management and intelligence analysis work for the Philadelphia Police Department at the entry level.  Employees in this class may attend training sessions and participates on assignments and/or other tasks designed to familiarize them with law enforcement information management systems, crime analysis, intelligence analysis, and research. Assignments vary in nature and increase in complexity as the employee gains proficiency. Employees work on assigned shifts in the Real-Time Crime Center assignment. Work is performed under the supervision of a technical superior.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS:

 

1.    Positions in this class are responsible for learning to perform quantitative or qualitative analysis to support decision-making for law enforcement or other agency purposes.

2.    Positions in this class must be in the Philadelphia Police Department’s Intelligence Bureau.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

ALL ASSIGNMENTS

 

Learns how to perform analysis using crime, economic and demographic data to project crime trends and patterns in accordance with industry tradecraft; performs evaluation and analysis of less complex data using standard qualitative and quantitative methods; assists with writing reports documenting results of findings; may assist in formal presentations of findings.

Under technical direction, assists with planning intelligence or research questions and objectives for crime analysis products; collates data collection from computer systems, field observations and interviews; learns to apply appropriate analytical tradecraft, and develops output reports for analysis products; collects, collates, and analyzes less complex data and information from various sources including police information systems, surveillance cameras, confidential sources, public records, social media, and other open source systems.

Confers with departmental representatives; learns and reviews departmental policies; learns procedures, forms and systems used for crime data collection and analysis.

Under technical direction, collaborates with representatives from other government organizations to share information or coordinate intelligence and research activities.

Learns to design or modify current information systems and programs using a variety of methods and techniques.

Learns to review literature and investigates new analytical techniques in program analysis and evaluation.

Performs related work as required.

 

REAL-TIME CRIME CENTER ASSIGNMENT

 

Supports the 24-hour Real-Time Crime Center’s (RTCC) operations through the analysis of 911 calls, radio, crime tips, and other sources; responds directly to Requests for Information from Philadelphia Police or external agency partners; redirects requests to the appropriate unit within the Intelligence Bureau.

Learns to provide operational or tactical-level crime analysis or intelligence products to support real-time field operations.

 

OPERATIONS ASSIGNMENT

 

Learns to perform tactical-level crime analysis or develop intelligence products for field operations and the needs of the Police Captain; employees in this assignment are detailed to Operational Units such as Police Districts or Investigative Units.

Learns to serve as analytical liaison between the assigned Operational Unit and the Intelligence Bureau.

 

INTELLIGENCE ASSIGNMENT

 

Studies activities relating to narcotics, human trafficking, cyber security, money laundering, gangs, crime, terrorism, or other national security threats.

Learns to produce strategic, operational, or tactical analytical reports; learns to establish criminal profiles to aid in connecting criminal organizations with their members.

 

RESEARCH ASSIGNMENT

           

Performs quantitative analysis techniques, including using geospatial analysis software tools, for the development of analytical crime data products; conducts literature reviews and best practice research to identify innovative policing programs to recommend for implementation; works with program managers and unit commanders to establish performance measurement systems.

Learns to perform comparative research on data compiled by law enforcement or intelligence agencies in other jurisdictions.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         standard sources and uses of information in law enforcement

·         qualitative and quantitative analysis techniques

·         application of basic statistics

·         principles, methods and techniques for research, analysis and data collection

·         computer based information systems used for data gathering, organization, and analysis

·         the principles and practices of utilizing computers for management reporting and analysis

·         information system resources needed to design computerized data entry

 

SKILL IN:

 

·         using analytical techniques and scientific principles to evaluate data


ABILITY TO:

 

·         learn to perform crime analysis using economic and demographic data

·         learn to project crime trends and patterns

·         learn additional query languages, report writers, interactive database programs, spreadsheets and/or application generators or other software as necessary

·         learn database management, geospatial software, and other software packages to manage data and produce tables, maps, and graphic displays if required by assignment

·         learn to design qualitative studies for intelligence analysis

·         write a variety of narrative reports

·         process and analyze data using query languages, report writers, interactive database programs, spreadsheets and/or application generators.

·         express ideas and facts clearly and effectively both orally and in writing

·         establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/21.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Completion of a bachelor’s degree program at an accredited college or university with major coursework in criminology, criminal justice, crime science, data science, geography, intelligence analysis, mathematics, statistics, economics, or a closely related social science field.

 

 PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

             Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

 

            Must be able to pass, prior to appointment, a local, state and national criminal background check including a fingerprint-based check as required by the Federal Bureau of Investigation's (FBI) Criminal Justice Information System (CJIS) Security Policy.
