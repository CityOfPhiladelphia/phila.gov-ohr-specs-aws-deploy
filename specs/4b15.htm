CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

4B15
RESIDENT CARE SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is resident care work supervising personal care services rendered to adult and elderly residents on an assigned shift. Employees are responsible for the personal hygiene of male and female residents and for the maintenance of their quarters. Work is performed under the supervision of a higher level supervisor. Employees participate with others in caring for the residents on a shift basis. Work frequently requires light physical exertion.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • The position must supervise personal care services to adult and elderly residents.
  • The position must supervise Health Care Aides engaged in providing personal care services to adult and elderly residents.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Assigns work to a group of aides; inspects grounds and cottages to see that residents are properly cared for; inspects residents to see that they are neat, clean and properly dressed.
  • Directs aides in proper methods and techniques of lifting, turning, bathing and feeding incapacitated residents, handling difficult residents and making beds and other duties; conducts in-service training classes.
  • Schedules subordinates' daily work assignments; assigns subordinates to various locations within the home to ensure adequate coverage in all areas; approves overtime to maintain necessary coverage; approves requests for shift changes, as appropriate.
  • Participates in caring for residents as a member of a team providing residential services.
  • Attends in-service training classes as assigned.
  • Maintains records and prepares reports.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and techniques of resident care in the areas of feeding, bathing and dressing.
  • housekeeping and sanitary requirements in a large residential care facility.
  • personal hygiene requirements of adult and elderly residents.
ABILITY TO:

  • supervise and instruct a group of subordinates engaged in residential care.
  • maintain routine records and prepare reports.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with residents, staff and associates.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/95.)

EDUCATION:

  • Equivalent to completion of the tenth school grade.
AND
EXPERIENCE:
  • One year of full-time paid experience caring for or having custody of adults and elderly residents.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Certification of attendance at an approved school of nursing aides and eligibility for state registration.
