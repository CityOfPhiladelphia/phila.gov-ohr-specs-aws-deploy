CROSSING GUARD DISTRICT MANAGER

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

             This is second level supervisory administrative and field work directing the activities of crossing guard staff and clerical support staff in an assigned geographic district.  Employees in the class have primary responsibility for ensuring optimal crossing guard coverage throughout their district, particularly at critical intersections, and providing safe routes for K-5 students within the district who walk, cycle, and get on/off transit and walk to schools.  The work of the class involves directing the activities of a large staff of crossing guards and clerical support staff.  Contact with the general public and communicating effectively with subordinates, other City agencies and City officials are significant aspects of the work.  The employee reports to an administrative superior.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

            Evaluates and defines the overall objectives and priorities within the assigned district in coordination with program leadership; reviews, recommends, and provides input to program policies; evaluates, records, and reports conditions of student crossings within assigned districts; assess requests for new crossing guard locations within assigned district, in coordination with program leadership.

            Establishes safe walking routes to schools for K-5 students within assigned district; works with schools and community representatives to define, standardize, and coordinate safe walking routes for K-5 students.

            Plans and assigns the work of subordinate staff; schedules and ensures adequate staffing; approves leave requests; prepares subordinates time sheets; arranges for duty coverage during emergencies and staff shortages in adherence with program policy; ensures work is performed in accord with appropriate standards; conducts comprehensive formal classroom and field training for newly hired crossing guards to ensure they are comprehensively instructed on relevant policies, procedures and techniques.

            Reviews various operational, activity, vacancy and attendance reports and makes decisions to improve overall program efficiency and performance; prepares reports pertaining to program operations and staff and recommends operational and efficiency changes to improve program performance.

            Supervises employees assigned to direct and control the movement of school children at an assigned intersection.

            Serves as primary program representative to address and resolve community issues; answers inquiries and gives direction to the public; investigates complaints; attends community meetings and presents program information to the public as needed; plans and carries out assignments while resolving conflicts and problems.

            Serves as mandated reporter under the Child Protective Services Law; maintains records of all activities and actions taken              

            Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         principles, practices, and techniques to effectively supervise a large staff

·         general safety and security principles and practices.

·         City of Philadelphia motor vehicle regulations

·         geographic layout of the City of Philadelphia

·          principles and methods of organization, program funding, planning and scheduling

·          best practices for community engagement related to program activities

 

SKILL IN:

 

·         communicating with diverse audiences

·         assessing and interpreting large amounts of data

·         influencing and negotiating

·         effective conflict resolution approaches

·         writing reports

·          making program recommendations aligned with community voices, departmental program goals and safety principles

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·         supervise a group of field personnel engaged in directing children

·         establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, officials and the public

·         remain alert and to act quickly and calmly in traffic emergencies

·         communicate specific instructions clearly, firmly, and courteously

·         understand laws, statutes and regulations affecting pedestrian safety

 

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/22.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

 

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

            Two years of experience in traffic or pedestrian safety, program enforcement or compliance, or high-volume operations, maintenance, or administrative work.

 

OR

 

            Two years of experience as a School Crossing Guard.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

            Two years of supervisory experience in any of the following:

 

·         traffic or pedestrian safety

·         program enforcement or compliance

·         high-volume and large-scale operations

·         commercial or industrial maintenance

·         a variety of administrative services for a division directing clerical staff.

 

OR

 

            Two years of experience as a Crossing Guard Supervisor or equivalent.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

 

            Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Common­wealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of appointment as a Crossing Guard District Manager.

            Child Protective Service Law requires FBI criminal history background check as well as the Pennsylvania State Police criminal history background check and Pennsylvania child abuse background check as a condition of initial employment and during tenure of employment.

NOTE: If the FBI/Pennsylvania State Police criminal background checks reveal the existence of a disqualifying offense, additional documentation must be submitted as required by the City of Philadelphia to determine eligibility for appointment to or continued employment in the class under the Child Protective Services Law.
