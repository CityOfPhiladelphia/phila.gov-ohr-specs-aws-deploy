CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

6H23
CONSTRUCTION FIRE PLANS SPECIALIST

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is code compliance work examining plans and blueprints for the construction of multi-story commercial, industrial and institutional establishments and multiple-occupancy dwellings for conformance with the requirements of the housing and fire code and related codes and ordinances incorporated into the fire code. This class differs from the next lower level in the series by the greater difficulty and variety of plans examined. Discussing construction plans and code requirements with applicants is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the supervision of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)
  • Examines plans and blueprints for construction of multi-story commercial, industrial and institutional establishments and multiple-occupancy dwellings submitted by builders, engineers and architects for conformance to the housing and fire code and related codes and ordinances incorporated into the fire code; makes determinations of compliance in situations not specifically covered in codes on the basis of fire and safety standards; notates code infractions discovered and licenses and permits required on plans and blueprints.
  • Explains to builders, contractors and architects the necessary changes for code compliance; makes suggestions for changes in construction to conform to codes and prepares sketches for applicants to follow; answers requests for information pertinent to the fire and related codes and ordinances.
  • Testifies before review board when applicants appeal code compliance determination, explaining provisions of the codes involved and reasons for plans not meeting code requirements.
  • Maintains records of licenses needed by builders; periodically checks to ensure that licenses required have been issued; files complaint with appropriate inspection districts if license has not been issued.
  • Administers, scores and reviews multiple choice written examinations for individuals requiring City licenses for trades occupations relating to mechanical equipment installation and operation.
  • Prepares and maintains routine records and reports.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the fire code and related codes, ordinances and regulations incorporated into the fire code as they apply to the inspection of commercial, industrial and institutional establishments and large, multiple-occupancy dwellings.
  • fire safety practices and equipment as they relate to commercial, industrial and institutional establishments, as promulgated by the National Fire Protection Association, National Board of Fire Underwriters and similar associations.
  • the housing code and related regulations.
  • basic industrial processes and practices with emphasis on the handling and storage of volatile and flammable materials.
  • methods and techniques of plans examination.
ABILITY TO:

  • express ideas effectively orally and in writing.
  • read and interpret plans, blueprints and sketches.
  • enforce codes, ordinances and regulations firmly, tactfully and impartially.
  • administer, score, review, and maintain security of written examination for City licenses.
  • prepare reports and maintain routine records.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with applicants, associates and the public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/1988.)

  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

  • Five years of field inspection and/or fire code review experience, two years of which has included inspecting commercial, industrial, and institutional establishments for conformance with fire codes and ordinances, or one year examining plans and blueprints for the construction or alteration of commercial, industrial and institutional establishments for conformance with the fire codes, ordinances and regulations.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience that has been determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
