JUVENILE DETENTION FACILITY GUARD MANAGER

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is juvenile detention security guard work at the managerial level for the Department of Human Services. The employee in this class manages through subordinate supervisor’s lower level staff responsible for performing security and custodial services for the Juvenile Justice Services Center. The employee in this class is responsible for preparing work schedules, ensuring all safety standards are met and recommending and implementing approved measures, procedures and policies to ensure the safety of the facility. Reviewing and finalizing incident and accident reports and making recommendations to remedy any reoccurrence of security breaches is a significant aspect of the work. The employee has significant contact with the general public and with the Police and Fire Departments in the event of security threats or safety issues. Work is performed under the supervision of an administrative superior.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.    The employee must perform youth detention security guard work at the managerial level for the Department of Human Services.

2.    The position must be located in the Juvenile Justice Services Center.

3.    Only one position will be allocated to this class.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

Recommends and implements approved security measures to prevent theft of property; recommends and implements approved custodial standards to ensure cleanliness and to prevent bodily injury attributed to slip and falls; ensures proper and adequate responses to protect against fire, vandalism, disruptive and illegal actions; recommends and implements approved procedures for screening visitors; inspects motor vehicles entering and leaving the grounds; assess incidents and reports, fire, vandalism and other disturbances to the appropriate agencies; confers with administrative personnel to recommend new security and custodial policies and procedures; implements methods to advise staff of new procedures and departmental policies; operates an automotive vehicle to transport staff to hospitals, training and in snow emergencies.

Manages, through subordinate supervisor’s, the activities of subordinates responsible for patrolling building and facility premises to provide security services and performing custodial services for the facility; ensures compliance with the Juvenile Justice Services Center policies and security and safety procedures; prepares work schedules based upon need for continuous coverage and shift requirements; reviews work assignments for security guards; may alters work schedule and assignments as needed with prior approval from administrative superior.

Patrols grounds and facility to determine if any breaches of security exist, to ensure that all facility guards are at their assigned stations and to ensure compliance with safety standards; may participate in security activities of lower level staff, including screening visitors, answering complex inquires and directing visitors to appropriate areas;  reviews and finalizes incident and accident reports for conformance to format, parties involved, explanation of facts, action taken and any further actions required; investigates incidents, accidents, vandalism and other reported problems; may interview parties involved and witnesses; obtains description of alleged violators, stolen property and sequence of events; may discuss findings with the Police Department as necessary; prepares reports of findings, conclusions and makes recommendations to prevent reoccurrence.

Participates in administering the in-service training program in security and custodial techniques and procedures; provides direction to new employees in security and custodial techniques, duty assignments and special security and custodial problems; explains new directives or regulations; submits recommendations to superiors for training needs.

Oversees all custodial activities to ensure completion, adherence to procedure and cleanliness of the facility.

Maintains appropriate records and prepares reports.

Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

  • City of Philadelphia practices and procedures used in guarding facilities and property and regulating the activities of the public
  • relevant equipment, policies, procedures, and strategies to promote effective security operations

·         security techniques and principles

·         approved safety and security practices and procedures for screening individuals entering and exiting the Juvenile Justice Services Center

·         Juvenile Justice Services Center security policies and procedures

  • principles and processes for providing customer service
  • scheduling methods and techniques
  • the typical security problems involved in protecting a facility
  • the standard methods for reporting disturbances and fires
  • the occupational hazards and safety precautions used in protective work
  • methods, materials and equipment used in performing a variety of custodial tasks
  • managerial and training methods and techniques.

 

SKILL IN:

 

·         analyzing situations and objectively and determining the proper course of action

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·         manage and direct the work of lower level staff

·         make work schedules and assignments for operational efficiency

·         apply routine security techniques and principles

·         report or communicate unusual incidents, disturbances or maintenance problems to superiors

·         prepare clear, concise, and accurate written reports

·         understand and follow verbal and written instructions

·         establish and maintain effective working relationships with police, public officials, associates and the general public

·         react quickly and efficiently in emergency situations

·         work safely without presenting a direct threat to self or others

·         oversee custodial duties for compliance to recognized standards

·         recommend and implement approved security measures

·         understand, speak, read and write in the English language

·         participate in the administration of in-service training programs in security and custodial techniques

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/21.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

 

AND GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

            Three years of experience performing protective work enforcing security and conduct, rules and regulations for a public building or facility.

 

AND SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

            One year of experience a Juvenile Detention Facility Guard Supervisor or as a Municipal Guard Supervisor or as a Security Officer 2.

 

OR

 

            Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined acceptable by the Office of Human Resources.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

 

            Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of appointment as a Juvenile Detention Facility Guard Manager.

 

Ability to pass and maintain the following clearances at the time of appointment and during tenure as a Juvenile Detention Facility Guard Manager:

  • Pennsylvania State Police Criminal Record Check
  • Pennsylvania Child Abuse History Clearance
  • Federal Criminal History Background Check - (FBI) fingerprint based record check
