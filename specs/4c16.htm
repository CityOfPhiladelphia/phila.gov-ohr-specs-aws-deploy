PATIENT CARE COORDINATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is nursing work planning, reviewing and coordinating the provision of medical, nursing, therapy, and related care and rehabilitative services for patients in a skilled nursing home facility. An employee in this class coordinates the development of comprehensive individual patient care plans to meet the specific needs of residents. Contact with departmental personnel in other disciplines in the development and delivery of patient care services is a major aspect of the work. Work is performed under the general supervision of a nursing home administrator.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Coordinates the development of individual patient care plans for residents in a skilled nursing home facility; conducts patient care, plans meetings with representatives from various disciplines in the facility including medicine, nursing, therapy, social service and food service; participates in discussions pertaining to assessment of patients' needs and problems and derives a group consensus concerning the short and long-term care and rehabilitation goals, and the anticipated time required for achieving these goals; maintains minutes of the meetings and documents patient care plans on charts; meets with nursing personnel to select patients for review and gather data to be presented at meetings.
  • Conducts utilization review for patients on a scheduled basis and as patients' conditions warrant; compiles data from medical records to assess patients' requirements and level of care to be provided; makes recommendations to relocate or change level of nursing care for individual patients.
  • Develops and makes presentations to nursing home personnel for instruction on the objectives of the patient care plan and to improve patient care procedures.
  • Makes rounds with the medical staff to keep aware of the condition of patients and insure that the nursing home staff is aware of the patient care plan and adheres to its schedule; consults with representatives from various disciplines to resolve problems encountered in implementing the patient care plan.
  • Performs quality assurance reviews of patient care services; identifies and recommends solutions to problems; composes periodic reports on findings; assists in the development of monitoring methodologies; monitors problem areas to determine progress being made; reports ongoing monitoring activities to quality assurance committee; prepares annual summaries of activities by discipline.
  • Conducts audits on selected nursing home procedures and activities; researches and establishes criteria for audits; develops recommendations to improve nursing home procedures and promote more effective and efficient care of patients.
  • Attends meetings with medical, nursing, administrative and support personnel to discuss procedures, standards, and policies pertaining to nursing home operations.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • nursing theory, principles, practices, and techniques.
  • the purpose, direction and function of nursing homes and their relationship with hospitals, other health agencies and the community.
  • current developments in the field of nursing particularly as they pertain to the operation of a skilled nursing home.
  • laws and regulations governing skilled nursing home operations.
  • the theories of gerontology and the application of these theories to a skilled nursing home facility.

ABILITY TO:

  • plan, organize and coordinate the development and implementation of patient care plans among various disciplines in a nursing home setting.
  • evaluate the effectiveness of patient care functions and develop procedural changes to provide for more effective and efficient operations.
  • prepare and analyze reports as they pertain to nursing and patient care services.
  • establish and maintain adequate records relating to patient care services.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with professional and non-professional nursing home personnel.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 8/89.)

  • Graduation from an accredited school of nursing.
  • Three years of nursing experience, two years of which has been in a long-term care facility providing nursing care to patients or residents which has included patient care planning.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

NOTE:

Two years of experience coordinating the provision of various patient care and housekeeping services in a nursing home facility may be substituted for the two years of specific experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a license as a registered nurse issued by the Pennsylvania State Board of Nurse Examiner prior to appointment.
