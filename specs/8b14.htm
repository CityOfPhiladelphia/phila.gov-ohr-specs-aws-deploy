FOOD PROGRAM COORDINATOR

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is administrative food program work coordinating, through seasonal neighborhood site staff, a year-round nutritional meal and snack program serving children at various remote sites throughout the city.  Work includes meeting with neighborhood, civic and religious groups and representatives of other City agencies to elicit cooperation with the year-round food program, which consists of an After School Snack Program and Summer Food Program. Recruiting and selecting seasonal neighborhood site staff, and staffing food distribution sites, and providing training to seasonal program staff are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

  1. The position in the class must coordinate all operations of the year-round nutritional meal and snack program for children.
  2. The position in the class must be located in the Parks and Recreation Department.
  3. Only one position will be allocated to this class.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

            Directs, through seasonal program staff, the After School Snack and Summer Food Program; coordinates food/snack distribution at recreation centers, community centers, playgrounds and playstreets; directs site coordinators in monitoring site operation for conformance to State and departmental program guidelines; reviews reports of site operations; contacts site leaders and vendor to address complaints and correct problems concerning food delivery and service; performs unannounced inspections of site operations to ensure conformance to program guidelines.

            Selects and trains temporary and seasonal program staff; recruits site coordinators; develops and conducts individual and group training sessions on program guidelines; develops and distributes training manuals to seasonal program staff; meets with program officials to provide feedback concerning problems in adhering to program guidelines, and communicates changes in program guidelines and procedures.

            Meets with program officials, civic and community groups, and representatives of other agencies to explain program objectives and to elicit cooperation with program operation in the community; explains state requirements for site approval; meets with interested individuals at proposed sites to explain parameters to meet State approval; approves sites for food distribution in accordance with requirements; coordinates with other city departments to facilitate program activities; confers with appropriate city departments when services are needed at distribution sites; participates in requests for proposals and contracting process for vendors; confers with Procurement officials regarding vendor contract issues.

            Reviews program accounting records to ensure salary, vendor and miscellaneous expense data is maintained according to State and City guidelines; submits reimbursement vouchers to the State; makes program operational and financial and documentation available for independent audit.

            Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         the regulations, rules, and guidelines promulgated by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania applicable to the operation of the After School and Summer Food Program

·         the methods and techniques used in the formation and operation of neighborhood service groups

·         local community, civic and social organizations as well as other resources for recruiting neighborhood workers

·         the principles and practices of public speaking

·         food program operations

SKILL IN:

 

·         ensuring safe and effective food program operations

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·         coordinate the activities of a large number of site coordinators in a program designed to provide meals and/or snacks to children

·         prepare substantiating financial and administrative documentation necessary for food program funding

·         express ideas effectively both orally and in writing

·         establish and maintain effective working relationships with program officials, associates, neighborhoods, and civic groups, representatives of other City agencies, and recipients of the food service

·         develop and conduct individual and group training sessions

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experi­ence requirements as approved on 2/21.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

 

AND

EXPERIENCE:

 

            Three years of experience coordinating the operation of citywide food program distribution sites, ensuring conformance to City and State program guidelines in the Department of Parks and Recreation.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Common­wealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of appointment as a Food Program Coordinator if required by work assignment.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the envi­ronmental conditions required of a position in this class.
