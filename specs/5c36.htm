SENIOR HUMAN RELATIONS REPRESENTATIVE

                                                                                                     (Bilingual)

 (General)

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

This is advanced, specialized work in the field of human relations with a focus on ensuring equal opportunities and fair treatment across diverse community groups. Employees in this class perform complex and high-level assignments related to civil rights compliance, community engagement, conflict resolution, and fair housing initiatives. Work includes leading investigations, resolving sensitive neighborhood disputes, and serving as senior-level advisors and mediators in issues involving discrimination and civil rights. Employees in this class review the work of and provide guidance to lower-level representatives, ensuring the efficient delivery of human relations programs and services. Work is performed under the direction of higher-level administrative or technical superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

 

1.     No more than 6 positions shall be allocated to this class.

2.     Positions must be responsible for advanced, specialized work in the field of human relations with a focus of ensuring equal opportunities and fair treatment across diverse community groups.

3.      Positions must be located at the City's Human Relations Commission.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

BOTH SPECIALTIES

 

Leads complex investigations and case management for housing discrimination, community disputes, and civil rights violations; serves as a senior resource and leads lower-level human relations representatives, providing guidance, technical support, and training on handling sensitive cases; manages the intake process for complaints, determines the appropriateness of cases for department services, and makes recommendations for further investigation or referral; reviews case findings and directs team members in preparing reports and documentation for enforcement hearings and legal proceedings.

Leads outreach efforts to promote fair housing and civil rights awareness within diverse communities; serves as the primary liaison with community leaders, advocacy groups, and other stakeholders, fosters relationships and ensures productive collaboration; organizes and leads educational workshops, seminars, and public presentations on civil rights and community integration topics; represents the organization in meetings with local, state, and federal agencies, and at public hearings related to civil rights and housing policies.

Leads mediations on high-profile or complex neighborhood disputes, develops action plans, and negotiates resolutions between opposing parties; provides guidance on conflict de-escalation strategies and mediates conflicts that require specialized knowledge of civil rights law and community dynamics; leads fact-finding conferences and settlement negotiations to resolve complaints and achieve conciliation.

Analyzes community trends and data related to integration and discrimination in housing, education, employment, and public accommodations; develops and recommends strategies and policies to enhance fair housing practices and community integration, ensuring alignment with local, state, and federal regulations; monitors and evaluates the effectiveness of human relations programs and provides suggestions for program improvement.

Conducts complex investigations into allegations of discrimination, ensuring thorough analysis and proper documentation of findings; drafts written reports and prepares testimony for hearings; makes recommendations based on case findings and applicable law; ensures compliance with civil rights laws, including conducting follow-up investigations to verify resolution of discrimination claims.

Attends public demonstrations to assess community tensions; consults with business and labor organizations on fair employment practices; coordinates referrals to other appropriate agencies when cases fall outside the jurisdiction of the department.

          Performs related duties as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS, AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·        principles and practices related to civil rights law and fair housing regulations

·        community resources, including governmental, nonprofit, and advocacy groups for civil rights issues

·        methods of conflict resolution, negotiation, and mediation, particularly in the context of housing and community relations

·        social issues, especially related to discrimination in housing, public accommodations, and employment

·        techniques for collecting, analyzing, and reporting data related to inter-group relations and integration patterns

·        effective presentation methods for educational and advocacy programs

 

ABILITY TO:

·        lead and guide others in investigations and case management processes, ensuring compliance with laws and ethical standards

·        resolve complex and high-profile disputes through mediation and negotiation

·        conduct thorough analyses of community issues and develop strategies to address social and housing inequalities

·        establish and maintain effective working relationships with diverse community groups, government agencies, and organizational leaders

·        communicate effectively both orally and in writing with diverse stakeholders

·        work independently and as part of a team to achieve departmental goals

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 6/25).

 

BOTH SPECIALTIES

EDUCATION:

 

 

Completion of a bachelor’s degree program at an accredited college or university.

 

NOTE: Completion of an industry related certification or additional experience may substitute for the required education on a year-for-year basis.

 

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

Two years of experience in community organization, social work, labor management negotiations, counseling or guidance, adult education, or human relations.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

          One year of experience as a Human Relations Representative 2.

 

OR:

 

Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources which has include the specific experience.

 

BILINGUAL SPECIALTY

          Sufficient training and/or experience to be able to understand and speak fluently and clearly in a designated foreign language.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

          Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

 LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

          Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to and during tenure of employment as a Senior Human Relations Representative, if required to by work assignment.
