HOUSING AND COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT ANALYST 2  

 

 

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is analytical work at the advanced performance level planning, implementing, and evaluating funded housing, community and economic development projects and programs to effectuate conformance to federal, state and local funding requirements and to ensure effective delivery of services. Employees in this class serve as technical experts and project leaders and provide trainings to lower-level staff and funded providers. Employees in the Contracts Assignment process and track final contracts, administer complex contracted projects, review program proposals, and prepare recommendations aimed at developing new or improving existing housing and community development.  Employees in the Monitoring Assignment are responsible for the comprehensive monitoring, evaluation, and implementation of corrective action plans for agencies with new funding, complex projects, or which are defined as high risk. Serving as the lead contact with officials of public agencies, a diverse range of contracted providers, and internal staff to obtain information or render assistance are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative or technical superior.  

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS:   (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

1.     Employees in the Contracts Assignment must be responsible for leading, implementing, managing and evaluating program provider contracts and reports through ongoing tracking of provider agencies’ programs and services to ensure compliance with funding policies and guidelines.

2.     Employees in the Monitoring Assignment must be responsible for leading, implementing and evaluating the monitoring of contracted providers’ projects or programs and for working with agencies to implement corrective action plan strategies to ensure compliance with local, state, and federal regulations. 

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

Both Assignments

 

           Determines appropriate methods for collecting program service data to be used in evaluations and preparation of periodic reports; analyzes and collects production data and produces required governmental performance reports on a quarterly, annual or as needed basis; develops strategies to address issues identified with reporting or performance problems encountered in program agencies; provides technical assistance to agencies to implement strategies to improve delivery of services.

            Serves as a technical expert on Contract Review Committee to review issues or concerns raised with agency contracts; provides feedback and leads discussions with Committee members; makes recommendations to senior Committee members for approval or disapproval of contracts.

 

 

 

Monitoring Assignment

               

       Assists in the development of the Annual Monitoring Plan; serves as the lead in the comprehensive monitoring of organizations with new funding, reviews complex projects or those which are defined as high risk; conducts on-site entrance conferences with funded agencies and completes comprehensive onsite and desk reviews to include but not limited to the examination of contracts, invoices and reports, evaluation of agency administrative and programmatic procedures and operations, analysis of program outcomes, and facilitation of housing unit inspections, if applicable.

Prepares written reports detailing findings and recommendations and final monitoring reports for supervisory review and approval; conducts exit conference with provider to discuss results of Monitoring Review; consults with providers on corrective action plan if required; works with providers to implement corrective action plan and updates to the agency’s policies and procedures; performs follow-up reviews as needed.

Conducts in-house organizational quality control evaluations; interviews agency personnel to gather pertinent information; participates in formulating strategies to improve existing programs and procedures; develops and maintains tracking system of findings; assists in processing Conflict of Interest requests; investigates and assists in resolution of complaints/incidents related to program operations and services performed by contracted providers; manages federal contract database.

Plays a lead role in monitoring meetings and reviews; completes thorough research of regulations required for responses to findings and presents final draft responses for supervisory review.

Assists Supervisor in periodic updates to departmental policy and procedures manual to include outreach to delegate agencies; drafts policies for new programs and grants; reviews final draft.

Performs related work as required.   

 

Contracts Assignment

 

Identifies funding criteria and program guidelines of federal and state program legislation and ascertains their impact on provider's services; identifies programs qualified for funding under federal and/or state program legislation; writes requests for funding based on funding criteria or guidelines.

           Leads staff in the of Requests for Proposals (RFP) process for new funding and programs; participates in the RFP briefing and serves on Committee responsible for reviewing proposals for contracted services; and recommends selection of potential providers that meet necessary criteria for approval by senior management

            Develops and finalizes scope of service statements and program budgets for contracts or agreements recommended for funding; discusses contract terms with agency directors, legal representatives, and fiscal administrators of contracting agencies; delineates administrative and program breakdowns, production goals and units, and time frames; ensures that scope of service is feasible and conforms with approved program proposals; develops and incorporates designs for systematic reporting and monitoring of contract progress; discusses wording and format of contracts with legal staff to clarify terms and conditions.

Provides day to day financial and program support for funded agencies; advises agencies on appropriate management practices to follow in administering programs and documentation required for reporting services provided and costs incurred; refers agency administrators to other units or departments for consultation and assistance in specific management areas.

Directs the initial review of invoices for expenditures under contracts for both budgetary control and conformance with program intent; certifies payment for expenditures; forwards payment requests to Contract Administrator for final approval and responds to contractor inquiries concerning payment; coordinates and negotiates amendments to contracts.         

Orients representatives from contracted agencies on performance standards for compliance with contract requirements.

            Plays a lead role in provider meetings and program services sessions to provide assistance or to obtain program information; leads coordinated in-service sessions with a diverse range of partners and providers to evaluate program effectiveness; shares legislative, program and funding updates; discusses proposed policy and program changes.

Responds to provider, client and funder inquiries and requests for information. 

Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         the principles and practices of contract administration as they apply to the monitoring and evaluation of housing and community development programs

·         laws, regulations, and guidelines of federal, state, and local funding sources for housing and community development projects and programs

·         the organization and functions of government and other organizations involved in housing and community development  

·         the administrative principles and practices of contract compliance monitoring

·         the principles, practices, and procedures of preparing annual budgets for agencies and review of fiscal documents/records

 

SKILL IN:

 

·         applying principles and practices of contract development, administration, and monitoring as they relate to housing and community development programs in the area of assignment

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·        evaluate and monitor program quality and effectiveness and prepare recommendations based on evaluations

·          utilize required data tracking and City information systems including but not limited to the City’s Automated Contract Information System (ACIS), IDIS and QuickBase applications

·         incorporate the philosophy, objectives, and practices of governmentally assisted housing programs into assignment area

·         respond to inquiries across a broad range of housing and community development programs

·         research updates to federal, state, and local regulations governing housing and community development

·          prepare detailed and organized comprehensive reports

·          analyze data, procedures and make sound recommendations

·          present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing

·          establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and departmental and agency officials

·          understand and follow oral and written instructions

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/23.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.

 

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

            Two years of experience performing administrative analysis, program monitoring or housing and community development work.

 

OR

 

            Two years of contract and program compliance experience with responsibility for planning, evaluating, and monitoring housing and community development programs and control systems for federally funded housing programs.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

            One year of full performance level experience planning, implementing, and evaluating funded housing, community and economic development projects and programs.

 

NOTE: Completion of a master's degree program with major coursework in public administration, business administration, community planning or a related area may be substituted for one year of the general experience requirements.

 

OR

 

            Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources, which has included a bachelor's degree and specific experience as an educational minimum.

 

 

 PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
