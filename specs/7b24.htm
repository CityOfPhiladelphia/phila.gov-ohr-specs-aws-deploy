SEWER MAINTENANCE CREW CHIEF 1

GENERAL DEFINITION

  This is maintenance and repair work at the group leader level. Work includes responsibility for supervising the work of a small crew engaged in the various aspects of a citywide program designed to repair and maintain the City's sewer system. Assignments may be performed outside of regularly scheduled working hours. The employee participates with other group leaders in the implementation of a citywide maintenance and repair program. Work involves conditions, which can be only partially controlled due to outside environment and sewer conditions. Light physical effort may be required. Work is performed under the supervision of a higher-level supervisor.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

  • Positions in this class must supervise small crews engaged in sewer maintenance and repair.
  • Positions must be located in the Water Department.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  Keeps records of work performed, materials used, and employees' time; makes rough sketches of locations requiring work; makes simple oral and written reports on condition of structures which have been reported for repair or maintenance.

  Examines sewers and related areas and indicates proper location and depth for workmen to dig ditches; watches for indication of other utility structures and the need for shoring materials in the ditch; explains to workers the location and type of repairs to be made to various sewer structures; instructs sewer crawler to examine, and report condition of sewer structures; may make visual inspection of sewer interior; estimates and requisitions materials and equipment necessary to complete repairs.

  Determines that safety measures have been taken by inspecting for the ditch sheathing and shoring, the precautionary measures taken to prevent injury from utility power cables and transmission lines; the absence of poisonous sewer gases and surface traffic controls.

  Investigates complaints of sewer water in cellars and public utility subsurface spaces; orders the inspection of sewer laterals and main sewers connecting to dwellings or found near public utility structures for possible sources of sewer water seepage; determines and initiates needed repairs.

  Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

    KNOWLEDGE OF:

    • the methods, practices, materials, equipment and tools used in sewer maintenance and repair work
    • the occupational hazards present and safety precautions used in sewer maintenance and repair work
    • the methods, practices, materials and tools used in the brick mason trade, as they apply to sewer maintenance and repair work
    • the general physical layout, component structures and appurtenance of the municipal sewer system

    ABILITY TO:

    • assign and supervise the work of a small crew of employees engaged in sewer maintenance and repair
    • detect and correct structural defects, unsafe and dangerous conditions in sewers
    • interpret and work from rough sketches, blueprints, and to prepare rough sketches
    • make estimates of time, cost, labor and material
    • crawl through sewers
    • prepare and maintain routine records and reports
    • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 11/07.)

    EDUCATION

    Education equivalent to completion of the tenth school grade.

    AND
    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

    Three years of experience working as a member of a crew actively engaged in the construction, repair or maintenance of sewers.

    OR

    Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

Vision - Bilateral vision. Normal depth perception

Ability to meet other physical and medical standards approved for this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Sewer Maintenance Crew Chief 1.
