CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

7N76
RECREATION GROUNDS MAINTENANCE SUPERINTENDENT

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative recreation grounds maintenance work planning, directing and coordinating on a city-wide basis all grounds maintenance and improvements of Recreation Department lands. The employee in this class directs, through subordinate supervisors, the implementation of landscape designs and the overall maintenance of recreation grounds. Monitoring the completion of projects and assuring that work schedules are established and carried out are important aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of the Recreation Maintenance Director.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • This one position class must be located in the Maintenance Division of the Recreation Department.
  • This position must plan, direct and coordinate the landscape and grounds maintenance and improvements of recreation grounds and fields.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Manages and directs, through supervisory personnel, the activities of a citywide staff of grounds maintenance and gardening employees; plans and establishes work schedules and personnel assignments to ensure that work is completed; tours grounds and facilities throughout the City to ensure that work has been done and to determine other areas where work is to be done.
  • Receives and reviews non-routine work requests; determines their priority, staffing and equipment needs; makes certain that staff and equipment are available; informs subordinate supervisors of assignments.
  • Directs and participates in the maintenance of records concerning staff, facilities, equipment, work orders, materials, and supplies; schedules equipment maintenance and repairs.
  • Confers with subordinate supervisors at field locations to observe conditions and discuss problems in work areas; determines corrective action to be taken; requests additional resources as required; prepares progress and special reports; maintains records of work performed.
  • Submits recommendations for the purchase of equipment, materials, and supplies; prepares annual budget requests for City-wide maintenance and custodial programs.
  • Interviews and selects new employees; provides orientation; recommends disciplinary action as needed.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods, materials, tools and practices used in the maintenance of recreational fields and grounds.
  • the characteristics and most efficient use of a wide variety of mechanical equipment used in grounds maintenance and related work.
  • the principles and practices of turf and grounds maintenance.
  • methods, materials, tools and equipment used in the care of athletic fields.
  • the occupational hazards and safety precautions required in grounds maintenance.
  • the principles and practices of landscaping.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.

ABILITY TO:

  • plan work schedules in order to achieve maximum use and efficiency of personnel, equipment and materials.
  • prepare and maintain records and reports pertaining to daily operations.
  • plan, assign and supervise through subordinate supervisors, the work of a large number of workers engaged in the maintenance of recreational grounds and fields.
  • estimate the personnel, materials and equipment needed for the work.
  • understand and follow oral and written instructions.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with departmental officials and associates.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 8/1994.)

EDUCATION:

  • Education equivalent to the completion of the twelfth school grade.

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

  • Three years of experience in the cleaning and care of grounds or fields.

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

  • Two years of second level supervisory work as a Recreation District Caretaker Supervisor II directing, through one or more subordinate recreation labor crew chiefs, the caretaking grounds maintenance activities in a recreation district.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS:

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES:

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Recreation Grounds Maintenance Superintendent.
