3A81
ARCHITECTURAL TECHNICIAN I

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is architectural technician work of limited scope and complexity applying established architectural principles, practices and mathematical knowledges to the completion of assigned projects in support of architectural activities. Work assignments include completing architectural projects such as detailing, preparing standardized specifications, rendering drawings, constructing models, and plans, determining size and space requirements and completing related architectural assignments.

Work is performed under the supervision of a technical superior. Employees in this class apply technical principles to render architectural drawings and provide related technical services to architectural and engineering personnel.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Performs field and office technical assignments for the architectural phases of design, construction or maintenance projects; prepares layouts, renderings, diagrams, models, design details, specifications and other technical data; makes mathematical computations to determine size and space requirements for architectural projects; prepares scale and full-size drawings and other contract documents integrating structural, mechanical and ornamental elements into a unified plan; revises existing drawing to incorporate structural and/or mechanical modifications; prepares graphic representations of works in progress emphasizing particular features of the project.
  • Reviews aspects of new plans submitted by architectural consultants for accuracy and compatibility with existing plans and makes necessary computations to show exact dimensions, location and other information; meets with consultants to resolve drafting problems
  • Reviews specifications, plans, correction sheets and other technical correspondence for conformance to departmental, city, state and federal standards and regulations; revises correspondence to reflect changes and modifications.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • mathematics as it applies to drafting and the preparation of cost estimates, specifications, plans and diagrams in architecture.
  • the principles, practices, techniques and instruments of drafting.
  • the principles and practices of engineering as they relate to architecture.
  • the strengths and uses of construction material.

SKILL IN:

  • drawing architectural plans.

ABILITY TO:

  • read and interpret architectural and engineering plans and specifications.
  • gather, compile and analyze data pertaining to architectural investigations.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with contractors, associates and others.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 6/1986.)
  • Completion of a two year program at an accredited technical institute, junior college, college or university with major course work in architectural technology.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included completion of the twelfth school grade and one year of post high school technical training at an accredited technical institute, junior college, college or university in architecture or architectural technology as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to and during tenure of employment in the Architectural Technician I class if required by assignment.
