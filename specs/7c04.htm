7C04
PARK TROLLEY GUIDE

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is tour guide work driving visitors through Fairmount Park and providing them with pertinent scenic, geographic and historic information. An employee in this class drives a tour vehicle over an assigned route and comments on points of interest through Fairmount Park. Employees work during the tourist season. Dealing courteously with the general public is significant to the work. Work is performed under the close supervision of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Attends training course to become proficient in the duties of a Park Trolley Guide.
  • Collects fares; drives visitors through Fairmount Park in a tour vehicle; provides pertinent information and points out places of scenic, geographic and historic interest; gives descriptive talks on landmarks and other important sites; may drive visitors on special tours.
  • Distributes brochures and other materials describing the park's historical background and current activities related to Fairmount Park; answers related questions.
  • Observes instruments while tour vehicle is in operation and takes routine corrective measures when required; keeps vehicle clean.
  • Keeps daily records of the number of riders.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • successfully complete the training course for this class.
  • operate a tour vehicle safely and in a manner as to maximize passenger comfort.
  • speak clearly and distinctly.
  • learn a variety of pertinent facts about points of interest in Fairmount Park.
  • work effectively and courteously with the general public.
  • understand and follow oral and written instructions.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/87.)

EDUCATION

  • Equivalent to the completion of the twelfth school grade.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Six months of experience conducting tours of historic nature.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department. PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment in the class.
