 LABOR COMPLIANCE INVESTIGATOR

(Bilingual)

(General)

                                                                                                                                                           

 

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is investigative work conducting compliance investigations, delivering written findings, and issuing violations when applicable under laws related to wage and benefits administered by the City of Philadelphia Department of Labor. Employees in this class conduct external investigations and are responsible for investigating and responding to complaints originating from City employees. Employees support outreach and communication efforts to stakeholders to ensure compliance with federal labor standards, including receiving and responding to informational inquiries. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior. 

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS:  (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.         Employees in this class must perform labor relations investigative work responding to and resolving inquires and complaints related to benefits and wage compliance.

2.         Positions must be located in the Philadelphia Department of Labor.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

BILINGUAL SPECIALTY

            Performs the duties shown below with members of the English language speaking and designated non-English language speaking populations. 

BOTH SPECIALTIES         

 

Responds to and resolves complaints related to paid sick leave, wage theft, wrongful discharge of parking employees, Fair Workweek, and Domestic Worker’s Bill of Rights; receives and investigates inquiries and complaints; examines records and determines which labor laws apply; interviews various employees and managers to gather facts of the case and other pertinent information; resolves complaints through mediation where possible.

In the case of complaints from City employees, reviews timesheets and HR files; interviews other city employees and managers as necessary to gather facts of the case and other pertinent information.

      Issues determinations on investigations; writes reports that document the investigation and determination; levies fines and violations when appropriate and applicable under the law; writes subpoenas or notices to complainants and businesses regarding case determinations; maintains and updates case data and records in Excel or other electronic databases.

Responds to and resolves informational inquiries by advising on matters of compliance and interpretation of legislation and/or regulation; utilizes various communication channels to communicate with employees, businesses, or city departments; supports outreach efforts for Department of Labor by presenting trainings, performing as a speaker or represents the department at events sponsored by the department or in partnership with other stakeholders including, but not limited to, vulnerable populations, non/low-English speaking populations, the business community, and City Council.

Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

BOTH SPECIALTIES:

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         investigation methods and techniques

·         current federal, state, and local laws and regulations, and court rulings related to labor laws for wages and benefits

·         unfair labor practices

·         data entry, maintenance and retrieval procedures related to tracking inquiries and complaints

 

SKILL IN:

 

·         speaking effectively and discretely

·         effectively communicating complex rules and procedures to the public and to businesses

·         interviewing

·         customer service best practices

·         reviewing, understanding and interpreting employee or human resources documents and records

·         data collection

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·         learn departmental rules and regulations

·         analyze and evaluate facts involving labor law violations

·         investigate complaints and inquiries relating to labor laws and issue letters of findings

·         continuously stay informed on changes and updates to labor laws

·         track, maintain, and interpret data and case records regarding labor laws

·         establish and maintain effective working relationships with the business community, departmental personnel, City agencies, community groups, and the general public

·         present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing

·         prepare clear and concise narrative reports

·         disseminate information about labor laws

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/20.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.

 

AND

 

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

            One year of experience in investigations or labor relations.

 

AND

 

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

            One year of experience performing compliance investigations in the City of Philadelphia Mayor’s Office of Labor.

 

OR

 

            Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources.

 

NOTE: Additional general or specific experience may substitute for the education on a year for year basis.

 

ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENT FOR THE BILINGUAL SPECIALTY

Sufficient training and/or experience to be able to understand and speak fluently and clearly in English and the designated non-English language.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

 

            Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Common­wealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment, if required by work assignment.
