SYSTEMS ANALYST IV


GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory systems analysis work. An employee in this class directs the activities of lower-level systems analysts and/or applications programmers. Included in the work are preparing detailed systems specifications and ensuring overall systems integrity. Direct contact with representatives of user agencies, and technical and administrative data processing personnel is of utmost significance to the work. Work is performed under the general direction of an administrative superior.


TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans assigns, and reviews the work of a staff of systems analysts and/or applications programmers; ensures that systems analysis and programming projects are completed within accepted time limits; instructs and orients new employees; assists in the selection of new employees.
  • Teaches division and department-wide seminars; instructs staff on application and techniques of new software packages; recommends specific educational classes and seminars for staff.
  • Recruits system analysts and application programmers to form project teams; plans investigative survey strategy; segments survey into components and assigns to team members.
  • Confers with representatives of user agencies to define the needs, applications, and solutions in system problems, enhancements, and design; defines requirements for analytical studies; determines scope and specificity of survey.
  • Gathers systems data; interviews personnel concerned; investigates crucial factors such as paper flow, task time, human and other resources, and costs and benefits of system; consults with other data processing personnel to ascertain components of current automated system.
  • Documents feasibility studies and cost-benefit analyses; prepares systems flow charts; prepares decision tables; writes narrative explaining current systems; reviews reports and documents of assigned modules from other team members.
  • Analyzes existing system logic both holistically and compartmentally; analyzes organizational factors and resources such as amount of personnel invested and operational environment; analyzes weak links in systems and analyzes efficiency of forms and flow of clerical work; analyzes data flow; analyzes program efficiency; integrates findings of other team members into analysis at all stages.
  • Determines need for automation or revision of existing system in terms of operational and financial efficiency; applies data processing resources to revision of existing systems or design of new system; determines what output is required; determines the data items needed to produce the output; decides on the medium and format of all input and output files; devises efficient methods of data collection and clerical procedures; develops appropriate systems security and control procedures; prepares revised systems flow chart; prepares detailed specifications for applications programming.
  • Develops overall recommendation strategy; prepares written report detailing recommendations; outlines how revisions will affect the user agency, MIS facilities, and other inter- departmental relationships; presents recommendations to representatives of user-agencies; discusses and designs schedules for implementation of recommendations.
  • Develops system testing schemes and conversion plans; directs project members in implementing new or revised systems; directs testing of new or revised programs; consults with agency on a periodic and/or as needed basis on operational status of systems.
  • Keeps abreast of current developments in systems analysis and data processing; reads technical journals; attends training sessions and educational classes, sponsored by employer, by university, and by vendors; attends staff and organizational meetings; researches new software packages and other data processing concerns as assigned by supervisor; offers advice to senior management on equipment and software purchases.
  • Performs related work as required.


REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the theory and practice of systems analysis.
  • the theory and practice of systems design.
  • the theory and practice of organizational analysis.
  • the theory and practice of procedures analysis.
  • the theory and practice of forms control, forms design, records management and work simplification.
  • data processing systems.
  • operating systems.
  • cost-benefit analysis.
  • the techniques of applications programming.
  • the current developments in systems analysis, systems automation, data processing and major software packages.
  • the conventions used in systems flow charting.
  • the procedures for writing CICS programs or non-IBM based programs.
  • the applications and restrictions of major software packages such as VSAM or non-IBM based software.

ABILITY TO:

  • present ideas clearly, both orally and in writing.
  • supervise effectively.
  • direct a team of data processing personnel in completing a project within accepted time limits.
  • conduct educational classes and learning seminars.
  • communicate data processing concepts to non-technical personnel.
  • analyze complex organizational and procedural problems.
  • formulate systems recommendations to improve efficiency.
  • prepare systems specifications for submission to applications programmers.
  • complete work within the confines of a time schedule.
  • establish effective working relationships with associates, departmental and administrative officials, supervisors, and other data processing personnel.


MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/83.)

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university including and/or supplemented by completion of nine courses (27 credit hours) in computer programming, organizational analysis, systems analysis, or other approved subject, two courses (6 credit hours) of which have been at the full performance level.
  • Five years of systems analysis work, two of which have been at the full performance level.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
