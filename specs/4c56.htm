CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

4C56
NURSING DIRECTOR - PERSONAL HEALTH SERVICES

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative nursing work planning, directing and coordinating the clinic nursing services provided in all City health centers on a city-wide basis. An employee in this class is responsible for developing, establishing, implementing and evaluating standards for nursing service and providing technical consultation regarding nursing functions to divisional and health center personnel. Conferring with program and district directors and providing technical supervision, through subordinate supervisors, of nursing personnel are major aspects of the work.

Work is performed under the direction of a deputy health commissioner. The employee has controlling responsibility for the nursing services provided in the City health centers on a city-wide basis.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, directs, evaluates and has controlling responsibility for professional nursing services on a public health and city-wide center basis; makes recommendations for staffing, revisions in service standards and program changes for improvement of nursing services; directs the development, implementation and evaluation of new revision of existing nursing policies and procedures.
  • Provides technical supervision, through subordinate supervisors, to insure the delivery of nursing services consistent with departmental objectives, policies and procedures.
  • Plans, implements and evaluates the public health nursing services city-wide through the development and implementation of two contracts with a community home health agency.
  • Develops procedures for recruitment, selection, promotion and retention of nursing staff; develops, implements and evaluates an orientation and in-service education program to achieve service goals in consultation with appropriate divisional and health center directors; interprets nursing roles, analyzes staff requirements and provides staffing and supply data for budgets; allocates nursing personnel in consultation with
  • Directs and performs studies of existing nursing services using, among other methods, the department's Management and Information Billing System; utilizes this system to increase the efficiency of billing third party payers; directs and performs studies of existing nurse staffing patterns.
  • Participates in conferences and meetings to discuss and resolve problems pertaining to the delivery of health center services; assists in developing referral and reporting procedures to be followed with respect to field nursing services provided by outside health agencies; develops cooperative relationships with other institutions and service agencies for the exchange of information relating to nursing practice, education, and in-service training.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • nursing theory and practice and their application in providing nursing care and treatment to patients in an ambulatory health services facility.
  • the administrative and supervisory principles and practices involved in nursing services administration.
  • current developments in the field of nursing with emphasis on the administrative aspects as they pertain to ambulatory health services.
  • the purpose, direction and functions of ambulatory health services as well as their relationship with hospitals, other health agencies and the community.
  • the laws and regulations governing nursing practice.
  • medical, social service and administrative functions as they pertain to nursing services provided in an ambulatory health services facility.
ABILITY TO:

  • perform a variety of administrative and supervisory tasks related to the operation of a large nursing service provided in numerous health centers.
  • plan, direct, coordinate and evaluate nursing services in an ambulatory health services facility.
  • develop and coordinate referral and reporting procedures for the provision of home nursing services.
  • prepare and analyze reports as they pertain to nursing services.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with professional and administrative personnel.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 11/83.)

  • Completion of a master's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in nursing administration, public health, hospital or health administration, business administration, public administration, or political science with major concentration in public administration.
  • Five years of nursing experience, two years of which has involved total supervisory responsibility for all nursing services in an ambulatory health care facility providing multiple health services.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included completion of an appropriate master's degree program as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Eligibility for a license as a registered nurse issued by the Pennsylvania State Board of Nursing Education and Licensure at the time of application and possession of such licensure within time limits specified by the department.
