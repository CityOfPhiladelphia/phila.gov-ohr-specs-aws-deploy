GENERAL DEFINITION

 

           This is supervisory work directing staff and managing real estate transactions including acquisitions, dispositions, and the administration of space rental leases for the City agencies.  The employee in this class has responsibility for ensuring the efficient use of space in citywide properties and representing the department on a citywide basis for real estate planning and activities.  Work is performed under the direction of a Deputy Commissioner or Chief of Staff.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS:

 

1.    The position must be located in the Department of Public Property, Real Estate and Space Planning Division.

2.    Only one position shall be allocated to this class.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

          Plans, assigns, reviews and supervises technical and administrative staff engaged in various activities involving real estate functions.

Coordinates with project managers to design and propose efficient space layouts that fulfill the needs of City agencies; communicates with and guides departmental liaisons to better understand their appropriate space needs and to understand changes in programming requirements, upcoming new hires, and departmental goals; responds to complaints and ensures the City agency, as a tenant, receives the benefits and protections entitled in the lease.

Reviews the Citys real estate portfolio for opportunities to reduce expenses and consolidate leases with other City agencies or utilize City-owned space.        

Determines and collaborates on a strategic level for Citys real estate sustainability, planning vision, optimization of assets and mitigation of costs; participates in the review of annual operating budget for city rental needs; implements changes to departmental goals and strategy for real estate needs.

Forecasts future requirements and proposes annual budgets on a five-year basis for the Citys leases; assists other City agencies in preparing their own space rental forecasts that are required by the Office of the Director of Finance; tracks and records all tenant improvement allowances and recommends the utilization of funds to the Space Allocation Committee.

Collaborates with the Office of Sustainability to understand utility expenses, capture energy efficiencies, and recognize opportunities for savings in total lease costs.

Attends Department of Planning and Development meetings to understand the Citys long-term goals and community-based projects; collaborates on optimization strategies providing highest and best use for the Citys real estate portfolio; makes recommendations to the Space Allocation Committee based on modern space management principles and standards.

Reviews prepared ordinances prior to submission to City Council in coordination with the Law Department for long term leases; testifies on behalf of the City for property related depositions and arbitrations.

Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·       the principles, practices and techniques involved in the transfer of real property

·       the effects of mortgages, deductions, liens and other encumbrances and actions affecting real estate titles

·       the methods and techniques of commercial and industrial real estate appraisal

·       current developments and trends in the local real estate market

·       state and local laws relating to land title work and real estate transactions

·       documentation required to carry out real estate transactions such as deeds and leases

·       budget planning and forecasting related to space planning and real estate transactions

·       supervisory practices and techniques

 

SKILL IN:

 

·       performing research and conducting studies as required to determine real estate trends, property values, and leasing costs

·       responding to complaints regarding the condition of leased facilities

·       making recommendations for sustainability and efficiency of City property activities based on best practices, property management principles and standards

·       tracking and recording improvement allowances

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·       direct the activities of technical and administrative staff engaged in carrying out the various functions involved in a real estate program for a municipal government

·       act as a liaison and point of contact between tenant agencies and landlords to resolve disputes as they arise

·       serve as the representative of the real estate division with City officials and agencies for space management projects

·       negotiate and carry through to completion the most complex real estate transactions involved in meeting the real property needs of city departments and agencies

·       present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing

·       establish and maintain effective working relationships with real estate brokers, property owners and their representatives, federal and municipal government representatives, and associates

·       maintain ethical and professional standards

·       recognize opportunities for savings in real estate costs

 

 

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE  (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/21.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

          Completion of a bachelor's degree course at an accredited college or university with major coursework in finance, real estate, business administration, public administration, or a closely related field.

 

AND

 

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

          Two years of real estate experience buying, selling, or appraising commercial or industrial real property or negotiating for the sale or purchase of commercial or industrial real property.


AND

 

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

         

One year of experience as a Real Estate Specialist.

 

OR

 

          Any equivalent combination of acceptable education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources which has included the specific experience.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

          Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
