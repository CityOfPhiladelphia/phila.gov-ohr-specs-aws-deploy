HUMAN RELATIONS TECHNICIAN

Specialties
( Bilingual ) ( General )

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is community relations work of limited scope establishing communication with minority groups to obtain facts on alleged discriminatory practices and to promote understanding. Work also includes conducting investigations in the field in order to achieve compliance with civil rights legislation and neighborhood disputes resolutions. Gaining cooperation of various racial, ethnic, minority and religious groups, as well as members of the business community and the general public, is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the supervision of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

Bilingual

  • Performs the duties shown below with members of a foreign language speaking community.
General

  • Inspects construction sites and business establishments to determine the racial composition of the work force; gathers information concerning the contractors and business establishments' recruiting practices.
  • Reviews and analyzes employment statistics, union membership lists and apprenticeship programs submitted from various sources to locate possible discrimination in employment and admissions practices; advises higher level personnel of the existence of discriminatory practices.
  • Interviews and screens applicants for temporary real estate office tester positions; trains temporary employees in the techniques of real estate testing; assigns and reviews the work of temporary testers; prepares reports on the results of real estate office tests.
  • Receives and directs complaints and information requests from the public concerning discriminatory practices to the proper commission unit; receives and directs information requests concerning such non-discriminatory matters as evictions, rent increases and legal service to the appropriate City or private agencies.
  • Makes field investigations of complaints received concerning discrimination in public accommodations, employment and housing.
  • Performs intake interviewing on phone and in person to determine if problem is within the scope of agency responsibility; prepares and submits forms of acceptable cases to supervisor for further agency investigation; makes referrals to other appropriate agencies or institutions; contacts these agencies or institutions to arrange appointment; performs follow-up to determine if problem has been resolved.
  • Conducts investigations into neighborhood disputes; investigates the situation by contacting the complainant and respondent; defines and evaluates the problem situations; develops strategy and action plan; consults with superiors on problem solutions and/or referral to other agencies; may act as mediator and/or conciliator to resolve disputes; recommends case closing to supervisor.
  • Serves as a liaison and resource person with community organizations and special interest groups; maintains contact with organizations and groups to sustain communication and relationship.
  • Travels to neighborhoods throughout the city to gather information concerning sources of possible community unrest and answer questions of the public relating to civil rights legislation and human relations programs.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the community resources available to assist in meeting problems regarding race, sex, religion and ethnic discrimination.
  • recent civil rights legislation.
  • mediation procedures, principles and practices.
  • the methods and techniques of investigation as they relate to discriminatory practices.
  • social factors and causes of intergroup tension.
  • the social problems in the determination of discrimination in the employment, housing and public accommodations fields.
ABILITY TO:

  • analyze facts and exercise sound judgment.
  • work with a variety of individuals concerning sensitive or personal problems.
  • prepare a variety of records and reports.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with community organizations and the general public.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • speak, read and write for the bilingual specialty the job required foreign language and English fluently.
  • comprehend the special problems affecting persons of a foreign language speaking group for the bilingual specialty, relevant to the areas of employment, housing and education.
  • recognize and acknowledge for the bilingual specialty the multi-cultural backgrounds of the various groups in a foreign speaking community.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 9/80.)

EDUCATION

  • Education equivalent to the completion of the twelfth school grade.
EXPERIENCE
  • Two years of experience providing counseling, redress, or advocacy services in a public or community services organization.
NOTE:

A maximum of two years of college may be substituted for one year of experience.

Bilingual Specialty

  • Sufficient training and/or experience to be able to read, write, and speak fluently and clearly a foreign language as required by the job assignment.
PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Human Relations Technician if required by work assignment.
