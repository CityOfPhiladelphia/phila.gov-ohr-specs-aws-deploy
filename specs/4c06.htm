SEXUALLY TRANSMITTED DISEASE NURSING SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory specialized nursing work in the care and treatment of patients with sexually transmitted diseases. An employee in this class provides direction to STD Nurse Specialists and other subordinate nursing and clinic aide staff and insures the effective coordination of staff activities with clinic resources. Work includes conducting physical examinations, determining medical and personal histories of patients and providing treatment utilizing medical standing orders or program protocols as established by the Center for Disease Control (CDC) under the direction of a physician. Work is performed under the general supervision of a health care coordinator and the technical supervision of a physician.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • This position must supervise a nursing staff which must include at least two STD Nurse Specialists.
  • This position must perform nursing work in the care and treatment of patients with sexually transmitted diseases.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Assigns work to a small group of STD Nurse Specialists and subordinate nursing personnel, supervises work by observation and evaluates work performance; confers with health care coordinators on problems encountered in the application of nursing practices and gives recommendations for their solution; trains and instructs STD Nurse Specialists and subordinate nursing personnel in the administrative and clinical protocol.
  • Resolves problems pertaining to patient flow in assigned unit; insures that patients are processed expeditiously and clinic resources are properly utilized; answers phone inquiries of a non-routine nature and interviews walk-in patients including immunizations and pap services; supplies information to patients regarding program procedures and services; determines urgency for examination by physician for the patients and arranges for appointments.
  • Supervises the maintenance of records and medical treatments and related services as conducted by subordinates; insures that adequate follow-up is performed for broken appointments and abnormal laboratory test results.
  • Interviews patients to obtain general, sexual and health, history of present illness, review of medications used, social history as to activities with sexual partners, and past or pertinent illnesses including allergies and nutrition status; performs appropriate physical examinations which include checking vital signs, and pelvic and rectal examinations; identifies deviations from norms in the physical condition of the patient.
  • Orders appropriate lab tests and collects specimens including venipunctures, cervical cytology and gonorrhea cultures in conjunction with medical standing orders or clinical protocols as established by the center for disease control (CDC); consults with the physician as indicated.
  • Takes medical histories; identifies need to treat a symptomatic contact with STD cases; administers appropriate treatment under medical standing orders and program protocols.
  • Counsels patients and contacts in regard to risk reduction to STD, including human immunodeficiency virus (HIV); provides one to one educational services for the patient regarding diagnosis and treatment.
  • Evaluates patients on follow up visits to determine adequacy or treatments and presence of adverse reactions to medications, refers patients when indicated to medical, nursing or community resources; orients and instructs other nursing staff in program protocols and clinic procedures.
  • Conducts group teaching emphasizing prevention and treatment of STD's, including HIV education.
  • Interacts with departmental personnel in other disciplines in the development and delivery of patient care services.
  • Attends meetings with medical, nursing administrative and support personnel to discuss procedures, standards and policies pertaining to the STD clinic.
  • Responds to patient and public concerns and questions regarding program services and addresses problems from outside agencies and hospitals.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices and techniques of nursing and their application to patient care.
  • health principles, practices and techniques with respect to clinical and related services.
  • symptoms, diagnoses and treatments in the area of sexually transmitted diseases.
  • the anatomy and physiology of the reproductive system.
  • current trends, practices and developments of sexually transmitted diseases.
  • the application of principles of personnel administration in assigning, supervising and evaluating the activities of a patient care staff.
  • the resources available to persons in need of health and social services as they relate to sexually transmitted diseases.
  • the principles, practices and techniques of counseling patients diagnosed with a sexually transmitted disease.
ABILITY TO:

  • supervise the application of nursing techniques to routine and complex patient care situations.
  • orient and instruct nursing staff in program protocols and clinic procedures.
  • perform pelvic examinations and general physical examinations.
  • evaluate health histories and results of examinations, and identify medical deviations from the norm.
  • instruct and counsel patients particularly in the area of sexually transmitted diseases and general health maintenance.
  • evaluate the effectiveness of patient care functions and develop procedural changes to provide for more effective and efficient operations.
  • prepare and analyze reports as they pertain to nursing and patient care services.
  • follow oral and written instructions.
  • orient and instruct nursing staff in program protocols and clinic procedures.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with patients, physicians and associates.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/94.)

EDUCATION

  • Graduation from an accredited school of nursing

AND

  • Completion of a comprehensive training program for the prevention of sexually transmitted diseases mandated by the Center For Disease Control.

AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Two years as a registered nurse in an ambulatory care or community health setting.

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

  • Two years of nursing experience which has included work experience in the area of sexually transmitted diseases.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Eligibility for a license as a registered nurse, as issued by the Pennsylvania State Board of Nursing Education and Licensure at the time of application and possession of such licensure as specified within the length of time by the department.
