CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

9B01
LIBRARY TRAINEE

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is library work in a training capacity developing knowledges and skills in the field of library science and, as part of the training program, performing a variety of assignments throughout a municipal library system. An employee's entrance, within time limits specified by the department, in an approved program leading to a master's or fifth year degree in library science and continued enrollment and attendance in the program are significant to the work.

Work is performed under the close supervision of a higher level librarian. Employees in this class perform a variety of librarian duties as part of their training in library science.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

As part of a training program to develop knowledges and skills in the principles, practices, objectives and techniques of library science, reads text books, book reviews, library magazines, manuals, departmental regulations and related materials, and in a training capacity performs a variety of library tasks such as:

  • Explaining the general arrangement and resources of the library and use of library facilities; assisting readers to locate library materials; furnishing information about books, authors and reference materials.
  • Compiling bibliographies and booklists.
  • Conducting children's story hours; presenting book talks.
  • Conducting library programs for patrons such as film programs.
  • Cataloging books; processing books, periodicals and other materials into the collections; making additions to or corrections in data base material.
  • Visiting school and school librarians to foster use of the library.
  • Reviewing and evaluating books; choosing library materials for inclusion in agency collections; removing worn and obsolete materials from the collections.
  • Assisting in planning for agency service.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • English usage and grammar.

ABILITY TO:

  • learn the principles, practices, objectives and techniques of library science.
  • learn the organization and resources of the library system.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and library patrons.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 6/1953.)
  • Eligibility for admission to an accredited graduate course leading to a master's or fifth year degree in library science with entrance to such course within time limits specified by the department and after admission continued enrollment and attendance in this course.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
