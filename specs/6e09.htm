CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

6E09
WORKER'S COMPENSATION INVESTIGATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is investigative and field work gathering detailed evidence, through surveillance and research, to substantiate the validity of worker compensation claims filed by City employees. Employees in this class research and review claims submitted by City employees regarding work-related personal injury requests, review injury, physician, and hospital reports, perform surveillance activities, and confer with attorneys to discuss relevant issues and facts regarding claims filed. Adjusting medical bill claims resulting from work-related personal injury is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the general direction of a Divisional Deputy City Solicitor.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The employee must gather detailed evidence through surveillance and research for use by attorneys regarding worker's compensation claims filed.
  2. The employee must adjust medical bill claims resulting from work-related personal injury.
  3. The position must be located in the Law Department.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Reviews claims submitted by City employees regarding work-related personal injury; interviews employees and their immediate supervisors; requests and examines initial injury reports, physician and hospital reports to determine diagnosis, extent of injury, and type of treatment received, medical history of the claimant, employee attendance records, previous claims filed, and other related documents.
  • Performs surveillance activities by following and photographing claimants; interviews neighbors, friends, and associates regarding claimant's activities; confers with attorneys to discuss relevant issues and facts regarding claims filed; prepares a report of findings and makes recommendations concerning degree and amount of liability.
  • Reviews medical bills submitted by claimants; discusses medical treatment and disability assessments with the appropriate medical authority to evaluate the validity of the claim; consults with various hospitals and physicians to verify the legitimacy of fees which have been charged for services; may adjust claims by initiating and negotiating terms with medical practitioners to lower costs and recoup monies previously paid; authorizes medical payments; reviews existing compensation recipient files to determine claimants' ability to return to work, notifies claimant and claimant's attorney that a medical examination will be scheduled with a physician of the City's choice; arranges medical examination and notifies claimants of schedule; recommends and files petitions to remove able-bodied claimants from worker's compensation payroll.
  • Serves subpoenas, notices of deposition and Common Pleas Court complaints; testifies in court as a witness; prepares reports of various phases of the investigation.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • investigative methods and techniques used in investigating and adjusting personal injury claims.
  • the methods and techniques of dealing with the general public in investigational and surveillance situations.
  • basic anatomy and medical terminology.
  • the format and use of a variety of financial, contractual, and other records.
  • the procedures and legal requirements for serving and enforcing court orders.
  • the laws and rules of evidence, judicial processes and general principles of law relating to personal injury claims.
SKILL IN:

  • eliciting and providing clear and concise information.
  • the use of various photographic equipment.
  • the performance of surveillance procedures and techniques.
ABILITY TO:

  • learn the provisions of statutes, ordinances, codes, regulations and policies as they apply to the assigned phases of investigation work.
  • analyze and evaluate facts and evidence involving legal liability and medical treatment in order to effect proper adjustment and settlement of claims.
  • obtain information through interview, interrogations, and observations and to record details accurately.
  • use tact, diplomacy and judgment in dealing with the general public.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, attorneys, city and governmental officials, and the general public.
  • prepare and summarize investigation reports.
  • learn and understand The Pennsylvania Workman's Compensation Act and applicable Civil Service Regulations.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/91.)

EDUCATION:

  • Completion of two years of course work at an accredited college or university.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE:
  • One year of experience performing field investigation work.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:
  • Two years of experience adjusting work-related or other personal injury claims, or investigating the validity of such claims.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of appointment as a Worker's Compensation Investigator.
