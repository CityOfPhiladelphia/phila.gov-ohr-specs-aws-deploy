CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2C10
BUDGET ANALYST TRAINEE

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is budget analysis work at the trainee level developing the knowledges, skills and abilities required to examine, evaluate and control assigned portions of the City's operating and/or capital budget. Employees in this class develop skills required to analyze operating and capital budgets by reviewing a wide variety of fiscal and/or capital program documents and by conducting specialized detailed studies relating to assigned portions of the City's total fiscal operation. Assignments increase in complexity as training progresses. Employees are supervised by a higher level budget analyst.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The positions must develop knowledges, skills and abilities needed in the examination, evaluation and control of assigned portions of the City's operating and capital budgets.

  2. The positions must be located in the Finance Department.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Participates in a training capacity to learn the principles, practices, techniques, and skills required to perform the assigned phases of the following budget operations; analyzes assigned departments' operating and/or capital budget requests to determine their conformance with the City's fiscal policies and capital program, departmental functions, and historical trend; reviews a wide variety of fiscal and capital program documents for conformance with approved fiscal and/or capital budgets; reviews requests for budget revisions and fund transfers; recommends modifications in requests on basis of current policies, departmental operations, and fiscal impact; prepares a variety of capital and/or operating budget reports.
  • Reviews a wide variety of fiscal and capital program documents for conformance with approved fiscal and/or capital budgets; reviews requests for budget revisions and transfer of funds; recommends modifications and prepares administrative transfers of capital authorizations; analyzes expenditures and unencumbered balances of authorized loan funds, projects, future interest and principal payments; determines impact of revisions and transfers and recommends appropriate action.
  • Participates in a variety of special or recurring studies in the fiscal and related operations of assigned City agencies; attends meetings with appropriate officials to discuss proposals, considers alternatives or modifications, and/or resolve problems; evaluates results of studies and makes appropriate recommendations.
  • Attends and participates in budget hearings; provides information relating to budget requests and recommended changes; meets with departmental administrators to discuss budgetary impact of requests, to resolve disputes, and to implement budgetary policy.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of capital and operating budget formulation, evaluation, and administration.
  • the principles and practices of budget administration as applied to the analysis of budgetary objectives and procedures.
  • the principles of public finance.
  • economics, statistics and public finances.
  • the sources and methods used in funding capital expenditures for a governmental agency.
  • English usage and grammar.
  • research methods, techniques, and standard sources of information related to budget administration.

ABILITY TO:

  • learn the principles and practices of public administration with particular reference to government finance, accounting, and budgeting.
  • analyze facts and exercise sound judgment.
  • express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • understand and follow oral and written instructions.
  • exercise initiative and judgment in analyzing departmental needs and in making pertinent recommendations concerning related budget requests and administration.
  • prepare and interpret a variety of financial reports and statements.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/87.)

EDUCATION:

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in public administration, urban studies, business administration, accounting, economics or finance.
Or any equivalent combination of education which has included completion of a bachelor's degree program in an area determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
