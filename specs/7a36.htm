CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

7A36
ASPHALT PAVING SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is second level supervisory work, assigning and coordinating, through crew chiefs, the activities of skilled, semiskilled and labor crews engaged in resurfacing of City streets and thoroughfares. Employees in this class inspect streets for conditions warranting resurfacing and orders and schedules delivery of asphaltic materials used to resurface City streets. Planning work priorities and reviewing the activities of Asphalt Paving Crew. Chiefs are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the general supervision of a technical superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • The work must be performed in the Highway Division of the Streets Department.
  • A position in this class must assign and coordinate, through crew chiefs, the activities of skilled, semiskilled and labor crews engaged in asphalt street resurfacing.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Visually inspects streets scheduled for asphalt repair work; notes the number and type of street main openings in the area to be resurfaced; notifies utility companies of impending street repair work and coordinates such work with other city departments.
  • Assigns and makes daily inspections of street resurfacing projects performed by paving machine and hand paving crews; supervises ditch resurfacing operations by topping crews, and the raising and recementing of water and sewer main boxes and the cutting of adjustments in intersecting streets by box and header crews; assures performance in accordance with standard street repair practices.
  • Orders and schedules delivery of asphaltic materials by city forces or private contractors.
  • Maintains time and attendance records for all crews supervised; approves vouchers submitted by foremen for incidental expenses; reassigns personnel and crews as necessary to insure completion of the projects and to resolve emergency street repair problems.
  • Submits oral and written reports as required.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods, materials, tools, equipment and practices used in resurfacing streets and highways.
  • asphaltic mixes and of the practices and equipment related to the application of paving materials involved in resurfacing activities.
  • the occupational hazards and safety precautions involved in the operation and maintenance of asphalt machinery and equipment and the resurfacing of streets and highways.
  • water drainage as it applies to the resurfacing of streets and highways.
  • qualities and quantities of various asphalt mixes used in street resurfacing work.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.

ABILITY TO:

  • direct and coordinate the work of various city groups and outside agencies in the scheduling and planning of street and highway repair work, the resurfacing of streets and highways and the delivery of asphalt mixes.
  • make estimates of time, labor and materials.
  • understand and carry out oral and written instructions.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/97.)

EDUCATION

  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Four years of experience as a member of a crew engaged in the maintenance, repair and/or resurfacing of streets and highways.
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

  • Two years of experience supervising a crew of skilled, semi-skilled and labor crews engaged in asphalt street resurfacing work.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to meet the physical and medical standards approved for this class.
LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES
  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as an Asphalt Paving Supervisor.
