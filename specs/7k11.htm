CABLE SPLICER

Specialties
( Communications ) ( Power )

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is full performance level work in the installation, maintenance and repair of the City's underground communication or traffic signal system cable.

Work is performed under the supervision of a group leader and involves conditions which can only be partially controlled. Physical effort is required in the pulling of cable from large rolls and the lifting of manhole covers on the streets.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

    Communications

  • Enters manhole to locate trouble in multiple conductor communication cable; uses various testing devices such as twenty-cycle tone, headset and 76-A test set to determine the presence of open circuits, shorts, grounds or crosses; removes and pieces in new wires to re-make junction boxes.
  • Splices multiple conductor communication cable at junction boxes, expressway phones, two-way radios, fire boxes and to various types of communication equipment; tests completed splices for electrical continuity and insulation by means of special test devices such as volt meter, milliammeter and headset.
  • Installs terminal strips into police and fire signal system cable pole boxes, installs TV system and microwave cable.

    Power

  • Locates trouble in traffic signal system cable by using a test lamp to detect grounded and ungrounded cable.
  • Makes spot openings into the electrical company's cables and conducts statiscope tests to locate secondary power lines to be tapped to obtain a power supply for the traffic signal system.
  • Splices into the traffic system.
  • Performs preventive maintenance on all traffic control cables by remaking original splices.
  • May drive a three quarter ton panel truck to and from work assignments.

    Both Specialties

  • Waterproofs splices by wrapping joints, and using plastic sleeves and guards, and epoxy and sealants; may use a paraffin lead sleeve and connect lead sleeve to cable sheathing by wiping on a solder joint.
  • Instructs helpers to perform various routine facilitating and repair tasks such as heating solder, cleaning lead sleeves and obtaining tools required to perform the task at hand.
  • Tests completed work for proper connection and operating efficiency.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods, practices and tools and equipment of the cable splicer trade.
  • the occupational hazards and safety precautions of the cable splicer trade.
  • the principles and theories of electricity.
  • the maintenance and repair of high voltage cable in the power specialty.

SKILL IN:

  • the care and use of tools and testing devices of the cable splicer trade.
  • wiping lead cable joints.

ABILITY TO:

  • test, repair, and maintain underground multiple conductor cables.
  • test repairs of wires for electrical continuity and effectiveness of insulation by means of standard testing devices.
  • locate flaws in the functioning of cables and appurtenances.
  • interpret and work from technical diagrams and blueprints.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 6/85.)
  • Education equivalent to completion of the eighth school grade.

  • Four years of experience learning the cable splicer trade as a helper or apprentice, six months of which shall have been in the area of specialization.

Or any equivalent combination of experience determined acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment if required by work assignment.
