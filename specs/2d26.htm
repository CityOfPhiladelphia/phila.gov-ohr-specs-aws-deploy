CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2D26
PERSONAL PROPERTY EVALUATOR II

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is personal property tax assessment work at the full performance level examining complex personal property holdings to determine tax liability. An employee in this class examines the holdings of individuals, trusts, and estates to determine taxable and exempt personal property. Work includes reviewing financial portfolios to ascertain the actual assessment value of taxable holdings, and examining accounts to uncover unreported assets. Work is differentiated from the next lower class by the responsibility for the more complex assignments and review of taxpayer accounts. Contact with taxpayers and their representatives to resolve problems is significant. Work is performed under the supervision of a technical superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The work must be performed in the City's Board of Revision of Taxes.
  2. A position in this class must review and evaluate the full range of complex personal property holdings.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Reviews and analyzes tax returns to determine taxable and exempt holding of intangible personal property; examines decedents' trusts, trust accounts, and custodial estates to determine taxable and exempt personal property; analyzes federal and state income tax returns; conducts call-in reviews of estates, trust accounts, and non-filers.
  • Consults reference sources to determine exempt status of corporate and municipal bonds, consults stock and securities publications; brokerage houses, and state agencies to determine year-end values of intangible personal property; researches taxability of mutual fund portfolios; evaluates stock of closely held corporations to determine market value; determines valuation of mortgages; calculates dividend and interest income; calculates taxpayers' capital gains and losses; calculates taxpayers' personal property holdings; determines tax liability based on value of holdings; determines residence of taxpayers, estates, and trustees.
  • Confers with taxpayers, attorneys, and bank trust officials to secure information and discuss interpretation of tax laws; answers telephone inquiries from taxpayers; attends staff meetings and training sessions; prepares reports on examinations; maintains monthly examination figures; maintains records and files on taxpayers' accounts.
  • Reviews and evaluates first level appeals of assessments; determines if appeal are viable and accurate; makes report to supervisor on status of individual appeals; prepares refunds for inaccurate assessments and overpayments; presents refund cases to supervisor for approval; initiates examinations of new and non-filers; scrutinizes returns and compares against other records for inaccuracies; determines if records presented are correct.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the Personal Property Tax Act, related legislation, and relevant case law.
  • the methods and techniques used in examining and assessing intangible assets for personal property taxation.
  • the Internal Revenue Code and the Pennsylvania Income Tax laws as they relate to the taxability of intangible personal property.
  • current developments in the areas of securities exchanges, estates, trusts and business entities.

SKILL IN:

  • interpreting current developments in the areas of securities exchanges, estates, trusts, and business entities.
  • arithmetic calculations used to determine the accurate assessment value of portfolios.

ABILITY TO:

  • analyze corporate balance sheets, and profit and loss statements.
  • detect errors in personal property assessments.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, government officials, and taxpayers and their representatives.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/1994.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of an Associate's degree program at an accredited college or university.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE
  • Two years of experience at the advanced clerical level learning and performing personal property tax assessments of moderate complexity.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:
  • Two years of personal property tax assessment experience at the limited performance level performing assessments on personal property for taxation purposes.

OR

Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included the specific experience listed above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
