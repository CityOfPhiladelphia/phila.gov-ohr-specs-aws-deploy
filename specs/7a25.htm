CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

7A25
WASTE COLLECTION SUPERVISOR I

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is field sanitation work at the second supervisory level, supervising employees engaged in the collection of refuse and garbage, street cleaning, and when necessary, the removal of snow within a geographical sanitation district of the city. Employees in this class are responsible for assigning workers and equipment to field crews, overseeing refuse collection and street cleaning operations, resolving complaints received from the public, and supervising the activities of snow emergency crews. Work includes coordinating field activities to effect completion of work within demanding time schedules, and taking corrective action so that the work meets departmental standards of performance.

Work is performed under the general supervision of a higher level waster collection supervisor. Work involves exposure to unfavorable weather conditions.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The position must supervise waste collection and street cleaning activities in an assigned geographical area for the Sanitation Division of the Streets Department.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Coordinate the activities of workers and equipment within an assigned sanitation district; assigns workers and equipment to field crews; directs crews along prescribed routes; gives special and continuing instructions to subordinate crew chiefs; checks the progress of refuse collection and street cleaning to determine that the work is being performed properly, and whether adjustments in distribution of workers and equipment are warranted; re-assigns workers and equipment when necessary; takes appropriate corrective action to meet departmental standards.
  • Provides input on the restructuring of routes and boundaries; assists superiors with data on the numbers and locations of disposable loads; helps identify conditions on specific streets so that maps can be prepared.
  • Prepares the necessary personnel, equipment, and work records; goes to scene of accidents involving sanitation crews; prepares accident reports; recommends disciplinary action.
  • Resolves refuse collection and street cleaning complaints received from the public; speaks with complainants at their residence; takes the appropriate corrective action to resolve problems; discusses special rubbish removal and street cleaning programs with community representatives during block parties and other special events.
  • Issues citations for improper rubbish disposal.
  • Contacts snow removal crews; checks that snow chains and plows are properly attached; checks snow plowing activity within district.
  • Assigns workers to hang temporary "no parking" signs, preparatory to street cleaning.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • equipment, methods, and techniques commonly used in refuse and garbage collection, and street cleaning operations.
  • occupational hazards present, and safety precautions used in sanitation operations.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
  • city rules and regulations pertaining to street sanitation operations.
  • city geography and street layout.
  • public health aspects of solid waste sanitation.

ABILITY TO:

  • plan, assign, coordinate, and review the work of large groups of employees engaged in refuse and garbage collection and street cleaning operations.
  • prepare reports and maintain records pertinent to waste collection activities.
  • estimate materials, supplies, tools, and equipment needed to complete assigned laboring tasks.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 10/92).

EDUCATION:

  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

  • Two years of municipal waste disposal plant maintenance or waste collection field experience.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:
  • Two years of experience supervising a number of labor and equipment operator crews engaged in the activities of a municipal waste collection program, or two years experience at the Crew Chief level supervising the operation, maintenance, and repair activities in a solid waste incinerator or transfer plant.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
