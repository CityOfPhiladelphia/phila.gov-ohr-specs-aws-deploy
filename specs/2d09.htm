CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2D09
REAL PROPERTY APPEALS ADMINISTRATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative real property abatement and appeals processing work, supervising, through subordinate supervisors, a staff of technical and paraprofessional personnel engaged in a City-wide property tax appraisal appeal, abatement and exemption determination processes. The employee in this class is responsible for developing policy and procedure for the administration of real property tax exemptions and abatements, and presenting property appraisal appeals to the Board of Revision of Taxes. Work includes responsibility for formulating intermediate and long range program plans and monitoring the property tax exemption process to return exempt properties to the tax roles. Working with departmental officials in the City Solicitor's Office and the Department of Licenses and Inspection in complying with councilmanic ordinances developed to grant partial real estate tax exemptions are significant aspects of the work. An employee in this class works under the direction of the Executive Director of the Board of Revision of Taxes.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. Only one position can be allocated to this class.
  2. This position must be responsible for supervising a City-wide tax appraisal appeal, abatement and exempt determination processes through Real Property Evaluation Supervisors and a staff of technical and paraprofessional personnel.
  3. The work must be performed for the City's Board of Revision of Taxes.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Supervises, through subordinate evaluation supervisors, a staff of paraprofessional and technical personnel engaged in evaluating individual real property accounts for possible exemption or abatement from real property taxes; processes exemption applications, and maintains exemption records; presents real property tax appeals to the Board of Revision of Taxes.
  • Develops policy and procedure strategies used to monitor exempt or abated properties of public charities and for such structures as churches, and hospitals; establishes special technical units to review properties and determine whether they qualify for exemption from real estate taxes; makes decisions to return exempt properties to the tax roll; enforces ordinances enacted by City Council to grant temporary tax liability abatements from real property taxes; instructs staff to perform random audits of specific property accounts to verify whether property values are assessed properly; directs staff to monitor building permits obtained through Licenses and Inspection for the purpose of tracking construction renovations made to both taxable and exempt status properties; increases property market values and identifies properties for audits; instructs staff to comply with specific Council ordinances passed to ensure abatement applicants are current with payment of city taxes, district taxes, liens, fines, etc.
  • Reviews all real property tax appeals submitted to the Board of Revision of Taxes; monitors the review of appeals made by subordinate paraprofessional and technical staff; reviews assessment factors used to determine property taxes; submits appeals with merit to the Board for its review.
  • Plans, assigns and reviews the work of subordinate staff; holds planning, problem solving and communication conferences with unit supervisors; delegates work assignments and establishes priorities; directs the maintenance of various records and reports required for assigned units; coordinates activities of appeals and exemption staff with data processing units to ensure full and proper utilization of automated exemption techniques.
  • Assists in the formulation of intermediate and long term policies and procedures published in the State mandated Board of Revision of Taxes annual precepts; develops procedures for implementation of program designed to carry-out Board policies; confers with other departmental officials and unit supervisors on the development of intermediate and long range assessment plans, new and revised policies and procedures and improvement of program direction and control.
  • Confers with officials in other departments to relate pertinent information regarding property tax appeals that are settled in court; meets with taxpayers, property owners and their agents and others in the private sector for the purpose of settling tax assessment problems.
  • Prepares special reports on aspects of the department's operations, programs and policies; serves in a coordinating capacity on special projects affecting different sections of the department; compiles and prepares the property exemption, abatement and appeal segments included in the department's annual financial report; reviews and monitors market value reports prepared by the evaluation staff; directs the preparation of reviews, detailing a synopsis and critique of all appraisal appeals for final review by the Board of Revision of Taxes and the Solicitor's Office.
  • Determines technical training needs of staff; directs the maintenance of an in-house educational training program for all Board of Revision of Taxes staff.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, methods and techniques of the abatement and exemption process under Pennsylvania law.
  • the principles, methods and techniques of the appeals process under Pennsylvania Law.
  • the theory of values as applied to real property appraisal.
  • the cost, sales comparison, and income capitalization approaches to value.
  • descriptive and inferential statistics including sampling techniques.
  • building construction methods and analyzing costs.
  • record maintenance procedures and standards.
  • current real property values and trends in the local market.
  • supervisory principles and practices.
  • the legal requirements affecting real property appeals under Pennsylvania law.
SKILL IN:

  • evaluating and analyzing trends and activity in the real estate market.

ABILITY TO:

  • supervise a paraprofessional and technical staff engaged in real property exemptions and abatements and appeals processing.
  • direct the real property appeals and exemption and abatement program for the City, through Evaluation Supervisors.
  • interpret income and expenditure statements as they relate to market value and assessment.
  • read and interpret complex building plans and blue prints.
  • analyze program procedures and policies and make recommendations to improve operating efficiency.
  • interpret leases, deeds, and statements.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • resolve conflicts with the general public using tact and diplomacy.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, taxpayers, and their representatives.
  • make real property related mathematical computations.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 11/92).

Education:

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
And
General Experience:
  • Five years of experience participating in the appraisal of real property for tax purposes or property appraisal appeals, abatement and exemption work for a wide variety of residential, commercial and industrial properties within an assigned district or City-wide program.
And
Specific Experience:
  • Three years of Administrative real property assessment or appeals work, supervising a staff of Real Property Evaluators and technicians engaged in either assessing real property, or investigating real property tax appeals and processing exemption and abatement applications.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES:

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania if required by work assignment.
