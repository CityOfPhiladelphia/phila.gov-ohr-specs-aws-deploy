CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

3A40
BUILDING CONSTRUCTION CODE TECHNICIAN

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is complex technical building inspection or plans examination work.

Employees in this class examine and approve or disapprove plans, blueprints and specifications for the construction, alteration or reconstruction of buildings or other structures for conformance to the governing building code, or perform complex field inspections, applying a variety of codes and technical building construction knowledge to the testing and inspection of private and public works construction projects to ensure that work performed complies with contracts, codes, plans, permits, specifications and acceptable trade practices. Inspections performed by employees in this class differ from those performed by other lower-level building inspectional titles by the performance of inspections of, but not limited to, complex private and public works construction projects, including the inspection of high-rise fire suppression and related systems in new structures, structural assemblies fabricated off-site, and structures utilizing innovative architectural designs.

Work is performed under the supervision of a technical supervisor.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

  • Eight positions will be allocated to District Services.
  • Two positions will be allocated to Contract Services.
  • Six positions will be allocated to Permit Services.
  • These positions must be located in the Department of Licenses and Inspections.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Attends job conferences with contractors, engineers, architects, owners, representatives from other City agencies and related parties before construction projects begin and again at specific times during the project to interpret and advise regarding construction and code requirements; recommends specific and/or alternative courses of action to resolve disputes regarding deficiencies in building construction and regulatory requirements; may approve minor changes in plans to meet field conditions; reviews and/or approves contractors, engineers, and other related parties inspection records of progress and/or certifications; prepares technical reports and correspondence; interprets approved plans and specifications, and establishes inspection priorities; reviews approved plans and specifications to ensure departmental standards, regulations and requirements are met.
  • Visits construction sites and reviews the work of City and contracted inspectors and certified contractors who inspect or certify construction, reconstruction, rehabilitation, alteration and repair projects in progress and upon completion to ensure compliance with plans, specifications, the governing building and related codes and ordinances; discusses and resolves problems related to the application of the governing building code; may issue stop work orders; testifies in municipal and common pleas court to provide expert testimony regarding inspection of findings; confers with owners, engineers, architects and contractors to identify and resolve disputes regarding deficiencies in building construction and regulatory requirements; issues violation notices and reinspects to ensure that corrective measures have been enacted.
  • Receives and examines plans, blueprints and specifications for the construction, alteration or reconstruction of buildings and other type structures; reviews plans for conformance to the building code; disapproves plans which deviate from code requirements; discloses to architects, builders, constructors, and others, areas of non-conformance; consults with higher level administrative and technical personnel regarding complex plans and blueprints requiring application of the building code.
  • Interprets and explains the provisions of the building code for contractors or members of the general public.
  • Prepares reports indicating the status of assigned cases.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

    KNOWLEDGE OF:

    • current developments and trends in the building construction trade
    • the methods, materials, practices, and principles used in building construction, alteration, repair, demolition, and site improvement work
    • ordinances and regulations governing building construction, alteration, repair, demolition, and site improvement work
    • the principles and practices used in testing and inspecting materials used in construction projects
    • the structural elements required for building stability
    • the methods, materials, practices, principles, and preparation used in building demolition
    • improper building trade practices which result in faulty, substandard, or hazardous conditions
    • approved installation and testing methods of fire suppression systems and fire detection equipment

    SKILL IN:

    • detecting deficiencies in building construction and structural instability
    • recognizing deviations from approved blueprints, plans, diagrams, and specifications

    ABILITY TO:

    • interpret and apply the provisions of the governing building code, and the applicable sections of the housing, fire electrical, plumbing, zoning and other related codes
    • make visual and physical inspections to detect structural instability, defects and other potential life and safety hazards
    • enforce regulations firmly, tactfully, and impartially
    • maintain records and prepare reports
    • express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/99.)

    EDUCATION

    • Completion of twelfth school grade.

    AND
    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

    • Three years of building inspector experience performing building construction, alteration, repair, or reconstruction inspections to ensure compliance with the provisions of the governing building code, related ordinances, and approved permits, plans and specifications, which has included reading and interpreting blueprints and plans.
PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Completion of the National Certification Program for Construction code Inspectors - Test Modules 1A, 1B, 1C and 3B as administered by Building Officials and Code Administrators (BOCA) International, Incorporated, within thirty days of the announcement closing date, and completion of course work or continuing education credits necessary to maintain certification during tenure of employment as a Building Construction Code Technician.
  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Building Construction Code Technician.

NOTE:

Employees with three years of experience in the Construction Fire Specialist class (6H23) or Construction Fire Plans Examiner class (6H62) as of September 1, 1993, will be eligible to compete in examinations for this class. Employees must meet all certification requirements as described above in order to be appointed to this class.
