7C27
HORSE VAN OPERATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is skilled work transporting riding horses and tack to assigned locations. Work includes responsibility for loading horses into the van, securing them in stall areas, administering, if necessary, minor first aid during transit, and removing them at destinations. When not engaged in horse van operations, the employee operates a wide variety of multi-purpose cab-connected equipment.

Work is performed under the close supervision of a maintenance supervisor. Work assignments are stable in nature and are carried out in accordance with instructions and established routines. An employee in this class is responsible for the safe and efficient operation of equipment to provide transportational and related services. Work involves conditions which can only be partially controlled and light physical effort.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Transports horses to riding shows, demonstrations, new working locations, etc.; adjusts the ramp leading from ground level to interior of the van, places a mat over the ramp; leads horses from stable to ramp holding reins to prevent rearing; maneuvers and backs horses into stall; fastens halters to post to retain horse in stalls.
  • Drives cab with attached horse van; receives notification from van rider of unusual occurrences during transit and stops van or otherwise rectifies the situation; administers first aid to horse when necessary; removes and places horses in stalls at destination.
  • Operates a trailer to transport vehicles to various locations; hooks-up and operates a winch to load and unload equipment and to raise, lower, or remove, overturned or sunken vehicles.
  • Services equipment with water, gas and oil; observes instruments while vehicle is in operation and takes routine corrective measures if malfunction is indicated; performs first echelon maintenance.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the component parts and operating principles of the specialized pieces of automotive equipment.
  • the occupational hazards and safety precautions of the work.
  • the traffic laws of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania.
  • horse reaction during transportation.

SKILL IN:

  • the operation of a tractor-trailer safely and efficiently, on or off the highway, in traffic and under adverse climatic conditions.

ABILITY TO:

  • learn the geography of the area served.
  • control horses under varying circumstances and to administer minor first aid if necessary.
  • service the tractor-trailer and to make minor running repairs and adjustments.
  • understand and carry out written and oral instructions.
  • perform manual labor under adverse climatic conditions.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 10/64.)
    EDUCATION:
  • Equivalent to completion of the tenth school grade.

    AND

    GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

  • Two years of experience operating a large horse van, which experience has included as a regular work assignment the loading and unloading of horses.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to the scheduled examination and during tenure of employment in the class.
