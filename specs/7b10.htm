GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative and technical work developing, implementing, and monitoring emergency response plans for the Philadelphia Water Department. The employee in this class is responsible for facilitating the development of comprehensive and coordinated emergency plans and procedures for natural and man-made disasters and incidents, assisting in the development and implementation of training programs related to the emergency plans developed, and review and support of the refinement of Standard Operating Procedures to ensure consideration of emergency response activities.  Interaction with emergency management officials at the local, state, and federal levels as well as surrounding regions is significant to the work. Work involves extended hours given short notice and responding to emergencies outside of regular business hours. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS (The following conditions must be met in order for positions to be allocated to this class.)

1.    The position must be responsible for the development and facilitation of emergency response management program for the Water Department.

2.    Only one position shall be allocated to this class.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK:

Leads development of plans, policies, procedures, and training for emergency response to enhance the department’s risk, resilience, and response posture; develops planning strategy for departmental anti-terrorism, threat and security-related initiatives, and emergency planning initiatives in coordination with executive leadership and division managers; develops and updates plans and other emergency planning documents; assists in the coordination of disaster response / recovery operations and crisis management; leads interagency approval of departmental emergency response plans and reports required for regulatory compliance.

Coordinates departmental Incident Command System (“ICS”), liaises with Incident Commanders; coordinates emergency response training and exercises; approves release of images and videos containing departmental infrastructure;  provides direction and guidance for issues related to emergency management; develops and conducts emergency preparedness drills for staff; incorporates best practices and required measures/policies into emergency plans and procedures; coordinates departmental activity that affects City or State security or emergency response efforts.

Integrates units across department with responsibility for physical security, safety, cybersecurity, and flood/drought resilience; coordinates facility security and vulnerability assessments; conducts assets assessment; establishes and evaluates Mutual Aid Agreements

Administers cost accounting efforts related to emergency incidents, special events and weather events that involve PEMA, FEMA, or City of Philadelphia interagency funds;
engages with industry groups focused on emergency management and wastewater conveyance and treatment to import best practices and lessons learned.

Directs contractors and/or consultants engaged in emergency planning and response work; oversees Emergency Service employees.

Manages documentation from emergency incidents, maintains emergency equipment inventory spreadsheets; coordinates the preparation of after-action reports and tracks the implementation of recommendations developed.

Facilitates communications and interagency response during emergency incidents and special events; performs communications planning and incident status reporting; acts as a liaison to the Office of Emergency Management (OEM), and other interagency partners; distributes relevant information to departmental personnel to promote situational awareness: sends/receives (where appropriate) communications to OEM Regional Integration Center, National Response Center, and Everbridge secure notifications; builds relationships with emergency response partners and serves on committees; interacts with and shares information with Police Department, Fire Department, Health Department, Streets Department, Delaware Valley Intelligence Center, Department of Homeland Security, Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency, Federal Bureau of Investigation, Pennsylvania Emergency Management Agency and other relevant local, state, and federal task force groups.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS, AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·       theories, principles and practices of emergency preparedness responses and recovery  

·       emergency management/response and/or business continuity plans

·       federal, state, and local regulations and guidelines governing emergency management

·       federal, state, and local laws governing environmental agencies or utilities

 

 

SKILL IN:

·       editing and written communications skills and oral communications skills

·       project management

·       building collaborative professional relationships to achieve shared goals for emergency preparedness and response

 

ABILITY TO:

·       deliver high-level presentations in various subject matters to executive leadership and key

·       manage deadlines and multitask in a high-pressure emergency situation

  • complete required records and documentation accurately concerning emergency incidents.
  • assess areas of staff development and training needs for emergency preparedness.
  • ensure compliance with procedural and policy directives from federal, state, and local agencies
  • direct and coordinate the training activities of department wide emergency management initiatives
  • interpret procedure and policy related to emergency management
  • work effectively with other departments, government agencies, and elected officials

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE REQUIREMENTS: (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/24.)

EDUCATION

Completion of a bachelor's degree program from an accredited college or university.

AND

EXPERIENCE:

          Four years of experience requiring independent decision-making concerning program management or planning, allocation for resources and the scheduling and assignment of work for a water utility or emergency management center or government agency.

NOTE:  Completion of an industry recognized certification or additional experience may substitute for the education on a year-for-year basis.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

          Ability to meet the physical and medical standards approved for this class.

          Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

 

Possession of a PA-DEP Operator Certification within six months of appointment to the class.

 

Possession of a motor vehicle driver’s license valid in the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania upon appointment and during tenure of employment in the class.
