CUSTOMER CARE SPECIALIST

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

          This is customer service work at the advanced performance level in the Philadelphia Water Department Customer Contact Center or in the Philadelphia International Airport’s (PHL) Guest Experience unit. Employees in this class serve as a lead worker providing technical direction to lower-level customer service representatives and are responsible for training employees in applying established departmental customer service proficiencies. The work of this class differs from lower-level classes in that employees are responsible for investigating unresolved, complex customer inquiries, reviewing inquiry history and determining next steps for complex customer issues.  Recommending and implementing new procedures to improve customer service is a significant aspect of the work. Employees have significant contact with the general public and other city departments and perform work under the direct supervision of a Customer Care Supervisor. 

           

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

 

1.    Employees in this class must perform customer service work at the advanced performance level for the Water Department Customer Contact Center or for the Philadelphia International Airport’s Guest Experience unit.

2.    Employees in this class must provide technical direction and provide training to lower-level Customer Care representatives.

3.    No more than ten (10) positions will be allocated to this class in the Water Department.

4.     No more than ten (10) positions will be allocated to this class in the Philadelphia International Airport.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

BOTH ASSIGNMENTS:

 

Under the direction of the  Customer Care Supervisor, trains employees in customer service protocols including appropriate methods, procedures, and techniques; coordinates meetings, trainings, and daily activities for staff; reviews the work of assigned employees for accuracy, proper work methods, techniques, and compliance with applicable standards and specifications; informs the Customer Care Supervisor of deficiencies and recommends courses of action; participates in the development of goals and objectives; makes recommendations to supervisor for changes and improvements and participates in the implementation of existing standards, policies, and procedures.

Communicates with customers via telephone, mail, email, or other means of communication; uses various computer systems to enter data and make data changes; obtains other information to assist customers.

          Performs related work as required.

 

WATER CUSTOMER CONTACT CENTER ASSIGNMENT

 

Leads, guides and provides technical direction to lower level utility customer call center staff responsible for providing customer service in support of the City’s utility service and billing functions; corresponds with customers regarding sensitive and complex inquiries in a courteous manner; researches unresolved customer problems and complaints in an efficient and timely manner; explains departmental billing and collection policies and procedures to the public; establishes payment plans and extensions for customers with delinquent accounts; responds to adjustment requests and claims as necessary; verifies and approves customer account adjustments; handles water and sewer emergency situations as required.

Researches and responds to customer inquiries; generates account history as requested by customer; direct the customer to assistance for resolution of account problems; receives and processes requests for water service including new service, restoration and termination; prepares and maintains a variety of files, logs, records, and reports related to customer inquiries, service requests and call volume; researches and reviews accounts for accuracy; prepares reports and process orders to discontinue water utility service; verifies the status of meters; researches property ownership and account status.

Locates utility infrastructure in a GIS-based system; researches, resolves or refers to investigators reports of unauthorized utility usage and uses discretion to determine deposit and restoration of service requirements; provides information and identifies solutions for “no water” requests.

Uses various computer applications to manage utility customer accounts and make data changes and service requests.

         

 

AIRPORT GUEST EXPERIENCE ASSIGNMENT

 

Leads, guides, and provides technical direction to lower-level Guest Experience staff responsible for providing customer service and resolving complaints and inquiries; corresponds with customers regarding sensitive and complex inquiries in a courteous manner; investigates complex unresolved guest inquiries and facility issues, determines next steps, and keeps the appropriate airport staff informed of progress; builds positive working relationships with stakeholders to ensure the Airport and related facilities meet guest expectations.

Corresponds with airport guests in person, via telephone, and in writing to resolve complaints and inquiries and ensure a favorable travel experience; investigates and responds to complaints pertaining to Airport procedures; responds to inquiries and provides information and services regarding Airport facility issues; provides guests with reference materials as necessary or directs guests to online resources.

Utilizes computer systems to enter inquiry and complaint data; obtains other information to assist guests; provides information and identifies solutions pertaining to guest experience requests; provides on-going follow up with airport staff and stakeholders until resolution.

Responds to guests’ calls regarding inquiries, guest experience complaints, and requests; interviews guests via telephone to gain an accurate account of the nature of the related inquiry or request.

Communicates with customers via telephone, mail, email, or other means of communication; refers unresolved or complex customer grievances to a supervisor for further investigation.

Provides accurate processing information to guests arriving from international flights in customs as needed. Assists with PHL’s special events and tours of airport facilities.

 

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

BOTH ASSIGNMENTS

 

·       principles of training and performance management

·       modern office procedures, methods and equipment including computers

·       computerized software applications and system for billing and account management 

·       principles and practices of administrative record keeping and reporting

·       business communication and basic report preparation

·       methods and techniques used in customer service

·       English usage, spelling, grammar, and punctuation

 

WATER CUSTOMER CONTACT CENTER ASSIGNMENT

 

·       principles and procedures of the Water Department Customer Contact Center

·       Water Revenue Bureau policies and procedures pertaining to water and sewer billings and associated codes and fees

·       Water Department rules and regulations pertaining to water and sewer services

·       Water Department operations, services, and programs related to customer accounts

·       state and federal regulations that dictate the operational requirements of the Water Department, e.g., the Clean Water Act and Safe Drinking Water Act

·       methods, procedures, practices, and terminology used in billing and financial record keeping work

 

SKILL IN:

 

·     investigating unresolved, complex customer inquiries and determining next steps for inquiry resolution

·     utilizing a computerized software application to track and edit customer inquiries

·     interpreting and applying departmental policies and procedures and customer service techniques effectively

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·       provide technical direction and training to lower-level staff

·       lead and foster teamwork among staff

·       monitor, guide and test in proficiencies required for customer service complaint resolution

·       independently perform the most complex customer service duties involving the use of independent judgment and personal initiative

·       make recommendations and assist in implementation of policies and procedures to improve customer service

·       exercise tact and judgment in responding to inquiries and resolving complaints and problems

·       perform mathematical computations rapidly and accurately

·       type and enter data at a speed necessary for successful job performance

·       communicate clearly and concisely, both orally and in writing

·       establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and the public

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/24.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

          Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

 

 

AND

EXPERIENCE:

 

OPTION 1:

 

          Two years of experience at the full performance level as a Water Customer Care Representative.

 

OR

 

Option 2:

 

          Two years of experience in the Airport’s Guest Experience Unit resolving in-person customer service inquiries and reporting facility issues, one year of which as a Customer Care Representative.

 

OR

          Two years of experience as an Airport Communications Operator 2 providing a variety of telephone communication or maintenance dispatch services at a large international airport.

 

 

OR

 

          Any equivalent combination of training and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources, which has included the experience as specified above.
