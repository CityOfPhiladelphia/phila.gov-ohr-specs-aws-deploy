JUVENILE JUSTICE OPERATIONS ASSISTANT DIRECTOR

Specialties
(Child Care ) (Professional Services ) (Support Services )

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative work directing a major section of the City's detention facility for delinquent youths. The employees in this class direct, through subordinate supervisors, child care and custody operations or the provision of specialized service programs designed to serve and support the needs of detained juveniles. Work includes planning, developing and implementing programs and activities and modifying existing programs and services in accordance with operational policies and procedures and state, federal and court mandates. Directing work through subordinate supervisors and assuming responsibility for all programs and activities in the facility, in the absence of superiors, are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

  • Positions in this class must direct, through subordinate supervisors, the child care and custody or specialized support service programs of the facility.
  • Positions in this class must assume responsibility for all program and activities in the facility in the absence of superiors.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

All Specialties

  • Plans, directs and evaluates, through subordinate supervisors, the activities of a staff providing juvenile detention services in the area of specialization; plans, organizes and directs program implementation and operations; establishes goals and objectives within framework of the Division's overall policy and mission; confers with subordinates on implementation of program plans, priorities and policies; analyzes administrative and managerial problems affecting provision of service; examines the utilization of resources and personnel; directs the formulation of staffing and overtime schedules based on operational needs at the facility; develops and ensures the implementation of staff performance standards; selects, orients and trains subordinates in pertinent policies, practices and procedures; defines responsibilities of subordinates; analyzes staff and material requirements for budgetary requests and allocations; writes contract specifications; inspects services performed by contractors for adherence to specifications.
  • Directs and participates in the coordination of staff activities with other sections and service programs of the facility; discusses problems with subordinates and contracted agencies to ensure that security and resident care needs are being met; directs program activities designed to meet the security and resident care needs.
  • Directs and participates in the investigation of employee disciplinary problems; conducts hearings and interviews on cases and approves disciplinary actions, participates in monthly labor management meetings.
  • Prepares a variety of records and reports; prepares, through subordinate supervisors, written, oral and statistical reports on child care, contracted and support services and program needs and objectives; insures accurate collection and distribution of resident record-keeping data and maintenance of the record-keeping system.
  • Meets with departmental personnel, staff and administrators to discuss status of service activities and the problems and strategies for increasing program effectiveness; insures compliance of program activities with appropriate federal, state and local standards; directs or participates in committees, conferences and meetings with public and private service agencies to develop more effective programs for juveniles in detention; attends a variety of meetings for the formulation of program goals and coordination of service activities; administers facility programs and operations as a whole at designated times and in the absence of superiors.
  • Performs related work as required.

Child Care Specialty

  • Directs a large detention counselor staff engaged in security, counseling, education and resident care activities to provide a safe and secure living environment in a detention facility for juveniles.

Professional Services Specialty

  • Directs the social service, medical service, mental health, substance abuse counseling, educational services and resident intake programs and maintenance of detention files and statistical data for the juvenile detention facility.

Support Services Specialty

  • Directs the operation of building maintenance, dietary, security, housekeeping/laundry, recreation and volunteer/religious programs for the juvenile detention facility.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

    KNOWLEDGE OF:

    • the management principles, practices, and techniques applied in operating a youth detention or child care facility
    • laws and regulations pertaining to the custody and care of detained youths and delivery of support and ancillary services to detained juveniles
    • all phases of modern detention and protective care methods related to the care and custody of youth
    • the juvenile justice/child welfare system as it relates to youth in placement
    • the behavior patterns and group dynamics of youths in custody
    • the concepts and methods utilized in the prevention of self destructive behavior.
    • the organization and functioning of the juvenile justice and/or child welfare system
    • the social, economic, environmental, psychological and health factors underlying the breakdown of family structure
    • the philosophy and objectives underlying juvenile detention
    • the principles, practices and procedures and literature and current developments in the field of juvenile justice detention services in the area of specialization
    • supervisory methods and techniques

    SKILL IN:

    • communicating program goals and objectives to subordinates and ensuring their effective attainment

    ABILITY TO:

    • plan, organize, direct and coordinate the activities of a large multi-disciplined staff providing care and custody of youths and support and ancillary services to both detained juveniles and the physical plant in the facility
    • observe, recognize, analyze and evaluate the problems of programs and operations in a juvenile detention facility and implement changes to provide for more effective and efficient operations
    • supervise effectively and to motivate and direct subordinate supervisors.
    • evaluate service quality and effectiveness
    • express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing
    • establish and maintain effective working relationships with subordinates, supervisors, departmental officials, educators, and the public
    • prepare and analyze reports as they pertain to support and ancillary services for detained juveniles
    • learn and apply the policies and procedures of the City's juvenile detention facility
    • formulate program goals and coordinate service activities
    • develop and ensure the implementation of staff performance standards

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 01/03.)

    EDUCATION

    Child Care and Support Services Specialties

    • Completion of a master's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in social work, social work administration, or any of the social or behavioral sciences.

    Professional Services Specialty

    • Completion of a master's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in social work.

    AND
    GENERAL EXPERIENCE

    For All Specialties:

    • Three years experience in the youth services field.

    AND
    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

    Child Care Specialty

    • Three years of experience supervising child care counselors, through subordinate supervisors, in a large residential child care or juvenile detention facility.

    Professional Services Specialty

    • Two years of experience supervising social workers in a juvenile detention facility.

    Support Services Specialty

    • Three years of experience supervising child care counselors, through subordinate supervisors, in a juvenile detention facility.

    OR

    Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above and a master's degree as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

NOTE:

In accordance with Pennsylvania Act 33 or 2985, State Criminal Record and Central Register reports may be required prior to appointment to positions in this class having responsibility for direct child care.
