HOUSING & COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT SUPERVISOR

(Community Engagement)

(Monitoring)

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

This is specialized technical and administrative supervisory work implementing and directing either the comprehensive outreach and engagement of neighborhood organizations and community residents to promote equitable access to programs, services and planning initiatives, or the monitoring of subrecipient government (delegate) and non-profit agencies receiving federal, state, and local funding through the City’s Department of Planning and Development Division of Housing and Community Development (DHCD).

The employee in the Monitoring specialty plans, assigns, reviews, and coordinates staff engaged in the monitoring, evaluation, and audit of non-profit subrecipient agencies and their contract activities, policies, and procedures for compliance with federal, state, and local programmatic and fiscal regulatory requirements. The employee in this specialty also internally monitors administrative and program operations and delegates government agencies to ensure compliance with federal, state, and local funding regulations.

The employee in the Community Engagement specialty supports the implementation and evaluation of new and existing programs with funded partners, advances planning initiatives, and provides ongoing technical assistance and capacity-building activities for community-based organizations, residents, and stakeholders. This employee also oversees the Division of Housing and Community Development’s (DHCD) network of Neighborhood Advisory Committees (NACs) to strengthen neighborhood-level engagement and resident participation in housing and community development programs. Establishing and maintaining effective working relationships with a wide range of government and housing/community development officials, community representatives and funded agencies are significant aspects of the work.  Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

 

 ALLOCATING FACTORS:  (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.     Positions in this class must supervise analytical staff engaged in performing comprehensive monitoring reviews of funded-providers and other contracted agencies.

2.     Positions must be located in the Department of Planning and Development.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

BOTH SPECIALTIES


          Supervises subordinate staff; assigns, plans, delegates, and prioritizes work assignments; periodically confers with subordinates on handling of projects; reviews work for effectiveness and conformance to departmental practices and procedures; counsels employees on work rules, policies, and procedures; identifies and recommends professional development activities for staff.

          Performs related work as required.

 

MONITORING SPECIALTY

 

Plans and develops Annual Monitoring Plan for the comprehensive monitoring of subrecipient agency contract activities, policies, and procedures, and annual inspection of randomly selected affordable housing and tenant-based rental units; develops monitoring review criteria to include checklist, and applicable federal, state, and local regulations.

Assigns and supervises staff to implement the Annual Monitoring Plan, and reviews of programs/operations through completion of entrance review conference, desk- and on-site review of non-profit/agency policies, procedures and contract activities for compliance with regulations.

Reviews and evaluates initial monitoring report in accordance with federal, state, and local monitoring guidelines; makes recommendations to improve subrecipient performance and findings of non-compliance; reviews and approves Corrective Action Plan: directs staff to manage Corrective Action Plan process and issues Final Monitoring Report to subrecipient agencies.

Directs, through subordinate staff, the development and maintenance of internal tracking systems to ensure monitoring of provider agencies.

Participates in City Controller and US Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) annual monitoring of division/department;  reviews and finalizes staff responses to clear any findings identified in the monitoring review; directs staff to assist in the development and submission of HUD’s annual Consolidated Annual Performance and Evaluation Report (CAPER) and Federal Funding Accountability and Transparency Act (FFATA) reporting requirements; provides periodic updates of the Uniform Program Management System (UPMS).

Directs staff to conduct ongoing review and interpretation of federal, state, and local legislation, ordinances, court decisions, and legal opinions regarding housing and community development projects and programs; conducts training programs for subrecipients, staff and partners concerning the requirements of federal housing and community development programs; reviews staff responses to ongoing internal and external requests and interpretations of regulations to clarify grant requirements.

Directs staff participation in Contract Review Committee; determines eligibility and reviews activities for conformance; serves as a decision-making member on final approval of contracts and programs.

Participates in senior management meetings and funding proposal review committees.

 

 

COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT SPECIALTY

 

Organizes and manages the network of nonprofit neighborhood partners to assist residents in accessing housing, health, and job programs and services; directs the development, implementation, and management of pilot programs with funded partners to increase resident access to critical housing and community development programs and services.

Coordinates outreach and engagement activities within the Department of Planning and Development (DPD) and across other city departments on special initiatives including tangled title assistance; directs and facilitates focus groups, planning, and outreach for grants including Community Development Block Grant (CDBG)-Disaster Recovery, HOME – American Recovery Program, Choice Neighborhood Implementation funds, and the William Penn Foundation’s Democracy and Civic Initiatives.

Attends civic, community, and social service orientated meetings to promote available programs and explains application processes and eligibility requirements; encourages agency participation in outreach; participates in citywide and interdepartmental activities and programs; identifies internal and external resources to help promote programs and community participation; serves as the department’s representative to community groups and resident organizations; meets with city agencies, partner organizations, stakeholders, and residents; serves as departmental representative on advisory boards, commissions, and other organizations; leads, conducts, and participates in internal and external assessments of program performance and impact.

Communicates budget and resource allocations to community stakeholders; provides guidance and feedback on community group participation; develops, negotiates, and prepares scopes of service and budget statements; may participate in the contracting process by reviewing and drafting scopes of service to ensure alignment with contract terms and program objectives.

Directs technical assistance and capacity-building training for NAC staff on the processing of invoices and delivery of services; assists in coordinating and conducting meetings with DHCD’s network of housing counseling agencies, NACs, and legal services organizations.

Collaborates with city agencies, external partners, and stakeholders on upward mobility, community engagement, affordable homeownership, landlord engagement, affordable rental preservation, and health and housing; works with Division management to develop and implement standards for data collection and management to support reporting needs and program evaluation; works with technical staff to improve existing and create new data and reporting systems as needed.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE: (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 8/25.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

Completion of a bachelor's degree program in public policy, social science, public administration, business administration, community planning, urban planning, social work or a related field from an accredited college or university.

 

NOTE: Completion of an industry related certification or additional experience may substitute for the required education on a year for year basis.

 

 

AND

 

MONITORING SPECIALTY

 

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

Three years of housing and community development experience planning, managing, or monitoring programs or contracts.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

Two years of advanced performance level experience monitoring contract or program compliance at the Department of Planning and Development.

 

COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT SPECIALTY

 

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

Three years of experience in affordable housing, community development or a related field.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

 

One year of Administrative Specialist Supervisory experience directing community engagement at the Department of Planning and Development.

 

NOTE: Completion of a master’s degree may substitute for one year of the general experience required.

 

OR

 

Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources.

 

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
