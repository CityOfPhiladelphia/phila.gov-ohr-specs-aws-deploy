HOUSING & COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT MONITORING SUPERVISOR

 

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

This is specialized technical and administrative supervisory work implementing and directing the comprehensive monitoring of subrecipient government (delegate) and non-profit agencies receiving federal, state, and local funding through the City’s Department of Planning and Development Division of Housing and Community Development (DHCD). The employee in this class plans, assigns, reviews, and coordinates staff engaged in the monitoring, evaluation, and audit of non-profit subrecipient agencies and their contract activities, policies, and procedures for compliance with federal, state, and local programmatic and fiscal regulatory requirements.

Employees in this class also internally monitor administrative and program operations and delegate government agencies to ensure compliance with federal, state, and local funding regulations. Establishing and maintaining effective working relationships with a wide range of government and housing/community development officials, community representatives and funded agencies are significant aspects of the work.  Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

 

 ALLOCATING FACTORS:  (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.    Positions in this class must supervise analytical staff engaged in performing comprehensive monitoring reviews of funded-providers and other contracted agencies.

2.    Positions must be located in the Department of Planning and Development.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

Plans and develops Annual Monitoring Plan for the comprehensive monitoring of subrecipient agency contract activities, policies, and procedures, and annual inspection of randomly selected affordable housing and tenant-based rental units; develops monitoring review criteria to include checklist, and applicable federal, state, and local regulations.

Assigns and supervises staff to implement the Annual Monitoring Plan, and reviews of programs/operations through completion of entrance review conference, desk- and on-site review of non-profit/agency policies, procedures and contract activities for compliance with regulations.

Reviews and evaluates initial monitoring report in accordance with federal, state, and local monitoring guidelines; makes recommendations to improve subrecipient performance and findings of non-compliance; reviews and approves Corrective Action Plan: directs staff to manage Corrective Action Plan process and issues Final Monitoring Report to subrecipient agencies.

Directs, through subordinate staff, the development and maintenance of internal tracking systems to ensure monitoring of provider agencies.

Participates in City Controller and US Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) annual monitoring of division/department;  reviews and finalizes staff responses to clear any findings identified in the monitoring review; directs staff to assist in the development and submission of HUD’s annual Consolidated Annual Performance and Evaluation Report (CAPER) and Federal Funding Accountability and Transparency Act (FFATA) reporting requirements; provides periodic updates of the Uniform Program Management System (UPMS).

Directs staff to conduct ongoing review and interpretation of federal, state, and local legislation, ordinances, court decisions, and legal opinions regarding housing and community development projects and programs; conducts training programs for subrecipients, staff and partners concerning the requirements of federal housing and community development programs; reviews staff responses to ongoing internal and external requests and interpretations of regulations to clarify grant requirements.

Directs staff participation in Contract Review Committee; determines eligibility and reviews activities for conformance; serves as a decision-making member on final approval of contracts and programs.

Participates in senior management meetings and funding proposal review committees.

Performs related work as required

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

§  laws, regulations, and guidelines for the federal Community Development Block Grant (CDBG), Housing Opportunities for Persons with Aids (HOPWA), HOME Investment Partnership, and Emergency Solutions Grant programs and related state and local funding requirements

§  administrative principles and practices as they apply to compliance monitoring, evaluation and reporting for housing and community development programs

§  research methods, techniques and standard sources of information that relate to federally funded programs

§  federal regulations governing the administration for all local, state, federal funding streams

§  databases and reporting systems required to complete the monitoring process

§  project planning methods and techniques

 

 

 

SKILL IN:

·         evaluating the effectiveness of functions and programs, and implementing changes to provide for more effective and efficient operations

ABILITY TO:

 

§  organize and direct analytical staff engaged in the monitoring of housing and community development contract activities

§  establish and maintain effective working relationships with subordinates, associates, department representatives, officials of public and private agencies

§  communicate ideas effectively, both orally and in writing

§  write comprehensive monitoring reports and evaluate reports developed by analysts

§  interpret funding regulations and procedures and incorporate them into operations

§  evaluate administrative operations and program activities for effectiveness and make recommendations

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE: (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/23.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.

 

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

Three years of monitoring, contract, or program compliance experience with responsibility for planning, evaluating, and monitoring housing and community development programs and control systems.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

Two years of advanced performance level experience monitoring contract or program compliance at the Department of Planning and Development.

 

NOTE: Completion of a master’s degree may substitute for one year of the general experience required.

 

OR

 

Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources which includes a bachelor’s degree and the specific experience.

 

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
