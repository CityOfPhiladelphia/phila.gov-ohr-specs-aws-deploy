CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

6E14
SPECIAL INVESTIGATIONS CHIEF

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is confidential investigation work directing a staff of field investigation personnel engaged in investigating the activities of City employees, City agencies and private contractors doing business with the City. The employee in this class plans, assigns and coordinates the field activities of a staff gathering data concerning compliance with City ordinances, regulations, and established specifications and standards; reviews investigation activities and reports; and recommends appropriate actions. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior. Employee may be required to work irregular hours and week-ends.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, assigns and reviews the work of a staff engaged in confidential field investigation pertaining to compliance with City activities, regulations and established specifications and standards of field investigations; assigns personnel to investigations; confers with investigators on incomplete cases and recommends further investigations as indicated.
  • Reviews investigation activities and findings; reviews field investigation reports for content, accuracy, and completeness; summarizes findings in written form; makes preliminary recommendations concerning the final determination of the completed case.
  • Trains new investigators in the policies of the department, various functions of the departmental units, methods of investigation, and the rules and regulations applicable to the investigation function; evaluates progress of new investigators; instructs investigators on the methods and procedures to be followed in special investigations.
  • Participates in field investigations; performs investigations of highly sensitive and confidential nature; prepares reports on the investigation activities of the unit.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • interviewing methods and techniques used to investigate the activities of employees, City agencies and private contractors as it relates to compliance with ordinances, regulations and standards.
  • investigatory principles, practices, and techniques.
  • supervisory methods and techniques used in directing and coordinating the work of a staff of investigative personnel.
  • the various governmental and private sources of records and other information used in the conduct of investigation activities.
  • the format and use of a variety of financial, contractual and other records.
SKILL IN:

  • eliciting information tactfully and courteously.
ABILITY TO:

  • to learn the provisions of statutes, ordinances, codes, regulations and policies as they apply to the assigned phases of investigations work.
  • to plan, supervise and review the work of field investigations engaged in the investigation of a variety of City and employee activities.
  • interpret and explain statutory and other requirements pertaining to the City's charter, rules and regulations.
  • analyze and interpret a variety of facts and findings.
  • obtain information through interviews and observations.
  • evaluate, prepare, summarize and make recommendations based on findings of investigation reports.
  • organize and conduct training programs in investigatory procedures and techniques.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, governmental officials, and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 8/84.)

  • Education equivalent to the completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • Five years of field work performing investigations and inspections by inquiry, observation and examination, two years of which has been investigating the activities of City employees, City agencies and private contractors doing business with the City for compliance with City ordinances, regulations and established specifications and standards.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience which has been determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure as Special Investigations Chief.
