2H72
PERSONNEL SELECTION SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is examinations development work directing the development and administration of personnel selection instruments for the City's uniformed police and fire titles and providing psychometric consultation to the central personnel agency selection function. The employee in this class supervises a subordinate staff engaged in performing job analyses, selection plan development and test results analysis and provides advisory services to staff and management in the central personnel agency on selection strategies, issues and psychometric standards. Contact with departmental and union officials, legal representatives, selection consultants and officials of other jurisdictions to gain cooperation in testing projects and resolve selection problems and issues is a major aspect of the work. Work is performed under the general direction of the Personnel Director.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Supervises the activities of lower level analysts engaged in conducting job analyses, developing testing plans and instruments and analyzing selection results for uniformed titles and specially assigned classes; provides direction on problems encountered and reviews work for quality and conformance to prescribed standards; develops selection plan objectives and schedules for meeting goals; conducts post examination statistical analysis to determine difficulty levels, reliability and validity of measuring instruments; develops and makes recommendations for procedures to be followed in test reviews and appeals by candidates.
  • Studies, analyzes and prepares position reports on a wide variety of issues relating to the selection process for police and fire titles; evaluates the nature and impact of changes necessitated in selection practices by litigation, grievances, and federal consent orders; presents the Personnel Department's position in conferring with the City's legal representatives to respond to court actions; testifies as expert witness on selection and psychometric practices.
  • Conducts a variety of special assignments in the development and evaluation of employment selection devices and related procedures; reviews proposals submitted by consultants and outside agencies for new testing or employee screening devices; ascertains if methods meet appropriate technical and professional standards; recommends approval or rejection based on findings; monitors program for pre-employment psychiatric evaluations; selects, trains and assesses performance of contractors who do evaluations and recommends renewal of contracts.
  • Confers with departmental staff to resolve problems pertaining to statistical methodology for job analysis or testing projects; recommends alternative psychometric techniques to be used.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the professional and legal standards for employment selection.
  • the practices and techniques of test development and administration.
  • the statistical concepts and techniques appropriate to analyze validity and reliability and other issues relevant to test performance.
  • the principles and practices of public personnel and merit system administration.
ABILITY TO:

  • plan, assign and review the work of lower level analysts.
  • determine the appropriate testing instruments and measurements based on technical, practical and feasibility factors.
  • analyze and interpret the performance of tests and test items.
  • research and evaluate various employment selection literature and consultant proposals.
  • analyze facts and exercise sound judgment in arriving at conclusions and recommenda- tions.
  • provide training and instruction for lower level analysts in the concepts and techniques of statistics.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with candidates, associates, subject matter experts, and departmental officials.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 6/1988.)

  • Completion of a master's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in psychology with a concentration in psychometrics.
  • Five years of experience in a public personnel selection program, three years of which has been in psychological analysis work providing technical assistance to a central personnel agency selection program.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included a bachelor's degree in psychology or a closely related area and included course work in testing and measurement, statistics and data processing as the educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
