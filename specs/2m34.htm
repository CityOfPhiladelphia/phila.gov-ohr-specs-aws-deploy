2M34
TRIAL SERVICES DIRECTOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative work directing and coordinating the provision of support services to prosecuting attorneys. Work includes responsibility for administering a program to aid victims and witnesses of crimes. Contact with prosecuting attorneys, court officials and the Police Department to insure the provision of all material needed to prosecute individuals accused of committing crimes is of major significance to the work.

Work is performed under the general supervision of an administrative supervisor. Assignments require analytical thinking and are carried out in accordance with precedents and well-defined policy. The employee in this class has controlling responsibility for trial listing operation.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs through subordinate supervisors the activities of a large clerical staff providing a variety of support services to prosecuting attorneys; plans, assigns and reviews the work of a staff engaged in reviewing arrest files, issuing subpoenas, and controlling and issuing files to prosecuting attorneys; analyzes trial listing operations to identify problem areas; formulates recommendations and prepares reports detailing methods of improving operations through expanded programs, procedural changes, intensified training of personnel, and the application of electronic data processing equipment and techniques to work processes.
  • Devises systematic approaches to the implementation of procedural changes dictated by modification in court policy or revisions and state law; confers with attorneys, representatives of the courts and police employees to coordinate the implementation of new policies and procedures.
  • Directs the victim/witness program of the District Attorney's Office; supervises a reception area providing babysitting services to witnesses appearing in court; develops procedures to provide transportation to witnesses as required; prepares pamphlets explaining the victim/witness program; advises victims on the procedures for reclaiming evidence; explains the victim compensation system.
  • Analyzes the operations of Victim/Witness Units in other jurisdictions to adopt different features and services; prepares statistical and narrative reports on victim/witness services.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of methods and procedures analysis, work simplification, and forms and record control.
  • administrative principles and practices as they relate to the operations and programs of agencies of the criminal justice system.
  • the principles and practices of office management.
  • supervisory methods and practices.
  • the uses and applications of various types of data processing equipment as they relate to the administrative aspects of an agency of the criminal justice system.
ABILITY TO:

  • evaluate, analyze, modify and develop procedures to increase administrative efficiency and operations of the criminal justice system.
  • prepare comprehensive reports describing findings, recommendations and progress in meeting program objectives of the criminal justice system.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and the public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 10/80.)

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
  • Two years of experience in the analysis and evaluation of operations and programs in a court or court-related agency of the criminal justice system.
Or any equivalent combination of acceptabe education and experience which has included a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
