7N09
GREENHOUSE CREW CHIEF

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory and manual work performing skilled gardening, greenhouse, nursery and arboretum work propagating and cultivating a variety of flowers, shrubs, trees, and other plants for eventual transplanting to park sites for City-wide ornamentation. An employee in this class plans and participates in the work to be performed in the greenhouse nursery and surrounding grounds, as well as scheduling and supervising the work of lower level employees.

Work is performed under the general supervision of a technical superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, schedules, assigns, reviews, and participates in the planting, transplanting, pruning, fertilizing and watering of flowers, shrubs, trees, and other plants in the nursery and greenhouse; maintains grounds around facilities.
  • Tours greenhouse and related structures to site check the condition of plants, shrubs, trees and flowers, inspects mechanical environmental systems; observes employees in the performance of their work; checks work performance for skill and conformance to instructions; provides technical assistance to subordinates and demonstrates procedures and techniques.
  • Advises superiors of any problem areas and recommends corrective action be taken.
  • Propagates and cultivates plants in greenhouses and cold frames; mixes soil; makes cuttings; plants, transplants, and waters plants; sprays insecticide, herbicides, fungicides, and fertilizers; applies growth regulators.
  • Maintains inventory of materials for all Horticultural Center facilities.
  • Operates a Cushman or van to transplant floral displays to other areas.
  • Advises the public and horticultural groups in correct measures for plant care.
  • Operates hand and power tools and equipment such as outdoor vacuum sweepers, snow blowers, hedge trimmers, sprayers, soil mixers, rakes, shovels, and hoes.
  • Maintains employees daily attendance sheet and prepares performance reports of subordinates.
  • Schedules blooming times to correspond to the departmental demands for floral displays; forces blooming when necessary.
  • Rejuvenates potted plants, trims dead or unwanted branches, changes soil.
  • Transplants maturing plants from cold frames to protected plots into nursery fields.
  • Maintains automatic greenhouse control system; regulates temperature and humidity by adjusting heat, ventilating and cooling controls; responds to alarms during non-working hours to resolve system failures.
  • Coordinates plant pickups and deliveries to City and park locations.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods and materials used in propagating, cultivating, forcing and transplanting stock in greenhouses, gardens, and nurseries.
  • the common insects; fungi, and diseases which affect flowers, shrubs and trees.
  • the flowers, shrubs and trees which will grow well in an urban environment.
  • tropical and sub-tropical plants.

SKILL IN:

  • mixing soil, planting and transplanting a variety of seeds, cuttings and bulbs.
  • identifying and treating a variety of fungi, insects and diseases which will attack flowers, shrubs and trees.
  • the use and care of gardening tools and equipment.
  • the application of growth regulators, herbicides, and fungicides.

ABILITY TO:

  • propagate and cultivate a wide variety of greenhouse and nursery stock.
  • supervise gardening employees in the work.
  • keep simple records and make reports pertaining to the work.
  • apply growth regulators, herbicides and fungicides.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 2/1986.)
  • Education equivalent to the completion of the twelfth school grade.

  • Four years of grounds or horticultural maintenance experience, two years of which shall have included propagating and cultivating plants at the full performance level.

Additional education above the high school level in the horticultural field may be substituted for two years of the experience requirements on a year for year basis.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of appointment as a Greenhouse Crew Chief.
