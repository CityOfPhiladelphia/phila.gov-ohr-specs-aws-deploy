CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

5D09
EMPLOYMENT COUNSELOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is employment counseling work in a specialized city program providing vocational guidance, developing job opportunities, and/or monitoring the placement of clients as part of an employment services program for prison inmates or a summer youth employment program. Employees in this class interview and counsel clients concerning job interests, employment and training opportunities, and provide advice on appropriate employment goals and objectives. Work includes determining client vocational skills, interests and aptitudes, and primary and secondary vocational plans. Establishing and maintaining effective working relationships with representatives of community businesses and departmental personnel regarding client performance in the program are important in the work. Work is performed under the supervision of an administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • Position must provide clients with employment opportunities and job search skills in a specialized city program.
  • Position must interview and counsel client concerning job interests and training opportunities, and provide advice on appropriate employment goals and objectives.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Interviews clients to determine their vocational skills, interests and aptitudes, as well as training needed; reviews client folder to assess their educational background and work experience history; advises client on available job openings and departmental vocational training programs; develops primary or secondary vocational training plan for clients based on their educational level and work experience; orients clients to the program rules and regulations; refers clients to substance abuse treatment program if necessary.
  • Assists clients in securing employment; contacts community businesses and commercial establishments for job openings for clients; arranges job interviews; provides clients with job-search skills; encourages clients' participation in the program; may accompany client to job interviews.
  • Monitors client job performance, and participation in other programs; contacts employers regarding client job performance and/or verification of their employment information; prepares and reviews client progress report with other program personnel; may visit client job site to verify attendance, the legitimacy of their employer business, and/or claims concerning transportation inconveniences.
  • Provides clients with individual and group counseling on job attendance and work performance.
  • Develops and maintains current literature on job search skills, and list of community businesses and commercial establishments offering employment opportunities to clients; prepares handouts for distribution.
  • Prepares periodic reports of client attendance and job performance.
  • Works cooperatively with other departmental personnel to provide clients with training opportunities for employment.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the theories, principles and techniques of employment counseling.
  • the sources of employment and vocational training in the metropolitan area.
  • the methods and techniques used in developing job and training opportunities, especially as it concerns the unskilled, disadvantaged and/or chronologically unemployed.
  • the goals and plans of governmental, industrial and civic groups involved in job development and placement activities.
  • the methods and techniques used in the vocational rehabilitation of ex-drug and alcohol abusers as well as the chronically unemployed.
  • the sources of information and of current literature and developments in the field of vocational and employment counseling.

SKILL IN:

  • interviewing.
ABILITY TO:

  • assess and determine client vocational skills, interests and aptitudes, and training needed.
  • develop primary or secondary vocational training plan based on client educational level and work experience.
  • present ideas effectively both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with representatives of community businesses and commercial establishments, and departmental personnel involved in the program.
  • learn and maintain current literature, and materials on job search skills, and a list of community businesses and commercial establishments offering employment to clients.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/87.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
EXPERIENCE

  • Two years of employment placement experience in a program designed to provide employment for disadvantaged, unskilled and/or chronically unemployed or underemployed persons, or two years of employment, counseling individuals in a program designed to resolve employment problems.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included completion of a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
