CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2D25
PERSONAL PROPERTY EVALUATOR I

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is personal property tax assessment work at the limited performance level examining personal property holdings to determine tax liability. An employee in this class receives on-going training and assignments with increasing complexity and scope. Gaining experience with the more complex personal property assets, and training in the valuation of those assets are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of a technical superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The work must be performed in the City's Board of Revision of Taxes.
  2. A position in this class must review and evaluate personal property holdings for taxation purposes.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Reviews and analyzes tax returns to determine taxable and exempt holding of intangible personal property; analyzes federal and state income tax returns; conducts call-in reviews of individuals.
  • Consults stock and securities publications, brokerage houses, and state agencies to determine year-end values of intangible personal property; evaluates stock of closely held corporations to determine market value; determines valuation of mortgages; calculates dividend and interest income; calculates taxpayers' capital gains and losses; calculates taxpayers' personal property holdings; determines tax liability based on value of holdings; determines residence of taxpayers.
  • Confers with taxpayers to discuss interpretation of tax laws; answers telephone inquiries from taxpayers; attends staff meetings and training sessions; prepares reports on audits; maintains monthly audit figures; maintains records and files on taxpayers' accounts.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the Personal Property Tax Act, related legislation, and relevant case law.
  • the methods and techniques used in assessing intangible assets for personal property taxation.
  • the Internal Revenue Code and the Pennsylvania Income Tax laws as they relate to the taxability of intangible personal property.
SKILL IN:

  • arithmetic calculations used to determine the accurate assessment value of portfolios.
ABILITY TO:

  • make accurate valuations of portfolios and the individual assets within those portfolios.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, government officials, and taxpayers and their representatives.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/94.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of an Associate's Degree program at an accredited college or university.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE
  • Two years of clerical experience in a personal property assessment program.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE
  • Two years of advanced level clerical experience in a personal property assessment program.
OR Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
