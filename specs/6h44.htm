CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

6H44
L & I ASSISTANT DISTRICT SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative and technical work supervising inspectional personnel in securing code compliance to the municipal building, electrical, plumbing, housing and fire codes in an assigned district. An employee in this class functions as an assistant supervisor of the district office in directing the day-to-day activities of subordinate personnel in order to achieve program objectives. Contact with contractors, landlords, tenants, public officials and community groups to resolve compliance problems and promote understanding and adherence to municipal codes is of major significance to the work.

Work is performed under the general supervision of a district supervisor. Assignments require evaluating thinking and are carried out in accordance with precedents, policies and applicable codes and ordinances. An employee has controlling responsibility for the activities of subordinate inspectional personnel within an assigned district.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs inspectional and subordinate supervisory inspectional personnel within an assigned district; plans and reviews the work of staff engaged in performing building, electrical, plumbing, and housing and fire code inspections; insures that appropriate departmental procedures are being adhered to and program goals and objectives are being met; evaluates staffing requirements to carry out programs and recommends changes in assignment of manpower to improve program efficiency and effectiveness; consults on problem areas encountered during inspections or regarding code interpretation; may consult the appropriate code specialist on plans or code requirements; interprets code administrative policy as it applies to district situations; approves deviations from approved plans as required by field conditions.
  • Meets with contractors, landlords. tenants, community groups and other interested parties; explains applicability, scope, and limits of municipal codes, ordinances and regulations; explains departmental policy and advises on requirements to achieve compliance; explains departmental inspection programs; establishes and maintains contact with representatives of governmental and quasi-governmental agencies; confers with such officials on activities, proposals and programs bearing on departmental function.
  • Prepares and maintains reports and records; maintains personnel records; requisitions supplies and materials.
  • Administers and directs all inspectional and related activities within the district in the absence of district supervisor.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the philosophy, principles and purposes of municipal codes and related ordinances.
  • the laws, ordinances and departmental policies pertaining to the enforcement of building, electrical, plumbing, fire and housing codes.
  • the impact of variations in the uniform application of code standards
  • inspectional methods and techniques.
  • office practices and procedures.
ABILITY TO:

  • interpret, analyze and explain the provision, scope and limits of municipal codes and related ordinances.
  • plan, assign and review the work of a group of employees engaged in inspections pertaining to the building, electrical, plumbing, housing and fire codes.
  • read and interpret blueprints, plans and diagrams and to recognize deviations from such plans in the building construction, alteration, repair and demolition process.
  • express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and the public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/82.)

  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • Five years of field inspectional or trades experience, three years of which has been inspecting properties for conformance to municipal building, electrical, plumbing or house and fire codes.
Or any equivalent combination of acceptable education and experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as an L & I Assistant District Supervisor.
