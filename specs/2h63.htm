2H63
HUMAN RESOURCES POLICY, PLANNING AND ANALYSIS ADMINISTRATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative public personnel work directing the Human Resources Policy, Planning and Analysis activities of the City's central personnel agency. The position serves in a staff or supervisory capacity engaged in human resources planning and policy development activities for the Personnel Department and for city-wide application. Developing policy interpretations and implementation plans for Human Resources programs, conducting special projects and studies of personnel practices and contact with City administrators and departmental managers to plan, analyze and evaluate human resource services are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the general direction of the Personnel Director and/or Deputy Director.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Performs work in a staff or supervisory capacity, as required, developing proactive program policies and implementation strategies to ensure an effective, state of the art personnel management program that meets the current and anticipated human resources requirements of City departments; makes recommendations for implementation of such policies and strategies to the Personnel Director; prepares manuals and other written materials; develops personnel procedures and policy statements for review by the Director which serve as guidelines for operating departments and in-house personnel staff; identifies problems and issues that impact adversely on the achievement of the City's Human Resources program goals and objectives; reviews such situations, evaluates results and makes appropriate recommendations; reviews proposals for new or modified personnel programs; recommends to the Director the feasibility of implementing such programs; reviews civil service regulations for obsolete procedures and those regulations that require revision or deletion; makes recommendations for such changes.
  • Develops long range and short term strategic plans for the provision of City-wide human resources services; drafts personnel department goals and objectives in consideration of human resources trends and projections; develops recommendations for changes in existing and new personnel program activities, goals and objectives; develops procedures to monitor and report on implementation progress; analyzes and prepares recommendations on personnel issues as required by the Director; coordinates the evaluation of existing programs and activities to determine if they are meeting departmental goals and objectives.
  • Confers with departments to keep abreast of the quality of services and service delivery by the central agency; develops and applies survey instruments; analyzes collected data from various studies and apprises the Personnel Director of survey results; recommends resolutions to problem areas; develops, as a result of departmental request, incentive programs designed to reward above satisfactory employees and increase departmental production levels.
  • Keeps abreast of human resources trends and projections in the public and private sectors; confers with private sectors; confers with private sector businesses to observe employment and organizational trends; serves as liaison with departmental and outside planning and policy groups and agencies concerned with human resource issues.
  • Confers with the educational community as a recruiting source to develop programs to educate students about local government; develops guidelines for effective communications with citizen and civil groups, the press, legislative representatives and other public and private groups; represents the Director as needed.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of public personnel and merit system administration.
  • the principles and practices of organizational administration and management.
  • current developments in the field of human resources administration.
  • the principles, practices, and methods used in program and organizational planning and analysis.
  • long range planning principles, practices and techniques.
  • supervisory principles and practices.
ABILITY TO:

  • work independently, generating new and modified human resources concepts, policies and strategies.
  • plan, implement and direct special human resources projects independently or through subordinate personnel.
  • represent the department on policy, technical and procedural matters.
  • apply supervisory principles and practices if required by work assignments.
  • evaluate program quality and effectiveness.
  • apply sound management practices in the administration of assigned human resource management program.
  • analyze a wide variety of complicated facts and to exercise a marked degree of independent judgment in arriving at conclusions and recommendations.
  • formulate new or alternative strategies to achieve organizational goals and objectives.
  • perform organizational analysis, staffing studies, and other human resources quality assessment initiatives.
  • plan, organize and implement administrative and other ancillary support services to meet program goals.
  • present ideas with a high degree of effectiveness, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with a substantial range of individuals and groups, including associates, departmental and union officials, outside agencies and the public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/94.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE
  • Four years of experience in public personnel work.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE
  • Three years of public personnel analyst experience in a supervisory capacity.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum and the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
