PARKS AND RECREATION DISTRICT MANAGER

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is second supervisory level Parks and Recreation management work planning, coordinating, evaluating and supervising, through subordinate supervisors, one of eight geographic districts of the Department of Parks and Recreation. An employee in this class is responsible for the maintenance, operations, and programming at all parks and recreation facilities in their designated district. Planning and coordinating with staff and management for recreation and athletic programs, cultural and environmental programs, and park events is a significant aspect of the work. Employees are responsible for working with their Regional Manager to manage the district budget and determine district priorities. Working closely with subordinate supervisors to monitor and evaluate day-to-day operations and personnel and working with community and volunteer groups to elicit support and promote public awareness of parks and recreation programs is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the direction of a Parks and Recreation Regional Manager. 

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS:  (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

·                     Employees in this class must perform second supervisory level Parks and Recreation management work planning, coordinating, evaluating and supervising, through subordinate supervisors, one of eight geographic districts of the Department of Parks and Recreation.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

            Directs, through subordinate supervisors, parks and recreation programs for an assigned district of the Department of Parks and Recreation; participates in the development of maintenance plans, operating policies, and programming for the district; develops short- and long-range plans for arboricultural, landscape, grounds, and custodial operations.

            Confers with subordinate supervisors to plan and develop parks and recreation program schedules; recommends programs for parks and recreation centers, playgrounds, and swimming pools and participates in the development of overall citywide parks and recreation programming; evaluates schedules in light of community needs and adjusts schedules accordingly; gathers and analyzes data regarding parks and recreation area usage and program participation and recommends, initiates and implements program or maintenance changes based upon the data.

            Confers with subordinate supervisor on the maintenance and landscaping of park and recreation areas, and the planting, trimming, and removal of park and street trees; reviews construction and landscape plans for appropriateness and adherence to Department of Parks and Recreation standards; observes problem areas in the field and determines appropriate action to be taken; submits suggestions and recommendations for capital improvements and new functions; provides professional guidance to capital project managers regarding parks and recreation area or facility construction.

            Determines district-wide priorities; manages district budgets, involving program, maintenance, equipment, materials, staffing and supplies; ensures adequate supplies; allocates budgeted funds among subordinate supervisors; approves requisitions and expenditures of budgeted funds; evaluates equipment needs within the district and participates in the preparation of specifications and requisitions for the purchase of equipment, materials and supplies; identifies various sources of revenue for an assigned district, such as grants and fundraising events/programs; monitors and controls these fundraising efforts.

            Advises subordinates of program goals; evaluates the progress of programs and recommends changes; evaluates population figures to determine program needs for all age, gender, racial, ethnic, special needs and economic groups; develops, manages, reports and audits resources available for programs; submits regular and special reports on district operations; gathers and analyzes information for these reports; organizes and prepares reports.

            Tours parks and recreational facilities to observe staff effectiveness promoting such activities as sports, arts and crafts, drama, and cultural/ environmental activities; directs, through subordinate supervisors, the custodial maintenance and grounds keeping of facilities; takes necessary corrective action to insure that program goals and safety and sanitary standards are met; stays abreast of current local, state and federal regulations regarding cleanliness, accessibility, and safety of parks and recreation areas and facilities; determines acceptable standards for each area and facility within an assigned district and ensures adherence to these standards.

            Monitors staff levels, site supervision and provides assistance with interviewing, selection, orientation and training of new employees; determines, plans, and schedules the work of employees assigned to participate in district-wide duties; reviews work for timeliness, accuracy and completeness; reviews recommendations for disciplinary actions and conducts disciplinary hearings and makes decisions based on findings in accordance with human resources polices and procedures; mediates problems that arise between supervisors and employees; conducts district staff meetings; informs subordinates about departmental policies and plans; makes adjustments and corrections to procedures as needed.

            Coordinates and manages various social service programs administered in parks and recreation facilities.

            Attends civic, community and social agency meetings to explain the purposes, facilities, uses, and objectives of parks and recreational areas and facilities and to elicit the participation of the agencies in the programs; recruits, coordinates and supports volunteer groups and activities; confers with representatives from schools, religious institutions, housing projects, and voluntary youth service agencies to discuss use of their facilities for recreational purposes; interacts with public schools to coordinate the use of school facilities, such as fields or gyms; inspect these sites to ensure they are acceptable; acts as liaison for volunteer groups with other City departments; responds to group requests for park and recreation area improvements; advises these groups on horticultural projects; attempts to resolve complaints; mediates and resolves any conflicts that may occur between community groups and staff.

            Chairs or serves as a member of district and citywide activity committees; attends staff meetings with other departmental personnel; represents the department at hearings, inquiries, and meetings of various kinds; interfaces with various city agencies regarding programs/activities; meets with representatives of private and public agencies, City departments, other district managers to coordinate a department-wide program such as, automotive and mower repairs, snow removal, tree planting, recycling or equipment purchases.

            Manages all emergencies within the assigned district; acts as the first point-of-contact for subordinate managers for any emergencies occurring within the district;  deploys staff and resources as necessary, notifies upper management and ensures protocol is followed; takes responsibility for all off-hour emergencies.

            Approves and manages permits for unmanned sites within the region; serves as the primary point-of-contact for citizens looking to utilize any unmanned field or park within the region; monitors availability and ensures proper application process is followed.

            Oversees adherence to Department of Parks and Recreation rules and regulations; notifies park vendors and users of violations; takes steps to seek compliance; contacts police and/or License and Inspections to enforce rules and regulations.

            Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

  • the principles, practices, methods and equipment used in parks and recreation
  • parks and recreation program administration, management and coordination principles and techniques
  • the methods, materials and tools used in the maintenance and improvement of parks and recreation grounds including turf maintenance, tree maintenance, landscape gardening, and custodial care of buildings and related facilities
  • laws, regulations and rules pertaining to the provision of parks and recreation programs, such as ADA and playground safety regulations
  • the occupational hazards and safety precautions associated with parks and recreation maintenance
  • group dynamics and group leadership as applicable to a large and varied parks and recreation program involving all age groups
  • fine and performing arts, arts and crafts, organized games, gymnastics, athletics, sports, cultural, environmental and social recreation activities
  • the organization, program planning and scheduling, and techniques of volunteer and community group relations involved in directing parks and recreation activities
  • recognized service and civic community groups and of their objectives and programs
  • the principles, practices, methods, and equipment used in a horticultural program
  • supervisory methods and techniques

 

ABILITY TO:

 

  • organize, oversee, integrate, and evaluate large and varied recreation, social service, environmental and cultural programs on a community-wide basis
  • plan and coordinate district-wide horticulture, arboriculture, landscape and grounds programs
  • prepare reports, plans, and costs estimates for parks and recreation maintenance projects
  • plan, assign, and supervise, through subordinate supervisors, the work of a number of skilled and unskilled workers engaged in the maintenance of park and recreation grounds, recycling, and the custodial care of parks and recreation buildings and related facilities
  • supervise, orient, and train a large group of professional employees
  • recruit, orient, evaluate, and retain volunteers on a district-wide basis
  • identify and monitor sources of revenue for an assigned district and/or the department such as grants and fundraising events
  • review grant proposals and budgets and make sound recommendations
  • evaluate program quality and effectiveness as they relate to an individual's or a group's needs, recommending modifications when appropriate
  • learn the use of personal computer applications in the field of parks and recreation management
  • present ideas effectively both orally and in writing
  • prepare and maintain work records and written reports
  • establish manpower, material, and equipment requirements for parks and recreation maintenance activities
  • understand and follow oral and written instructions
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, City agencies, community groups and the general public

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE        (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 10/10.)

EDUCATION:

 

            Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.

 

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

            Four years of grounds care, horticulture, arboriculture, park administrative, and/or recreation program experience.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

            Two years of recreation experience involving responsibility for the direction and supervision of a large and diversified program of athletic and cultural activities, special events and clubs or responsibility for a recreational field on a comprehensive basis in a staff capacity OR two years of experience at the second supervisory level reviewing and controlling landscape or arboriculture maintenance programs OR two years of full performance level park management experience reviewing, analyzing, modifying and/or developing systems and procedures to resolve operating problems or to improve productivity.

 

OR

 

            Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources, which includes a bachelors degree as an educational minimum and the specific experience.
