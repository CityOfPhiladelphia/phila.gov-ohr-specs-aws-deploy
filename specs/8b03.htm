CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

8B03
FOOD SERVICE SUPERVISOR I

Specialties
( General ) ( Correctional )

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is food service work within the area of specialization directing and participating in the preparation and service of food in a municipal institution or prison. An employee in the general specialty of this class supervises Cooks and Food Service Workers in the preparation and service of food. An employee in the correctional specialty supervises inmates in the preparation and service of food. Work in this class includes assigning, reviewing and training subordinates; and preparing and serving food and beverages to residents, inmates, and/or staff members. Employees in this class perform their duties on an assigned shift.

Work is performed under the general supervision of a higher level food service superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

Both Specialties

  • Reviews and/or prepares work schedule; assigns and reviews the work of employees engaged in preparing simple foods and beverages, setting tables, setting up trays, serving meals, cleaning food service areas, and washing food service equipment and kitchen utensils; instructs employees in proper sanitary techniques and methods of food preparation and service; orients new employees to the work; maintains discipline among employees; participates in the work as required.
  • Prepares daily requisitions for commissary items such as milk, butter, and bread and maintains a census of quantities consumed; prepares weekly requisitions for other staples such as sugar, salt, coffee, and tea.
  • Periodically inventories dishes, glassware, utensils, and cleaning supplies on hand; requisitions replacements as necessary; inspects conveyor belts, refrigerators, and other mechanical equipment for proper functioning and contacts maintenance personnel as required.
  • Answers complaints from patrons regarding food service; arranges dining room for special functions.
  • Checks trays on trayline or in dining room areas for completeness and accuracy of diets; makes any changes as needed.
  • Performs related work as required.

Correctional

  • Assigns a variety of food service tasks to inmates; instructs inmates in serving methods including setting up dining areas, measuring portions of foods to be served; observes serving line to insure portion control; oversees cleaning of cabinets, counters, tables and chairs, floors and other surfaces, dishes and utensils, pots and pans, machines, and other equipment; monitors inmates disposing of trash and garbage; counts utensils before and after use by inmates.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods and practices used in preparing and serving food in a large institution.
  • the methods and procedures used in maintaining cleanliness and safe environment in dining rooms, work rooms, and serving equipment.

ABILITY TO:

  • supervise the work of a group of inmates engaged in routine aspects of food preparation, service and related tasks for the Correctional Specialty.
  • supervise the work of a group of employees engaged in routine food preparation, service and related tasks.
  • maintain personal cleanliness.
  • maintain records and to prepare reports.
  • understand and follow oral and written instructions.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, patients, and the public.
  • resolve a variety of operational problems.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 11/1984.)
  • Education equivalent to the completion of the eighth school grade.
  • Two years of experience in the service and routine preparation of food in an institution or commercial firm.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
