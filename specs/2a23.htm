CHIEF ACCOUNTING OFFICER

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is accounting management work directing all sections and activities of the City's central accounting agency through subordinate managers and supervisors.  The employee in this class provides high level strategic thinking to the Director of Finance, advocates for key outcomes and strategies to improve the city’s financial health and recordkeeping; provides oversight, leadership and direction to central Finance accounting staff, as well as sets policy and direction for, and gives guidance on, all accounting matters to departmental accounting staff citywide and high-level city officials.  The employee is responsible for establishing and implementing City-wide accounting policy and for setting overall goals, objectives and priorities within the Accounting Bureau.  Significant to the work is the overall responsibility for implementing financial mandates set forth by the Director of Finance and oversight of all comprehensive financial reporting for the City.  Working with administrative, legislative and departmental officials to implement fiscal policies and testifying at City Council and before other boards and hearings, and providing information on the financial state of the City to media, legislators, government agents, and representatives of the financial and economic community are significant to the work.  The employee reports to the Director of Finance.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS:   (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.    The position must direct all central finance agency accounting functions through subordinate managers.

2.    Only one position will be allocated to the class.

3.    The position must perform work in the City's Central Finance Department.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

           

            Directs the City’s Central Accounting Division, which includes General Accounting, Grants, Accounting, Financial Verification and Procedures, and the City Payroll Division; provides necessary leadership and management to build a high performing team of employees and strong collaboration with other city agencies by clarifying priorities, delegating objectives, and driving and monitoring progress towards organizational and individual goals.

Oversees the Accounting Division to ensure successful completion of their mission to:  maintain the City’s central accounting system, record all City financial activity and prepare and issue all required financial reports in strict accordance with Government Accounting Standards Bureau (GASB) requirements, review and process vendor payments for all city agencies, and administer the City’s payroll activities and process payroll payments for all City employees.

            Formulates and implements accounting policy directives and standards on a city-wide basis; assures that all accounting activities comply with general accepted accounting standards and ethics; develops and implements new and effective accounting processes; oversees the control of both subsidiary and general ledger accounts and the provision of reports reflecting the financial positions of the municipality.

       Commissions studies and select audits on matters of critical financial importance to the City; may engage, and negotiate contracts with, professional accounting/auditing firms when necessary to further the mission of the City’s financial interest or Accounting Division and ensure proper financial controls,

            Directs City-wide accounting functions, including the computerized maintenance of all financial transactions and statements for City funds; oversees the preparation of the City's Comprehensive Annual Financial Report (CAFR) for the City of Philadelphia as mandated by the City Charter within specified time frames; assures that the Comprehensive Annual Financial Report complies with all government, accounting, and ethical standards and practices; directs, through subordinate managers,  the preparation of complex financial statements such as the Supplemental Reports for Revenues and Obligations, Bonded Debt Report, and Schedule of Financial Assistance.

             Establishes, and enforces through subordinate managers, a strong system of internal controls that mitigate risk to the City’s financial condition, protect the City’s assets, ensure all accounting data is correct for reliable financial reporting, maximize the efficiency of City operations, ensure compliance with laws, regulations and policies, and promote an atmosphere of compliance among employees.

            Meets with department heads, Controller, City Treasurer, vendors and others to establish the most efficient flow of financial documents to provide current and accurate information for the City’s financial statements and reports.

            Establishes policies determining the flow of financial information to and from departments; evaluates and determines the best and most efficient format for providing that information.

            Leads various citywide committees on behalf of the Finance Director.

            Serves as senior technical financial consultant to the Finance Director, Mayor’s Cabinet, and department heads, as well as to other City-related agencies, on major financial issues to assure that appropriate issues and concerns are addressed, while ensuring proper safeguards and internal controls are in place.

Communicates with Bond rating agencies such as Moody’s, Fitch and Standard & Poor’s regarding the City’s Comprehensive Annual Financial Report and City’s financial condition. Responsible for overseeing the on-going recordkeeping and compliance on all City's grants monies; directs the monitoring of accounts for independent and delegate agencies to ensure conformance with municipal accounting standards.

Directs, through a subordinate director, the processing of city payrolls in accordance with all federal state, and local regulations, Civil Service Regulations, Administrative Board Rules and Union Contracts.

Develops training and professional development, succession planning and employee retention strategies to provide stability and success for the workforce in the Accounting division.

Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

•           current standards, guidelines and requirements for governmental accounting as promulgated by the Government Accounting Standards Board at an expert level.

•           the principles, standards and practices of the accounting profession.

•           the principles and procedures used in the analysis, maintenance, development and installation of accounting systems, methods and procedures.

•           principles and practices of public administration and their application to a large centralized accounting operation.

•           financial statements and reports used in municipal accounting.

•           the principles and practices of modern management as applied to management problems in the accounting field.

•           the uses of computer systems and applications for accounting, financial statement preparation, reporting, and recordkeeping and payroll operations.

•           the principles, practices and techniques of accounting as they refer to municipal bond indebtedness and bond sales.

 

SKILL IN:

 

•           examining, analyzing and interpreting financial reports and records.

•           interpreting comprehensive accounting statements and implementing new accounting regulations effectively in a variety of financial reports

·                     evaluating budget agency activities and functions and implementing changes to provide for more effective and efficient operations.

·                     strategic thinking and planning for the advancement of the City’s financial health and recordkeeping

 

ABILITY TO:

 

•           enlist the cooperation of officials to implement policy and procedural changes.

•           present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.

•           exercise judgment and discretion in applying and interpreting policies and procedures consistent with overall policy and objectives.

•           establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, citywide officials, and the public.

•           direct the work of a large staff of managerial, professional, technical, and clerical personnel.

•           administer a large centralized municipal accounting program.

•           establish and implement city-wide accounting policy.

•           develop and install standard and special accounting methods, procedures and forms.

·                     develop and enforce an effective system of internal controls to mitigate financial risk in a large, complex organization.

•           resolve the most complex technical accounting problems and issues encountered in municipal accounting.

·                     assemble resources to accomplish financial and organizational goals;

·                     manage numerous initiatives concurrently while using resources effectively and efficiently. 

·                     Collaborate with key partners across multiple departments and quasi-governmental organizations to accomplish goals

·                     Serve as a neutral, and skilled facilitator to guide the staff, partners, and stakeholders towards consensus and defined, shared vision and results

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 9/18.)

 

EDUCATION

 

            Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major coursework in accounting.

 

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE

 

                        Nine years of accounting experience, five of which includes supervisory accounting experience.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

 

            Two years of managerial accounting experience as the Accounting Director.

 

OR

 

            Any equivalent combination of experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources that has included the bachelor’s degree with major coursework in accounting and specific experience described above.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
