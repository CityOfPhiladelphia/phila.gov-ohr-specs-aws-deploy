DENTAL HYGIENIST

Specialties
( General ) ( Bilingual )
GENERAL DEFINITION

This is technical dental work in the assistance of dentists for the care and treatment of the teeth and gums of patients in the City’s Dental Health Clinics. Employees in this class assist the dentist as they examine and treat patients during the provision of preventive maintenance and restorative services, and perform hygienist duties such as examining and cleaning teeth, oral structures and soft tissue. Employees may be assisted by lower-level auxiliary personnel in the dental clinic. Employees additionally maintain records of dental treatment and make dental appointments.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

  • Positions in this class must be located in one of the City's Health Clinics.
  • Positions in this class conduct a thorough examination of patient’s oral health to collect data to identify dental hygiene care needs.
  • Positions in this class must work at multiple health centers and on varying shifts as assigned based on health center needs.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)
BILINGUAL SPECIALTY

Performs the duties described below with English speaking and designated non English speaking populations.

BOTH SPECIALTIES

Assesses dental condition and needs of patient; uses patient screening procedures to include medical history review; conducts dental charting and periodontal charting.

Delivers direct patient care to patients as assigned, using established dental hygienist procedures; performs routine treatment procedures such as cleaning, polishing, x-rays, application of sealants and fluorides and related procedures.

Develops and implements individualized dental care plans for patients; performs patient education, discharge planning, and patient/family teaching under the supervision of a qualified dentist.

Assists with emergency measures for sudden adverse developments during treatment of patients.

Assists in preparation of patient care areas; prepares reports and assists as required with patient reception, telephone calls, appointment scheduling, routine triage and other office duties.

Orders and maintains dental hygiene instruments and supplies.

Takes, develops, duplicates and mounts dental x ray films, changes film processing solutions as required.

Instructs children and adults in the health center, at schools and in the community on proper dental care and oral hygiene.

May direct activities of lower-level dental auxiliary staff.

Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES
KNOWLEDGE OF:
  • the types and uses of dental instruments and of the preparation and use of dental materials
  • technical dental treatment procedures and dental nomenclature
  • general office procedures and standard clerical operations as they apply to a dental clinic
  • the procedures and techniques of restoring teeth
SKILL IN:
  • the application of the procedures and techniques of restoring teeth
  • non-professional dental procedures and techniques such as sterilization and care of instruments
  • taking and developing x-rays
  • instructing patients, especially children and adolescents in proper care of teeth and gums
ABILITY TO:
  • perform a full range of established dental hygienist protocol and procedures
  • analyze medical records and prescribed protocol to collect data in order to formulate effective therapeutic treatment plans
  • assess the nature and extent of dental, periodontic and associated conditions and pathology in dental outpatients
  • communicate technical information to non-technical personnel
  • carry out proper procedures, practices and manners in assisting a dentist while giving treatment
  • obtain the confidence and cooperation of pre school and school age children while rendering dental treatment
  • instruct children and parents in proper dental care and oral hygiene
MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 11/09)

BILINGUAL SPECIALTY
Sufficient training and/or experience to be able to understand and speak fluently and clearly in English and a designated non English language.

EDUCATION
Graduation from a 2-year dental hygiene school training program accredited by the American Dental Association.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS
Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES
Possession of a Pennsylvania State license as a Dental Hygienist prior to application.
