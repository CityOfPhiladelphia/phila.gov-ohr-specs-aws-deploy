TAX ASSESSOR SUPERVISOR

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is supervisory taxpayer discovery and assessment work in the determination of liability for taxes payable to the City or School District.  Employees in this class participate in the discovery and assessment process and plan, assign, and review the work activities of a group of Tax Assessors engaged in discovery of confidential federal and state tax documents and other information to identify and assess tax liabilities payable to the City Philadelphia. Employees in this class supervise Tax Assessors and clerical staff responsible for billing taxpayers for liabilities and also provide training to Tax Assessors to ensure they utilize the City’s tax applicable tax rate and apply regulations appropriately to determine tax liabilities. Contact with the general public and their accounting or legal representatives to discuss findings and resolve liabilities to come into compliance, and maintaining confidentiality of tax materials received from the Internal Revenue Service and other governmental sources are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the general supervision of an administrative superior.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.    Positions in this class must supervise the work activities of a group of Tax Assessors and clerical staff engaged in the discovery and assessment work in the determination of liability for taxes payable to the City or School District.

2.    Positions in this class must be located in the Tax Discovery Unit of the Department of Revenue.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

Supervises the work activities of a group of Tax Assessors engaged in the identification and assessment of unreported or underreported Philadelphia Business Income and Receipts Tax, Net Profits Tax, Earning Tax, School Income Tax and other taxes; plans, assigns, and reviews Tax Assessors’ and clerical staff’s work activities; assists subordinate staff with interpretation of the City’s tax ordinances and regulations in determining assessment; checks on status of work assigned to subordinate staff; ensures that current and delinquent taxes are assessed and liability is determined in accordance with the City tax ordinances and regulations: safeguards confidential tax information from Internal Revenue Service and other governmental sources; resolves most complex liability issues for taxpayers and their accounting or legal representatives; responds to correspondence involving the most complex discovery and assessment work pertaining to unreported City taxes; explains interpretations of tax ordinances; interacts with attorneys and other employees of the City Law Department to assist in the litigation and collection of unpaid taxes; reviews appeals of tax assessments and methods used by subordinate Tax Assessors in deriving assessments; reviews computation of charges and penalties by subordinate staff to give or for Tax Assessors to give sworn testimony; ensures the adherence to departmental and Internal Revenue Service requirements for the safeguarding of confidential taxpayer information.

            Reviews disposition of cases by subordinate Tax Assessors to determine that taxes are assessed in accordance with the City’s tax laws and regulations and that the tax due is computed correctly; trains and instructs new employees in the provisions and interpretation of the City’s tax ordinances and regulations, the techniques of telephone and in-person interviewing, and the entering of tax returns and liabilities on the departments tax assessments module; supervises clerical staff responsible for preparing related correspondence and processing related payments.

            Performs administrative duties related to the completion of weekly and monthly reports for unit activities for management review; prepares status reports on tax cases resolved and on unit performance; reviews work processes to identify areas for improvement, automation, or evaluation; redistributes work when necessary to maintain specific level of production; evaluates performance and approves time and leave requests for subordinate staff. 

            Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         business English, spelling and arithmetic

·         the format and use of federal and state income tax returns and related business documents

·         tax ordinances and their application in determining tax liability

·         investigative practices as they apply in the determination of assessments

·         discovery and assessment techniques and procedures

·         interviewing principles and techniques

·         supervisory Practices

·         provisions of a variety of City tax ordinances and regulations related to tax discovery and assessment

·         computer software used for the assessment of unpaid and delinquent taxes payable to the City

 

SKILL IN:

 

·            making varied arithmetic computations with speed and accuracy

·            eliciting and giving information easily, clearly and concisely

·            research and analysis of various sources of taxpayer documentation and

       information in verifying residency and determining tax liabilities

·            applying various tax rates and interpreting tax regulations in the

       computation of City taxes owed

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·            train Tax Assessors in the discovery, identification, and computation of a variety of taxes payable to the City

·            plan, assign and review the work of a group of Tax Assessors

·            express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing

·            establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, department/City officials, taxpayers and their accounting or legal representatives

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/17.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Completion of the equivalent of the twelfth school grade.

 

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

            Three years of clerical experience.

 

AND

SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

 

            Two years of experience as a Tax Assessor determining liability for business, earnings, and school income taxes payable to the municipal government and/or the school district. 

 

OR

 

Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
