MUNICIPAL ENGINEERING AND CONSTRUCTION DIRECTOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative engineering and construction work directing the activities of the Engineering Division of Public Property. The employee in this class, through subordinate engineering and construction project supervisors, directs and coordinates capital improvement and renovation projects for municipal buildings and facilities. Work includes supervision of the preparation of engineering project plans and specifications, construction projects in City agencies and departments, and the development of engineering and construction policies and procedures. The employee directs and supervises the activities of engineering and construction staff, and private consultants and contractors engaged in the planning and executing of capital improvement and renovation projects. Contact with staff, other City agencies and departments, consultants, contractors, and state and federal officials is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the general direction of a Deputy Commissioner.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

  • This is a one position class located in the Engineering Division of Public Property.
  • The position must plan and direct capital improvement and renovation projects in municipal buildings and facilities.
  • The position must, through subordinate engineering and construction project supervisors, plan and direct the preparation and revision of engineering project plans and specifications, coordinate architectural projects under construction, supervise the development of engineering and construction policies and procedures, and direct the activities of engineering and construction staff, consultants and contractors engaged in the planning and executing of capital improvement and renovation projects.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, directs and coordinates, through subordinate engineering and construction supervisors, the engineering and construction aspects of the City's municipal facility construction program; directs the provision of engineering services to other City departments in the development and completion of capital construction and renovation projects; develops and establishes engineering standards, procedures and policies to be applied in projects; participates in the development and planning of the capital budget; evaluates and advises on the engineering and construction feasibility of projects and their priority; assigns personnel to projects and directs the development of project control time schedules, reporting mechanisms and cost control procedures; makes periodic review of financial and progress status of projects; coordinates architectural projects under construction.
  • Directs and supervises, through engineering supervisors, the preparation and review of civil, electrical, mechanical designs, specifications and other documents for a variety of building construction and rehabilitation, asbestos abatement, and transit related projects funded through the City's capital program; directs and supervises the review and revision of project plans and specifications prepared by consultants; evaluates the feasibility of ordered plans and designs; reviews and approves contract documents, partial and final cost estimates, progress reports, engineering studies and budget documents.
  • Directs and supervises, through construction supervisors, the work of engineers, technicians, and inspectors engaged in inspecting construction projects to insure that contractors are complying with plans and specifications; approves changes in contracts or plans to compensate for field conditions; supervises attempts to resolve scheduling, work quality and payment problems with contractors.
  • Consults with departmental administrative officials, consulting engineers, architects and contractors, and representatives of government, industry and the general public regarding municipal construction projects; serves as central liaison with federal, state, and regional transit authorities concerning City sponsored transit capital projects; meets with union representatives and employees affected by project activities to discuss safety related issues and concerns; advises departmental officials of feasibility of implementing rehabilitative projects to improve facility operations and possible alternatives; acts as liaison between City agencies and private engineering firms.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the modern theories of engineering and construction planning.
  • the modern methods, practices and procedures of planning and implementation of large scale engineering and construction projects.
  • the methods, practices and procedures involved in administering the Philadelphia municipal capital improvement and renovation projects.
  • the review methods and implementation practices of engineering and construction projects, and architectural projects under construction.
  • cost estimate and budgetary preparation as they relate to the planning and implementation of engineering and construction projects.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.

ABILITY TO:

  • plan, coordinate and direct engineering and construction projects, and architectural projects under construction in municipal buildings and facilities, and for City agencies and departments.
  • determine feasibility of engineering and construction projects, and plan and direct, through subordinate engineering supervisors, the preparation and revision of project plans and specifications.
  • interpret, through subordinate engineering and construction supervisors, architectural plans under construction, and coordinate and resolve inconsistencies in project plans and specifications.
  • determine costs of engineering, architectural and construction projects, and dates for their completion, and decide best ways to accomplish project plans and specifications on time.
  • direct, through subordinate engineering and construction supervisors, the activities of engineering and construction staff, and consultants and contractors engaged in the planning and executing of project plans and specifications.
  • establish and enforce engineering and construction project policies and procedures.
  • monitor performance standards on all engineering and construction projects.
  • make changes in project plans and specifications and resolve personnel and contract problems.
  • accurately compile financial and operational reports, and analyze statistical and budgetary statements.
  • meet with representatives of City, State and Federal agencies, and department officials concerning project plans and specifications.
  • present ideas effectively both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, consultants and contractors.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/1993.)
    EDUCATION:
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in civil, electrical or mechanical engineering.

    AND
    GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

  • Six years of engineering and/or construction experience.

    AND
    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

  • Three years of administrative engineering experience as project manager for successive design and construction projects of considerable diversity and complexity.

    OR

  • Three years of administrative engineering work directing, through subordinate supervisors, the construction management and inspection program for projects of considerable size and complexity.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included the bachelor's degree and the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Registration as a professional engineer with the Pennsylvania State Registration Board for Professional Engineers, or registration as a professional architect, by the Pennsylvania State Board of Examiners of Architects, prior to and during tenure of employment.
