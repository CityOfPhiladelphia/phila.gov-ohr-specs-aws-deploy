HUMAN RESOURCES MANAGEMENT SYSTEM TECHNICAL ANALYST

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

This is specialized information technology work providing technical support for the Oracle Human Resources Management System (HRMS) modules deployed city-wide and managed by the City’s central human resources agency in conjunction with the central information technology agency. The employee participates in the technical deployment and upgrades of new HRMS modules and the operational support of existing components. An employee is responsible for representing the human resources agency during the development, revision and implementation of technical Human Resources Management System modules unique to the agency. A significant aspect of the work is the development, preparation, production and security of confidential management reports for use in human resources analysis, including the collective bargaining process. Employees in this class are supervised by an information technology manager.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

  1. Positions must provide technical leadership and support for the Oracle Human Resources Management System.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

Participates with the central Information Technology agency in the evaluation, design,  installation, testing and deployment of Oracle eBusiness Suite database and application patches and version releases; collaborates with central Information Technology agency staff to ensure business requirements are met throughout the systems development life cycle; supports existing modules through the use of PL/SQL, Oracle reporting tools, and SQL utilities; implements changes at the operating system, database, or application level; analyzes and develops a thorough understanding of Oracle eBusiness system architecture; uses appropriate change management procedures; determines operating characteristics and requirements; develops or modifies and documents general system design; writes detailed design specifications; conducts tests for proposed solutions to system problems;  evaluates the scheduling of changes, and the proper testing and implementation of any software changes; assesses the impact of new modules and upgrades on the Oracle HRMS users; coordinates work with the business analyst , the central IT agency and  department users to ensure that upgrades or patches do not have a negative impact on operations.

Assists other technical staff in defining and maintaining proper user role definition, user ID creation, and role assignment; determines security levels, view definition and access  to the Oracle HRMS to ensure data integrity; ensures the security of confidential data; maintains and monitors interfaces that have been established for availability; responds to issues related to interface data feeds; tests the application to assure functionality and effectiveness in performing to desired specifications.

Participates in resolving any application related problems; contacts central IT agency staff and reports issues to be analyzed and resolved; may take the lead in coordinating activities across numerous agencies with the City.

Leads the design, development and implementation of new reports that serve the continuing needs of central human resources agency and the departmental counterparts;  develops, prepares and produces a variety of confidential reports for use by management during the collective bargaining process; supports  existing reports by resolving any report execution or distribution problems, identification of data anomalies, and testing of existing reports as new reporting tool versions become available for deployment; conducts performance tuning of new reports; monitors response time of all reports; works with other agency information technology staff to assure user satisfaction.

Conducts interviews with business owners and end users, performs workflow analysis, documents scope and objectives, and participates in design sessions;  serves as liaison between department staff and information technology staff in the design and implementation of technology solutions;  supports strategic and operational planning activities, collective bargaining, process development, business continuity and disaster recovery plan development activities; develops and maintains implementation, conversion and test plans; confers with representatives of the management bargaining team to determine data needs, format reports and deliver requested data; provides guidance in gathering, formatting, analyzing and presenting data required for bargaining.

Serves as a technical innovator in the identification, evaluation and adoption of new HR modules of the Oracle eBusiness Suite; maintains awareness of current trends in HRMS with a focus on product and service development, delivery and support through regular participation in technical training, attending professional conferences, and continuous review of all available sources.

Coordinates with outside vendors and contractors to complete projects and service requests; defines, assigns and evaluates their work.

Designs, develops, tests and generates routine and ad hoc reports in response to user needs.

Documents processes, IT systems, applications, products and/or services. Provides user support and training in the use of systems and solutions, and analyzes system/user problems.

Perform related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

  • Relational databases and SQL-based database applications programming environments
  • Oracle E-Business foundation, interfaces, and implementation
  • Applications programming tools and techniques and end user tools and techniques for complex distributed computer systems involving Windows database and application Servers, LAN Servers, desktop and laptop computers.
  • Oracle E-Business Suite Human Resources Management System
  • Windows Servers
  • Oracle Report Writer
  • Oracle Reports and table/architecture
  • Oracle Workflow , Forms Development and Troubleshooting
  • Data warehousing concepts
  • Microsoft Office (Access and Excel)
  • Business analysis

PREFERRED KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

  • Oracle RDBMS 9i/10g or higher
  • Oracle Developer 6i (Forms and Reports)
  • 3 Oracle Discoverer 3i or higher
  • Oracle Web Tool Kit (Web PL/SQL, HTML, XML, Java Script)
  • Oracle Workflow 2.3 or higher
  • Oracle SQL Loader 4.0 or higher
  • Oracle J Developer (Java) 3.0 or higher
  • J2EE

 

ABILITY TO:

 

  • Learn the operations, functions, data and reporting needs and requirements of the agency
  • Learn the regulations, ordinances, policies and procedures related to the work of the agency
  • Read, interpret, and understand technical information and apply cross-functional knowledge
  • Maintain current knowledge of developments and trends in HRMS
  • Effectively present information and respond to questions from managers, co-workers, and system users
  • Communicate well verbally
  • Write clearly, to create technical documentation related to report development activities
  • Train individual or groups of users of systems
  • Lead and to assist others when appropriate to achieve effective results in a timely manner
  • Work independently and on a team composed of representatives of multiple departments
  • Work independently with little supervision
  • Exercise discretion in the storing, accessing and dissemination of confidential data
  • Perform efficiently under strict deadlines
  • Handle multiple project responsibilities simultaneously

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE  (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on .)

 

EDUCATION

 

Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in Computer Science, Information Systems, Systems Engineering or a closely related field.

Formal training in Oracle E-Business foundation, interfaces, and implementation is preferred.

 

EXPERIENCE

 

Five years of progressive post-baccalaureate information technology experience in an Oracle HRMS module support role, two years of which must have involved implementing module and patch releases within a controlled full life-cycle environment subscribing to rigid change management processes.

 

OR

 

Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources that has included a bachelor's degree and the specific experience described above.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
