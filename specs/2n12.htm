ASSISTANT FINANCE DIRECTOR - LEGISLATIVE AFFAIRS

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is responsible administrative work directing the development and implementation of the City's legislative program and serving as the official representative of the City to the Pennsylvania General Assembly and to the executive branch of the state government. The employee may be required, without prior consultation with the Mayor or cabinet officers to formulate and present the position of the City on matters pending before the legislative and executive branches of the state government. Representing the City before the legislative and executive branches of the state government is of major significance to the work.

Work is performed under the general direction of an administrative superior. Assignments require the use of analytical thinking and are carried out in accordance with broad general policies. The employee provides consultative services to City officials to achieve implementation of the City's legislative program.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Confers with the Mayor and cabinet officers to develop the City's legislative program and to suggest lobbying strategy designed to achieve implementation of the program.
  • Convenes caucuses of the Philadelphia members of the General Assembly to advise them of the City's position on bills to be introduced, bills in committees scheduled to be considered, and bills on the calendar in a position to be voted on; recommends action to be taken by members on each of these matters.
  • Confers with chairmen of standing committees and their staffs and with representatives of the Governor's Office; testifies before standing committees on bills affecting the City; accompanies other City officials who testify before standing committees.
  • Establishes and maintains cooperative working relationships with the Pennsylvania County Commissioners Association, the Pennsylvania League of Cities and other local government groups on legislative matters; confers with mayors and other elected officials of Pennsylvania local governments to advise them of the City's position on various legislative matters and to enlist their concurrence.
  • Directs through a research assistant the analysis of bills to determine the effect of such bills on City agencies and their programs; monitors proposed changes in rules and regulations of state agencies to determine their possible effect on City operations; works with department heads in improving their relationships with state agencies and officials.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and practices of public administration.
  • the operations and functions of municipal government.
  • modern management principles and practices.
  • legislative research methods and techniques and of their application to problems affecting local and state governments.

ABILITY TO:

  • plan, organize, and direct programs designed to provide effective liaison among municipal governments and state legislative and executive branches.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with governmental officials, legislators, and associates.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/77.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
  • Six years of general administrative or technical staff experience in a large organization, two years of which has been performing technical staff work in the analysis of bills placed before legislative bodies to determine their relevance to the interests and programs of local or state governments and one year of which has been representing a state or large local government before the United States Congress or representing a large local government before a state legislature.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
