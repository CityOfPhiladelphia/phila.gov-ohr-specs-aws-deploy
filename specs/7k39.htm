LIBRARY AUDIO-VISUAL TECHNICIAN

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is technical work repairing and operating a variety of audio and video equipment in support of educational productions for library facilities. An employee in this class is responsible for providing quality video tape programs and motion picture programs and repairing electrically and electronically powered equipment and systems for the Free Library of Philadelphia. Work includes functioning as cameraperson, projectionist, tape editor and repairworker and installing and maintaining library communications and security systems. Supervising lower level technicians on large maintenance and production projects and when there is a backlog of equipment and systems repair orders is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative or technical superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • The work must be performed in the Free Library of Philadelphia.
  • The position must operate and repair a variety of audio and video equipment in support of educational productions and special events for library facilities.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Connects and operates video tape cameras, projectors, a satellite dish, phonographs, recorders, audio equipment, closed circuit TV cameras and monitors and lighting equipment in Free Library facilities; films and tapes programs; edits tapes of programs to obtain a finished product; discusses limits of taping equipment with program initiators.
  • Runs telephone and cable TV wires; troubleshoots telephone problems; reconfigures telephone lines when changes are made.
  • Services and makes necessary repairs to a variety of equipment including projectors, recorders, cameras, black and white and color TV equipment, microphones, speakers, compact disc players, amplifiers, audio and video tape equipment, telephones, computers, and closed circuit TV security equipment; may supervise the work of electricians on large projects or when there is a backlog of broken equipment.
  • Operates a microwave satellite dish to access requested cable channels; sets up televisions and rooms for viewing.
  • Inspects defective communication equipment to determine malfunctions; replaces or repairs defective parts.
  • Interacts with special events coordinators to determine their audio-visual needs.
  • Installs and repairs public address systems at the main library and all branch locations.
  • Interprets and traces schematic drawings of equipment to locate malfunctions.
  • Operates a variety of test equipment to locate malfunctions and check completed repairs.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, operation and capability of video cameras, projectors, CD players, phonographs, taping equipment, satellite communications systems and closed circuit television security systems.
  • the maintenance and repair of audio and video equipment, taping equipment and communications systems.
  • the principles and theory of electricity and electronics.

SKILL IN:

  • the operation of video tape cameras, projectors, recorders, and audio equipment.
  • the use and care of tools and equipment used in the work.

ABILITY TO:

  • maintain and repair cameras, projectors, taping equipment, black and white and color TV, recorders, telephones, compact disc players, and computer equipment.
  • maintain and repair a variety of communications equipment.
  • edit tapes to obtain a quality finished taped product.
  • read and interpret blueprints and schematic diagrams.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with program directors and special events coordinators.
  • understand and follow oral and written instructions.
  • adapt to new technologies and changes in the communications, electronics and electrical field.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/78.)
    EDUCATION:
  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • Successful completion of an electronics training course of at least one year's duration at a state licensed trade school.

    AND

    GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Four years of experience installing and repairing a variety of communications equipment.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Library Audio-Visual Technician.
