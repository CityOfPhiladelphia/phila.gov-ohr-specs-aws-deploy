SENIOR SCIENCE TECHNICIAN

 

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is advanced level technical work performing a variety of assignments in support of engineering or technical staff in a water treatment plant in the Water Department.  Employees perform such assignments as water quality investigations, collecting and evaluating water samples, and making mathematical calculations required for dosage changes. This class differs from similar classes by the complexity of the assignments and responsibility for making regular, timely decisions to maintain process control. Work will involve rotating shift work and disagreeable aspects. Work is performed under the supervision of an engineering, scientific, administrative or technical superior.

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

 

1.    Positions must perform a variety of assignments in support of engineering and scientific projects in a water treatment plant at the Water Department.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

            Performs water quality surveys; gathers data and water and wastewater samples and performs field and laboratory, physical, chemical or biological tests to determine water quality contents; performs moderately complex sample analysis using quantitative and qualitative methods; logs samples; prepares sample bottles, including specialized cleaning, adding special reagents, and labeling.

            Performs and documents required quality control tests consistent with laboratory standard operating procedures, good laboratory practices and lab accreditation requirements; processes samples within required holding times and laboratory goals, understands & follows QA/QC protocols to ensure all data generated meets accrediting authority standards as well internal laboratory requirements; interprets results for alignment with process control targets, regulatory limits, and facility goals ; makes timely notification of results deviating from pre-established ranges; takes action to correct deviation.

            Prescribes, on the basis of established tests, the amounts of chemicals to be added to the treatment process; calculates various ratios and dosages in response to changing conditions; makes operational adjustments as needed to comply with state and federal water quality standards; notifies plant operators of test results requiring changes in chemical treatment; reads maps and diagrams in order to determine appropriate sample locations and problem sites; performs treatment chemicals concentration testing to verify chemical deliveries meet the Water Departments specifications.

            Acts as a resource to and participates in training new technicians or colleagues to develop their competence in water quality and process control.

            Operates and maintains a variety of laboratory equipment; identifies routine problems and conducts basic troubleshooting and maintenance of analytical equipment and processes to ensure continued sample analysis and optimum instrument performance.

            Calibrates laboratory equipment utilizing a variety of controls and prepared samples; maintains laboratory environment in a sanitary condition.

            Continuously monitors online water quality analyzers and responds to alarms on both local monitoring panels and treatment plant computer-based process control systems; reports and investigates discrepancies between online water quality analyzers and bench analyses to appropriate staff.

            Performs statistical analysis of data, using mean, standard deviation, and various other formulae; compiles and enters data into Laboratory Information Management System (LIMS) or other programs; performs calculations; prepares written reports on projects, tests results, and basic interpretation for engineers, scientists, and technical staff; prepares a variety of charts, graphs and tables for the presentation of collected data; distributes reports to city, state and federal agencies.

            Performs various quality control functions in the laboratory as required.

            Maintains a written log of field observations and writes reports of relevant findings, including the results of samples.

            Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         the principles and practices of water treatment

·         mathematics and its application to basic chemistry, biology and engineering calculations

·         the nomenclature, technical symbols, equipment and materials used in chemistry, biology and/or basic engineering

·         the principles and techniques for setting up and operating analytical and monitoring instrumenta­tion

·         the principles, practices and methods used in basic chemistry, biology and/or hydraulics

·         the principles of Good Laboratory Practice (GLP)

·         the techniques used in conducting basic chemical testing

·         computer software and hardware required by the assignment

·         the hazards and safety precautions of laboratory work

 

SKILL IN:

 

·         the use and care of instruments, tools, materials, and equipment used in chemistry, biology and/or engineering

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·         learn[JL1]  the applicable City, State and Federal regulations and department procedures and policies

·         learn the fundamental causes and effects of water quality degradation and improvement

·         prepare, read and interpret technical plans, layouts, charts, diagrams, schematics, maps, estimates, specifications and reports if required by assignment

·         make accurate observations of test results and to prepare accurate reports and records

·         prepare and interpret reports showing test results and analyses

·         perform mathematical calculations to determine various ratios, dosages, volumetric calculations that are required for the operation of treatment plants

·         interact effectively with co-workers and clients

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/23).

 

EDUCATION:

 

Education equivalent to completion of twelfth school grade.

 

AND

EXPERIENCE:

 

Two years of experience as a Science Technician for the Philadelphia Water Department.

 

OR

 

Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources which has included the specific experience.

 

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

 

            Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Common­wealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Senior Science Technician if required by work assignment.
