CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

5A90
JUVENILE JUSTICE COMMUNITY SERVICES ADMINISTRATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative work directing prevention services for youths at risk of delinquency in the community. The employee in this class, through subordinate supervisors, directs the administration and monitoring of funded neighborhood groups, persons and community-based organizations providing prevention services to youth. Work includes reviewing and approving prevention service proposals/grant applications, and analyzing and evaluating prevention services provided by contracted agencies. The employee develops policy guidelines and procedures for providing services, and directs and develops training for staff and provider agencies. Contact with neighborhood groups, community-based organizations, and city and state agencies involved with the juvenile justice system is significant to the work. Work is performed under the direction of a Juvenile Justice Community Services Director.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • The position must direct, through subordinate supervisors, the administration and monitoring of funded neighborhood groups, persons and community-based organizations providing prevention services to youth.
  • The position must review and approve prevention service proposals/grant applications, analyze and evaluate prevention services provided by contracted agencies, develop policy guidelines and procedures for providing services, and direct and develop training for staff and provider agencies.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs, through subordinate supervisors, the administration and monitoring of contracted agencies providing prevention services; directs the development of information-gathering, case management and referral service procedures; reviews and approves service proposals/grant applications with subordinate staff; develops and recommends appropriate budget submission procedures for grant awards; directs the monitoring of grant programs and technical assistance to provider agencies.
  • Directs, through a subordinate supervisor, the analysis and evaluation of prevention services provided by contracted agencies; identifies agencies with administrative or service problems; develops appropriate policy guidelines and procedures for prevention services and/or agency studies or investigations; assigns and supervises investigation of isolated incidents related to prevention services.
  • Coordinates prevention service information such as behaviors at risk of delinquency with provider agencies, and city and state agencies involved with juvenile justice system; prepares handouts for distribution.
  • Directs and develops training for staff and provider agencies; directs the development of special training for staff engaged in prevention services for youth; administers a summer intern scholarship program for disadvantaged youths; establishes guidelines for nominations by community groups and organizations; supervises and participates in the evaluation of nominated candidates, and the determination of work sites for interns; directs the monitoring of placements for conformance with the community service goals and programs.
  • Serves as a member of the Division Management Team responsible for discussing and considering broad policy and procedural issues affecting agency operations; makes recommendation on issues pertaining to prevention services for youth.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices, methods, techniques, literature, and current developments in the field of social services for children and youth.
  • the principles, practices, and procedures used for social service program planning and evaluation.
  • the laws, regulations, and guidelines affecting funding and implementation of social service programs for children and youth, especially as they relate to the operation of programs within the context of the juvenile justice services system.
  • administrative principles and practices as they relate to the operation of programs within the social services system.
  • the philosophy and objectives underlying social services for children and youth.
  • the principles and practices of managerial and administrative analysis.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
ABILITY TO:

  • plan and direct a program of contract monitoring and evaluation in the field of children and youth services.
  • manage the administration of a community outreach grants program.
  • exercise sound judgment in the review and revision of agency monitoring procedures and in the development of recommendations for program change.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with subordinate supervisors, contracted agency directors, departmental administrators, and representatives of outside agencies.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 2/95.)

EDUCATION:

  • Completion of a master's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in social work or social work administration.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE:
  • Four years of social service program monitoring or social work experience.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:
  • One year of supervisory social service program experience in monitoring funded neighborhood groups, persons and community-based organizations providing prevention services to youth in a juvenile justice related program.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included a master's degree as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
