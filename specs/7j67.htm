City Of Philadelphia Office Of Human Resources

7J67

WATER CUSTOMER SERVICES MANAGER

Specialties

  1. Field Services
  2. Cross-Connection

General Definition

  • This is second level supervisory work directing a citywide utility customer service program. Employees in this class are responsible for either supervising the investigation and/or repair of customer complaints and claims pertaining to water and sewer services and the water code compliance program, or managing and overseeing the investigation and/or repair of lateral connections and identification of defective laterals, or for the administration of residential water and sewer loan and repair programs. Responsibility for ongoing process and service improvements and remediation of minor claims for water damage to property are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the general direction of an administrative superior.

Allocating Factors:

(The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)
  • Work must be performed within the Water Department.

Typical Examples Of Work (Illustrative Only)

  • Field Services Specialty
    • Supervises through subordinate supervisors the investigation and resolution of customer complaints concerning water service failures including short supply, discolored and/or foul tasting water, defective draining systems, flooded basements, and customer damage claims.
    • Oversees residential plumbing repairs through office and field personnel, subordinate inspectors and sub-contracted plumbing contractors; reviews the accuracy of completed program applications via unit office staff; investigates and reviews escalated customer complaints in the completion of residential sewer and water repairs.
    • Directs the investigation of complaints from the public concerning the distribution, pumping and filtration of water; works cooperatively with officials in other divisions and City departments to resolve complaints; oversees billing and revenue investigations of unaccounted for water usage and problem accounts.
    • Develops and implements an instruction program for field personnel in the correct use of materials used in the installation of residential, commercial and industrial water connections; directs the proper handling of water code regulation and ordinance violations pertaining to water service connections; designs and implements training sessions on safety, courtesy, inspection procedures and other related water operations to employees.
    • Participates in the administration of Homeowners Emergency Loan Program; authorizes the approval of emergency loans to homeowners for City Fund Abatement repairs; negotiates contracts with contracted plumbers and approves work assigned to the City Fund; reviews charitable water accounts; approves or rejects new charity applications based on the findings of field investigations; reviews or suggests a recommended settlement offered to customers filing small claims for water damage to property.
    • Reviews the effectiveness and appropriateness of method(s) and equipment for use in the Plumbing Repairs unit programs; monitors the effectiveness of processes and procedures utilized to make repairs in the Basement Protection Program; evaluates and determines sub-contractor eligibility for unit program participation; assesses sub-contractor qualifications in order to determine the level of program participation; negotiates contractor fees in order to establish a fixed pricing guideline.
    • Examines reports documenting Homeowner Emergency Loan Program re-payment history; develops and determines the distribution of budgetary funding for use by the Plumbing Repairs Unit; assigns and delegates tasks to both office and field personnel; maintains reports documenting the program activity; communicates with various departments in order to initiate residential loan repayment and property liens;

    Cross-Connection Specialty
    • Supervises field operations through subordinate supervisors; develops priority investigation target areas based on sample data analysis in consultation with departmental engineers; coordinates program activities with the Plumbing Repairs unit and other departmental units and City agencies.
    • Reviews and monitors field activities through discussion with subordinate supervisors and review of field reports; periodically conducts site visits to spot-check supervisor and/or crew activities; monitors compliance with department field safety procedures; provides dye testing support and expertise to other departmental units.
    • Reviews office and clerical activities through direct supervision of the administrative staff; monitors staff communications with customers; reviews updates of the Defective Lateral Computer System database; recommends software enhancements and improvements.
    • Develops program service level goals; tracks progress on monthly and annual bases; adjusts goals as required; prepares reports summarizing work performed in detecting and abating defective laterals; notes progress; estimates pollution removed as result of abatement program.

    ALL SPECIALTIES

  • Meets with officials of the Water Department, other City departments, representatives of commercial and industrial organizations, and community representatives to recommend policy, resolve and adjust problems and facilitate activities of each respective unit.
  • Analyzes the operations of the unit and prepares reports and recommends solutions to problems as they effect unit operations; determines best method to efficiently utilize available resources; develops program policies and procedures; confers with administrative superior in preparation of budget; directs the requisition of materials and supplies needed for unit operations.
  • Prepares and reviews reports on customer relations activities; analyzes daily operations of the unit and recommends solutions to problems; maintains a data base on a personal computer.
  • Interviews and selects subordinate personnel; develops criteria for on-the-job training of new employees; recommends employee attendance at departmental training courses.
  • Performs related work as required.

Required Knowledge, Skills And Abilities

Knowledge Of:

  • codes, regulations and ordinances pertaining to water and sewer services for the Field Services specialty
  • techniques, procedures, and regulations used in water billing and repair for the Field Services specialty
  • methods, materials, tools, and practices used to identify defective laterals for the Cross-Connection specialty
  • rules and regulations regarding water shutoffs and customer notifications
  • equipment and procedures used to perform dye tests to determine the condition of sewer connections for the Cross-Connection specialty
  • supervisory methods and techniques
  • the techniques and procedures utilized to answer questions pertinent to the installation and repair of industrial, commercial and to the installation and repair of industrial, commercial and residential water services connections
  • the organization and operation of the City government as it affects water operations
  • practices and techniques of resolving customer services problems
  • approval procedures for the Home Emergency Loan Program
  • departmental regulations as they pertain to water repair and loan programs
  • applicability of plumbing permits, codes, and regulations
  • building code as they relate to water repair projects

Ability To:

  • supervise a diverse staff of office, shop, and/or field employees engaged in work in the area of specialization
  • perform analysis of information that includes fact finding, organization of material, evaluation of data and preparation of recommendations either orally or in writing
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, administrative officials and the public

Minimum Acceptable Training And Experience

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 2/11.)

ALL SPECIALTIES

Education

  • Completion of the twelfth school grade. AND

General Experience

  • Two years of experience performing utility customer support work, receiving and interpreting requests or complaints related to water and sewer service or semi-skilled work in the disassembly, repair and installation of large and complex water meters. AND

Specific Experience

    Field Services Specialty

  • Two years of experience supervising employees engaged in the inspection and investigation and settling of customer complaints concerning water and/or sewer service OR
  • Two years of water meter field or shop work at the crew chief level supervising the work of a large number of water meter repair and semi-skilled workers engaged in the installation or repair, storage, assembly, disassembly and testing of industrial and residential water meters. One year must include supervising plumbing repairs done for water repair loan programs for city residents.

    Cross-Connection Specialty

  • Two years of experience supervising employees engaged in the investigation and identification of defective lateral connections.

OR

  • Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Office of Human Resources which has included the specific experience described above.
Physical And Medical Requirements
  1. Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
Licenses, Registrations And/Or Certificates
  1. Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of appointment as a Water Customer Services Manager.
