AIRPORT COMPUTING SYSTEMS MANAGER


GENERAL DEFINITION

This is computing systems management work directing a mixed vendor, multi-network computing and telecommunications program for the City's airports. The employee in this class directs the activities of a group of telecommunications and computing specialists engaged in maintaining and providing telecommunications, LAN and systems and applications programming services in support of the airports' security access, billing, engineering, emergency services and message paging computing programs. Work includes planning, developing and implementing operations policies, procedures and guidelines and formulating systems enhancement strategies to maintain an efficient level of operation and services to the Airports and its tenants. Insuring systems integrity and security and maintaining all the requirements of an integrated computing network are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the general direction of the Director of Aviation and the Deputy Director of Administration.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The position must perform the work for the City's International Airport and the Northeast Philadelphia Airport.
  2. The position must be responsible for directing the Airport's entire computing program, which includes automated voice and data transmission, cable administration, a local area network, an automated airport security system and information systems programs.
  3. The position must supervise a group of telecommunication and computing specialists, each responsible for administering and maintaining one of the following: LAN, Stratus system and telecommunications system.
  4. The position must be responsible for computing services in a shared tenant service environment.


TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs the activities of a multi-network, mixed vendor computing program for the City's International Airport; supervises the work of a technical staff engaged in systems implementa- tion, application development, and telecommunication systems management; determines, with the assistance of appropriate subordinate staff, corrective actions and alternatives required to resolve systems and programming problems and satisfy user requirements; insures that users of a new system are properly trained in system access procedures; discusses with subordinates methods to facilitate work flow and maximize workforce and equipment utilization; reviews periodic written reports and records pertaining to work progress and equipment utilization prepared by subordinate staff; provides technical advice and assistance.
  • Plans strategies to reduce cost and increase systems efficiency; establishes and directs the implementation of program objectives, policies and procedures in support of telecommunications and computer application development, the needs of users and overall information management operations.
  • Confers with unit managers to discuss the nature of new data processing or telecommuni- cations requests; directs the development and implementation of proposed operational, data and voice systems, security, financial, engineering and information computing systems and the modification to existing systems; determines, size, scope, and feasibility of requests; makes assignments, sets priorities, and plans schedules for project completion; for large or major programs, analyzes need for contracted consultants for selected systems development projects; selects vendors or contractors as needed; administers or directs the administration of contracts for provision of computing services and hardware maintenance; reviews completed designs and computer programs for conformance to program objectives.
  • Directs systems hardware/software configuration; analyzes and evaluates equipment performance and efficiency for both Stratus and IBM computer systems; projects need for acquisition of new hardware and software; selects, procures, and directs the installation of new equipment; creates and monitors the program budget for systems development hardware/software acquisition.
  • Reviews and evaluates hardware performance through review of reports and on-line monitoring tools; determines when and where equipment and systems utilization peaks occur and observes trends; analyzes findings to prepare projection reports on hardware/software requirements for the following fiscal year; maintains effective working relationship with the City's Central Computing Agency.
  • Establishes security standards and methods for departmental computing systems and assigns security monitoring responsibilities to subordinates; directs system auditing procedures to evaluate existing computing services; reviews new hardware and software packages to keep abreast of new developments with possible applicability to the Airport computing systems; directs standards for development and the provision of end user and staff training and systems documentation, either through in-house or contracted staff; develops marketing plans for selling telecommunications computing services to airport tenants; develops billing and support provisions for customers.
  • Analyzes performance and requirements of existing telecommunications facilities to determine need for enhanced services; develops procedures for meeting user/tenant requirements; analyzes requirements for cabling plant utilization and determines when expansion is needed; oversees engineering of voice and data circuits within the cabling system; verifies that all cabling administration and maintenance meets the requirements of AT&T PDS certification.
  • Oversees the development of marketing plans for the DOA telephone system in line with division standards and requirements; directs the implementation of integration requirements of airport telecommunications and computer systems into airport PDS cabling system.
  • Performs related work as required.


REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices and procedures used in the development and implementation of management information systems.
  • the principles, procedures and methods used in applications programming, system analysis, and development and design for information management and telecommunications systems.
  • modern administrative practices, methods and techniques with emphasis on the use of automated data processing telecommunications systems.
  • the principles, practices and techniques used in the installation, maintenance and configuration of data and voice transmission and local area networks.
  • current technologies, uses, capabilities and configuration in the field of personal computers, mini-computers, and local area and voice transmission networks and their compatible software.
  • the current developments in the field of information management, telecommunications, and end user network technology.
  • two or more operating systems, including the VOS system used in STRATUS computing equipment.
  • one or more fourth generation computer programming languages.
  • STRATUS computing equipment or the equivalent of a fault tolerant system.
  • principles and techniques of database management.
  • on-line computer information systems and remote retrieval systems.
  • applying information systems applications to common business requirements.
  • telephone switching equipment provided by independent interconnect companies.
  • telephone service requirements commonly used in the operational activities of a modern airport facility.
  • established procedures of the telephone utility company and independent vendors providing telephone service.
  • current costs for various telephone services and equipment provided by public utility and vendors as contained in tariffs.
  • environmental and engineering considerations for voice/data installations.
  • cabling systems utilized to carry voice/data signals, specifically premise distribution systems (PDS).
  • principles, practices and techniques used in the development of training programs for the user network.
  • the principles, practices and techniques used in the development and maintenance of computer security systems.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.

ABILITY TO:
  • plan, organize, and direct the activities of a staff of computing specialists engaged in the administration and maintenance of computerized information and telecommunications systems.
  • develop, implement, and enhance computerized information systems, methods and procedures.
  • determine feasibility and application of automated management information systems for the City's International Airport.
  • evaluate hardware and software efficiency and system utilization and configuration and project future program requirements.
  • recommend the most efficient hardware and software products that meet the needs of the department's computing programs.
  • plan, develop and implement training programs for user network and subordinate computing staff.
  • analyze and resolve complex computing and system development problems.
  • advise management with respect to proposed and existing system development projects.
  • direct, monitor and coordinate the work of outside contractors and consultants.
  • develop, implement and maintain a computer security system.
  • present ideas effectively and clearly.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, departmental officials, governmental agencies, outside consultants and contractors, and the Central Computing Agency.


MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/92.)

EDUCATION:

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program in computer science, decision science, information science, business administration or management or closely related field.

AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

  • Four years of experience performing information systems analysis, programming or systems programming activities in a computing environment supporting both real time and interactive computer applications for a multi-user network.

AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

  • Three years of management experience at the Assistant Director level or above directing a technical staff engaged in the design, implementation, administration and maintenance of information management systems in a multi-user operations environment which serves a large organization with a large variety of user application requirements.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience which has included completion of a bachelor's degree program, including or supplemented by twenty-one (21) semester hours of course work in computer science, decision science or information science determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department and which includes the specific experience as indicated above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
