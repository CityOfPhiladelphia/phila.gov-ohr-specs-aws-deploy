NURSING HOME SUPPORT SERVICES ADMINISTRATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative work directing various support services functions, through subordinate supervisors, for a large skilled nursing home facility. An employee in this class ensures the adequate provision of several support services which include personnel administration, security, communications, environmental services and transportation. Work includes performing a variety of assignments pertaining to the monitoring and analysis of nursing home operational efficiency and compliance with federal and state mandated regulations concerning health care services. Coordinating facility support services with medical and patient care functions is a significant aspect of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The position must be located in a large skilled nursing home facility.
  2. The position must be responsible for directing personnel administration, security, communications, environmental services and transportation support services in a nursing facility.
  3. The position must report directly to the Executive Director - Philadelphia Nursing Home.
  4. Only one position may be allocated to this class.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, organizes and directs, through subordinate supervisors, the personnel administration, security, communications, environmental services and transportation services for a skilled nursing home facility.
  • Monitors the operational efficiency of assigned support services functions; develops and implements systems and procedures designed to increase the efficiency level of assigned support services functions; meets and confers with subordinate supervisors to discuss problems and discrepancies in the work performed and to determine the proper course of action to alleviate such problems; establishes work priorities and communicates them to the subordinate staff; assigns work and special projects to subordinates as deemed necessary; ensures that support services comply with federal state mandated regulations; directs the maintenance of records required of assigned support services functions.
  • Directs labor relations activities for the nursing home; receives and reviews employee grievances submitted by bargaining units; initiates investigations into such problems; confers with concerned employees and supervisors; evaluates and determines the most appropriate courses of action to alleviate labor relations problems; schedules and attends meetings with union officials to discuss resolutions to labor relations problems.
  • Reviews all disciplinary recommendations forwarded by nursing home facility supervisors; conducts investigations on disciplinary actions to ascertain the nature of the problem; determines if recommended actions are warranted or whether there are more appropriate disciplinary measures.
  • Ensures that adequate security and communication services are provided at the facility; makes certain that custodial and vector control activities comply with federal and state health regulatory requirements; supervises the distribution of facility mail and the provision of duplicating services; confers with Public Property personnel and telephone service agencies on placement of telephone lines and the maintenance and repair of communication systems and equipment; directs the distribution of keys to facility employees making certain that only appropriate employees have access to security and restricted areas.
  • Performs special research projects as assigned by the Executive Director; conducts investigations and evaluations on administrative problems on such matters as organization, staffing and cost comparisons, and recommends measures to address those problems; analyzes new federal and state mandated regulations and interprets their resulting impact on facility operations; meets and confers with officials from regulatory agencies to keep abreast of changing regulations and certification requirements.
  • Coordinates and schedules state mandated training and education programs; ensures that all newly hired nursing home employees undergo orientation training and education sessions; maintains records documenting such sessions were held.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices and techniques of personnel administration.
  • in-service training principles, methods and techniques.
  • current trends, development and theories in personnel management.
  • the practices, methods and techniques involved in protecting and safeguarding facility property.
  • security rules, regulations and procedures.
  • the principles and practices of methods and procedures analysis.
  • the operation of a large skilled nursing home.
  • scheduling, work standards, equipment, materials, methods and practices used in large scale housekeeping operations.
  • the practices and procedures utilized in managing the communications and transportation services of a large skilled nursing home facility.
  • the theories, practices and application of the techniques of nursing home organization and administration.
  • laws and regulations governing nursing homes.
  • legal and administrative functions as they pertain to a skilled nursing home.
  • civil service regulations and administrative policies affecting employee relations.
  • legal, regulatory and contractual requirements affecting labor management relations.
ABILITY TO:

  • plan, organize and direct the activities of administrative and support services in a large nursing home.
  • evaluate the operational efficiency of support services functions.
  • develop recommendations to correct administrative and operational problems in a large nursing home facility.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates and the public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/91.)

EDUCATION:

  1. Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.

    AND
    GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

  2. Three years of administrative experience.

    AND
    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

  3. Two years of experience directing an administrative function of a large nursing home.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Eligibility a license as a Professional Nursing Home Administrator granted by the Pennsylvania State Board of Examiners of Nursing Home Administrators at the time of appointment and during tenure as a Nursing Home Support Services Administrator.
