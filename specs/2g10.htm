CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

2G10
HISTORIC PRESERVATION OFFICER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative work directing a program of historical preservation designed to identify areas and structures of historic value in the city and to acquire and maintain current, detailed information regarding their original state and subsequent modifications. The employee in this class participates in a variety of research projects and publicizes the results of such research in journals, newspapers and other media in order to develop the climate of opinion sympathetic to the preservation of historic sites. Work entails maintaining liaison for the City and private parties with federal and state agencies for compliance with federal historic preservation requirements and regulations and for utilization of federal tax incentives for historic preservation, and includes representing the city at meetings of professional organizations, historical societies and community groups. Work is performed with guidance with administrative superiors.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Reviews project plans and permit applications concerning projected rehabilitation or development within the city; assigns to others or performs himself on-site inspection and subsequent research of areas and buildings of potential historical importance; performs the more detailed and difficult research; prepares data for environmental impact statements, nominations and eligibility determinations for the National Register, or other special reasons.
  • Advises municipal agencies, developers and property owners on use of federal tax incentives for restoration and rehabilitation of historic properties on the preparation of grants applications and other required documents, and on easements donations and related matters.
  • Participates on a variety of boards and committees to establish and implement goals and strategies; advises board members on governmental aspects of historic preservations and related matters.
  • Confers with historical commission members in both formal meetings and informal conferences; recommends certification of houses and other buildings as worthy of preservation and restoration.
  • Maintains liaison with redevelopment and planning authorities regarding the preservation of historical values in areas considered for development.
  • Finds means to prevent or minimize the destruction or alteration of valuable historic sites; publicizes impending demolitions and attempts to arouse public and private support for alternative solutions that maintain the historic and architectural authenticity of certifiable sites.
  • Supplies, orally and in writing, details on the architecture and history of certified sites as requested by private citizens, students, historical associations, and governmental agencies.
  • Writes and delivers speeches to a variety of professional and community organizations describing the aims and progress of current programs.
  • Acts as official representative at meetings of historical societies and professional organizations.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

    KNOWLEDGE OF:

    • U.S. history with emphasis on Philadelphia
    • methods and techniques used in historical research, and of the sources for research in the history of Philadelphia
    • federal, state and local regulations and guidelines relative to historical and architectural determination and environmental impact, tax incentives and easements donations
    • characteristic architectural and ornamental features of American private and public buildings of the Colonial period to the present

    ABILITY TO:

    • do advanced, detailed historical research and to correlate and evaluate source materials
    • evaluate and criticize the findings of subordinate researchers
    • accurately identify an structure as belonging to a definite period or phase of U.S. history on the basis of its architectural and ornamental features
    • establish and maintain effective working relationships with other governmental agencies, associates and the general public
    • express ideas clearly and concisely, orally and in writing

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/05.)

    EDUCATION

    • Completion of a doctoral program at an accredited college or university, with major course work in U.S. history, American studies, architectural history, urban studies.

    AND
    EXPERIENCE

    • Two years of research experience in the history of Philadelphia.

    OR

    Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
