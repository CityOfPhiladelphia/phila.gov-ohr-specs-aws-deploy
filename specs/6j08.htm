CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

6J08
AIRPORT COMMUNICATIONS CENTER SUPERVISOR II

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is second-level supervisory work directing all operational activities of an airport communications center of the City's large-hub international airport. The employee in this class directs, through subordinate supervisors, the provision of communication and information services to airport personnel and the public. Work includes developing efficient scheduling of operator coverage for all shifts, ensuring communications equipment is serviced and repaired as necessary and serving on an on-call basis for potential emergencies. Contact with airport operations personnel, contractors, and the general public and monitoring the activities of the communications center to ensure practices and procedures followed in the center are in accordance with FAA and departmental rules and regulations are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the general direction of an administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • only one position will be allocated to this class.
  • the position must be responsible for directing the operational activities of the City's airport communication center.
  • the position must supervise operations through subordinate Airport Communications Center Supervisor I's.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs the operational activities of a centralized airport communications center; supervises, through subordinate supervisors, the communication center operations and ensures process and procedures are in accordance with FAA and departmental rules and regulations; coordinates emergency communications procedures with Airport police, fire, and operations personnel.
  • Reviews subordinates' work in progress; points out problems and discrepancies and instructs on corrective measures; approves leave and overtime requests; initiates disciplinary action as needed; ensures adequate operator coverage for all shifts; ensures the efficient preparation of weekly work schedules for subordinate staff; plans and arranges for city coverage and overtime.
  • Ensures the appropriate training of new communications employees; arranges for classroom instruction of communication processes and procedures; assigns trainees to work with experienced operators for a period of time, for on-the-job training; issues independent assignments to new employees when they are adequately trained.
  • Ensures the ongoing and efficient operation of the Center's communication equipment; identifies and evaluates any equipment problems; ascertains whether the equipment should be serviced by contracted workers or on-staff trades persons; notifies appropriate personnel for the maintenance and repair of malfunctioned equipment.
  • Works with superiors in ascertaining possible improvements to the Communications Center operations and processes; may recommend additional acquisition of equipment; discusses, with superior, the appropriate annual budgetary allocation for the unit; makes recommendations on specific budgetary items.
  • Prepares productivity reports on center operations; tallies operators' activities, including calls received, calls lost, number of pages issued, etc.; submits copies of productivity reports to the appropriate parties.
  • Stays on-call 24 hours a day, seven days a week; responds to emergency situations as needed; ensures emergency procedures are followed during a crises.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • FAA and Division of Aviation rules and regulations pertaining to airport communications.
  • practices and procedures involved in airport communications.
  • a variety of communications equipment/parts and their uses.
  • computerized communications systems and their uses.
  • voice, video, and data transmission systems
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
ABILITY TO:

  • direct and coordinate the activities of a large communications center and staff.
  • evaluate the quality and effectiveness of airport communications center activities, in relation to airport operational needs.
  • develop procedures and policies to enhance the efficient operation of the communications center.
  • supervises and coordinate the handling of emergency procedures under adverse conditions.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with subordinates, tenants at the airports, vendors, and airport operations personnel.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/95.)

EDUCATION:

  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE:
  • Four years of airport communications or information services experience, three years of which has included operating telecommunications equipment and providing a variety of communications service at a large or medium hub international airport facility.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:
  • Two years of experience supervising subordinate personnel in the operation of telecommunications equipment and providing a variety of communications services at a large or medium hub international airport facility.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included the specific experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
