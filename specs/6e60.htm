CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

6E60
UTILITY SPECIAL INVESTIGATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is specialized investigatory work conducting investigations into allegations of fraud, corruption, misconduct and other illegal or prohibited activities committed by employees, contractors and users of Water Department services and facilities. Securing witnesses and gathering and verifying evidence are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of the Security Services Manager.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The employees must perform specialized investigatory work, conducting investigations into allegations of fraud, corruption, misconduct or other illegal activities committed by employees, contractors and users of Water Department services and facilities.
  2. The positions must be located in the Security Services Division, Water Department.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Conducts investigations concerning allegations of such illegal activities as: employee violation of department regulations; employee theft; violations of contract provisions by contractors; contractor fraud or theft; customer fraud such as bypassing meters or conspiring with City employees to circumvent billing for services or service shut-off; illegal dumping of waste materials; and vandalism of department property.
  • May investigate alleged violations of regulations which govern the receipt of injured on duty benefits, residency requirements, sick leave usage and related matters.
  • Contacts physicians, former employers, company representatives, government agencies and other relevant parties to elicit information pertinent to investigations; solicits the cooperation of other City investigative agencies.
  • Review evidence, employee records, accounts, contracts and other documents; reviews laws and legal decisions relevant to suspected illegal activities; verifies accuracy of evidence; develops department position on cases; recommends whether department should pursue prosecution; develops strategies for use in court cases; interviews potential witnesses; prepares reports on incidents.
  • Confers with representatives of law enforcement agencies to review prior criminal activities of individuals under investigation; confers with lawyers to determine if activities have violated the law.
  • Conducts surveillance of individuals suspected of illegal activity; places video and electronic surveillance equipment in the appropriate location; monitors activities using video and electronic equipment.
  • Presents testimony in court cases.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods and techniques used in interviewing.
  • the methods and techniques used in conducting investigations.
  • research methods used in determining violation of laws.
  • basic accounting principles and practices.
  • basic statistics.

SKILL IN:

  • obtaining information by interview, interrogations, observation, and investigation.
  • communicating effectively both orally and writing.
ABILITY TO:

  • determine if laws and regulations, relevant to conducting business with a municipality, have been violated.
  • obtain and record detailed information.
  • develop detailed and accurate descriptions of alleged illegal activity.
  • learn the operation of video and electronic surveillance equipment.
  • work effectively with law enforcement officials, lawyers and co-workers.
  • handle situations firmly, courteously, tactfully and with respect for the rights of others.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/95.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of the twelfth school grade.
AND SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

  • Two years of security or investigatory experience which has included investigating and preparing reports on accidents, burglaries and other incidents and performing surveillance to detect illicit activities within municipal installations, or investigating and gathering evidence on accidents in response to claims against the City and testifying in court as a witness.
PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of appointment as a Utility Special Investigator.
