CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

9G13
MURAL ARTS ASSISTANT DIRECTOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative public art work assisting with the direction of the Mural Arts Program for the Recreation Department. The employee in this class is responsible for the design and creation of murals throughout the city and for overseeing a City-wide art education program. Establishing and maintaining effective working relationships with departmental personnel, community groups and art institutions, and coordinating available private and public resources is important in the work. The employee in this class reports to the Mural Arts Administrator.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

  • The position in this class must be responsible for assisting with the direction of the Mural Arts Program for the Recreation Department.
  • Only one position will be allocated to this class.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Assists with the coordination of the design and creation of murals city-wide; works with neighborhood organizations to develop theme, content, or informational purpose for murals; recommends location for murals; determines timeline for completion; designs murals; prepares preliminary sketches of proposed murals; participates in the painting of murals; prepares walls for painting; orders and may erect scaffolding for projects; directs artists, laborers, and seasonal employees in the design and creation of murals; coordinates scheduling of painting crews; ensures crews are at designated sites; reviews mural work in progress and suggests changes if necessary; ensures that mural projects meet deadlines.
  • Plans and coordinates the art education program; designs curriculum for the after-school and summer art program; directs part-time art instructors; observes and evaluates instructors; conducts mural arts workshops for youths.
  • Maintains inventory of supplies and equipment.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

    KNOWLEDGE OF:

    • the principles, methods, tools and techniques of mural painting.
    • the methods of erecting and using scaffolding, ladders and other positioning devices.
    • the principles of art education.
    • the computer hardware and software relating to work assignment.
    • the methods and techniques of organizing and implementing programs in the area of mural arts and art education.
    • the equipment and supplies required to conduct programs in the area of mural arts and art education.

    ABILITY TO:

    • develop and maintain effective working relationships with recreation staff, community groups and volunteers, representatives of private agencies, and the general public.
    • lead the work of a small group of skilled painters and laborers.
    • utilize the computer hardware and software relating to work assignment.
    • erect and work from scaffolding, ladders, and other positioning devices.
    • learn to estimate time, costs, labor and materials.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 4/1999.)

    EDUCATION

    • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in fine arts, art administration, or art education.

    AND

    GENERAL EXPERIENCE

    • Two years of experience performing public art work, which includes conducting classes and/or instructing individuals on the principles and techniques of art.

    AND

    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

    • Completion of a minimum of ten murals, commissioned either publicly or privately, which includes a minimum of five outside murals.
  • Or any equivalent combination of education and experience which has been determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to and during tenure of employment in this class, if required by work assignment.
