PARK PLANNING AND DEVELOPMENT ADMINISTRATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is managerial planning and development work to improve park operations, to maximize efficient use of park resources and to develop public support and other funding. The employee in this class also directs an operating information systems bureau with significant data base maintenance and application development programs; plans and assigns projects for research, analysis and program development, and manages contracts for feasibility planning and research services. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Analyzes operating methods to determine problem areas; designs studies and projects to collect data for analysis of users' needs; makes recommendations to improve park operations; develops strategies and methods to correct problem areas; recommends means to maximize the efficient use of park resources.
  • Prepares applications for federal and state grants; meets with federal, state and city officials to discuss types of proposals available; reviews grant proposals and recommends departmental changes to meet funding requirements; contacts governmental officials to insure approval of applications.
  • Directs the parks' management information system; supervises a staff performing research, analysis and program development; oversees a staff directing consultants engaged in programming, data collection and system design.
  • Plans and assigns projects; monitors projects by reviewing status reports; reviews information collected before adding to the data base.
  • Attends seminars and visits other municipalities to obtain information to update park programs.
  • Directs staff engaged in organizing community or special interest groups to raise money or provide volunteer services to support projects.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • management principles and practices as they relate to the identification of means to improve organization, productivity and efficiency.
  • the principles and practices of public administration especially as they relate to grants development, public funding, and private financing.
  • long range planning methods.
  • the applications of EDP management information systems.
  • the practices of data base management.
  • public relations principles, practices, and procedures.
ABILITY TO:

  • learn and apply urban park management practices and procedures.
  • apply recognized analytical techniques to organizational and operational problems.
  • identify park management problems and to specify research parameters in order to develop recommendations based upon fact to resolve them.
  • supervise professional and clerical employees engaged in park planning, systems development, and data base management.
  • express ideas effectively both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, civic, community and business groups and city officials.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 11/84.)

  • Completion of a Master's Degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in administration, organizational management, computer sciences, city planning or any similar field.
  • Five years of administrative and/or planning experience, at least two years of which have been supervising a program in a recognized administrative discipline within a park agency.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department and which has included completion of a bachelor's degree from an accredited college or university as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
