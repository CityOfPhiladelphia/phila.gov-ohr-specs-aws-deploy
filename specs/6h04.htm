CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

6H04
ZONING EXAMINER SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory code compliance work overseeing the examination of applications for zoning and use permits for conformity with established zoning standards. The employee in this class helps formulate and develop procedures necessary to implement administrative policies, supervises lower level examiners, and makes determinations, evaluations and interpretations relative to the more complex zoning and use applications, inquiries and/or requests. Contact with the public, professional, community and City agencies is an important aspect of this position. Work is performed under the general supervision of a Zoning Administrator.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. Employees in this class must perform code compliance work at the supervisory level.
  2. This position reports to a Zoning Administrator.
  3. The positions in this class must be located in the Department of Licenses and Inspection.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Recommends procedures to best implement current policies to Unit Supervisor, aids in developing and implementing operational programs and procedures; recommends staffing realignment to adhere to departmental administrative standards, programs and directives to ensure that goals and objectives are being met.
  • Conducts on-going reviews with staff members which entail monitoring of applications being processed for permits and/or refusals.
  • Plans, assigns and reviews the work of subordinate zoning examiners; provides technical advice to lower level examiners in gathering information and reviewing the applications.
  • Reviews preliminary applications for zoning permits for the construction of high-rise and multi-structure complexes, and for the planning of institutional development districts.
  • Confers with the general public, City agencies, the professional community and other departmental personnel on unusual or difficult problems and explains and interprets zoning ordinances and policies; advises applicants on how to appeal disapproved applications to the zoning board of adjustment.
  • Applies regulations enacted to protect the aesthetic and environmental posture of the City.
  • Coordinates the zoning aspect of certification requests with the Certification Unit to assure a reasonable time response and to review difficult and unusual requests for clarification and proper evaluation.
  • Coordinates zoning remapping programs to ensure proper classification review.
  • Trains all new zoning examiners in the use of the zoning code; provides training to all staff on zoning code changes, and provides training to lower level Zoning Examiners; provides technical advice in interpretation of the zoning and use codes.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the codes and regulations required by all public jurisdictions to govern land use within zoning requirements.
  • mathematics including algebra and plane geometry.
  • the building, fire and housing codes and related ordinances and processes as they relate to the examination of applications for zoning and use permits.
  • interviewing techniques.
  • departmental functions and organizations as they relate to the application and enforcement
  • of the various codes.
  • architectural features and building construction.

SKILL IN:

  • reading and interpreting blueprints and plot plans.
  • eliciting information quickly and easily.
  • providing information clearly and concisely.
ABILITY TO:

  • implement departmental policies and procedures.
  • supervise and review the work of other zoning examiners.
  • make judgmental decisions where several courses of action are presented.
  • meet the general public and to explain, interpret, and enforce ordinances and regulations firmly and courteously.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 12/95.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of an associate's degree program with major coursework in construction technology, surveying or a similar discipline, at an approved college or university, or other technical institute.
AND GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Three years of experience in code compliance work.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

  • Two years of experience examining complex plans or other documents for compliance with the city's zoning and related codes and regulations governing land use.
Or any equivalent combination of training and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department, which has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
