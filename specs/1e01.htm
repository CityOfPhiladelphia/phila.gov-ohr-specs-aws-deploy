CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

1E01
PROGRAMMER TRAINEE


GENERAL DEFINITION

This is computer programming work at the trainee level, developing and applying knowledges and skills used in the preparation of program instructions for computing systems. As the training period progresses and instruction decreases, employees perform increasingly difficult and more responsible work approaching the full performance level. Work is performed under the close supervision of a technical superior.


TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Performs a variety of tasks required to learn to program data such as, preparation of block diagrams, flowcharts, gathering material and the preparation of reports; prepares programs of limited complexity.
  • Reads text books, manuals, instructions and regulations to develop knowledges, skills and abilities in the programming field.
  • Keeps abreast of current developments in the field of computing by attending training courses, professional meetings and visiting computing equipment plants.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

ABILITY TO:

  • reason quickly and accurately in situations involving abstract concepts.
  • reason quickly and accurately in situations involving numeric concepts.
  • think logically and sequentially.
  • learn the principles, practices, and techniques used in the computing field.
  • prepare reports and present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • understand and follow oral and written instructions.


MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/96.)

EDUCATION

  • Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade, supplemented by at least 18 semester hours of college level computer programming course work from an accredited college, university or technical school.

AND
WORK EXPERIENCE

  • Three years of full time experience within the last five (5) years operating a computer console and\or peripheral computing equipment; creating and maintaining computerized master files and records; or, maintaining a library of computer readable magnetic data storage media in such classes as:
  • Computer Console Operator
  • Data Services Support Clerk
  • Data Systems User Support Specialist
  • Magnetic Media Librarian
  • Computer Peripheral Equipment Operator.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included as an educational minimum completion of the twelfth school grade, supplemented by at least 18 semester hours of computer programming course work from and accredited college, university or technical school.

NOTE:
Successful completion of the required college level computer programming course work must have been within the last five years.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
