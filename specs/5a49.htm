CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

5A49
RESIDENTIAL CASEWORKER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is social service work interviewing, screening, placing and counseling clients to provide emergency shelter as part of the City's program for the homeless. Employees in this class receive clients directly and from a central intake unit of the homeless services agency. They screen candidates to determine their eligibility status for services; refer clients to appropriate human services agencies, place needy clients in emergency shelters, and provide ongoing counseling to clients in placement. Employees must be able to interact positively with clients in need of a wide variety of social and medical assistance. The employees in this class report to a social service supervisor.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Interviews clients to determine the benefits, if any, to which they are entitled; ascertains clients' housing needs and medical employment and educational problems; directs and assists clients with completion of intake forms.
  • Develops and prepares service plans outlining the nature of social service and referrals needed to meet and resolve clients' problems; identifies problems to be addressed; formulates a workable relocation plan; develops social, health, and economic goals and objectives.
  • Arranges for immediate placement of indigent, homeless clients, fire victims and individuals and families whose dwellings have been declared structurally unsound; decides which facility to use to place individuals and families; considers all relevant factors for placement such as school and work locations, personal backgrounds, age category, and personal appearance and attitude; works with clients to obtain alternative temporary shelter with friends or relatives; prepares necessary documentation when placing clients in private homes.
  • Provides housing counseling to clients; act as liaison between housing officials, landlords, and real estate brokers and clients when necessary; prepares applications for public housing; works out repayment plans for pre-existing debts; performs general counseling and referral duties utilizing various social and health service agencies; provides counseling on household management, budgeting, and parenting skills.
  • Arranges for transfer of clients from one assistance agency to another; arranges transportation and babysitting services for clients; helps clients secure furnishings and clothing; advises and assists clients in transferring children to schools in their new neighborhood; guides parents in monitoring school attendance; inspects new living areas with clients for acceptability.
  • Prepares payment forms to obtain checks from an emergency housing account when appropriate; prepares detailed activity reports for time spent on each case; prepares case folders on an on-going basis and documents the closing of cases when housing problems are resolved.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices, and techniques of family counseling, particularly as it relates to the relocation of low income and homeless individuals and families.
  • the socio-economic problems involved in relocation.
  • the functions and resources of public and private housing and social welfare agencies which can be employed in meeting social, financial, and medical problems.
  • casework principles, practices, methods and techniques.
  • the principles, practices, methods, and techniques of relocating homeless individuals and families.
ABILITY TO:

  • apply social service techniques to complex problems involved in relocating indigent and homeless clients.
  • observe, recognize, analyze, and report individual behavioral, attitudinal, emotional, and social problems and develop a plan for their resolution.
  • employ tact, diplomacy, and judgement in dealing with sensitive or personal problems.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with social service officials, representatives of community and housing agencies, and clients with a variety of social, medical, and emotional problems.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 9/88.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
EXPERIENCE

  • Four years of public contact work assisting individuals and families with housing and other social problems and providing information and referral service to them.
NOTE:

Additional experience of the kind specified may be substituted on a year for year basis for the required college education.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
