                        PUBLIC SAFETY TRAFFIC ENFORCEMENT SUPERVISOR

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

            This is supervisory non-sworn traffic enforcement work supervising and participating in the work activities of Traffic Enforcement Officers engaged in regulating vehicular, pedestrian, and bicycle traffic, supporting special events, and issuing code violations for traffic infractions.  Contact with the general public and communicating effectively with subordinates, other City agencies and City officials are significant aspects of the work.  Work requires standing for long periods of time and extended hand and arm movements and exposure to inclement weather.  The employee reports to an administrative superior.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

            Plans and assigns the work of subordinate staff; schedules and ensures adequate staffing; approves leave requests; prepares subordinates time sheets; arranges for duty coverage during emergencies and staff shortages.

            Supervises employees assigned to direct traffic at highly congested areas, collision scenes, and special events; patrols and responds to traffic related calls for service; investigates traffic collisions and interviews witnesses; enforces traffic and parking regulations and explains laws and ordinances to the public.

            Answers inquiries and gives direction to the public; investigates complaints; enforces quality of life crimes; enforces municipal code violations pertaining to traffic enforcement.

            Maintains records of all activities and actions taken; may testify in court as to facts related to citations issued and violations;        

            Performs related work as required.

 

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

 

KNOWLEDGE OF:

 

·         safety and security principles and practices.

·         City of Philadelphia motor vehicle regulations

·         geographic layout of the City of Philadelphia

·         supervisory methods and techniques

 

SKILL IN:

 

·         verbal communication

 

ABILITY TO:

 

·         supervise a group of field personnel engaged in directing traffic

·         establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, officials and the public

·         work with large crowds

·         communicate specific instructions clearly, firmly, and courteously

·         understand laws, statutes and regulations essential to law enforcement activities

·         stand for a prolonged period in all types of weather

 

 

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 720.)

 

EDUCATION:

 

            Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

 

AND

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

 

            Two years of experience as a Public Safety Traffic Enforcement Officer or equivalent, or as a School Crossing Guard.

 

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

 

            Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

 

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

 

            Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Common­wealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of appointment as a Public Safety Traffic Enforcement Supervisor.
