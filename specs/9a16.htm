LIBRARY COLLECTION CONSERVATION SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is library book conservation and supervisory work directing the conservation, preservation and restoration of library books and related printed matter, including works of art, documents and photographs for a large municipal library system. Work focuses primarily on rare and special collection materials, as well as image and contents of web pages. The employee in this class directs the activities of Library Conservation Technicians, and other employees engaged in library collection conservation work, and participates in and oversees the preparation, binding and finishing of library books and related materials. Work includes prioritizing assignments, maintaining appropriate records, and operating related equipment. Work is performed under the supervision of an administrative superior. Light physical effort is required in a laboratory environment with some disagreeable aspects.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

  • This is a one position class located in the Collection Care Unit of the Free Library of Philadelphia.
  • The employee must supervise Library Conservation Technicians and direct all conservation, preservation and restoration of library books and related materials of the Free Library.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Supervises the activities of a staff engaged in conservation, preservation, and restoration of library materials; operates binding, and other related specialized equipment; sets quality standards; schedules and assigns work; reviews completed work to ensure conformance to instructions and quality standards; evaluates performance of subordinate staff.
  • Determines the proper course of treatment for library and archival materials; performs conservation treatments on materials from both the general and special collections; determines basic repairs for circulating materials, or designates materials for commercial bookbinding; designs custom temporary or permanent enclosures; consults with commercial binder on acceptable treatments.
  • Operates binding and related equipment as required; presses books; mounts and places prints and posters in acetate; stamps, affixes bar codes, and pockets finished volumes; sorts and places books in appropriate bins for shipping.
  • Supervises and participates in special binding and repair operations such as those relating to rare and valuable books and manuscripts; instructs technicians in special binding and/or preservation techniques; trains new employees in all phases of conservation, preservation, restoration and bindery operations; prepares, conducts, narrates group tours; conducts book repair session for Library Branch agency staff.
  • Provides training and advice to Collection Care staff and department heads on collection care, handling and preservation of materials.
  • Participates in the determination of, and procedure for digital reformatting; editing of images and information and production of digital surrogates.
  • Measures and cuts window mats for fine art prints, documents; assists department heads in exhibition preparation.
  • Teaches and replicates historical text block and binding structures and reviews text blocks in a variety of styles.
  • Maintains production records; requisitions materials, supplies and equipment; prepares reports on production.
  • Performs minor repairs on equipment used in the collection care unit; contacts dealer representatives when major repairs are required.
  • Performs related work as required.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 8/04.)

    EDUCATION

    • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university, with a major course work in art, art history or a related field.

    AND
    GENERAL EXPERIENCE

    • Three years of experience in book conservation in a library or museum setting.

    OR

    Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the completion of a bachelor's degree as an educational minimum.

NOTE:

Completion of a master’s degree program in Art, Art History or a related field may be substituted for one year of the required general experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
