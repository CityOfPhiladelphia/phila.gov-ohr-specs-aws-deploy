CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

5H51
UNDERSHERIFF

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is criminal justice and administrative work, advising the Sheriff and his deputies on the legal process, its policies, procedures and ramifications, and the criminal and civil justice systems and how they relate to the Sheriff's office operations. An employee in this class directs and supervises, through uniformed officers and a clerical staff, the administration of the Sheriff's real and personal property sales which includes executing all deeds and presiding at all real estate sales. The employee is expected to render decisions on all property and exemption claims and administer direction in the processing of claims and petitions. Working with the courts and law enforcement agencies and interacting with judges and court officials are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the general direction of the Sheriff.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  1. The position must perform the work in the Sheriff's Office and report to the Sheriff.
  2. The position must have responsibility for advising the Sheriff and his deputies on legal matters relating to the Sheriff's office.
  3. The position must have responsibility for legal administration and execution of real and personal property Sheriff Sales.
  4. Only one position can be allocated to this class.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Renders technical advice to the Sheriff and Chief Deputy Sheriff on laws and procedures relating to Sheriff office operations; bases advice on the interpretation of the rules of Civil Procedure, the Pennsylvania Rules of Court and Criminal procedure, the Criminal Code for Pennsylvania, and the book of Pennsylvania Statutes; represents the Sheriff's office in departmental and disciplinary proceedings; acts as a liaison between Sheriff's Office and the Law Department and other agencies.
  • Presides at all Sheriff sales of real and personal property; supervises the sale of real estate by the Sheriff's office; ensures that all rules and regulations and laws are followed and ensures the auctioneer is conducting a fair auction; verifies bankruptcy claims during auction; reviews and signs the deed; anticipates problems at the time of sale; executes all deeds; processes and renders decisions on property and exemption claims; receives and processes claims and petitions for the City's court system.
  • Interacts with various other law enforcement agencies such as, the Police Department, the F.B.I. and other law enforcement jurisdictions in criminal cases of mutual interest; interacts with the Law and Revenue departments.
  • Participates with administrative superiors in determining plans and policies for the Sheriff's office; reviews and authorizes requisitions, budget requests and other financial documents; supervises the recording and processing of a variety of court documents;
  • Trains Deputy Sheriffs on laws concerning arrest, search and seizure, deadly force, legal issues regarding handling prisoners with AIDS and the search principles relative to street enforcement with non-criminals; trains civilian staff on the rules of civil procedure and standard operating procedures as it relates to processing claims.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the Criminal Justice system applicable to the City's Court system.
  • the principles, practices, methods, techniques, literature and current developments in the field of criminal justice.
  • the administrative principles and practices as they relate to operations within the criminal and civil justice systems.
  • the rules of civil procedure, the Pennsylvania rules of court and criminal procedures, the criminal code for Pennsylvania and the book of Pennsylvania statutes.
  • the laws and procedures as they relate to the Sheriff's enforcement personnel and the control and confinment of prisoners.
  • the forms and procedures applicable to serving legal process.
  • the accepted methods and principles of controlling and confining prisoners on a short term basis.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
  • office management practices and procedures.
  • budget preparation methods and techniques.
  • the legalities, methods and principles governing real estate transactions.
  • city geography.

ABILITY TO:

  • analyze a variety of complicated facts and to exercise sound judgement with established rules and regulations in arriving at conclusions.
  • prepare detailed written reports of findings conclusions and recommendations.
  • analyze a variety of data and prepare concise reports in written and graphic form.
  • analyze and evaluate results and determine the progress of programs in achieving particular goals.
  • interpret laws pertaining to the operations of the Sheriff's Office.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with judges and other court officials, attorneys, subordinates and the general public.
  • speak and write effectively.
  • train and develop employees in required procedures and techniques of assigned tasks.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/1992.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program in criminal justice, criminology or related degree from an accredited college or university.

AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Three years of experience in a law enforcement or criminal justice system environment with responsibility for the application and interpretation of technical and legal procedures.

AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

  • Three years of experience providing technical advice and interpretation of laws and procedures for an officer of the Court on court room security, transportation of prisoners to and from holding facilities, and civil and criminal court services within a municipal environment.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included the specific experience and a bachelor's degree as the educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
