CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

7F24
AUTOMOTIVE EQUIPMENT INSPECTOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is skilled automotive trades work inspecting city-owned automotive equipment for the purpose of determining the extent of preventive and corrective maintenance and repair requirements and the preparation of estimated repair costs.

Work is performed under the general supervision of a technical superior, and involves some disagreeable aspects.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Inspects city-owned vehicles to determine extent of corrective maintenance or repair required; uses micrometers, calipers and feeler gauges to check mechanical components; uses compression and vacuum gauges, engine analyzers, dynamometers and oscilloscopes for a detailed analysis and determination of specific troubles.
  • Details all materials, supplies and repair work required by standard nomenclature on shop repair orders; uses flat rate manuals to determine standard hours of repair estimates based on similar operations previously performed; estimates cost of materials and supplies required to complete repair work.
  • Inspects repair work in process to determine if more extensive repairs are necessary or for unusual damage or deterioration making further repair work unsound.
  • May make inspections of newly-purchased city automotive equipment; makes estimates of accident damage; may testify in accident litigation cases.
  • Makes repairs and adjustments to automotive equipment.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the methods, materials, tools and practices of the automotive mechanic trade.
  • the techniques and equipment used in diagnosing mechanical difficulties in automotive equipment such as engine analyzers, dynamometers and oscilloscopes.
  • the principles of operation of internal combustion engines.
  • the occupational hazards and safety precautions of the automotive mechanic trade.

SKILL IN:

  • diagnosing and locating mechanical defects in automotive equipment, including diesel engines, heavy duty transmissions, vehicle pneumatic systems, high pressure air purification systems, hydraulic and mechanical systems such as aerial equipment on fire vehicles, and high voltage equipment.

ABILITY TO:

  • make repairs on a wide range of automotive equipment and related equipment systems.
  • make accurate inspections of city-owned equipment.
  • prepare accurate automotive maintenance, repair and vehicle damage estimates.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/1963.)
  • Education equivalent to completion of the tenth school grade.

  • Six years of experience in the automotive mechanic trade including two years at the journeyman level and which has included utilizing specialized testing equipment such as engine analyzers, dynamometers and oscilloscopes.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as an Automotive Equipment Inspector.
  • Certification as an Official Inspection Mechanic issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania within six (6) months of appointment and during tenure of employment as an Automotive Equipment Inspector.
