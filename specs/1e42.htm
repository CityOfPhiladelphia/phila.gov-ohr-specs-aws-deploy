1E42
DEPARTMENTAL COMPUTER INFORMATION SYSTEMS DIRECTOR


GENERAL DEFINITION

This is administrative information systems management work, directing through subordinate technical supervisors, systems analysis and applications development for a departmental management information system. An employee in this class is responsible for directing the development of new applications through an in-house mainframe system or through the mainframe of the City's Central Computing Agency (PCC). An employee in this class establishes programming and analysis policy, procedures and practices, ensures that departmental computing systems are on-line and operating efficiently. Work may include responsibility for systems and telecommunication programming activities and/or micro computing end user group programs. Ensuring system security and maintaining adequate levels of hardware, software tools, personnel and training to support subordinate staff and user requirements are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of an Administrative superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • The position must have responsibility for management of a departmental computing information system through subordinate computing supervisors.
  • The position must be responsible for mainframe applications development in-house or through the City's Central Computing Agency, (PCC).
  • Only one position can be allocated to a department.


TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Directs, through subordinate technical supervisors, systems analysis and applications development for a departmental information management system; plans, assigns and evaluates the work of programmer analysts and technical support staff engaged in the study, development and implementation of data systems; reviews completed designs and computer programs for conformance with the overall program objectives; confers with divisional managers and departmental officials to determine the nature of data system and hardware needs prior to staff study; determines size and scope of requests; makes assignments and establishes project objectives, sets priorities and time frames for completion of projects and assignments; schedules use of personnel and equipment; provides technical advice and assistance; insures that intended objectives of each project are met; determines, with the assistance of appropriate subordinate staff, corrective actions and alternatives required to resolve programming problems and/or satisfy user requirements; insures that users of a new system are properly trained in system access procedures.
  • Discusses with subordinate supervisors methods to facilitate work flow and maximize workforce and equipment utilization; reviews periodic written reports and records pertaining to work progress and equipment utilization prepared by subordinate staff.
  • Prepares requests for proposals, including project plans, schedules, and specifications; evaluates proposals submitted by vendors; interprets technical language in the contracts; obtains approval of all system hardware and software contracts and requisitions from the City's Central Computing Agency (PCC); confers with consultants to develop plans for system installations and maintenance and assure compliance with user needs; ensures that consultants conform to the department's hardware configuration and system requirements; maintains working relationship with software and hardware consultants and contractors; reviews and evaluates hardware performance through review of reports and on-line monitoring tools; may determine when and where equipment and systems utilization peaks occur and observes trends; may analyze findings to prepare projection reports on hardware/software requirements for the following fiscal year.
  • Confers with the department administrators of PC end-user groups to discuss problems, training requirements and modifications to existing automated systems; recommends automation of existing manual processes; reviews project requests for resource estimates, and cost/benefits analysis; prioritizes project request based on user needs of the department.
  • Plans, and directs the development, modification and implementation of proposed mainframe data systems to improve the department's functional processing systems to reduce costs and increase efficiency; directs ongoing review and modification to existing manual systems and automated processes; plans, develops and implements systems analysis policies, objectives and procedures in support of center activities and/or user requirements; directs the maintenance of database efficiency; processes applications and programs through the City's Central Computing Agency or through the department's in-house computing system.
  • Directs the testing, monitoring and modification of installed LAN or PC operating systems; directs troubleshooting and diagnosis of systems malfunctions and provides problem resolution; plans and directs the development, modification and implementation of proposed systems improve system design, reduce cost and increase efficiency.
  • Directs the acquisition of PC hardware and software; provides technical oversight for a local area network; plans, develops and implements system analysis policies, objectives and procedures in support of center activities and/or user requirements; directs the maintenance of LAN or PC databases, telecommunications and/or operating systems efficiency.
  • Establishes security standards and methods for departmental computing systems and assigns security monitoring responsibilities to subordinates; directs system auditing procedures to evaluate existing computing services; reviews new hardware and software packages to keep abreast of new developments with possible applicability of a department computing system.
  • Performs related work as required.

Duties Performed in the Bureau of Revision of Taxes

  • Plans and directs, activities in a departmental data processing and analysis operation which utilizes electronic computers and related equipment to process tax assessment records; directs units engaged in developing automated mass appraisal models, conducting assessment ratio studies and research projects, screening, editing and processing sales data and income and expense data; develops and maintains the organization's mainframe computer systems which includes systems programming and telecommunications activities.

Duties Performed in the Health Department

  • Directs the computing activities related to health statistical reporting; supervises system development and information management support for disease control efforts; manages the compilation, analysis, maintenance and reporting of vital statistics, such as births, stillbirths, deaths, immunization and sexually transmitted diseases; provides technical assistance to contracted consultants providing the hardware and software used to process reports and maintain mandated statistics.

Duties Performed in the Police Department

  • Directs, through subordinate supervisors mainframe, telecommunications and systems programming operations for the Police Department's information management center; plans, develops and directs the operation and modification of an on-line police information retrieval system and the emergency call telephone system.

Duties Performed in the Revenue Department

  • Directs, through technical staff, the development and implementation of new and existing data management systems for tax billing, dunning, refunding, non-filer enforcement, and payment processing; develops computing programs and applications in-house through the mainframe of the Philadelphia Computing Center (PCC); determines systems development requirements for tax administration and accounts receivables programs; confers with revenue agency management operating personnel to conduct in depth studies of current and automated tax billing procedures.


REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices and procedures used in the development and implementation of management information systems.
  • the principles, procedures and methods used in applications development, system analysis and design and their implementation as applied to the provision of all departmental computer information management services to a network of users.
  • modern administrative practices methods and techniques with emphasis on the use of automated data processing systems.
  • the principles, practices and techniques used in the installation, maintenance and configuration of local area networks if required by work assignment.
  • the principles, practices and techniques used in system programming, if required by work assignment.
  • systems programming analyses and design as applied to a multi-system computer and telecommunication environment that includes multiple central processing units, if required by work assignment.
  • current technologies, uses, capabilities, application and configuration in the field of micro computers, mini- computers, and local area networks (LAN) and their compatible software if required by work assignment.
  • the current developments in the field of micro-computing end user network technology if required by work assignment.
  • the principles, practices and methods used in the application of computer technology to enhance departmental service delivery.
  • one or more computer operating systems.
  • one or more fourth generation computer programming languages as required by job assignment.
  • principles and techniques of database management.
  • on-line computer information systems and remote retrieval systems.
  • principles, practices and techniques used in the development of training programs for a user network.
  • the principles, practices and techniques used in the development and maintenance of computer security systems.
  • principles and procedures used in the development and implementation of a staff training and development plan.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.

ABILITY TO:

  • plan, organize, and direct, through subordinate computing supervisors, the activities of a technical staff engaged in the development, implementation and administration of computerized information systems.
  • develop, implement, and enhance computerized information systems, methods and procedures.
  • determine feasibility and application of automated management information systems.
  • evaluate hardware and software efficiency and system utilization and configuration and project future program requirements.
  • recommend the most efficient hardware and software products that meet the needs of the department's computing programs.
  • plan, develop and implement training programs for the user network and subordinate computing staff.
  • learn and understand the operating and administrative functions of the department concerned.
  • analyze and resolve complex computing and system development problems.
  • advise management with respect to proposed and existing system development projects.
  • present ideas effectively and clearly.
  • direct, monitor and coordinate the work of outside contractors and consultants.
  • develop, implement and maintain a computer security system.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, departmental officials, governmental agencies, outside consultants and contractors, and the Central Computing Agency.


MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/93.)

EDUCATION

  • Completion of bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university in the major course work in computer science, decision science, information science or a closely related field.

AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Four years of full performance level computing experience performing computer programming and analysis or systems programming work.

AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

  • Three years of experience supervising subordinate computer analysts or systems programming staff engaged in systems programming or analysis, applications development and/or end user support activities in the area of departmental specialization.

OR


Any equivalent combination of education and experience which has included completion of a bachelor's degree program, including or supplemented by twenty-one (21) semester hours of course work in computer science, decision science or information science determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department and which includes the specific experience as indicated above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

NOTE:
For the position in the Revenue Department, completion of an additional six semester hours in accounting from an accredited college or university will be required.
