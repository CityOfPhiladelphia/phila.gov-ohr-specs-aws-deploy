4C65
DISEASE CONTROL NURSING SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is second level supervisory and specialized nursing work planning, implementing, coordinating, and evaluating public health epidemiologic activities relating to a health risk or disease control program. The employee in this class develops, coordinates, monitors and evaluates the surveillance control and intervention aspects of the program. Supervising, through subordinate supervisors, the activities of a staff engaged in collecting data and tracking and counseling clients, and analyzing surveillance results to identify areas requiring intervention are significant aspects of the work. Work also includes providing educational services to medical and lay communities regarding increased incidents or outbreaks of diseases or health risks and strategies for their control. Work is performed under the direction of an administrative superior.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, develops and implements program surveillance systems; determines methodology for obtaining required data; designs surveillance reporting forms incorporating input from contracted epidemiological consultants; develops contract specifications for private data collection agency; monitors data collection activities performed by private contractors and departmental personnel; implements changes in data collection methods as required.
  • Performs epidemiologic analyses of surveillance data collected to identify frequency and severity of cases; determines areas requiring additional research; develops protocol and methodology required for further studies; develops research tools for in-depth studies; performs epidemiologic analyses of additional research results to identify areas requiring intervention; reviews medical records; interviews patients and medical personnel.
  • Directs, through subordinate supervisors, disease control personnel interviewing patients, locating infected or at risk persons and/or their contacts, and obtaining personal and sensitive data; assigns caseloads; evaluates collected data; trains subordinates in health risks or disease control field investigation and interviewing techniques and in the medical aspects of health risk and disease programs.
  • Plans, develops, and implements prevention projects based on priorities identified by analysis of data collected; enlists the participation of public and private agencies in the development and implementation of intervention activities; establishes roles of departmental and contracted personnel involved in prevention activities; trains and coordinates the activities of prevention project personnel; monitors project progress and evaluates results; recommends corrective action.
  • Performs program evaluation providing administrators with analysis of programmatic activities; recommends program modifications based on findings.
  • Develops education programs for selected cases and means to prevent and control them; makes presentations to community and professional groups; responds to inquiries from the general public on matters relating to prevention and control activities.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the theories, principles and practices of nursing and their application in community health and clinical service settings.
  • principles and practices of epidemiology and biostatistics as applied to diseases and conditions of public health importance.
  • current developments in health research as they relate to control of conditions that has significant impact on public health, including health risks and disease reduction.
  • laws and regulations governing nursing practice.
  • collect, analyze and summarize data in epidemiologic reports.
  • prepare reports and present findings and conclusions in a clear and comprehensive manner both orally and in writing.
  • plan, organize and coordinate the development and implementation of surveillance and intervention activities among various disciplines in a health risk prevention or disease control program.
  • train, supervise, and direct employees in health risks or disease surveillance, control, and intervention activities.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with physicians, nurses, other professional and administrative personnel, and community groups.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 11/85.)

EDUCATION:

  • Completion of a master's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in nursing, nursing education, or public health.

GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

  • One year of community health nursing experience.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included completion of a master's degree as an educational minimum.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Eligibility for a license as a registered nurse issued by the Pennsylvania State Board of Nurse Examiners at the time of application and possession of such licensure within the time limit specified by the department.
  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Disease Control Nursing Supervisor.
