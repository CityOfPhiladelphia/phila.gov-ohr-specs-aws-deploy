CUSTOMER CARE REPRESENTATIVE

(General)

(Bilingual)

 

GENERAL DEFINITION

 

          This is customer service work at the full performance level in the Philadelphia Water Department Customer Contact Center or for the Philadelphia International Airport’s Guest Experience unit to improve the experience of passengers throughout the seven terminals and Customs area. Employees in the Philadelphia Water Department Customer Contact Center apply established Water Department customer service proficiencies and provide information and assistance to the public related to water and sewer services. Employees are responsible for investigating and resolving complaints pertaining to utility billing, enrolling customers in payment agreements for residential and commercial accounts, responding to utility account issues and providing information with regard to legal proceedings related to delinquent accounts or accounts in collections. Employees in the Airport’s Guest Experience unit provide customer service to achieve guest expectations, resolve in-person customer inquiries, report facility issues to maintenance job control and provide on-going follow up with Airport staff and stakeholders until resolution.  This class differs from the lower level class in that employees perform work independently with minimal guidance. Employees have significant contact with the general public and other city departments and perform work under the supervision of a Customer Care Supervisor.        

 

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

 

1.    Employees in this class must perform customer service work at the full performance level in the Water Department Customer Contact Center or in the Airport’s Guest Experience unit.

 

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

 

BOTH ASSIGNMENTS:

 

Communicates with customers via telephone, mail, email, or other means of communication; refers unresolved or complex grievances to lead worker or supervisor for further investigation.

 Participates in training as required to develop knowledge of customer service and  department processes and procedures.

 

          Performs related work as required.

 

ASSIGNMENT-PWD CUSTOMER CONTACT CENTER

 

Corresponds with water customers via telephone and in writing; investigates and responds to complaints pertaining to utility billing procedures; reviews, analyzes and explains customer billing records and makes adjustments as needed; enrolls customers in payment programs; provides information regarding collection agencies; responds to Help-desk mail as required; responds to inquiries and provides information and services regarding commercial accounts; provides customers with reference materials as necessary or directs to online resources; provides information regarding water customer service facilities, payment options and water assistance programs, including grant status requests, applicant eligibility and discount programs.

Uses various computer systems to enter data of new utility customers and make data changes; obtains other information to assist customers; locates utility infrastructure in a GIS-based system; researches, resolves or refers to investigators reports of unauthorized utility usage and uses discretion to determine deposit and restoration of service requirements; provides information and identifies solutions to contact for water service requests.

Coordinates efforts with outside agencies for payment of utility accounts; assists customers who have had their service disconnected or who have received notice to interrupt service, including granting extensions of service.

Handles all calls relating to water/sewer emergencies; meter appointments; general water and sewer service requests; provides general information regarding the Water Department’s core services; handles all water related calls to improve the level of customer service and increase first call resolution.

Responds to customers’ calls regarding water/sewer emergencies and customer service requests; interviews customers via telephone to gain an accurate account of the nature of the service related request; creates the work orders that result in the necessary field response to abate emergencies and protect public health and safety.

Prepares change of address records; assists customers with change of record accountholder name requests and inquiries; makes revisions to account identifying information.

         

 

ASSIGNMENT-AIRPORT GUEST EXPERIENCE TEAM

 

Corresponds with airport guests in person, via telephone, and in writing to resolve complaints and inquiries and ensure a favorable travel experience; investigates and responds to complaints pertaining to Airport procedures; responds to inquiries and provides information and services regarding Airport facility issues; provides guests with reference materials as necessary or directs guests to online resources.

Utilizes computer systems to enter inquiry and complaint data; obtains other information to assist guests; provides information and identifies solutions pertaining to guest experience requests; provides on-going follow up with airport staff and stakeholders until resolution.

Responds to guests’ calls regarding inquiries, guest experience complaints, and requests; interviews guests via telephone to gain an accurate account of the nature of the related inquiry or request.

Communicates with customers via telephone, mail, email, or other means of communication; refers unresolved or complex customer grievances to a lead worker or supervisor for further investigation.

Assists in providing accurate processing information to guests arriving from international flights in customs as needed. Assists with the Airport’s special events and tours of airport facilities.

·       English usage, spelling, grammar, and punctuation

 

AIRPORT GUEST EXPERIENCE TEAM ASSIGNMENT

 

·       principles and procedures of the Airport Guest Experience team

·       computerized software applications and systems used in documenting and tracking customer requests

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE (The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests.  Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/24.)

 

BOTH SPECIALTIES:

 

EDUCATION:

 

          Education equivalent to completion of the twelfth school grade.

 

AND

EXPERIENCE:

 

OPTION 1:

 

          One year of experience as a Water Customer Care Trainee in the Water Department’s Customer Call Center arranging for payment agreements and responding to customer inquiries and service requests.

 

 OR

 

Option 2:

 

          One year of experience as a Service Representative in the Airport’s Guest Experience Unit resolving in-person customer service inquiries and reporting facility issues.
