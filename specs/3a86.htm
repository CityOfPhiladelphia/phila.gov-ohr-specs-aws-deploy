CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

3A86
COMMUNICATIONS TECHNICAL SPECIALIST

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is engineering technical work designing and installing basic public address communication systems and repairing communications equipment. The employee in this class also prepares contract specifications and engineering drawings in conjunction with system design.

Work is performed under the general supervision of a technical superior. Assignments require evaluative thinking and are performed in accordance with accepted communications standards and applicable regulations. The employee in this class is responsible for the design, completion and installation of small-scale communications engineering projects. Work involves some disagreeable aspects and requires moderate physical effort.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Determines wiring and equipment connection and placement for both permanent and temporary public address systems; prepares sketches and plans for use by self or others; prepares contract specifications for communications equipment and materials.
  • Designs electrical and electrical-mechanical devices at requests of other city departments; assembles and installs electrical and electrical-mechanical devices such as wiring for temporary lighting and electrical doors.
  • Examines defective communication equipment and systems such as the Laserfax Fingerprint System, loud speakers, closed circuit television cameras and screens, switches and amplifiers to determine the need of simple or extensive repair; repairs systems and equipment which can be done on the spot; assigns extended repair jobs to repair shop.
  • Prepares scaled pencil and/or ink drawings for communications projects.
  • Analyzes needs of assigned communications work in terms of equipment and materials; recommends purchase of specific type and quantities of equipment and materials.
  • Reviews applications by city departments for federal radio licenses; checks records for radio frequency assignments on other licenses; recommends refusal of license applications which will interfere with existing radio frequencies.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the basic theories, practices and techniques of communications engineering technology.
  • the methods, materials, tools and practices used in the maintenance and repair of a variety of communications equipment.
  • the occupational hazards and safety precautions of the communications equipment mechanic trade.
  • the operation and use of gas and electric welding equipment and the acetylene torch.
  • communications engineering mathematics and standard tables as required by assignments.
  • the nomenclature, technical symbols, equipment and materials used in communications technology.
  • principles, practices, techniques and instruments of drafting as required for work assignments.

SKILL IN:

  • the use and care of tools and equipment used in the work.

ABILITY TO:

  • prepare, read and interpret technical plans, layouts, charts, diagrams, schematics, maps, specifications, estimates and field inspection reports appropriate to communications technology.
  • disassemble, repair and reassemble a variety of communications equipment.
  • read and interpret blueprints and sketches.
  • use electrical testing devices to locate defects in the operation of communications and related equipment.
  • assign and review the work of a small group of employees engaged in the installation, maintenance and repair of a variety of communications equipment.
  • estimate time and materials needed for the work.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, contractors and representatives of other city departments.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 10/79.)
  • Education equivalent to the completion of the twelfth school grade.
  • Five years of experience installing, maintaining and repairing communication systems and equipment, two years of which shall have been at the journeyman level.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Communications Technical Specialist if required by work assignment.
