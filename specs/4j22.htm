CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

4J22
ASBESTOS PROJECT REVIEW OFFICER

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory and technical plans examination work for asbestos abatement projects. An employee in this class provides city-wide technical review and oversight of asbestos project layouts, plans, and work methods submitted by contractors to determine conformance with City, State and Federal requirements for asbestos work. An employee in this class also reviews contractor applications for major asbestos project permits, annual permits, and certifications. Contact with contractors, project consultants, and building owners to explain procedures and insure compliance with asbestos abatement regulations, and supervision of project site evaluation are significant aspects of the work. Work includes occasional visits to potentially hazardous asbestos abatement sites which requires wearing appropriate protective apparel. Work is performed under the supervision of a technical superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • An employee in this class must supervise lower-level technical staff.
  • An employee in this class must provide technical oversight on a city-wide basis for asbestos abatement projects.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Supervises inspectional staff performing on-site evaluation of asbestos projects; participates in on-site evaluations of complex asbestos projects; receives, examines, and evaluates layouts, plans, and work methods submitted by contractors applying for permits to perform asbestos abatement projects; approves contractor submissions conforming to established technical criteria and applicable city, state and federal ordinances, laws and regulations; disapproves contractor submissions deviating from standards.
  • Interprets regulations, ordinances and laws and explains to contractors and project consultants; recommends revisions to bring plans into conformance with standards; consults with higher level technical personnel on complex plans, if necessary.
  • Explains permit procedures and asbestos regulations to contractors, project consultants and building owners; supervises subordinate staff providing technical information by telephone; prepares responses to requests for information relating to technical standards and applicable regulations for asbestos projects; speaks to professional groups on notification and permit procedures for asbestos projects.
  • Checks permit applications for asbestos projects to assure that appropriate fees are being submitted; reviews applications by contractors to become certified asbestos contractors; reviews appeals to decisions regarding non-conformance or denial of permits; testifies in court enforcement proceedings; issues violation notices and recommends additional enforcement steps.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the City Asbestos Projects Ordinance and regulations, the applicable portions of the Philadelphia Health Code and applicable State and Federal laws and regulations.
  • the methods, materials, practices, equipment and principles used in asbestos abatement projects.

ABILITY TO:

  • read and interpret asbestos project layouts, plans, blueprints, and work methods presented by contractors applying for permits.
  • supervise subordinate technical staff.
  • determine appropriate revisions necessary to bring plans into conformance with established technical criteria and applicable regulations.
  • enforce technical standards and applicable laws, codes, and regulations firmly, tactfully and impartially.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, contractors and project consultants.
  • express ideas, both orally and in writing.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/89.)

EDUCATION

  • Education equivalent to the twelfth school grade.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE
  • One year of experience inspecting and investigating pollution-generating industrial equipment for a municipal agency or performing work which includes removal, encapsulation, or enclosure of asbestos in asbestos abatement projects.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE
  • Two years of full-time technical field work inspecting and investigating asbestos project sites for conformance with municipal, state and federal codes, laws, and regulations.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of certification from an Environmental Protection Agency approved asbestos training course in Inspection and Management Planner or a certification from a City of Philadelphia approved asbestos training course.
