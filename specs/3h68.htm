CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

3H68
MICROBIOLOGY SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is supervisory medical technology work directing technical laboratory personnel engaged in diagnostic laboratory tests in the analysis of human tissue and body fluid samples in the area of microbiology. An employee in this class is responsible for scheduling and assigning test workload, monitoring laboratory supplies, performing diagnostic tests, evaluating and implementing new testing techniques, and ensuring the accuracy of test results. Supervision and training of lower level personnel, analyzing data using laboratory standards and applicable regulations, and contact with other laboratory and clinical program personnel are significant aspects of the work. Work is performed under the direction of a technical superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • The employee in this class must supervise and train lower level staff and participate with technical laboratory personnel in performing diagnostic tests in the area of microbiology.
  • The position must be located in the Microbiology Section of the Public Health Laboratory.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Supervises laboratory staff and participates in performing diagnostic tests; reviews work results of subordinates by analysis of data found through periodic retesting, observation of test records, and reports of work performed; evaluates the performance of technical personnel and reviews procedures used; consults in the solution of any testing problems encountered.
  • Researches test modifications of existing techniques by searching literature, conferring with superior, planning experiments, interpreting results, and standardizing final methods or procedures evolved; reviews methods and procedures regularly used in standard determinations to ascertain that they are adequate.
  • Calibrates, operates, and maintains laboratory instruments equipment; oversees the preparation of media, solutions, standard reagents and stains; performs quality control procedures; monitors supply of media and requisitions supplies, equipment and materials used in the work; monitors and assures safety measures are observed when handling biohazardous agents.
  • Ensures that all remedial actions are taken whenever test systems deviate from the performance specifications and the patient results are not reported until corrective action have been taken and the test system is functioning properly.
  • Performs complex tests and examinations having a large number of variables and non-standardized techniques.
  • Maintains records; participates in training new staff.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices, methods and current developments in the field of clinical/environmental microbiology.
  • the use, operation and care of standard laboratory equipment and materials.
  • research methods and techniques including the statistical treatment of scientific data.
  • laboratory management including the recommendation of equipment, assignment of personnel and determination of work priorities.

SKILL IN:

  • the use of laboratory equipment in the performance of microbiological laboratory testing procedures.

ABILITY TO:

  • simplify, improve, adapt and standardize tests and laboratory procedures.
  • make accurate observations and interpretations of test results and to treat test data statistically in interpreting results.
  • plan and supervise the work of laboratory personnel engaged in performing diagnostic examinations.
  • prepare technical reports and to supervise the maintenance of test reports and records.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 1/96.)
    EDUCATION:
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in medical technology, biology, immunology, serology, microbiology, chemistry or other closely related field in the chemical, physical, or biological sciences.

    AND

    GENERAL EXPERIENCE:

  • One year of medical technology experience performing diagnostic laboratory tests.

    AND

    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:

  • Two years of recent experience at the full performance level performing microbiologic diagnostic tests in a clinical laboratory.

NOTE: The specific experience must have been obtained no more than five years prior to the date on which examination eligibility is based.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included the education and the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
