CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

3E53
ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM SUPERVISOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is economic development work at the supervisory level directing a major economic development program designed to revitalize economic conditions in distressed areas of the city. An employee in this class develops projects, prepares applications for project funding, supervises the activities of program monitors, and monitors contracted program activities. Contact with and coordination of the efforts of other governmental and quasi-governmental agencies, the private business sector, and community organizations, groups and residents is a major significance to the work. An employee in this class is supervised by a Deputy Commerce Director or an Assistant Commerce Director.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Develops economic development projects and strategies designed to revitalize business and industry, create and retain jobs, and improve quality of life in state and city designated low and moderate income areas; analyzes existing conditions and programs; prepares descriptions of economic development project objectives, financing, schedules, and estimates of impact on target areas; determines governmental and private sources of funding for projects; notifies quasi-governmental agencies and private developers of economic development program funding available for use in targeted areas.
  • Reviews proposals requesting funding of economic development projects; conducts financial feasibility analysis of project proposals; evaluates potential for revitalization of business in targeted areas; estimates governmental and private funding which can be generated for economic development projects; determines if projects are appropriate for targeted areas.
  • Proposes and markets economic development projects designed to promote business, industry, and employment in the city; conducts exploratory meetings with developers; reviews and approves applications for funding prepared by developers, businesses, community organizations and delegate agencies; evaluates developers' proposals; presents projects for departmental approval and arranges for their review by other City agencies; prepares appropriate legislative resolutions and conducts public hearings; prepares draft and final grant applications to the federal and state funding sources; provides background information on projects for reviewing officials of City, State and Federal government; reviews contracts submitted by developers and delegate agencies.
  • Monitors contracted development and rehabilitation work in targeted areas; conducts inspections of projects to insure compliance with contract provisions; reviews reports submitted by delegate agencies and private developers.
  • Develops, reviews and approves plans for establishing new or strengthening existing business associations and community organizations in targeted areas; informs businesses in targeted areas of available city services; resolves disputes between businesses and city agencies; evaluates city services being provided and recommends improvements or expansion of services that could promote business activity in targeted areas; arranges provision of city services in targeted areas; provides technical assistance to business associations and neighborhood organizations; meets with neighborhood organizations, community groups and individual residents in targeted areas to discuss projects, gain community support and elicit suggestions; encourages cooperation between area business, organizations and residents.
  • Supervises a group of employees engaged in the planning, implementing, analyzing and monitoring of projects; reviews work of subordinates; provides advice and direction.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles and methods used in revitalizing private industry and commerce in economically-depressed urban areas.
  • the organization, functions, procedures, and resources of City agencies, boards and commissions, quasi- governmental agencies, and private organizations whose activities affect urban area economic development programs.
  • sources of funding for urban economic revitalization and area improvement.
  • funding source rules, regulations, and guidelines in the application for and administration of commercial, industrial and area improvement projects which are both publicly and privately financed.
  • the principles of economics and the techniques of economic analysis.
  • real estate principles and practices used in industrial and commercial revitalization projects.
  • legislation regulating area economic development activities.

ABILITY TO:

  • understand and relate to the motivations and goals of private developers, businesses, community groups and organizations and individual residents of targeted areas.
  • understand and relate to the needs of the public for commercial and industrial revitalization projects.
  • analyze complex problems and reach sound conclusions.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, civic, community, and business groups, and the general public.
  • present ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 3/86.)
  • Completion of a bachelor's degree program at an accredited college or university.
  • Four years of experience in economic development project analysis, planning or monitoring, two years of which shall have been at the full performance level.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
