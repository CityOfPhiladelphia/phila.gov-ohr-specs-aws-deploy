CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

5B32
JUVENILE JUSTICE OPERATIONS DIRECTOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is managerial juvenile justice work directing the provision of a full range of services for juveniles in detention. The employee in this class directs, through subordinate managers, a large staff engaged in providing custody, counseling, recreation, social service, medical, food service and ancillary services for youths in a City detention facility. Work involves developing, interpreting and implementing juvenile justice related policies and procedures and ensuring facility compliance with appropriate regulations and legal mandates. In the absence of the Executive Director, the employee assumes responsibility for the Division of Juvenile Justice Services. Contact with public and private officials, contracted service providers and community groups and organizations to discuss juvenile justice system issues and regulations is a major aspect of the work. The employee reports to the Executive Director, Juvenile Justice Services Division.

ALLOCATING FACTORS:
(The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • This is a one position class in the Department of Human Services.
  • The employee is responsible for the day-to-day operations of the City's youth detention facility.
  • The employee must direct a large variety of staff performing child care, custody, counseling, support service and facility management functions.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Plans, directs, and evaluates, through subordinate supervisors, the operational activities of a large youth detention facility; establishes staff performance objectives and controls; analyses operational and managerial problems; initiates organizational and/or procedural changes to resolve these problems; directs the formulation of staffing and overtime schedules for shift coverage based on security and care needs at the facility; instructs subordinate managers in pertinent policies, practices and procedures; identifies, initiates, evaluates and approves in-service and contracted training programs for staff; ensures that facility operations conform to sound detention principles and statutory and administrative rules.
  • Directs a large residential service staff engaged in security and counseling functions; coordinates staff activities to ensure that security and resident care needs are being met; discusses resident problems with staff and directs the implementation of appropriate procedures and actions through subordinate detention managers; makes decisions on the required use of interventional measures and techniques in resident disturbances; directs staff in responding to riotous or hazardous conditions; participates in the investigation of allegations of resident abuse; determines and approves disciplinary actions.
  • Directs, through subordinate managers and supervisors, a variety of support service functions including building maintenance, food service, volunteer services, recreation, housekeeping, social service, records maintenance, medical care, and educational services; confers with subordinates to develop plans, priorities and policies to ensure the coordination of all program components and services; issues directives and policy statements to implement new and revised programs and to effect internal reorganization; participates in contract negotiations and develops agreements with private agencies for the provision of medical, evaluative and counseling services to juveniles in detention; monitors and coordinates the activities of these contracted agencies to ensure compliance with department's objectives and mission.
  • Directs or participates in committees, conferences, and meetings with public and private agencies to develop more effective programs for children and youth within the Juvenile Justice system; confers with appropriate federal, state and local officials on the formulation and coordination of program objectives and applications of regulatory standards; meets with union officials to discuss operational and disciplinary issues; provides cost data and statistical analyses to departmental administrators for budget requests and justifications; interprets and explains programs to the community and news media through conferences with individuals and groups and addresses to various organizations.
  • Directs the activities of the Juvenile Justice Services Division in the absence of the Executive Director.
  • Performs

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the management principles, practices, and techniques applied in operating a youth detention facility.
  • the philosophy and objectives underlying detention of juveniles.
  • laws and regulations pertaining to the custody and care of detained youths.
  • the principles, practices, and procedures of public administration.
  • the juvenile justice system as it relates to youth in detention.
  • the behavior patterns and group dynamics of youth in detention.
  • short term counseling, brief intervention techniques, and detention programming.
  • statistical analysis, budgeting, and fiscal analysis.
  • the functions, resources, and management of public and private agencies detaining juveniles.
  • current developments in the field of juvenile detention.
  • the legal provisions and regulations applicable to the delivery of services to detained juveniles.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
  • the social and emotional factors which result in the detention of youths.

SKILL IN:

  • evaluating the effectiveness of administrative and operational programs and in implementing changes to provide more effective and efficient operations.

ABILITY TO:

  • plan, organize, direct, and coordinate the activities of a large staff providing detention services and counseling for delinquent youth.
  • coordinate program activities in a manner that is designed to meet objectives and goals.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with juvenile justice system personnel, other public officials, civic and community groups and the general public.
  • exercise sound judgement and discretion in applying and interpreting policies and procedures and in making decisions consistent with policies and objectives.
  • express ideas effectively, both orally and in writing.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with associates, pubic and private officials, civic groups, community groups, the judiciary, and the general public.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 6/91.)

EDUCATION:

  • Completion of a master's degree program at an accredited college or university with major course work in Social Work, Social Work Administration, Criminal Justice, Public Administration or a related field.
AND
GENERAL EXPERIENCE:
  • Three years of experience in the youth services field.
AND
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE:
  • Four years of experience supervising, through subordinate supervisors, the care and custody of delinquent youth in a youth detention facility, and involved directing residential and/or ancillary service programs.
Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department that has included a master's degree and the specific experience described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.
