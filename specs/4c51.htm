CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

4C51
CORRECTIONAL HEALTH PROGRAM COORDINATOR

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is health care program compliance and supervisory work planning developing, coordinating and evaluating the provision of health care services performed by contracted agencies to inmates in the City's correctional system. The employee in this class develops, implements and monitors policies, standards and procedures for a multi-discipline health care program for inmates. Supervising nursing and technical staff in the monitoring of inmate medical treatment plans, and contact with officials of regulatory agencies and health service agency administrators to resolve problems and insure compliance of standards are of major significance to the work. Work is performed under the general direction of an administrative superior. The incumbent in this position has controlling responsibility for medical services carried out in the city's correctional institutions.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be approved for this class.)

  • The position must perform health care program compliance and coordination work, planning, reviewing, evaluating and monitoring the provision of health care to patients in the City's correctional system.
  • The position must supervise nursing and technical staff.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

  • Develops and initiates new programs and standards for evaluation; researches needs for new programs through meetings with staff members and provider agency officials; reviews agency plans for integration with existing city plans and priorities.
  • Oversees the selection and placement process; reviews overall program goals to determine applicability to projected needs of the City's inmate population.
  • Formulates policies, standards and procedures governing the provision of health care programs administered by contracted agencies; provides advisory services to officials of public and private agencies in the planning, development and implementation of services.
  • Evaluates the provision of patient services in accordance with program policy.
  • Monitors and reviews provision of patient services.
  • Evaluates and ensures the application of health care principles and practices and applicable professional standards, and makes recommendations to improve standards of care as well as the quantity and quality of patient services; reviews the work performance of the health services delivery staff; conducts meetings to discuss changes in procedures and to resolve problems.
  • Reviews reports submitted by contracted agencies and subordinates; consults with physicians; attends staff meetings; implements standards for nursing and patient care; makes recommendations for facility changes to bring nursing and patient care up to desired standards; makes personal inspection of clinic and infirmary areas.
  • Reviews selected records to ascertain whether services were provided and recorded properly; consults with medical, administrative and professional personnel concerning the interpretation of records and appropriateness of services provided.
  • Keeps informed of, and evaluates federal, state and private sources for funding of services and research; interprets funding laws and regulations and advises health and welfare agencies in procedures for submitting applications.
  • Monitors the provision of health services in the City's correctional system in accordance with policies to promote compatibility and continuity of care; monitors and reviews provisions of patient services.
  • Directs, through subordinate nursing personnel the review of patient care programs at the institutions; determines the specific functions to be performed by nursing personnel within the program; assigns duties to subordinate nursing and technical staff; reviews reports from subordinate personnel and confers with such personnel concerning problems of nursing and patient care services; discusses with subordinates infractions of departmental procedures and insures that corrective actions are taken; institutes disciplinary action if necessary.
  • Develops training programs for subordinate nursing, administrative and technical personnel; instructs subordinates and implements changes to improve training programs; evaluates necessity for revising nursing procedures and implements new procedures to improve patient care efficiency and effectiveness.
  • Attends administrative staff conferences to coordinate the patient care activities performed by subordinates with security and institutional regulations; maintains contact with other departments and health agencies for the exchange of information relating to health care practices, education, recruitment and in-service training.
  • Discusses with subordinates' infractions of departmental procedures and insures that corrective actions are taken, responds to patient and public concerns and questions regarding program services and addresses problems from outside agencies and hospitals.
  • Completes appropriate reports; meets with patients and their representatives, contractors, subcontractors and managers; maintains records of nursing and medical treatment and related services as required by the nursing staff.
  • Performs related work as required.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGES, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

KNOWLEDGE OF:

  • the principles, practices and techniques of program and policy development as applied to the care and treatment of inmates.
  • the administrative and supervisory principles and practices involved in patient care and nursing administration.
  • the systems, methods and techniques of comprehensive patient care in a correctional setting.
  • applicable regulations and laws pertaining to the provision of medical services to inmates.
  • medical, social and support services as they apply to correctional health care.
  • supervisory methods and techniques.
  • prison security, practices and policies pertaining to the operation of a nursing and medical services program.
  • current developments in the field of nursing with emphasis on the administrative aspects as they pertain to a clinic operation.
  • in-service teaching principles and methods as they apply to nursing.
ABILITY TO:

  • evaluate and resolve health service delivery problems within a correctional setting.
  • gather information on service programs and operations to analyze and evaluate service data for compliance with contract terms.
  • write policies pertaining to the provision of medical services to inmates.
  • prepare detailed written reports of findings, conclusions and recommendations and to express ideas effectively both orally and in writing.
  • plan, organize and supervise nursing and patient care service for a penal institution.
  • perform a variety of administrative and supervisory tasks related to the operation of the nursing and patient care services.
  • interpret local, state and federal laws, regulations and ordinances and to resolve related agency problems.
  • perform a variety of administrative and supervisory tasks related to the operation of the nursing and patient care service.
  • prepare and analyze reports as they pertain to nursing and patient care services.
  • maintain a sympathetic attitude toward and harmonious relationships with patients and to develop such attitudes in the employees supervised.
  • supervise the implementation and maintenance of adequate records relating to nursing and patient care services.
  • establish and maintain effective working relationships with Federal, State and local agencies officials, staff associates, and court officials.

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE

(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 5/1995.)

EDUCATION

  • Graduation from an accredited school of nursing.
GENERAL EXPERIENCE

  • Two years of nursing experience.
SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

  • Two years supervising a small nursing service in a correctional institution.

Or any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department which has included the specific experience and education described above.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS, AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Eligibility for a license as a registered nurse by the Pennsylvania State Board of Nursing Education and Licensure at the time of application and possession of such licensure within time limits specified by the department.
