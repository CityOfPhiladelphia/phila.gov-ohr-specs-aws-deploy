CITY OF PHILADELPHIA PERSONNEL DEPARTMENT

6G02
HOUSING AND FIRE INSPECTOR 3

Specialties
(General ) (Court Resolution )

GENERAL DEFINITION

This is advanced level inspectional and code compliance work performing citywide inspections of residential and mixed use occupancy high-rise buildings to determine compliance with the provisions of Property Maintenance and Fire Prevention Codes, related ordinances and regulations. Work is distinguished from the next lower level class in the series by the complexity of assignments, the inspection of fire suppression systems and testifying in court regarding code violations.

Contact with property owners, building managers, tenants landlords, and/or the appropriate representatives to facilitate code compliance and ensure the safety of the public is a significant aspect of the work. Work involves some disagreeable aspects and is performed under the supervision of an administrative or technical superior.

ALLOCATING FACTORS: (The following conditions must be met for a position to be allocated to this class.)

  • Employees must perform advanced level inspectional and code compliance work performing city-wide inspections of residential and mixed use occupancy high-rise buildings which include fire suppression systems to determine compliance with the provisions of Property Maintenance and Fire Prevention Codes, related ordinances and regulations.
  • Work must be performed in the Housing Code Enforcement Unit of the Department of Licenses and Inspections.

TYPICAL EXAMPLES OF WORK (ILLUSTRATIVE ONLY)

GENERAL SPECIALTY

  • Receives list of high-rise, residential and mixed use properties to be inspected; searches automated systems for ownership records, case history, and related information; proceeds to site; shows proper identification to landlord, operator, manager, owner or tenant; inspects facilities for conformance to codes, standards and ordinances such as the Philadelphia Property Maintenance and Fire Prevention Codes, their related standards and various local ordinances and regulations; determines nature of inspection and reviews applicable codes; reviews pertinent information such as approved variances, and previous inspection reports; makes arrangements with the appropriate representative to perform inspection.
  • Inspects fire exits to ensure that they are clean, unobstructed, illuminated and that signs are properly displayed; checks storage and utility areas for fire hazards and Fire Department inspection tags; determines that stairways are equipped with self-closing doors and are fire safe; ascertains that other sections of the building have required fire resistant materials; locates all extinguishers and insures that there are a sufficient number for each floor, that they are in working condition and are properly mounted; ensures operation of fire safety equipment; inspects smoking assembly areas to insure conformance to housing and fire codes; reviews type and conditions of fire fighting equipment, means of egress, fire resistant materials and doors, type of ventilation and other pertinent information.
  • Initiates and observes tests of smoke evacuation and pressure-differential systems; observes testing of sprinkler and standpipe systems to insure that they meet fire code standards.
  • Completes inspection reports describing conditions found during field investigations; cites violations of the Property Maintenance and Fire Prevention Codes and related regulations and ordinances; confers with property owners and tenants to explain the conditions and hazards found; makes recommendations for specific actions to correct violations; informs violators of consequences of not taking corrective action; may effect compliance by ordering the immediate evacuation of occupants upon notification of the appropriate authorities.
  • Conducts re-inspections to insure that corrective action is taken; testifies in court regarding violations when necessary.
  • Performs duties as those typically assigned in the Housing and Fire Inspector II class specification.
  • Performs related work as required.
COURT RESOLUTION SPECIALTY

  • Performs the duties described above.
  • Conducts re-inspections of residential and mixed-use properties to provide evidence in court on code violations; photographs areas of premises not meeting codes.
  • Prepares cases for court; schedules cases, attends court hearings; testifies at administrative hearings, Tax Review Board Hearings, testifies in Municipal Court, Common Pleas Court and state courts on code violations and conditions found during re-inspections.
  • Executes court orders; notifies various departments and services for assistance when necessary; may notify the Office of Emergency Services (OESS) to relocate occupants if necessary; may notify the Police Department for protection if threatened; may notify the Clean and Seal Unit to secure the property; may notify the Department of Health if there are health violations; may notify the Sheriff’s Department to serve warrants and execute certain court orders.
  • Attends training sessions for the International Fire Codes and International Building Codes; attends training sessions to provide expert testimony in court resolution matters; receives state certifications as a Code Specialist.

REQUIRED KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS AND ABILITIES

    KNOWLEDGE OF:

    • Property Maintenance and Fire Prevention Codes, related ordinances, and regulations as they apply to residential occupancies and buildings/structures
    • Fire Prevention Codes, ordinances, and regulations as they apply to the inspection of high rise buildings
    • methods and techniques of dealing with the general public in situations requiring the interpretation of codes, and the inspection of facilities and dwellings
    • sprinkler and other fire suppression systems
    • the uses and applications of computers pertinent to Property Maintenance and Fire Prevention Code enforcement operations

    SKILL IN:

    • recognizing deviations from the provisions of applicable codes, ordinances and regulations
    • obtaining information by interview, observation and investigation
    • recording accurate details

    ABILITY TO:

    • learn the fire provisions of federal codes as promulgated by the Occupational Safety and Health Administration
    • learn the applicable accepted codes and standards of fire safety as promulgated by the International Code Council, National Fire Protection Association, National Board of Fire Underwriters and similar associations
    • make accurate decisions regarding the appropriate course of action based upon facts uncovered
    • enforce codes, ordinances, and regulations firmly, tactfully and impartially
    • use a considerable degree of tact, diplomacy and judgment when dealing with the general public
    • make visual and physical inspections
    • provide information clearly and concisely
    • communicate effectively both orally and in writing
    • maintain records and prepare reports
    • establish and maintain effective working relationships with property owners, building managers, tenants, landlords, other city agencies and representatives, associates and the general public
    • perform research necessary to present effective testimony in court
    • effectively present testimony in court

MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE TRAINING AND EXPERIENCE
(The following statement represents the minimum training and experience standards which will be used to admit or reject applicants for tests. Applications submitted by candidates for this class will be reviewed based on training and experience requirements as approved on 6/05.)

BOTH SPECIALTIES

    EDUCATION

    • Education equivalent to the completion of the twelfth school grade.

    AND
    GENERAL EXPERIENCE

    • Two years of field inspectional and investigative experience which has included at least one year inspecting houses, other residential buildings and/or residential day care facilities for conformance to Property Maintenance and Fire Prevention Codes and related ordinances and citing violations of these codes.

    AND

    GENERAL SPECIALTY

    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

    • Two years of experience inspecting personal care facilities such as rooming houses, boarding homes, shelters, institutional care facilities and other residential buildings for conformance with Property Maintenance and Fire Prevention Codes and related ordinances to insure the health, welfare and safety of the residents of the facility.

    OR

    COURT RESOLUTION SPECIALTY

    SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE

    • Two years of experience testifying in court resolution matters related to housing inspections and inspecting houses, other residential buildings and/or residential day care facilities for conformance to Property Maintenance and Fire Prevention Codes and related ordinances and citing violations of these codes.

    OR

    Any equivalent combination of education and experience determined to be acceptable by the Personnel Department, which has included the specific experience.

PHYSICAL AND MEDICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Ability to physically perform the duties and to work in the environmental conditions required of a position in this class.

LICENSES, REGISTRATIONS AND/OR CERTIFICATES

  • Possession of a valid proper class motor vehicle operator's license as issued by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania prior to appointment and during tenure of employment as a Housing and Fire Inspector 3 if required by work assignment.
